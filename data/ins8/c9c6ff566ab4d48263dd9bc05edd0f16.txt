Some businesses will be trialling a 4-day work week (Image: Unsplash.com)The pilot will run for six months from June 6 and won't reduce employees' pay - all while giving them a three-day weekend every week. It works on the basis that everyone involved in the scheme will get 100% of their salary for 80% of the hours, but with 100% productivity.More than 60 firms with around 3,000 members of staff have signed up to take part in the pilot, reports the Mirror, which is being run by campaign group 4 Day Week Global.
