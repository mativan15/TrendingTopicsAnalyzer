Epsilon Carbon subsidiary is scouting investors for EV cell material biz
indiatimes.com
energy
2022-10-21 08:00:00

Mumbai: Epsilon Advanced Materials, a subsidiary of Epsilon Carbon, is looking to raise about Rs 1,200 crore from investors to fund its operations to manufacture components for lithium-ion cells used in electric vehicles (EV) and other consumer products, a top executive said. The company makes graphite anodes or negative terminals of lithium-ion cells. It has invested about Rs 300 crore in this business so far and is now looking for external investment, according to Vikram Handa, managing director of Epsilon Carbon.
