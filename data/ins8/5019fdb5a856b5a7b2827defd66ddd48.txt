Ireland's deputy premier Leo Varadkar has held a series of meetings with his British counterparts to discuss bilateral issues and to 'deepen the relationship' between Ireland and the UK, his spokesman has said.The Fine Gael leader travelled to London on Wednesday to hold a number of bilateral meetings while on a 'trade mission' to the UK.As part of the trip, Mr Varadkar met with the British deputy prime minister Therese Coffey, and the Foreign Secretary, James Cleverly.Later on Wednesday afternoon he met with Labour Party leader Sir Keir Starmer.
