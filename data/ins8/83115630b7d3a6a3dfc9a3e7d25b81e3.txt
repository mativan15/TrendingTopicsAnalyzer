The lessons from the powerhouse but ill-fated 1972-73 Celtics team
bostonglobe.com
news
2022-10-21 17:57:37

The 1972-73 team was coming off a 56-win season that had ended abruptly in a five-game loss to the Knicks. It was evident that they needed a little more oomph up front — specifically to contend with the great Dave DeBusschere — and Red Auerbach had addressed that issue by obtaining 29-year-old Paul Silas from Phoenix in exchange for the NBA rights to ABA expatriate Charlie Scott. The 6-foot-7-inch Silas was a superb rebounder and defender who was fresh from a career year in Phoenix. Whereas once strictly a bruiser, he had shed 30-some pounds and could now get up and down the floor, making him exponentially more useful.
