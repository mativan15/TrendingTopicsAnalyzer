It should come as little surprise to this cynical nation that advance hype for anything – a movie, TV series, book or even political initiative – too often raises expectations to unrealistic levels, leading to disillusionment.
So it proved in the case of devolution, the great constitutional experiment of the New Labour years. Even Tony Blair was known to harbour reservations about granting partial autonomy to Scotland and Wales, but recognised that nearly two decades of Labour promises could not be discarded once in office.
