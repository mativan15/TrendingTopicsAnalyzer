The top 32 teams in each of the state's four divisions, based on the MIAA power rankings system, qualified for the tournament. Teams outside of the top 32 that had a .500 record or better also qualified.

In Division 3, Newburyport, Pentucket and Triton came in seeded Nos. 8-9-10, respectively, and will all host a first-round game. The Clippers (12-6) will welcome No. 25 Swampscott on Thursday at 4 p.m., while the Vikings (10-8) will also play on Thursday when they host CAL foe North Reading at 5 p.
