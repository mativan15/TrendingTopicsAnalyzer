After allowing 14 goals in two games, Wild concerned, not alarmed
startribune.com
news
2022-10-16 22:15:40

No one in the NHL has been scored on more than the Wild.

Trips to the penalty box outnumber their goal celebrations while their own net has been tumultuous, what with their starting goaltender already getting pulled.

Oh, and the Wild haven't even reached their toughest assignment of this season-opening four-game homestand.

That's on Monday when the reigning Stanley Cup champion Avalanche stops by Xcel Energy Center to rekindle its Central Division rivalry with the Wild.

"We've got a good Colorado team coming in with a high-end offense," Mats Zuccarello said.
