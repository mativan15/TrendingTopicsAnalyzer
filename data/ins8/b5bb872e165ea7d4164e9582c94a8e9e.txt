(Bloomberg) -- The long-simmering idea that the US government should stand ready to buy back Treasury securities from investors in order to improve market functioning is moving closer to reality at a surprising clip.

While the Treasury Department has done buybacks in the past -- most recently in the 2000 to 2002 period -- and while its industry advisers since then have urged it to consider establishing a program, steps taken in that direction last week were more than experts anticipated.
