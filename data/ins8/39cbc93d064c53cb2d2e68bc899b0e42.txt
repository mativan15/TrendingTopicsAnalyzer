India's reputation as the 'pharmacy of the developing world', built assiduously by the country's pharmaceutical companies since the time of the Aids epidemic, is in serious jeopardy. The World Health Organization (WHO) sounded the alarm last week when it said the deaths of 66 children in the Gambia could be linked to adulterated cough syrup manufactured by the Indian pharmaceutical company Maiden Pharmaceuticals.Adulteration of cough syrup with diethylene glycol has been a known issue since 1937, when more than 100 Americans died after consuming a contaminated cough medicine manufactured by a US company.
