Mr Darling, who oversaw the Labour government's response to the 2008 financial crash while chancellor between 2007 and 2010, said capability to manage the current economic turmoil is 'completely absent'.He told BBC Scotland's Sunday Show that what happened in 2008 'was not self-inflicted'.He added: 'It was in continental Europe and the European Union, it was in America, it was right across the world.People need to have confidence that they know you know what you're doing, and that you're capable of seeing this through.
