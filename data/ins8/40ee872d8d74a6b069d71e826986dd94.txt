Published: 06:09 EDT, 19 October 2022 | Updated: 06:09 EDT, 19 October 2022 By Dharamraj Lalit Dhutia MUMBAI, Oct 19 (Reuters) - Indian government bond yields ended higher on Wednesday, reversing intraday moves, as U.S. yields rose and a stronger dollar pushed the rupee to a record low. The benchmark Indian 10-year government bond yield ended at 7.4510%. The yield had fallen to 7.3886% earlier in the day and had ended at 7.4261% on Tuesday.
