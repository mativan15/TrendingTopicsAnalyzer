By Reuters Published: 12:53 EDT, 31 May 2022 | Updated: 12:53 EDT, 31 May 2022 By Sarah N. Lynch WASHINGTON, May 31 (Reuters) - A New York couple accused of laundering $4.5 billion in cryptocurrency tied to the 2016 hack of digital currency exchange Bitfinex are still negotiating a possible plea deal while reviewing more than 1.1 gigabytes of evidence in the case, prosecutors said. Ilya "Dutch" Lichtenstein, 34, and his wife, Heather Morgan, 32, the self-proclaimed "Crocodile of Wall Street," were due to appear in a federal court in Washington this coming Friday.
