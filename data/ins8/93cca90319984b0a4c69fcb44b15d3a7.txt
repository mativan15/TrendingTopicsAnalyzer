Liz Truss is eligible for a taxpayer-funded annual $200,000 allowance for life
smh.com.au
world
2022-10-22 02:53:52

London: Liz Truss, the departing British prime minister, is eligible for a taxpayer-funded allowance capped at £115,000 ($203,000) a year for the rest of her life.Despite her short time in office, Truss became eligible on Thursday for what's called the Public Duty Costs Allowance — a government reimbursement plan for staff and salary costs incurred by former prime ministers 'arising from their special position in public life' after they leave office, according to the government's website.Liz Truss arrives in Downing Street in London, Friday, a day after resigning.
