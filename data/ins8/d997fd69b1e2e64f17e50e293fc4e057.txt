BMW is investing $1.7bn (roughly R31,141,705,000) in its US manufacturing hub in South Carolina to produce electric vehicles domestically, part of a global overhaul to prepare for a new generation of EVs.The German luxury carmaker is spending $bn (roughly R18,319,220,000) to retool its Spartanburg plant to build at least six new EV models by 2030 and $700m (roughly R12,828,627,000) to build a battery assembly facility and hire 300 people in nearby Woodruff. BMW also announced a deal with Chinese-owned Envision AESC to build a new battery cell plant in South Carolina to supply the project.
