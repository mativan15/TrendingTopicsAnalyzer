By Reuters Published: 09:40 EDT, 17 October 2022 | Updated: 09:40 EDT, 17 October 2022 By Sarah N. Lynch WASHINGTON, Oct 17 (Reuters) - The U.S. Justice Department on Monday asked a federal judge to sentence former President Donald Trump's adviser Steve Bannon to six months behind bars, saying he pursued a "bad faith strategy defiance and contempt" against the congressional committee probing the Jan. 6, 2021, attack on the Capitol. Bannon, an influential far-right political figure, was convicted in July on two counts of contempt of Congress for defying a subpoena.
