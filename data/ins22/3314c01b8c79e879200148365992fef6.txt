By Alice Gainer May 31, 2022 / 5:57 PM / CBS New York BRICK TOWNSHIP, N.J. - You've heard of them at golf courses and sports fields, but now one Jersey Shore town is installing lightning detectors to try and keep people safe this summer. This comes after two people were killed by lightning last summer in our area. As CBS2's Alice Gainer reports, a wooden pole will soon hold a lightning detection system. "At the very top will be the horn, as well as the blinking strobe lights," said Township of Brick Mayor John Ducey.
