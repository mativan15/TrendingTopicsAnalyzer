LISBON, Portugal -- Portugal has sent 146 Marines to join a NATO force stationed in Lithuania as part of efforts to bolster the alliance's eastern flank in the wake of Russia's invasion of Ukraine. The deployment includes divers specializing in deactivating mines and other explosive devices. Portugal's defence ministry said the aim of the mission is to "support high levels of readiness and discourage direct or indirect threats" against NATO members, especially in the Baltic Sea region.
