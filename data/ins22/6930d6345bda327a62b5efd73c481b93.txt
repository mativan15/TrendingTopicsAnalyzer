Published: 13:53 EDT, 30 May 2022 | Updated: 13:53 EDT, 30 May 2022 By Jan Lopatka PRAGUE, May 30 (Reuters) - Czech investment group PPF and MONETA Money Bank have walked away from a deal to combine their assets, the two said on Monday, terminating a proposal that would have created a major competitor to foreign-owned market leaders. The proposed deal comprised a 25.9 billion crown ($1.1 billion) takeover of PPF's banking assets in Air Bank and the Czech and Slovak operations of consumer lender Home Credit.
