Cost of living crisis: 1.8 million children facing poorer quality school meals as food costs rise
sky.com
news
2022-10-15 00:42:00

Some 1.8 million children face poorer quality school meals as a result of the rising cost of food, according to a new survey.LACA, the school caterers trade body, says food prices have risen by 30% since May 2022, resulting in 28% of school caterers now using more processed foods.
More than a third of its members are also considering switching from British to imported meat.Dersingham School in Newham, one of London's most deprived boroughs, is set to launch a new menu after the autumn half-term holiday.
