That means the "r" word is top of mind for many: recession. With the Fed poised to bump rates up aggressively for the sixth time this year, a soft landing -- a downturn that doesn't crush the economy -- is becoming less likely.

So, how do we know if we're in a recession? And are we already? Per one popular measure, the country is considered in a recession when gross domestic product, the value of all goods and services produced during a specific period, falls during two quarters back to back.
