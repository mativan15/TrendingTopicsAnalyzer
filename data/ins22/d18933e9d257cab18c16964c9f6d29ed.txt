A MAN who forked out £23,000 for a van he says was riddled with faults that started just 30 minutes after he drove it away managed to get his revenge.
Electrician Stewart Logan decided to buy a new work vehicle from the Kilmarnock, Ayrshire, Scotland, branch of Evans Halshaw after clocking up nearly 70,000 miles in his previous transit van.

Electrician Stewart Logan says an MOT station found six faults with his Citroen vanCredit: Kennedy News
Stewart says a salesman assured him the problems would be fixed when he took the van for a test driveCredit: Kennedy News
The 50-year-old took a second-hand Citroen Relay on a test drive in June and pointed out several faults that flashed up on the dashboard, including it needing an oil change and headlight bulbs not working.
