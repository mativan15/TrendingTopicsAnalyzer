maybefalse/iStock Unreleased via Getty Images Alibaba (NYSE:BABA) shares gave up more than 3% Friday as the Chinese Internet giant named a new top executive in Southeast Asia and the company's hometown government pledged a big investment in the area's e-commerce industry. Alibaba (BABA) said it named James Dong as chief executive officer of Lazada, the company's Southeast Asian business arm. Dong joined Lazada in 2018 and had been in charge of the division's business in Vietnam and Thailand. He also previously served as Alibaba's (BABA) head of globalization strategy and corporate development.
