The food in the fridge has gone off, there's no hot water and the TV won't switch on."I've got nothing," says Emma, a domestic abuse survivor in her early twenties.Emma (not her real name) has no gas or electricity in her supported accommodation, and is phoning a financial charity for advice.A debt adviser answers her call. "Hello, you're through to StepChange. My name is Lynsey Muff, how can I help?"Emma talks Lynsey through her budget - her bills, food, travel and clothing costs. She says she has about £216 a month to live on once the utility company has taken its advance from her Universal Credit benefit payment.
