Published: 04:42 EDT, 27 May 2022 | Updated: 05:22 EDT, 27 May 2022 TAIPEI, May 27 (Reuters) - Taiwan's economy is likely to grow at a slower pace this year that initially forecast, the statistics office said on Friday, downgrading its outlook due to global inflation and COVID-19 dampening consumer demand at home and abroad. The downward revision came even as the statistics office raised its export growth forecast for this year, with continued strong demand for the island's technology products buoyed by 5G, electric vehicles and high end computing.
