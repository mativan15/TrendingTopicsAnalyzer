Six months ago, Doug Heye wrote a Washington Post op-ed that didn't cause much of a stir at the time, but which continues to be relevant. Heye, a former Republican National Committee communications director and a former aide to then-House Majority Leader Eric Cantor, effectively delivered a warning to his party about its election conspiracy theories.As Heye saw it, Republicans seemed unaware of an inconvenient fact: The GOP's outlandish post-2020 'voter fraud' allegations could very easily be applied to Republican primaries in 2022.
