June 1, 2022 / 10:53 AM / AP Someone turning 65 has nearly a 7-in-10 chance of needing long-term care in the future, but don't have the savings to manage the cost of assisted living. One way to help pay for it is grabbing a reverse mortgage and using the equity in their mortgage-free home.Here's how to evaluate whether a reverse mortgage might be a good option.What is a reverse mortgage?A reverse mortgage is a loan on the assessed value of a home. Homeowners must be at least 62 to apply. 
 
If you have at least 50% to 55% equity in your home, you have a good chance of qualifying.
