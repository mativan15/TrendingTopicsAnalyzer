Sheryl Sandberg, Meta's COO, is stepping down from her role after 14 years at the social media company.
Getty Images for Step Up


It didn't take long after Sheryl Sandberg joined Facebook in 2008 for critics to say that she wasn't providing enough 'adult supervision' as the company faced its first major privacy scandals.
In the past several years, many have been quick to criticize Sandberg — long regarded as the No. 2 person at Facebook, now known as Meta — for the company's many mistakes. Those include everything from allowing Russian bots to spread propaganda ahead of the 2016 US presidential election to its role in the January 6 insurrection.
