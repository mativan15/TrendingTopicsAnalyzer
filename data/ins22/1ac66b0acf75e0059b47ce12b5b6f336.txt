And this year's Sveriges Riksbank Prize in Economic Sciences in Memory of Alfred Nobel has gone to Ben Bernanke, Douglas Diamond and Philip Dybvig for their research on banks and financial crises. With that announcement on Monday, the Nobel Prize committee put its official seal on the centrality of banks - and, by extension the financial sector - in modern economies. While there is no disputing the trio's stature as economists, the choice of the area for which their contributions have been recognised by the Nobel may seem a bit puzzling.
