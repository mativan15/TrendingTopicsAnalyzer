Colombian left-wing presidential candidate Gustavo Petro waves as he arrives for a debate in Bogota, on May 26.LUISA GONZALEZ/ReutersColombians will choose among a leftist promising change, a centre-right candidate seen as the natural successor to the incumbent leader, and an eccentric business magnate in a presidential vote on Sunday.Gustavo Petro, the leftist, has consistently led opinion polls, with around 40 per cent of voting intentions. If no one secures more than 50 per cent a runoff will take place on June 19, which surveys suggest Petro would win.
