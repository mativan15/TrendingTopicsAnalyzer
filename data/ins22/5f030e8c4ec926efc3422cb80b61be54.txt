A file photo of a dredger in PEI. The Hay River channel in the N.W.T. used to be dredged regularly. Since the federal government stopped the program in the mid-1990s, there's been a buildup of sediment, sometimes preventing boats from getting through. (Brian Higgins/CBC)An N.W.T. MLA is pleading with the territory's infrastructure minister to dredge the Hay River harbour. Rocky Simpson, who represents Hay River South, said the process of removing silt and mud from the bottom of bodies of water is essential for the safety and navigation of vessels — which resupply communities, ship construction materials, and carry out search and rescue missions, among other things.
