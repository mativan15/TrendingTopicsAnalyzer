Ian Holloway sits at his easel in his warm, friendly house in a beautiful, historic village near Bath, breaking off from painting acrylics of Ian Wright and Paolo Di Canio to follow the matches on the screen above the roaring log fire. He sees all his managerial colleagues losing control, losing games, losing their jobs, seemingly losing their minds.Management is a brutal profession, well rewarded for some, but guaranteed to bring pain as much as joy for all involved. Holloway has criss-crossed the country over the past 26 years, managing Bristol Rovers, Queens Park Rangers, Plymouth Argyle, Leicester City, Blackpool, Crystal Palace, Millwall and, most recently, Grimsby Town, whom he left two years ago.
