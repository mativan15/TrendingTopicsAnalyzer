Vancouver Island clinic gets health authority help to stay open despite high operating costs
cbc.ca
news
2022-05-27 05:15:00

A clinic on Vancouver Island is getting some help from the local health authority in order to stay open. (iStock)The local health authority is stepping in to help a clinic in a small B.C. community remain open, after it had planned to close over the rising cost of doing business. Dr. Carrie Marshall, physician and owner of Ucluelet Medical Clinic, which serves the community of about 1,700, says increasing rent, the cost of medical supplies and operational costs associated with running a business have been growing, but the wages for physicians have not — and they're set by the province, not by individual doctors.
