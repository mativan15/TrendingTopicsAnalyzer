Indices surge to 3-week highs on global cues

AgenciesForeign portfolio investors (FPIs) were net sellers to the tune of Rs 153.4 crore on Tuesday, showed provisional stock exchange data. Domestic institutions purchased shares worth Rs 2,084.71 crore.Mumbai: Indian equities surged 1% on Tuesday to their highest in three weeks, riding the recent positive undertone in global markets aided by the rollback of the fiscal stimulus by the UK. The BSE Sensex ended at 58,960.60, up 549.62 points, or 0.94%, from the previous close. The Nifty gained 175.
