That rare creature the black swan may become a more regular visitor

This week I met the former finance director of one of Britain's largest companies, one in the top ten of the FTSE 100, a complicated beast with many different businesses spread throughout the world, a fiendishly complicated balance sheet and a big commodity trading division, to boot. Someone, in short, who understands finance and risk.At the end of our talk, he said, almost as an aside: 'What the hell is going on with the Bank of England and pension funds?' His waspish verdict was that everyone knew that pension funds were super-sensitive to long-term interest rates and had borrowed money to make bets on them staying low.
