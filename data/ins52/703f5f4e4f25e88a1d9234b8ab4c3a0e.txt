(THE CONVERSATION) Immigrant families in the U.S. are extremely resilient. Yet some immigrant parents struggle to raise children who can thrive in their new country's culture. Whether they are dealing with a language barrier or economic challenges, immigrants who bring their kids to the U.S. – or who become parents after arriving – face unique challenges American-born parents don't.

I see this often in my work as a general pediatrician at Children's Hospital Los Angeles and an associate professor of clinical pediatrics at the Keck School of Medicine of the University of Southern California.
