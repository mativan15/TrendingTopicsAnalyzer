PepsiCo shook off recession fears last week, reporting solid 2022 revenues and third-quarter earnings, beating analysts' estimates.PepsiCo (PEP) delivered better returns to Wall Street than Coca-Cola. Over the last five years, PepsiCo's shares are up 50%, compared to 20% of Coca-Cola (KO)."We are very pleased with our results for the third quarter as our global business momentum remains strong. Given our year-to-date performance, we now expect our full-year organic revenue to increase 12 percent (previously 10 percent) and core constant currency earnings per share to increase 10 percent (previously 8 percent)," Chairman and CEO Ramon Laguarta said in a press statement following the release of third-quarter financial results.
