Our Southern Front

IF the United States needed a motto for military emergencies, one could compress it in five words: When in danger, look South.One balance-of-power struggle in 1803 led to the Louisiana Purchase; another, in 1823, to the Monroe Doctrine. Now we are again threatened with the spread of war to the Western Hemisphere. Not only is war more swiftly paced today, but the United States is more exposed to its lightning strokes. Destruction of the Panama Canal in 1942 could cripple us simply by depriving us of our own capacity for swift movement.
