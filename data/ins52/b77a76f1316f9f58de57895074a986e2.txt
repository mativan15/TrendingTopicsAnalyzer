We recount our past as if it is a beacon to life. An annoying mistake all parents make. Even if they resolve not to. When I went to school, I walked those miles in rain and shine; when I was in college, I did not have so much money to spend; when I began to earn, I handed it to my elders; and so on. You get the drift. As Khalil Gibran reminds us, we cannot even in our dreams visit the tomorrow in which our children's souls live. But we parents are relentless. We must reconcile in our minds, the stark differences in our world and theirs.
