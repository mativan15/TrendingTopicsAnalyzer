When it's too hot to turn on the oven or stove, what can I make for dinner?Will, Bridport, DorsetYou're talking Emiko Davies' language, Will. From June to September, the Italy-based food writer and author of Cinnamon and Salt: Cicchetti in Venice keeps her cooker turned off, 'unless it's the early morning or late at night'. Wherever you're living la dolce vita, however, cheese makes a very good starting point for no-cook dinners. 'I might take mozzarella or fresh ricotta and some prosciutto, then add seasonal vegetables or even fruit,' says Davies, who dresses the lot with olive oil.
