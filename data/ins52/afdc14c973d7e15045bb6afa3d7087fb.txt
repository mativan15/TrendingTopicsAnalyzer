Published: 11:08 EDT, 31 May 2022 | Updated: 11:08 EDT, 31 May 2022 By Lucy Raitano LONDON, May 31 (Reuters) - The British pound fell against the U.S. dollar on Tuesday, wrapping up five months of straight losses for the currency as Britain's murky growth outlook and uncertain interest rate trajectory continues to weigh on sentiment. At 1423 GMT, the pound was 0.54% lower against the dollar at $1.2581, having extended earlier losses throughout the day. Despite the weakness, sterling remains well off its mid-May lows when it touched its lowest level since May 2020.
