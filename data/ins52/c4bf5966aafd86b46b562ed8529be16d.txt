But, of course, it is not just about price. MCC has done as much as any institution in cricket to advance the policy of the England and Wales Cricket Board of white-ball cricket. It has played a significant part in reducing interest in first-class cricket and, therefore, in encouraging people to pay exorbitant prices to watch it. And thanks to the poor relation status of county cricket (something also willingly endorsed by MCC in its obsession with the cash register), with championship matches reduced in number and pushed to the margins of the season, its ability to produce players of Test calibre has been seriously reduced, too.
