Good morning. A couple of weeks ago, to widespread condemnation, Suella Braverman accused backbench Tory MPs of staging a coup against the prime minister; if that's the case, the ex-home secretary has now become its latest victim. But the truth is that the mind-bending events of Wednesday in Westminster can't really be classified as a coup. They were abrupt, unsanctioned, and by evening allegedly featured the use of force, yes. But coups are organised. Coups involve government control of the media.
