Exercising may help beat cancer, study finds

Published: 12:35 EDT, 2 June 2022 | Updated: 12:43 EDT, 2 June 2022 Exercise can amplify the effectiveness of cancer drugs and boost survival rates, a study suggests. Scientists found proteins released by the body to help repair muscles worn out from exercise also attack cancer cells.After proving their theory in mice, they analysed data from a human trial of 75 patients with pancreatic cancer. One group was asked to do an hour of strength-training exercise and 90 minutes per of aerobic exercise per week before they had surgery to remove their tumours.
