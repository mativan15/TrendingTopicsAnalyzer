Should China spend more on infrastructure?
economist.com
finance
2022-06-02 10:18:17

Rarely can so much have been used by so few. During Shanghai's long lockdown, which mercifully eased this week, the city's impressive infrastructure stood in splendid isolation from most of the citizens it is meant to serve. The metro (all 831km of it) was eerily quiet. The two airports, which handled 120m passengers in 2019, operated at 99% below their normal level. The famous mag-lev train neither magnetised nor levitated. Six-lane highways provided an ocean of road space for handfuls of scooters.
