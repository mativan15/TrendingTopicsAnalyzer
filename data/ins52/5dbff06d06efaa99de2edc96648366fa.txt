Learn more. When buying new headphones, it isn't just the audio quality that matters — it's also important to consider whether the headphones are suitable for the environment you're going to be using them in. Open-backed headphones are fine if you're alone in your own home, and the average pair of earbuds will do for a quick phone call during a walk, but only true noise cancelling headphones will fully insulate you from unwanted noise when you want it the least — whether it's chatty co-workers, screaming children or the screech of the subway carriage pulling out of the station.
