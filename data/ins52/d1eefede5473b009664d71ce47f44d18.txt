More Pain for Canadian Stocks as Earnings Dampen, Scotiabank Says
bnnbloomberg.ca
news
2022-10-21 17:19:47

(Bloomberg) -- Canadian companies are barreling toward even more pain amid an earnings season threatened by market volatility and a recession.Companies listed on the S&P TSX Composite Index are headed for their first sequential decline in a year, according to analysts at Scotiabank. Earnings per share is on course to contract 4.2% quarter-over-quarter to C$374 from last quarter's all-time high of C$390 -- suggesting that growth has topped out.'At this stage, most indicators would argue that we are past peak growth,' Scotiabank strategist Hugo Ste-Marie said in a note to clients Thursday.
