Studying the expansion of herb production across US
msu.edu
news
2022-10-20 00:30:00

A Michigan State University-led team has received a $3.4 million U.S. Department of Agriculture grant to evaluate the profitability and environmental sustainability of fresh-cut and potted culinary herbs produced in controlled environments. The funding is part of the Specialty Crop Research Initiative of the USDA National Institute of Food and Agriculture.





Roberto Lopez, an associate professor in the MSU Department of Horticulture, leads a new $3.4 million USDA-funded research project. Photo courtesy of Roberto Lopez.
