Australia's housing crisis is self-inflicted. We need four reforms to reverse it

How is it that in Australia, one of the richest countries in the world, we have a housing crisis where hundreds of thousands of renters can't afford a roof over their head? To figure out why rents are soaring, we need to look at the broader political problem: we have spent about two decades trying to screw up the housing market and we have, catastrophically, succeeded.The Australian housing market is broken. For too many low- and middle-income earners the Great Australian Dream of owning a home is now just a fantasy.
