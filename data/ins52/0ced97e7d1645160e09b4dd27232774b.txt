Published: 01:45 EDT, 17 October 2022 | Updated: 03:30 EDT, 17 October 2022 By Georgina Lee and Summer Zhen HONG KONG, Oct 17 (Reuters) - Cash is leaving China's financial markets at the fastest clip in years as investors flee a falling currency and sputtering economy, and analysts point to hints that more money is being moved out of the country along back channels in a further sign of flagging confidence. The flows, mostly out of the bond market, reflect the attraction of higher interest rates elsewhere.
