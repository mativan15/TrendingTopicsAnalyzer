Building Materials Some market experts seem to think that the lumber bubble has finally burst as elevated interest rates cause a drop in demand
 May 27, 2022 
 Image: Stock.adobe.com Volatile lumber prices have plagued weary builders throughout much of the COVID-19 pandemic, but as interest rates rise, demand for lumber is falling, and prices are following suit. Lumber prices per thousand board feet were down from the January high of $1,329 to $651 on Wednesday, and some experts say that the boom and bust cycles could at last be behind us, Fortune reports.
