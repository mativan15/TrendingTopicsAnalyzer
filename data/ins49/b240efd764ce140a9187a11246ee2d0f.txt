By Reuters Published: 06:38 EDT, 1 June 2022 | Updated: 06:38 EDT, 1 June 2022 BENGALURU, June 1 (Reuters) - Indian shares settled lower on Wednesday, dragged by pharma and technology stocks, while data showed that the country's economic growth slowed to the lowest in a year during the March quarter. After see-sawing between gains and losses for most of the session, the NSE Nifty 50 index ended 0.37% lower at 16,522.75 and the S&P BSE Sensex finished down 0.33% at 55,381.17. In the previous session, both indexes snapped three straight sessions of gains as a rally in technology stocks paused and banking stocks fell.
