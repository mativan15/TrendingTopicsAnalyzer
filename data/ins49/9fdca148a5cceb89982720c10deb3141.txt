(Bloomberg) -- Stocks in Asia advanced after the S&P 500 closed above a key technical level and the UK reversed more of its unfunded tax cuts, bolstering global market sentiment.

Equities rose in Japan, South Korea and Australia and a gauge of US-listed Chinese companies advanced the most in two weeks. UK bonds surged and the pound rallied Monday in a boost to confidence that flowed through into the US session, with the S&P 500 rebounding above key support and the tech-heavy Nasdaq 100 notching its biggest gain since July.
