grandriver/E+ via Getty Images Schlumberger (NYSE:SLB) +2.3% pre-market Friday after posting Q3 adjusted earnings and revenues that beat expectations, as higher oil and gas prices led to more drilling activity. Q3 net income surged 65% Y/Y to $907M, or $0.63/share, from $550M, or $0.39/share, in the year-ago quarter, but fell 5% from Q2's $959M; adjusted EBITDA jumped 35% Y/Y and 15% Q/Q to $1.75B, with adjusted EBITDA margin rising to 23.5% from 22.6% in Q2 and 22.2% in the prior-year period. The company said its adjusted earnings and pre-tax segment operating margin of 18.
