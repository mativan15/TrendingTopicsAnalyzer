With the disabled population so diverse in so many ways, how can outside observers — or disabled people themselves for that matter — ever get a grip on how disabled people really feel and think? One way to understand disabled people's diverse approaches to disability-related needs and issues today is to look at the state of their faith in certain institutions, ideas, and values. For example, disabled people and disability culture is often discussed in terms of two approaches or 'Disability Paradigms,' the Medical Model and Social Model.
