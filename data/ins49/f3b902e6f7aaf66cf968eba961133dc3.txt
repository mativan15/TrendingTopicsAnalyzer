Published: 17:12 EDT, 30 May 2022 | Updated: 17:14 EDT, 30 May 2022 While everyone else has been shelling out more money for gas, food and housing as inflation hit 8.5 percent in March, an obscure pocket of Wall Street has been raking in the cash by betting on swings in inflation.Inflation trading has pulled in $2.3 billion in 2021 for the big banks, according to Vali Analytics Ltd., more than twice what they made the year before, reports Bloomberg.Goldman Sachs pulled in $450 million in this niche sector, which had been a relatively sleepy corner of the investing world over the last decade as the rate of inflation has remained low.
