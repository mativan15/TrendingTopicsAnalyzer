Updated: Oct 17, 2022, 21:30 ISTA dear friend, whom we shall name Mona, sought to divorce her husband of 27 years. She had recognised their incompatibility early on, but persisted in the marriage in the interest of her children. Now that both children were educated and employed, emboldened by what she saw as changing societal norms, she chose to walk out. Mona wanted to keep the process dignified. She did not bad mouth her husband, nor was she willing to quarrel and quibble. However, the questions of finance loomed large.
