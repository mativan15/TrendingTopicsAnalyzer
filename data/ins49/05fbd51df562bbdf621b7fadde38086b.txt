Shares of Meta, Facebook's Parent, Rise After Sheryl Sandberg Announces Exit
variety.com
news
2022-06-02 13:55:50

Shares of Meta Platforms were up 1.7% in early trading Thursday, coming after longtime COO Sheryl Sandberg — one of the most powerful women execs in the tech industry — announced she was stepping down from the role.
In after-hours trading Wednesday, Meta's stock price had dropped as much as 3% on the news that Sandberg, a 14-year veteran of the company that owns Facebook and Instagram, was departing. On Thursday, broader market indexes were down and the Nasdaq Composite was up 0.2% amid the rise in Meta shares.
