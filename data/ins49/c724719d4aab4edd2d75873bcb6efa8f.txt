getty Bias is in full force and hard to fix in the venture capital industry unless women start playing ball and setting the rules. One way women can do that is by becoming investors and limited partners in venture firms. Investors have immense power to impact the diversity of a VC's portfolio, and it starts with asking a few simple, yet pointed questions. Why? Despite decades of data showing women make more money for their investors, only 2% (paywall) of venture funding goes to women-founded companies, so it is a wise financial move to ask about portfolio allocation to women founders before making an investment.
