Free Press sports writer Tony Garcia looks at the Michigan football players who helped or hurt their stock after Saturday's 41-17 victory over Penn State:

Three up

RB Donovan Edwards: To call Edwards' Saturday a career day would be a vast understatement. In his first four games this year, Edwards had 27 rushes for 134 yards and two scores. He obliterated that against a previously vaunted Penn State defensive front, rushing 16 times for 173 yards and two touchdowns. He nearly matched his 2021 production of 174 yards and three scores (on 35 carries).
