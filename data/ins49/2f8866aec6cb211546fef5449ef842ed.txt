€30m to help rural post offices modernise and stay open
thetimes.co.uk
news
2022-06-01 23:01:00

The government has agreed a three-year direct subsidy scheme to help hundreds of rural post offices avoid closure as they modernise their business and provide new services.Eamon Ryan, the communications minister, and Hildegarde Naughton, junior minister for postal services, have announced €30 million in funding from 2023 to 2025 to 'support a sustainable, nationwide post office network' providing community banking and e-commerce services nationwide.Grant amounts will vary from one outlet to another with a guaranteed fixed payment per post office and 'a variable, performance-based element' to be agreed in talks between An Post and the Irish Postmasters' Union, and approved by the Department of Public Expenditure and Reform.
