1846BY BERNARD DEVOTOCHAPTERS XIX-XXVHOW THE DONNER PARTY BEGANIn the year 1846, when Polk was in the White House, forces were at work which would determine within the twelvemonth the destiny of Oregon, Texas, New Mexico, and California. At the beginning of the year the sovereignty of these huge, unoccupied territories was in dispute. Mountain men like Jim Clyman had explored the new country, looking for the openings which would let the immigrants in. And by 1846 they were guiding the white-tops West in an ever-increasing stream.
