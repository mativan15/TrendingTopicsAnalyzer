UniversityHQ, a website geared toward providing consumer information for students, ranks both UVA's undergraduate and master's degrees as the 'Best in Virginia.' Virginia Military Institute, the College of William & Mary, Virginia Tech, and Washington and Lee University rounded out the top five of the undergraduate rankings. In the master's rankings, UVA tops William & Mary, Averett University (non-traditional programs), Virginia Tech, George Mason University, and Washington and Lee. According to the website, both sets of rankings take into account tuition costs, admission rate, retention rate, graduation rate, graduating salary, the number of programs offered, online programs, loan default rates, diplomas awarded, the percentage of students receiving financial aid and 'reputation and expert opinion.
