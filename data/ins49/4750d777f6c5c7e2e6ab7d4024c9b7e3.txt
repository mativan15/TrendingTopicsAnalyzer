IN one respect at least we may speak of economic collaboration between the English-speaking peoples as having been secured. This is in reference to money. War-debt adjustment remains a will-o'the-wisp; the two countries still have to negotiate the shoals of a trade agreement; but there has already been a meeting of minds on money. This was expressed in the tripartite arrangement with France of September 25, 1936. Of course, the understanding is on a twenty-four hour basis, as everything seems to be in this world of flux.
