KanawatTH/iStock via Getty Images Sophisticated investors will often use derivatives to hedge their positions, especially options. The advantage of doing this is generally that you get to keep the potential upside of your position while fixing your downside. The other advantage to this is that it allows you to defer taxes compared with selling the position outright. The traditional way that investors hedge risk and control volatility in their portfolio is with bonds or cash, which both have an opportunity cost.
