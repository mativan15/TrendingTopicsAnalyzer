Ikea prices have soared by up to 80% amid surging raw material costs, says retail intelligence firm
sky.com
news
2022-10-18 08:58:00

Ikea has increased the prices of its furniture by up to 80% over the last year, research has found.The Swedish retail giant has blamed "surging raw material and transport costs" as well as the war in Ukraine and inflation for the rise in prices.
The budget homeware retailer's Jokkmokk dining table and four chairs set increased from £99 in 2021 to £179 in October 2022, an increase of 80%, according business intelligence site Retail Week.The price of Ikea's Glostad two-seat sofa was hiked from £90 in 2021 to £150 in October 2022, a 66% increase, as its Brimnes chest of four drawers rose 47% from £85 to £125.
