Naeblys/iStock via Getty Images The week arguably offered some clarity on key facets of today's murky financial, economic and policy backdrops. At this point, the U.S. economy is anything but falling off a cliff. A stronger-than-expected 390,000 jobs were added during May (April's revised slightly higher to 436k). The unemployment rate was unchanged at a multi-decade low 3.6%. Wednesday's JOLTS data registered a larger-than-expected 11.4 million available jobs in April, down from March's (revised higher) record 11.
