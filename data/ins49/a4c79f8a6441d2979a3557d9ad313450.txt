Wealthy buyers turn cautious on luxury home loans
ft.com
news
2022-10-17 03:00:05

Jeffrey Feinman, New York accountant to the super-rich, in February arranged an unusual mortgage for a client buying a home abroad. Exceptionally for today's mortgage market, the loan covered 100 per cent of the value of the home. It also came fixed at 3.25 per cent, comfortably better than the 3.9 per cent average for a 30-year fixed-rate mortgage in the US at the time. But the really striking thing was its size: $110mn. While the world's richest people could buy even the priciest homes for cash, the majority will — just like the rest of us — use a mortgage.
