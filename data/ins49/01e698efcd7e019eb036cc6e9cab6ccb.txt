(Bloomberg) -- Shares in Dubai's main utility dropped below their initial public offering price for the first time, six months after the company raised $6.1 billion in the city's biggest-ever listing.

Most Read from Bloomberg

Dubai Electricity and Water Authority PJSC fell as much as 2.8% to 2.47 dirhams on Wednesday, compared with the IPO price of 2.48 dirhams. More than 3.5 million shares were traded by 10:48 a.m. local time, with the average volume on the stock dwindling since it went public.
