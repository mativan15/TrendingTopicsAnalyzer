Houston bail company accused of charging low bonds raided by FBI: report
foxnews.com
news
2022-06-02 23:07:43

FBI agents on Thursday raided the Houston office of a bail bond company accused of charging low bond amounts, which has helped free some suspects in high-profile murder cases, according to local reports. The agents targeted Aable Bail Bonds in the downtown area, Fox Houston reported. Boxes taken from the office were seen being placed in an 18-wheel truck. The company is known for charging less than 10% of bond amounts and sometimes offers payment plans, the report said. "The FBI confirms that we are presently leading a legitimate multi-agency law enforcement operation with fellow members of Texas Anti-Gang Task Force at the 1600 block of Austin St.
