As Americans grapple with inflation, media companies are seeing steep increases in the cost of a valuable commodity: sports rights.

For years, companies like Walt Disney Co.'s ESPN, Comcast Corp.'s NBC, Fox Corp. and Paramount Global's CBS have spent more and more on the rights to broadcast games. From 1980 to 2023, the five largest professional US sports leagues will have earned more than $210 billion from domestic media rights alone, according to a Bloomberg News analysis (the National Football League accounting for 65% of it.
