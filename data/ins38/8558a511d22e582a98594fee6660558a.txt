At one point in his career, Mike Mills published a small body of work in the form of a catalog (really more of a 'zine — thin, saddle-stitched, a coated cover stock protecting matte-coated interior pages) with Nieves Publishing titled 'Fireworks'. He wrote that it was a time in his life when his doctor suggested he take anti-depressants. His refusal to comply was accompanied by a body of drawings that were simple in form, linear, graphic, and apparently absent of meaning.'I yearned to be creative, for years and years I was too scared to even try, so I did nothing…' — David RakoffHe later explained the drawings acted as a placebo of sorts, a method for dealing with personal issues, justifying the creation of the works.
