Voters line up outside a voting station to cast their ballot in Toronto's municipal election on Oct. 22, 2018.Chris Young/The Canadian PressThe Association of Municipalities Ontario recently published some remarkable figures about this month's municipal elections. About one fifth of the positions up for grabs, from mayor to councillor to reeve, have been filled without a fight. One position, one candidate. The 'winners' were acclaimed, which sounds as if they were carried to office by a cheering crowd, but really means that no one bothered to run against them.
