Meta, the company formerly known as Facebook, is in… rough shape.

Nearly a year after its much ballyhooed rebrand, the company's stock plummeted an eye watering 57 percent. CEO Mark Zuckerberg lost roughly $70 billion off of his net worth, making him just the 20th richest man in the world (cue the world's smallest violin). The financial strain also forced the company to slash budgets across its workforce and freeze new hiring—even ones that were already accepted.
