The Government has insisted works on its major high speed rail line project are 'within budget', despite a claim that the first phase could run to 'many billions' more than estimated.A report presented to the HS2 board by the project's deputy chair Sir Jon Thompson said it was 'very unlikely' that the £40.3 billion target cost for the London to Birmingham section would be met, the Financial Times reported.Phase One has a target cost of £40.3 billion, but the Government has previously said the 'funding envelope' for it is £44.
