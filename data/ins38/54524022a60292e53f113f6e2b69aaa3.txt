Published: 19:41 EDT, 27 May 2022 | Updated: 19:41 EDT, 27 May 2022 One of the most widely mocked ideas associated with the ‘blue-sky thinking' phase of the Cameron era (yes, I know there were many, mostly associated with me …) was the notion of the ‘Post-Bureaucratic Age'.First introduced in a David Cameron speech to the Google ‘Zeitgeist' conference in California (where else?), the theory was that thanks to technology, citizens could be given the ability to access and control many public services without the need for giant, faceless bureaucracies in Whitehall — or smaller but equally faceless ones in the town hall.
