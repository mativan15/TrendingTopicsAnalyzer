Nokia unveiled plans for a new research and development center in a suburb of Canada's capital Ottawa
 Finnish telecommunications giant Nokia on Monday unveiled plans for a new research and development center in a suburb of Canada's capital Ottawa to advance cyber security and mobile technologies. Alongside laboratories, the 26-acre facility in Kanata, west of downtown Ottawa, will include office and residential towers, as well as space for restaurants and retailers. "This is a project that will build a world class R&D hub that will transform the wireless technologies we rely on every day," Canada's Prime Minister Justin Trudeau told a joint news conference with Nokia chief executive Pekka Lundmark.
