Published: 13:32 EDT, 19 October 2022 | Updated: 13:37 EDT, 19 October 2022 Treasures from the 17th century have been discovered in a castle which once belonged to the publisher of the James Bond books.The incredible haul of about 460 items will be auctioned on October 28 and are expected to sell for upwards of £100,000.The items, many from the Cromwellian period, were found stashed in attics and in rooms of the former home of George Wren Howard.Mr Howard co-founded Jonathan Cape Publishers which famously published Ian Fleming's 007 novels.
