My first prediction relates to the foundation of modern data systems: the storage layer. For decades, data warehouses and lakes have enabled companies to store (and sometimes process) large volumes of operational and analytical data. While a warehouse stores data in a structured state, via schemas and tables, lakes primarily store unstructured data.
