The Government has defeated Labour's bid to ban fracking amid farcical scenes in the House of Commons.Conservative whips initially stated the vote on whether to allocate Commons time to consider legislation to stop shale gas extraction was being treated as a 'confidence motion' in Liz Truss's embattled Government.But after a series of Tory MPs signalled they would not take part in the vote, climate minister Graham Stuart caused confusion by telling the Commons: 'Quite clearly this is not a confidence vote.
