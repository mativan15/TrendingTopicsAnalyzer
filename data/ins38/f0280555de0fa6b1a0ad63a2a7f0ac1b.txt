All credit to the Booker Prize judges – this year's winner deserves your attention
telegraph.co.uk
news
2022-10-17 21:00:00

This year, the Booker was hard to call. If you don't believe a critic, ask the money-men: late last Friday, across six bookmakers, four of the shortlisted six were favourites, with the odds for two of those novels simultaneously drifting and shortening. This didn't feel auspicious to me. Last year, faced with another competitive field in which the standout novel, to my mind, was a sumptuous Sri Lankan tale – Anuk Arudpragasam's A Passage North – the judges instead plumped for The Promise by Damon Galgut, an anti-apartheid satire that steered clear of subtlety.
