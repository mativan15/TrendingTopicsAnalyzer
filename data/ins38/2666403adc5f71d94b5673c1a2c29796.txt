16 October 2022 by Lise Pedersen 
Variety - Film News At a time when heritage cinema is booming – thanks to outstanding progress in conservation standards and a growth in demand – Lyon's Lumière heritage film festival Lumière is playing a leading role in uncovering long-forgotten cinematic gems.'Dans la Nuit' ('In the Night'), widely considered one of the last, if not the last major French silent film, is one of them. It is the only film shot by French actor Charles Vanel, perhaps best remembered for his role as a desperate truck driver in Henri-Georges Clouzot's acclaimed 'The Wages of Fear,' which won both the Golden Bear and the Palme d'Or in 1953.
