Double-digit inflation. A weakened British pound that's hovered at near-parity with the dollar. Soaring energy costs. The death of a revered monarch.

And now, the prospect of a third prime minister in less than three months.

Even before the spectacular political flameout of British Prime Minister Liz Truss, Britain of late has often seemed like a country unsure of its place in the world: a medium-sized power, its empire long gone, teetering on the edge of becoming a small, isolated isle, battered by storms within and without.
