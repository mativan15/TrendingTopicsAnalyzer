By Reuters Published: 14:24 EDT, 21 October 2022 | Updated: 14:24 EDT, 21 October 2022 CONAKRY, Oct 21 (Reuters) - Guinea's military government has proposed a shorter timeline for a transition to elections of 24 months, government spokesman Ousmane Gaoual Diallo said, down from a 36-month timeline earlier rejected by the Economic Community of West African States (ECOWAS). The junta that took power in a coup last September has been under pressure to restore civilian rule sooner than the 39 and then 36 months proposed by interim President Colonel Mamady Doumbouya.
