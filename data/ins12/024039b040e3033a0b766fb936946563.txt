Brazil's semi-arid Sertao region is home to a unique rodeo tradition known as "Pega de Boi no Mato" -- which roughly translates as the "Grab the Bull Bush Rodeo" CARL DE SOUZA AFP Cabrobó (Brazil) (AFP) – Brazil's powerful agribusiness industry may back far-right President Jair Bolsonaro, but at the "Grab the Bull Bush Rodeo" in runoff rival Luiz Inacio Lula da Silva's home state, the death-defying cowboys love the leftist ex-president. Northeastern Brazil's harsh, semi-arid Sertao region is the birthplace of this unique rodeo tradition, in which cowhands on horseback chase a charging bull through thick, cactus-strewn scrubland, racing to grab a leather tag from around his neck.
