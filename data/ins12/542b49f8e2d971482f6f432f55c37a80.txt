Across the country, marginalized women — including Black and Hispanic women — are dying at a disproportionate rate of breast and cervical cancers. These cancers are highly treatable if caught early, but these women are not accessing regular, affordable screenings. And it makes me furious.

This is missional work for me. My fight against breast cancer began long before I myself was diagnosed and treated. When my sister, Susan G. Komen, was fighting the cancer that ultimately took her life, she noticed the under-resourced women around her and said, 'Where a woman lives shouldn't determine whether she lives.
