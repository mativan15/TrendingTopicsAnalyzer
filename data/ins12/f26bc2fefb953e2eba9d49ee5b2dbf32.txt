How censoring China's open-source coders might backfire

For now, there's little clue as to what prompted the change, but censorship of certain types of language—profanity, pornography, and politically sensitive words—has been creeping up on the platform for a while. On Gitee's official and public feedback page, there are multiple user complaints about how projects were censored for unclear reasons, possibly because technical language was mistaken for a sensitive word. The immediate result of Gitee's May 18 change was that public projects hosted on the platform suddenly became unavailable without notice.
