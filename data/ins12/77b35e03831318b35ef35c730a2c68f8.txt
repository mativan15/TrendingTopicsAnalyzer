Published: 10:02 EDT, 2 June 2022 | Updated: 10:02 EDT, 2 June 2022 In a country without landed aristocracy, the Vanderbilts, Morgans, Astors and Rockefellers, were as close to royalty that America will ever get - with the titles and palaces to prove it. These tycoons made fortunes in oil, steel, railroads and banking during the latter half of the 19th century; interrupting the staid respectability of the old guard with their lavish lifestyles, yachts, priceless art and palatial mansions on Fifth Avenue.
