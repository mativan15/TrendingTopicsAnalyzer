Two days after the Dialogue and Development Commission of Delhi (DDCD) vice-chairperson was issued a show-cause notice on Lieutenant-Governor V. K. Saxena's direction for allegedly using his public office for political activities, Chief Minister Arvind Kejriwal released a booklet, '7 years of DDC'. He also credited the think tank for being the brain behind many successful government schemes. 'In the last 7 years, the DDCD has put in unprecedented hard work to come up with brilliant projects for the development of the Capital.
