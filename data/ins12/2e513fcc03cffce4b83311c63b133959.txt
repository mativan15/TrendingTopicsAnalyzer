LA Local NewsBy City News ServiceOct 19, 2022Photo: Getty ImagesLOS ANGELES (CNS) - The county Board of Supervisors Tuesday called on the sheriff's department and the California Highway Patrol to work with local car clubs and communities to develop safe "cruising" events in Los Angeles County. Spurred on by Assemblywoman Luz Rivas, D-Arleta, and her efforts to pass a statewide resolution celebrating the history and culture of cruising, the motion also asks the sheriff and CHP to develop multilingual education and outreach strategies to connect with local communities with cruising scenes.
