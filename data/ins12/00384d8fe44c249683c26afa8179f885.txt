The government is facing mounting calls to hold a general election after Liz Truss resigned following 45 days in Downing Street. Labour leader Sir Keir Starmer has demanded a general election "now", with Scotland's first minister Nicola Sturgeon following suit, saying it is a "democratic imperative' after Ms Truss's dramatic resignation. The Independent is also calling for a national ballot, with our petition now racking up more than 300,000 signatures. Sign the Independent's petition hereBut what do British voters think? The Independent has gone out on the streets of Cambridge, held by Labour, and Mid-Sussex, one of the UK's most solid Conservative constituencies, to find out.
