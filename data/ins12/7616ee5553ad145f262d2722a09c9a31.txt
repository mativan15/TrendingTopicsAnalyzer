A law rolling out in Texas that calls for public schools to hand out DNA and fingerprint identification kits for students -- in an effort to help parents identify their children's in the event of an emergency -- is causing a wave of backlash from those calling for gun reform. Public schools in the state are begining thte distribution this week after the state legislature passed a spring 2021 law that would require the Texas Education Agency 'to provide identification kits to school districts and open-enrollment charter schools for distribution to the parent or legal custodian of certain students.
