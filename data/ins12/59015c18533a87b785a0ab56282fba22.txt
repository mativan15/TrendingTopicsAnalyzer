The shares of United Airlines (NASDAQ: UAL) have remained relatively flat since April despite a sizable contraction in broader markets. Rising energy costs and skyrocketing inflation have taken a toll on growth and profitability metrics of the transportation industry, but strong air travel demand is likely to drive UAL stock as macroeconomic tensions ease.
