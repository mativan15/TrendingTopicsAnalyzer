The mothers Tess Frear works with were already struggling. Most of them are single parents, typically from economically disadvantaged families. Sometimes they're escaping domestic violence; sometimes they're as young as 15. For these new mothers, it was hard enough just trying to get basics such as diapers or clothing. Then came the baby formula shortage.
