National handgun sale freeze 'the final culmination of disappointment' for Alberta retailer
cbc.ca
economics
2022-10-21 20:39:00

An Alberta firearms retailer says the national "freeze" on the sale of handguns that went into effect Friday is unfair to lawful gun owners and won't make a difference to violent crime rates.The decision was announced in May, but J.R. Cox, owner of The Shooting Edge in Calgary and Target Sports Canada near Toronto, hoped the federal government would take a pause after meeting with firearms stakeholders."It's just the final culmination of disappointment," said Cox. "The facts don't back up this message about legal, lawful firearm owners being the problem.
