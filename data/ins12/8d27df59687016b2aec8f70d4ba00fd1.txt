Jürgen Klopp went too far – but pantomime ranting is part of game's lure
thetimes.co.uk
news
2022-10-18 23:01:00

I know this is a tad unfashionable, but I have long believed that controversy, spats and angry words are part and parcel of football's appeal. Arsène Wenger and Sir Alex Ferguson doing handbags at dawn was terrific entertainment, as was José Mourinho sulking like a four-year-old if he lost.Even the so-called controversy on the pitch is part of the great football soap opera. Players throwing their arms up in disbelief after an accurate decision goes against them is the sporting equivalent of panto, as is the sight of managers gesticulating wildly like orchestra conductors at some manic convention.
