getty If you asked someone five years ago what their office was like, they might have described an open-concept floor plan with a limited supply of conference rooms. And while this is still the case for some, today, many workers would describe their offices as their laptop screens, their conference rooms as Zoom and their watercooler conversations as Microsoft Teams. Due to these changes, the attributes that drove prospective employees to companies, like the culture or location of the office, have changed too.
