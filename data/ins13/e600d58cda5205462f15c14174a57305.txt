'Ordinary people' club together to raise almost $4.5 million to buy combat drone for Ukraine
abc.net.au
news
2022-05-31 05:34:02

Hundreds of Lithuanians have donated money to buy an advanced military drone for Ukraine in its war against Russia, in a show of solidarity with a fellow country formerly under Moscow's rule.The drone's purchase is being orchestrated by Lithuania's Ministry of DefenceUkraine is using the Turkish-made Bayraktar TB2 in its ongoing conflict with RussiaUkraine's ambassador to Lithuania says the campaign was unprecedentedSome 3 million euros (AUD$4.5 million) have been raised in just three days — out of the 5 million euros needed — largely in small amounts, according to Laisves TV, a Lithuanian internet broadcaster that launched the drive.
