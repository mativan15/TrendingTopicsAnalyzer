Published: 20:07 EDT, 30 May 2022 | Updated: 20:07 EDT, 30 May 2022 Fathers are guilty of dissuading their sons from reading as they lead them to believe that it is a ‘girly' activity, best-selling author Joanne Harris has claimed.The former teacher, known for her 1999 novel Chocolat, said boys tend to be ‘put under much more pressure' to play sports, such as rugby.She told the Hay Festival: ‘When I was teaching boys particularly, I found that not only boys did not read as much as girls but they were put under much more pressure by parents, largely fathers, to do something else as if reading was girly.
