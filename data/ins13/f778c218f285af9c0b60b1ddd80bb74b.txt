By Reuters Published: 07:19 EDT, 19 October 2022 | Updated: 07:19 EDT, 19 October 2022 LONDON, Oct 19 (Reuters) - British banks are bracing for a potential tax hit to their profits as the government seeks out new sources of cash to support budgets and shore up its finances. A source familiar with the British government's plans said new finance minister Jeremy Hunt was reviewing the current surcharge on bank profits and would confirm the level when he delivers his medium-term fiscal plan on Oct 31.
