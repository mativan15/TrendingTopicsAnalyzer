Liz Truss' time as prime minister of the U.K. was short-lived, but the impacts of her policies were felt across the economy. Three charts show the effects her time in office had on the pound, government bonds and interest rate expectations. LONDON — U.K. Prime Minister Liz Truss was in office for just 44 days before she announced her resignation on Thursday. Her time as leader may have been short, but the impact her tenure had on the British economy was huge.  Truss and her former Finance Minister Kwasi Kwarteng announced a so-called mini-budget on Sept 23.
