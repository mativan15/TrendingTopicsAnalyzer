Is Inflation Leveling Off? Index Favored By Fed Provides Cautious Optimism
ibtimes.com
news
2022-05-27 15:16:37

Inflation showed some tepid signs of deceleration in April after months of being thrown upwards by surging prices on core goods like food and energy. On Friday, the Commerce Department released its latest reading for the Personal Consumption Expenditure (PCE) index that showed prices rose by 6.3% in April, down slightly from the 6.6% witnessed in March. This was the slowest rise seen for the metric since at least late 2020, according to the Wall Street Journal. The PCE index, which does not include more volatile food and energy prices among its basket of goods, rose by 4.
