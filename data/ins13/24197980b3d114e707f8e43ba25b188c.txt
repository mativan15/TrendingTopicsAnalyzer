REUTERS/Umit Bektas/File PhotoRegister now for FREE unlimited access to Reuters.comISTANBUL, June 1 (Reuters) - Turkish manufacturing activity contracted for the third month running in May as both output and orders slowed amid muted demand and ongoing price pressures, a business survey showed on Wednesday.The Purchasing Managers' Index (PMI) for manufacturing stood at 49.2 in May, unchanged from April, said a panel from the Istanbul Chamber of Industry and S&P Global, staying below the 50-point line that separates expansion from contraction.
