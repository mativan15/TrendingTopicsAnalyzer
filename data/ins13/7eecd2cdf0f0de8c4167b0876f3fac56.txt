Quebec skip Jean-Michel Ménard, seen above in 2017, led Canada to a quarter-final victory over Finland on Friday at the mixed curling world championship. (Andrew Vaughan/The Canadian Press)Canada's Jean-Michel Ménard scored three points in the extra end for a 9-6 victory over Finland's Markus Sipila on Friday at the world mixed curling championship.Ménard scored a deuce in the seventh end but Sipila tied the game with a three-ender in the eighth at the Curl Aberdeen facility.Canada advanced to play Switzerland's Ursi Hegner in the semifinals on Saturday.
