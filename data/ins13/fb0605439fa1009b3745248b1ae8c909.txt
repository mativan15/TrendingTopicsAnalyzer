Shark Tank's Barbara Corcoran: 'All the Best Things Happened to Me on the Heels of Rejection'
chron.com
news
2022-06-02 17:00:00

In 1973, Barbara Ann Corcoran was a 22-year-old diner waitress from New Jersey with about 20 jobs under her belt. She borrowed $1,000 to start a tiny real estate company, selling apartments in New York City. In the intervening years, Corcoran grew her tiny real estate office, The Corcoran Group, from a small business into a $5 billion real estate empire, selling the billion-dollar business for $66 million in 2001.We now know the entrepreneur as one of the savvy, high-powered investors on the hit ABC series Shark Tank, sitting alongside the likes of Mark Cuban, Daymond John, Robert Herjavec, and Lori Greiner.
