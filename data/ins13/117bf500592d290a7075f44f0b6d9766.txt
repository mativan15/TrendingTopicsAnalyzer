The NSW rail union is expected to scrap plans to turn off Opal card readers at some train stations tomorrow, the federal court has heard. Members will vote tonight on whether to suspend the actionIt comes after an urgent Federal Court hearing todayThe NSW government has pledged to seek damages from the union if Opal gates are turned offThe Rail, Tram and Bus Union (RTBU) was set to turn off Opal machines at all gated train stations from 3pm until 7pm each weekday from tomorrow. It claimed the action would give commuters "free travel indefinitely", but the government said it could result in passengers being charged hefty default fares if they couldn't tap off.
