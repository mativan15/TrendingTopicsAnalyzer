The Inflationary Impact Of Reshoring
seekingalpha.com
news
2022-10-19 15:47:43

Ibrahim Akcengiz Reshoring / nearshoring to improve the resiliency of supply chains has been proposed as a source of structural inflation going forward. While reshoring is currently a hot topic, a normalization of supply chains could make it a short-lived phenomenon. Even if reshoring becomes an ongoing trend, it is likely to be less inflationary than most expect, dependent on how quickly supply chains are relocated. Reshoring predates the pandemic and is being driven by a number of factors: Increasing geopolitical risks Rising trade tensions Technology Increased focus on supply chain resiliency in the wake of the COVID pandemic The relocation of supply chains is likely to occur on a case-by-case basis, with consideration given to the costs and strategic benefits.
