Climate Activists Throw Soup on Vincent van Gogh's ‘Sunflowers' to Protest Fossil Fuels
smithsonianmag.com
news
2022-10-17 20:32:04

After throwing cans of soup on Vincent van Gogh's "Sunflowers," two climate activists glued their hands to the wall, and one called for the end of fossil fuel production. Just Stop Oil On Friday, in room 43 of London's National Gallery, two young women opened cans of tomato soup and threw their contents onto Vincent van Gogh's famous painting Sunflowers. The soup-throwers donned shirts displaying the logo of Just Stop Oil, an activist group that has been staging nonviolent demonstrations across the United Kingdom to protest the production of fossil fuels.
