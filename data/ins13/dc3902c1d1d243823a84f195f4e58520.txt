Two men sentenced to 40 years' jail for Malta reporter Daphne Caruana Galizia's car-bomb murder

Two brothers have each been sentenced to 40 years in prison after they abruptly reversed course and pleaded guilty to the car-bomb murder of an anti-corruption journalist, which had shocked Europe and triggered angry protests in Malta.The brothers had earlier entered not-guilty pleas over the killing of Caruana Galizia in 2017Caruana Galizia investigated suspected corruption among political and business circles in the financial haven of MaltaFurther trials will be held for those alleged to be complicit in her deathHours earlier, at the start of the trial in a Valletta courthouse, George Degiorgio, 59, and Alfred Degiorgio, 57, had entered not-guilty pleas over the death of Daphne Caruana Galizia in the blast as she drove near her home on October 16, 2017.
