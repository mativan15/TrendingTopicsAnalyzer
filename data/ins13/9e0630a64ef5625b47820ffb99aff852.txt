Unlike artists like Michael Jackson or R.Kelly, whose controversies sat in the shadows until they were outed, Kanye West has, for years, been airing out his dirty laundry on a public clothesline.

In the last month or so, Kanye broke a streak of social media silence by appearing at Paris Fashion Week sporting 'White Lives Matter' slogan tees aside populist right-wing pundit, Candace Owens. A few days later he'd air private conversations with Supreme's Tremaine Evory on his instagram, distastefully discussing the late designer Virgil Abloh.
