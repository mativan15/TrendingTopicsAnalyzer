A. H. RASKINhas been covering major labor-management developments for the New York TIMES for almost thirty years and is now a member of the paper's editorial board. In World War II he received the Distinguished Service Medal for his contributions to the army's labor relations program. His journalistic awards include the Sidney Hillman Memorial Award, the George Polk Award, and the Page One Award of the New York Newspaper Guild.THE House of Labor, once as Spartan an abode as any cold-water flat in the slums, now is so comfortably cushioned with cash that doorbell ringers from the financial community have become regular visitors.
