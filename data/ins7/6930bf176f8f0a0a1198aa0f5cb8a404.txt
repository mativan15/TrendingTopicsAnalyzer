Facebook parent Meta Platforms (FB 5.42%) keeps getting kicked around by the market. In a digital advertising market that is navigating activity tracking changes hastened by Apple, Snap's (SNAP 6.96%) revenue and earnings downgrade in May (which Snap blamed on macroeconomic factors) sent Meta's stock retreating as well. Then on Wednesday, Meta COO Sheryl Sandberg abruptly announced her departure after 14 years, during which Sandberg helped oversee the company's rapid rise as the dominant force in social media.
