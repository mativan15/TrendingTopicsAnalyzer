Leseliey Welch has seen it firsthand. She was in the Detroit hospital room when her sister-in-law's preterm son died soon after being born. Welch has experienced it herself, too. She had her own preterm baby, who survived, in addition to the horror and heartbreak of a late-term miscarriage. Welch, who is Black, knows the grim statistics. In the profit-driven, hospital-centered American healthcare system, Black infants are 2.3 times more likely to die than white babies, and Black women have three times higher chance to die from a pregnancy-related cause than white women.
