American teenage chess grandmaster Hans Niemann sued the game's world champion Magnus Carlsen and the leading online platform Chess.com, accusing them and others of slander by making baseless accusations he's a cheat.Those allegations have robbed Niemann of appearance fees, competition prize money and also online chess streaming revenue, his lawyers argued in the October 20th US complaint that seeks $100 million in damages.[ Sex toys, cheating and Elon Musk - Chess but not as you know it ]Magnus Carlsen was beaten by Niemann at an event in St Louis, Missouri in September, robbing the world champion of a top performance rating and breaking his unbeaten streak, the lawyers said.
