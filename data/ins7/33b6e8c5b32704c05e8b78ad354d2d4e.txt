Fayez Sarofim, Texas investor known as 'the Sphinx', dies at 93
sfgate.com
news
2022-05-31 13:59:40

Fayez Sarofim, the Egyptian-born Texas money manager known for his discerning taste in stocks and fine art who became a billionaire through an investment strategy he summarized as "never sell," has died. He was 93.He died on May 28 at his home in Houston, according to a death notice in the Houston Chronicle. The newspaper said a spokeswoman for the family confirmed his death. A Harvard Business School graduate nicknamed "The Sphinx," Sarofim was known for buying and holding blue-chip stocks such as Philip Morris (which became Philip Morris International), Exxon (now Exxon Mobil), Coca-Cola and Microsoft.
