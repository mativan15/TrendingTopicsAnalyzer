By Reuters Published: 08:09 EDT, 21 October 2022 | Updated: 08:54 EDT, 21 October 2022 By Mehnaz Yasmin and Niket Nishant Oct 21 (Reuters) - American Express Co on Friday forecast its full-year profit to be higher than initially expected, but built bigger provisions to prepare for potential defaults as an economic downturn looms, sending its shares down 5% in premarket trading. Big U.S. banks have said consumers' financial health has remained strong despite aggressive rate hikes to curb soaring inflation that could tip the economy into a recession, but some believe the tightening could lead to a surge in defaults.
