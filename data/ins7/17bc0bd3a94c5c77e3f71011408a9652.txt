PTIPuri cited the example of the launch of Japanese car maker Toyota's first-of-its-kind pilot project on flex-fuel - a strong hybrid electric vehicle (FFV-SHEV) that can run on 100 per cent ethanol in India last week to assert how things are developing on the blended fuel front.New Delhi: Indian automobile industry needs to provide flexi-fuel vehicles at various price points quickly to accelerate the adoption of blended fuel technology, Union Minister Hardeep Puri said on Wednesday. The government will provide comprehensive support from the supply, policy and demand side for the sale of flexi fuel - E10 (a blend of 10 per cent ethanol with petrol) and E20 (20 per cent ethanol with petrol) - vehicles, a viable business proposition for the auto industry, he said here at an event organised by auto industry body SIAM.
