Published: 21:01 EDT, 15 October 2022 | Updated: 21:10 EDT, 15 October 2022 A huge fire has erupted at an infamous Iranian jail which houses political prisoners, including two American citizens and a resident, in the nation's capital tonight. Riots broke out at the notorious Evin prison in northern Tehran on Saturday night, the Oslo-based group Iran Human Rights reported, amid protests over the death of Mahsa Amini, 22, who was detained by police. Gunshots, explosions and chants of 'death to the dictator' were all heard in the capital, and witnesses told MailOnline they heard automatic guns being fired, thought to be by police.
