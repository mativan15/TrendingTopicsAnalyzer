Published: 01:03 EDT, 31 May 2022 | Updated: 01:03 EDT, 31 May 2022 By Rachel Savage and Leigh Thomas LONDON/PARIS, May 31 (Reuters) - China's lack of experience with tricky debt restructurings and slow coordination among its public lenders is holding up debt relief for Zambia, a test case for the top emerging market creditor, three sources familiar with the matter said. Zambia became in 2020 the first country to default in the COVID-19 pandemic era, struggling under a debt burden worth 120% of GDP.
