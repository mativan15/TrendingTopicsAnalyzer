There were telltale signs of money laundering. Why didn't Canada's major banks flag millions of dollars in suspicious wire transfers?
thestar.com
news
2022-05-30 09:00:00

For eight years, Chinese property developer Runkai Chen used hundreds of wire transfers to move tens of millions of dollars into accounts at Canadian banks — what experts call a hallmark of money laundering.Canada's big banks — including the Royal Bank of Canada, CIBC, TD and the Bank of Montreal — all accepted the money, no flags raised.But one financial institution acted: the Vancouver arm of Switzerland's UBS bank, which in 2012 zeroed in on the massive flows of unexplained money as 'suspicious' signs of potential money laundering.
