That climate change (E) and income inequality (S) are the two biggest existential threats of the 21st century is well understood. The call for urgent action is growing. However, some of today's most popular solutions like regulation, measurement, reporting, incentives, and cheaper capital are not enough to save Planet Earth and Humanity. Specifically, it might be time to upgrade the ESG framework to ESL. First, a point of clarification. Rather than looking at ESG as a narrow tool to evaluate investment decisions, we see it as a broader framework to drive responsible and proactive business behavior towards addressing environmental and societal challenges.
