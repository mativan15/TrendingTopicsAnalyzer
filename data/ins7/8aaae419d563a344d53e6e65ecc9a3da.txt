Freeport McMoRan surges after slim Q3 beat; copper market 'strikingly tight' (NYSE:FCX)
seekingalpha.com
finance
2022-10-20 14:31:31

SimoneN/iStock via Getty Images Freeport McMoRan (NYSE:FCX) +7.1% in Thursday's trading after Q3 adjusted earnings edged past estimates, even as revenues fell due to lower selling prices for copper and other metals, and the company said the current physical copper market is "strikingly tight." Q3 net income fell to $404M, or $0.28/share, from $1.4B, or $0.94/share, in the year-earlier quarter, and revenues fell to $5B from $6.08B but topped Wall Street estimates. Freeport (FCX) said its average realized price for copper was $3.
