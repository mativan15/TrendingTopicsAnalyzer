The New South Wales public health system is set to receive billions of dollars in extra funding to help with chronic understaffing and emergency wait times.The funding is set to tackle staff shortages across the boardNSW Shadow Minister Ryan Park says it will take a long time to rebuild the system under "enormous pressure"Greens spokeswoman Cate Faehrmann said a "generous wage rise" must accompany the funding commitmentSome of the state's busiest hospitals have been plagued with chronic staff shortages and emergency department delays due to the COVID pandemic, current flu outbreak and population growth.
