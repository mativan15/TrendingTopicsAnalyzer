House passes bill to codify contraception rights nationwideThe Right to Contraception Act heads to the Senate after House members voted 228-195 mostly along party lines.WASHINGTON – It's not just about abortion.After pounding away at Republicans over the Supreme Court's decision to overturn Roe v. Wade, Democrats are broadening their reproductive rights rallying cry to emphasize birth control.A new advocacy group is running ads both in competitive House races as well as in communities with large student populations warning that the right to birth control pills, condoms and 'your girlfriend's IUD' could be taken away by Republicans.
