Snape Maltings concert hall in Suffolk given Grade II* listing
theguardian.com
sport
2022-06-01 05:00:08

One of the world's finest acoustic concert halls that was converted from a 19th-century malthouse has been given Grade II* listing by the government.Snape Maltings concert hall is the home of the Aldeburgh festival, founded by the composer Benjamin Britten in 1948. This year's 24-day festival of music and arts opens on Friday.The building was originally one of the largest malthouses at Snape, from where barley was sent to brewers in London and Newcastle along a branch line of the East Suffolk Railway built for the growing industrial enterprise.
