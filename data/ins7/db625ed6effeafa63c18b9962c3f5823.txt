Big Amazon rollups are now looking for ways to reduce their reliance on Amazon's logistics infrastructure
businessinsider.com
news
2022-06-01 08:49:36

Buy a popular household item on Amazon and the chances are you're not buying from an indie seller or shop, but from a new breed of conglomerate aiming to be the 21st century equivalent of Unilever or P&G.These entities are called Amazon aggregators, or Amazon rollups, and have mushroomed by hunting out the fastest-growing third-place Amazon sellers and snapping — rolling — them up.London-based aggregator The Mothership buys up brands in health, fitness, beauty, home, garden, pets, and children. Olsam, also in London, hunts out third-party sellers with IP such as collapsible camping brand Colapz.
