Liz Truss has resigned as Prime Minister after just 44 days in office, making her the shortest serving PM in history.She quit on Thursday amid mounting pressure as she lost the confidence of Tory MPs after her chaotic and crisis-hit premiership.She entered office on September 6 after beating Rishi Sunak in the final two of the Tory leadership race that followed Boris Johnson being forced from office in July.But her hold on power unravelled at speed in the wake of her disastrous mini-budget.Here is a look at the timeline of her time in office:September 5: Liz Truss wins the the Tory leadership contest as she gets the most votes from party members.
