Billions of crabs vanished, and scientists have a good clue why
mashable.com
science
2022-10-22 09:00:00

While counting snow crabs at sea in 2021, fisheries biologist Erin Fedewa saw that something was deeply amiss.Fedewa, a National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA) scientist, spends three or four months with a team that collects crabs from 376 stations in Alaska's Bering Sea each year. Some of these areas always teem with crabs. Scientists count thousands. But in 2021, thousands dwindled to hundreds. "The survey last year was a huge red flag for me," she told Mashable.The harbingers proved right.
