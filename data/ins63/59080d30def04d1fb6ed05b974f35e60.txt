Detailed inquiry sought into farmer ‘suicides' in Andhra Pradesh

The members of the HRF-RSV fact-finding team interacting with the family of a farmer, who ended his life, at R. Papampalle in Uyyalawada mandal of Nandyal district.
The Human Rights Forum (HRF) and the Rythu Swarajya Vedika (RSV) on Tuesday demanded that Andhra Pradesh government must launch an inquiry into the alleged farmer suicides and implement provisions of the G.O.43 that mandates a financial package of ₹7 lakh to kin of ryots who have resorted to extreme steps. A joint fact-finding committee of the HRF and the RSV met the family members of 17 farmers, who allegedly ended their lives owing to agrarian crisis and financial distress, in Nandyal and Kadapa districts on May 28 and 29.
