'Healthy' and affordable housing keys for sustainable downtown Winston-Salem growth, panelists say
bizjournals.com
business
2022-10-21 11:51:00

Though a throng of high-wage tech and medical professionals are important to the development of downtown Winston-Salem, so are police officers, teachers, bus drivers and sanitation workers.

That's an issue Metropolitan Village, the 324-unit apartment community under development in a predominantly low-income area across U.S. 52 from Innovation Quarter in downtown Winston-Salem, is intended to address.

While development of upscale downtown apartments currently starting at more than $1,300 per month for the cheapest one-bedroom units has been steady in recent years, they aren't affordable for many people fulfilling important roles in downtown life.
