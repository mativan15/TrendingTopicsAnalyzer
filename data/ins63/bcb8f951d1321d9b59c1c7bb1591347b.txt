NATHAN DENETTE / AP Something is rotten in the state of Canadian hockey: five years after the emergence of the #MeToo movement, Hockey Canada, the national governing body of the country's most beloved sport, seems to have missed the point of the ongoing revolution. Beyond the seriousness of the accusations of sexual assault made against some of its players – that the federation has long cast as innocent – the strategy of the current leaders to protect themselves against this culture of violence and secrecy appears to be foot-dragging and delay.
