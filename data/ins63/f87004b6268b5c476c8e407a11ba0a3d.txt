Majorca holidays are a firm favourite with Brits but the Balearic island is looking to crackdown on overcrowding by introducing tourist number limitsMajorca could be set to introduce new tourism limits Brits planning Spain holidays may find that some hotspots are quieter than expected in coming months, as Majorca is looking to crack down on the number of tourists it receives following a summer of overcrowding. The island's governing councils says it intends to impose a new, tighter limit and will also employ extra inspectors to check on illegal holiday lets, and warn that owners of apartments and villas renting out their properties "under the radar" will be fined.
