Liz Truss, Britain's newish prime minister, has fallen from grace faster than any leader I can think of. According to a poll released Monday, she has only 9% approval among British voters — a performance that would probably be impossible in the United States, where extreme political polarization guarantees that even a leader who, say, instigated a violent insurrection will retain a large base of support. The Star, a British tabloid, has a livestream of a head of lettuce next to a picture of Truss, asking which will last longer.
