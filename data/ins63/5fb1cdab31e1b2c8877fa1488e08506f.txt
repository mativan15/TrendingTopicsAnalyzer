Published: 00:38 EDT, 2 June 2022 | Updated: 00:38 EDT, 2 June 2022 By Engen Tham and Winni Zhou SHANGHAI, June 2 (Reuters) - Banks in China are urging regulators to relax rigid documentation rules as companies there have not been able to fulfill them due to COVID-19 lockdowns, forcing the lenders to halt services such as loan disbursements, sources said. In China, contracts and transactions are legally recognised only if the documents they are captured on are stamped with the official red company seal.
