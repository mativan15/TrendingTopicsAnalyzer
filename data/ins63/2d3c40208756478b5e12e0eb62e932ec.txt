This should be a prime time for Michigan to celebrate its new era of achievements in the automotive industry. Ford is turning out all the units it can produce of the most exciting all-electric vehicle in America, the Ford F-150 Lightning, at a plant in Dearborn, while also spending nearly $1 billion to convert an old train-station campus in downtown Detroit into a research complex that will be devoted to future vehicles. General Motors is investing billions of dollars in new EV production in Lansing, Orion Township and Detroit.
