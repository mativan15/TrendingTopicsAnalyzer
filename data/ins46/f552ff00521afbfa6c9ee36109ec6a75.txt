fotokostic Higher Natural Gas Prices Lead To More Expensive Fertilizers For Farmers But Also Cause Disruptions In The Fertilizer Industry One of the industries most affected by the rapid rise in natural gas prices due to geopolitical tensions between Western countries and Russia is the fertilizer sector. This is because natural gas is the fossil fuel that powers the production process, but also the raw material that is needed to produce fertilizers for agriculture. The rise in gas prices means that many operators in Europe had to drastically reduce or even temporarily halt the production of fertilizers as it became economically unsustainable, at the risk of being unable to reopen.
