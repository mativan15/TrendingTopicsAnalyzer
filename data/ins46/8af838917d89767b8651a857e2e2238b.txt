In Georgia Senate race, Warnock needs his 2020 coalition. But inflation, fatigue could keep them home
usatoday.com
news
2022-10-21 09:02:26

JONESBORO, Ga. — Carl Cox. Jr., a 31-year-old software developer from Dallas, Ga., northwest of Atlanta, voted for Democrat Raphael Warnock in the 2020 special election to fill the remaining two years of a U.S. Senate seat. But this year, he's having trouble getting exited about Georgia's U.S. Senate race, in which Warnock is running for reelection to a full six-year term.Cox pointed to the souring economy and his own "fatigue" with politics.He called Warnock, a second-year senator, a "lesser of two evils" in his reelection campaign against Republican Herschel Walker.
