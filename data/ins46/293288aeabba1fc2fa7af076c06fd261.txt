Image caption, Nathan Mayall said he would like to see "some stability in the country".There may be 200 miles between Altrincham and Westminster, but everyone in this leafy Greater Manchester town I speak to has a view on the political turmoil that's enveloped the Government."Oh God," says Nathan Mayall. I ask him what he makes of events as I stop him outside a restaurant in the town centre. "There's been as many prime ministers in the last few years than I've had hot dinners, so it would be good to see some stability in the country, eventually," he ends with a nervous laugh.
