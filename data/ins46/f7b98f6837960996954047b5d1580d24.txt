DiDi Global stock gains on report of China's FAW interest in possible stake (NYSE:DIDI)

XH4D/iStock Unreleased via Getty Images DiDi Global (NYSE:DIDI) rose 10% in premarket trading on a report that state-owned carmaker China FAW Group is mulling purchase a "significant" stake in the ride-sharing company. The automaker is said to have reached out to DIDI executives and expressed interest in taking a stake in the company, according to a Bloomberg report. FAW is pitching that it can help DIDI resolve concerns related to data security so the ride-haling firm is able to list in Hong Kong.
