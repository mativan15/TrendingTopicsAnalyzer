Redfin sees home prices supported by demand even with mortgage pressures (NASDAQ:RDFN)
seekingalpha.com
finance
2022-05-31 16:00:59

Stephen Brashear/Getty Images Entertainment The rapid increase in mortgage rates in the first half of 2022 has "certainly put pressure on potential homebuyers," Redfin (NASDAQ:RDFN) Chief Financial Officer Chris Nielsen said during the Jeffferies Virtual Internet Summit on Tuesday. That increase, which brought the average 30-year mortgage as high as 5.3% in mid-May, up from 3.1% at the end of 2021, was faster than the company had expected. And after a white-hot housing market during the pandemic, "some" cooling "has probably started to happen," he said.
