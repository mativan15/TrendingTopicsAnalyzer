Published: 03:22 EDT, 30 May 2022 | Updated: 03:22 EDT, 30 May 2022 By Elvira Pollina MILAN, May 30 (Reuters) - Telecom Italia (TIM) and Italian state investor CDP have signed a preliminary agreement to combine the phone group's fixed network assets with those of state-owned broadband rival Open Fiber, they said late on Sunday. The long-awaited move aims to pave the way for the creation of a unified ultra-fast broadband network across Italy, as CEO Pietro Labriola works on a turnaround plan for TIM focused on a full-blown split of its landline grid from service operations.
