Published: 19:25 EDT, 17 October 2022 | Updated: 19:27 EDT, 17 October 2022 Millions of households face a ‘cliff-edge' of soaring energy bills next year after Jeremy Hunt drastically cut short the Government's support scheme from two years to six months.The new Chancellor said yesterday the scheme, which aims to keep average annual household bills below £2,500 amid soaring energy prices, will be replaced in April.Analysts warned the move could see average bills double to more than £5,000 for some households.
