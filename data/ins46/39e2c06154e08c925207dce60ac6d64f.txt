Merck's (NYSE:MRK) five-year earnings growth trails the decent shareholder returns
yahoo.com

2022-10-19 13:03:17

Generally speaking the aim of active stock picking is to find companies that provide returns that are superior to the market average. And while active stock picking involves risks (and requires diversification) it can also provide excess returns. To wit, the Merck share price has climbed 50% in five years, easily topping the market return of 39% (ignoring dividends). However, more recent returns haven't been as impressive as that, with the stock returning just 23% in the last year , including dividends .
