If there is a unifying sense among GAA referees, it might not even be alarm at the spate of recent attacks on them or the level of bad behaviour that is a constant challenge to their officiating. It is as likely to be the implicit dismissal of their status as part of the community of the games.Whereas there is a welcome for the GAA's attempts to encourage recognition as with this weekend's 'Respect the Referee' days, most match officials would simply prefer to be acknowledged without fuss.The often repeated truism that there can be no games without referees is in a way negative, depicting them as a necessary evil rather than just an integral part of the association's activities.
