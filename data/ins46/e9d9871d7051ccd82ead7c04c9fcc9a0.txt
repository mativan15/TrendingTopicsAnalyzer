One of the reasons we are seeing higher prices is market concentration. The baby formula situation is one of the examples where we have a small handful of companies that are really serving the market. This kind of market concentration gives a lot of market power to producers to increase prices, not because of any kind of fundamental issues in terms of the demand or what the president or Federal Reserve is doing.
