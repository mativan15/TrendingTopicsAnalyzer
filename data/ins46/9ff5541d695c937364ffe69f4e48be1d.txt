As Bitcoin Falters, Crypto Miners Brace for a Crash
wired.com
news
2022-05-31 11:00:00

Last year, as bitcoin's price rose as high as $68,000, miners were having a blast. Their profits, according to some estimates, were hovering just under 90 percent, and many of them decided to expand their operations at a frantic pace, bracing for an even larger 2022 bonanza.That windfall has not come to pass. Over the past few months, cryptocurrency markets have slid, with bitcoin's price hovering at $30,630 at the time of writing. At the same time, the price of electricity shot up across the world because of a bounce-back in demand and the war in Ukraine.
