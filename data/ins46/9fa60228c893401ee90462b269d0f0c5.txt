By Reuters Published: 02:30 EDT, 15 October 2022 | Updated: 02:30 EDT, 15 October 2022 LONDON, Oct 15 (Reuters) - Britain's new finance minister Jeremy Hunt said on Saturday that some taxes will go up and government spending will rise by less than previously planned as difficult decisions will have to be taken to restore Britain's fiscal credibility. "We will have some very difficult decisions ahead," Hunt, appointed on Friday after Kwasi Kwarteng was sacked in the fallout of a disastrous fiscal statement last month, said.
