One of opposition leader Lee's aides detained over suspected bribery SEOUL, Oct. 19 (Yonhap) -- One of opposition leader Lee Jae-myung's closest aides was apprehended Wednesday by prosecutors investigating alleged corruption in a land development project south of Seoul, informed officials said. The Seoul Central District Prosecutors Office detained Kim Yong, known as one of the Democratic Party (DP) chairman's close confidants, on suspicion of accepting bribes in connection with the Wirye New Town project on the southern outskirts of Seoul, the officials said.
