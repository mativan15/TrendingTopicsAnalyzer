Willem Marx: Economic activity in remote communities like this largely centers on food, and in this landlocked country, a lot is imported.But the 15-day truck journey south across the Sahara from Libya relies on affordable fuel, also now in short supply. The local mayor invites us to tour the town's main market, where some food still sits in the stores, but shoppers are scarce.At Moussoro's only bakery, bread still leaves the oven as reliably as the sun rises. But there are fewer buyers for these baguettes than before and business is suffering badly.
