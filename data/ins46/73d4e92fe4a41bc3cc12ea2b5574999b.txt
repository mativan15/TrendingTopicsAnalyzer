Given the time and effort it takes to develop and execute new strategies, it's best not to introduce them too often. But there are instances when short-term strategic shifts are unavoidable — especially in today's ever-changing business context. Take, for example, the need to respond to calls for social change or demands from investors to turn around poor financial results. When responding to these kinds of pressures, executives must take care to align the strategic shifts they introduce with the larger picture of where their organization is heading and what it aspires to accomplish in the future — the company's 'vision.
