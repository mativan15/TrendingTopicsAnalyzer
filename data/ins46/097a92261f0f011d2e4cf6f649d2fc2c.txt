Star Entertainment Group is "not suitable" to hold a licence for its Sydney casino, a public inquiry has been told.Closing statements have been heard in an inquiry investigating criminality at the casino in Pyrmont.A number of executives have recently resigned from Star Entertainment GroupThe publicly available outcome is due to be released by the end of June. An inquiry by the NSW gaming regulator is investigating the company's suitability to run its casino in Pyrmont Several top executives have resigned over the course of the inquiry, which has heard allegations of money laundering, fraud and criminal infiltration at the casino.
