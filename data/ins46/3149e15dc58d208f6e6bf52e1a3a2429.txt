Passengers prepare to board a VIA train at Union Station on March 16 2016.Fred Lum/The Globe and MailVIA Rail has begun running buffer cars on some of its aging passenger trains, a safety measure designed to better protect passengers and crew in the event of a crash.Canada's government-owned passenger train system is using the empty cars, which act as crumple zones, on trains that include the stainless steel coaches, which were built in the 1950s. VIA said in an e-mail to The Globe and Mail it is taking the steps on the advice of a third-party engineering report while addressing the structural weaknesses of the cars.
