Devyn Health; Elektra; Kegg; Marianne Ayala/Insider Last year, funding for women's health startups exploded to an impressive $2.5 billion, about a threefold increase from the previous year, according to data from McKinsey and Rock Health. Still, a report from Silicon Valley Bank shows that less than 9% of healthcare venture capital funding is spent on women's health startups."There are six femtech funds in the US, all sub-$75 million for a $1 trillion TAM," Priya Oberoi-Cattai, a Goddess Gaia Ventures managing partner, said.
