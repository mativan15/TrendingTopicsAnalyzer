Why Proxy advisers are losing their power
economist.com
business
2022-06-02 09:45:29

Annual general meetings (agms) of shareholders used to be dull affairs. A company's owners would gather to elect board members or, after the global financial crisis of 2007-09 exposed the gulf between fat-cat bosses and their workers, cast (mostly non-binding) votes on executive compensation. In the past few years, though, they have turned into corporate confessionals, with nothing short of a company's soul at stake. Motions are proliferating on decarbonisation and diversity targets, political donations, workers' rights and much else besides.
