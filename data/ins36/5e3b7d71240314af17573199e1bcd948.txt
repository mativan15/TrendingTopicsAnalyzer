Tired mosquitoes would rather catch up on sleep than bite you: study
phys.org
news
2022-06-01 15:13:05

UC biologists discovered that overtired mosquitoes choose to catch up on sleep rather than seek a blood meal. Credit: Andrew Higley/UC Creative 
Turns out you're not the only one who needs a good night's rest to function well the next day. Researchers with the University of Cincinnati found that mosquitoes whose slumber is disrupted are more interested in catching up on their sleep than looking for food the next day. The research demonstrates how vital this biological function is even among insects.
