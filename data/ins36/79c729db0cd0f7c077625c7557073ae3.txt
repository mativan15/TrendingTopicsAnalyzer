Investors may be hoping for a 'Fed pause' to save them from higher interest rates, but that would be a big policy mistake, Mohamed El-Erian says
Insider
business
2022-05-28 12:30:00

The US stock market snapped its longest run of weekly declines in two decades in part as investors latched onto hopes the Federal Reserve may ease up on hiking rates in the face of new economic data — but 'stop-go' moves by the central bank would be a significant policy error for the world's largest economy, famed economist Mohamed El-Erian told Bloomberg. The Federal Open Market Committee later this month will walk into its meeting with indications that US inflation is cooling. Among them, the core Personal Consumption Expenditures Index – the Fed's preferred inflation gauge – rose to 4.
