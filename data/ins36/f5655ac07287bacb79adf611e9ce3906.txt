Many insurance firms and a few banks have hired forensic investigators to look into their systems' weaknesses on concerns that their treasury staff may have engaged in front running or insider trading, similar to what happened at Axis Mutual Fund. After reports that two Axis MF executives were implicated in front running - or, purchasing or selling shares based on non-public information that could impact the price of the share - came out, most of the major private insurance companies have started investigations to check the prospect of any employee exploiting their systems, people aware of the development said.
