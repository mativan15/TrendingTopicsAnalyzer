Image caption, The Peterborough Arch is being removed from Thorpe Meadows for a full inspection ahead of urgent repairsA wooden landmark that overlooks a main road into a city has been taken down so it can undergo essential repairs.Peterborough Arch, by Essex-born artist Lee Grandjean, has stood on Thorpe Meadows in Peterborough for 34 years.The 4m (13ft) high artwork, carved from iroko wood and pegged together, needs to be restored because of exposure to the elements.Louise Thirlwall, of Cambridgeshire's Nene Park Trust, said: "It's a sad day, but hopefully it will be back soon.
