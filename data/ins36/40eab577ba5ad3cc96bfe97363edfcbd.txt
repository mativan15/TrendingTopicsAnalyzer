Published: 05:08 EDT, 3 June 2022 | Updated: 08:23 EDT, 3 June 2022 NEW YORK (AP) - Wall Street pointed lower ahead of the opening bell Friday ahead of the U.S. government's monthly jobs report, which analysts expect to show some softening in an otherwise strong labor market. Less than two hours before U.S. markets open, futures for the Dow industrials had slipped 0.5% while futures for the S&P 500 fell 0.7%. Major U.S. benchmarks are up slightly this week, aiming for a second straight week of gains after a seven-week losing streak.
