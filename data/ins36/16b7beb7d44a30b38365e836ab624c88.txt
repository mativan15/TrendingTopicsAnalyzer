These San Francisco restaurants are going to close. The owners just don't know when.

When Yasmin Points signed a lease for her new pie shop at 300 De Haro in Potrero Hill, she knew her days were numbered.

'At the time that we moved into the space at De Haro, we knew there was a cut-off date because the owner said she was in the process of selling the building,' Points said. 'We were hoping by year two, we would be financially stable to find another place and we would move on out.'

But that didn't happen.
