Japan, Australia prime ministers to issue security document amid China concerns
japantimes.co.jp
news
2022-10-16 11:45:04

The leaders of Japan and Australia are planning to release a new declaration on security cooperation when they meet next weekend, amid China's growing military power and maritime assertiveness, government sources said Sunday.
The declaration highlighting the importance of a 'free and open Indo-Pacific' will likely be announced by Prime Minister Fumio Kishida and his Australian counterpart Anthony Albanese, who are scheduled to hold talks on Saturday in Perth, according to the sources.
