Published: 17:00 EDT, 3 June 2022 | Updated: 17:00 EDT, 3 June 2022 It's enough to make you cry into your beer – the price of a pint has passed £8 for the first time.Drinkers paid £2.30 on average for a pint in 2008, but soaring ingredient costs pushed that to £3.95 this year.This represents a 72 per cent increase since the financial crash in 2008, according to hospitality industry consultancy CGA.The highest price it found was £8.06 in London, while the cheapest cost just £1.79 in Lancashire. It did not name the venues, but this is the first time that CGA, which regularly analyses prices from random samples of the UK's 90,000 bars and pubs, has seen the price of a pint pass £8.
