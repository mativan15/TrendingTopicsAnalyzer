By Reuters Published: 09:38 EDT, 3 June 2022 | Updated: 09:38 EDT, 3 June 2022 June 3 (Reuters) - U.S. stock indexes opened lower on Friday, dragged down by shares of Tesla and Apple, while a solid jobs report fanned worries over tighter monetary policy and soaring inflation. The Dow Jones Industrial Average fell 261.96 points, or 0.79%, at the open to 32,986.32. The S&P 500 opened lower by 39.25 points, or 0.94%, at 4,137.57, while the Nasdaq Composite dropped 219.78 points, or 1.78%, to 12,097.
