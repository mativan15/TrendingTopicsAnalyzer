What Is Moral Support? America's Gratuity to Europe

IN the ‘News of the Day,' as presented in a moving-picture hall last July, there was shown to the audience a photograph of President Coolidge speaking in Cambridge, Massachusetts, on the one hundred and fiftieth anniversary of Washington's taking command of the Colonial forces. The caption read: ‘President holds out helping hand to Europe.'Naturally the photographer did not know what was in Mr. Coolidge's outstretched hand; but the reporters for the press were better informed. The headlines of one newspaper, July 4, ran thus: ‘Coolidge Bids Europe Frame Security Pacts.
