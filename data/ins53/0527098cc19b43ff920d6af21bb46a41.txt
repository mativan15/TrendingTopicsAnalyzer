By Reuters Published: 00:53 EDT, 31 May 2022 | Updated: 00:53 EDT, 31 May 2022 BENGALURU, May 31 (Reuters) - Indian shares fell on Tuesday ahead of key economic growth data, while a rally in technology stocks lost steam and Sun Pharmaceutical Industries dropped after the company posted a loss for the March quarter. The NSE Nifty 50 index was down 0.58% at 16,563.20, as of 0427 GMT, while the S&P BSE Sensex fell 0.73% to 55,517.99. Over the last three sessions, both indexes rose helped by a rally in beaten down technology stocks.
