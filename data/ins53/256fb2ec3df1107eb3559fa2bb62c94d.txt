The spectacular unravelling of the Government's mini-Budget – and indeed of its entire economic strategy – raises questions about more than Liz Truss's premiership. The swift U-turn from a professed drive towards a lower tax, less regulated economy with a tighter control on government spending to a re-embrace of ratchetting up taxation and maintaining hugely high state spending places the UK back into a pattern of managed decline. The cause of meaningful economic reform may have been put back for years.
