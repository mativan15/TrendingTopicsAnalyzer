In 2008, at the height of the Global Financial Crisis that emanated in the US, debt mutual funds faced a massive liquidity crisis and had to be bailed out by the Reserve . Since then, it has been an endless litany of woes for mutual funds. The crisis in the NBFC industry in 2018, triggered by the collapse of ILFS and subsequent bankruptcy of DHFL, saw many investors exposed to significant losses. In March 2020, Covid and lockdown induced illiquidity in the corporate bond market to which mutual funds have significant exposure, forced RBI to step in with Targeted Long Term Repo Operations.
