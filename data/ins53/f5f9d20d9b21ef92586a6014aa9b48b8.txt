He was one of the most venerated actors of him time. Guinness starred in the likes of The Ladykillers, Lawrence of Arabia and The Bridge on the River Kwai – for which he received the 1957 Best Actor Oscar. He was nominated five times and received a Lifetime Achievement Oscar in 1980. Incredibly, one of those nominations came for the 1977 film that came to define his career to his enormous despair. He even said, "I shrivel inside each time it is mentioned," but was less upset that it also made him an absolute fortune.
