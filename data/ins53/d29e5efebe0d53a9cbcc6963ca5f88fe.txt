Te Anau tourism operators have seen a small amount of international visitors since borders opened to visa-waiver countries in April Some tourism operators are expecting their busiest season yet Fiordland Jets co-owner Chris Adams is worried New Zealand's reputation as a 'world-class' tourism destination could be lost if not enough workers become available before September. In the hallway of a Bed & Breakfast in Te Anau is a map of the globe, punctuated by hundreds of differently coloured pins. Each year co-owner Nathan Benfell used to empty the map ready for the next tourists to record where they hailed from.
