More Video Julia Love and Davey Alba, Bloomberg News (Bloomberg) -- Waymo, Alphabet Inc.'s driverless-vehicle unit, plans to expand its robo-taxi service to Los Angeles, the third region where it will be offered.The company will follow the same playbook for the service, called Waymo One, that it has elsewhere, according to an announcement Wednesday. Local employees will try the program first, followed by so-called 'trusted testers' who sign nondisclosure agreements, followed by unaccompanied, paying members of the public.
