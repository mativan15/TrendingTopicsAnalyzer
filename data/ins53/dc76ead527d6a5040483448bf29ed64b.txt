'Can I write the budget?': People with disabilities share ideas for using N.B.'s surplus
cbc.ca
news
2022-10-18 11:00:00

With New Brunswick expecting a budget surplus of $135.5 million, people living with disabilities say the Blaine Higgs government should take "a serious look" at another, more significant payment to people living in poverty.Haley Flaro, executive director of Ability New Brunswick, says her not-for-profit is helping clients to navigate more "poverty issues" than ever these days.She said it's a "perfect storm," with concurrent crises in food security, affordable housing and a shortage of home support workers.
