Published: 17:40 EDT, 18 October 2022 | Updated: 17:40 EDT, 18 October 2022 Millions of pensioners could face an income squeeze of up to £430 next year after Liz Truss ditched her pledge to protect the ‘triple lock'.In a surprise U-turn, Downing Street warned she was no longer committed to raising pensions in line with inflation, despite having guaranteed just that only two weeks ago.It said the Prime Minister was now ‘not making any commitments on individual policy areas' ahead of the Halloween Budget – when ministers need to find £40billion in cuts.
