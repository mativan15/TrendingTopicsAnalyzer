By Colin Kellaher Shares of Joann Inc. slid more than 15% and hit a new all-time low in early trading Friday after the fabric and craft retailer posted disappointing quarterly results amid soaring inflation and supply-chain disruptions. The Hudson, Ohio, chain posted an adjusted loss of 22 cents a share and a 13% drop in sales to $498 million for its fiscal first quarter ended April 30. Analysts polled by FactSet, on average, were expecting an adjusted profit of 5 cents a share on sales of $524.5 million.
