Tesla shares plummeted Tuesday after CEO Elon Musk reportedly told executives he needed to scale back hiring and cut jobs due to growing concerns over the economy, adding to worries about the electric-vehicle maker that have spurred staggering losses for its shares this year. Experts have warned recession fears are likely overblown, but Musk reportedly wants to lay off 10% ... [+] of Tesla's nearly 100,000 workers. Getty Images Tesla shares fell more than 8% Tuesday to below $710, pushing the stock down more than 40% from its all-time high in November and wiping $68 billion from Tesla's market capitalization, which now stands at $734 billion.
