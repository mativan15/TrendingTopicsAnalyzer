Shares of former high-flying smartphone brand HTC have doubled as investors feed on hope and hype. It's becoming a familiar story: A once-famous brand, beloved by fans but unable to keep up with changing times, loses money hand over fist and its shares get beaten down. Suddenly, retail investors decide it's worth another look, and off the stock goes — to the moon. Back in January, GameStop Corp. became the cause celebre for underdogs and small shareholders seeking to inject some democracy back into equities markets.
