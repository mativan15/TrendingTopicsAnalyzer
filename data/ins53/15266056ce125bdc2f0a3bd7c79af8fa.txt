Published: 21:44 EDT, 19 October 2022 | Updated: 21:44 EDT, 19 October 2022 By Sudipto Ganguly Oct 20 (Reuters) - Hampered by injuries to key players, India will need their batters to find an extra gear in Australia to compensate for their bowling limitations if they are to lift the T20 World Cup trophy for a second time. India, who won the first edition of the tournament in 2007, lost fast bowling spearhead Jasprit Bumrah to a back injury and all-rounder Ravindra Jadeja to a knee problem in the lead-up to the event.
