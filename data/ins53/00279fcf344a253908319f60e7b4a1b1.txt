Korea to cultivate over 3,000 semiconductor talents by 2027: science ministry DAEJEON, May 30 (Yonhap) -- South Korea aims to nurture over 3,000 semiconductor talents by 2027 to maintain its competitive edge in the global market that has faced a severe chip shortage in recent years, according to the science ministry Monday. Science Minister Lee Jong-ho laid out the vision in a meeting with executives from key domestic semiconductor companies, including Samsung Electronics Co. and SK hynix Inc.
