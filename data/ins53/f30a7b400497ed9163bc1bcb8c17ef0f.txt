Published: 04:19 EDT, 15 October 2022 | Updated: 04:19 EDT, 15 October 2022 Liz Truss and Sir Keir Starmer have led tributes to Sir David Amess on the first anniversary of his murder. Sir David, who was an MP for nearly 40 years, was stabbed during a constituency surgery at Belfairs Methodist Church in Leigh-on-Sea, Essex, on October 15, 2021. He is survived by his wife Lady Julia Amess and their three children. Tributes for Sir David have been pouring in to mark one year since his murder. Southend-on-Sea Council announced that a tree will be planted in the city in memory of the veteran Conservative MP, who represented Southend West.
