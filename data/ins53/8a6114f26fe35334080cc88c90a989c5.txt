By Alice Gainer October 19, 2022 / 5:56 PM / CBS New York NEW YORK -- A hearing was held at City Hall on Wednesday about a $2 billion development project in Astoria, Queens.It has the backing of the mayor and Queens borough president, but some residents there say it should include more affordable housing.As CBS2's Alice Gainer reports, there were dueling rallies on the steps of City Hall on Wednesday morning.Those in support of the Innovation QNS project -- labor unions, some community business leaders and the Queens borough president -- say it would create good paying jobs.
