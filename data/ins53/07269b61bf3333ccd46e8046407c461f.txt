Australian prime minister Anthony Albanese toured flood-hit parts of Victoria as three states continue to deal with the aftermath of the 'very severe weather event.'Emergency services warned that waters up to 12 metres are expected to inundate properties in some areas.Two days of rain had triggered flash flooding in southeast Australia displacing thousands of people across the states of Victoria state, New South Wales and the island of Tasmania.'Premier Daniel Andrews and I saw first-hand today how the rising water has damaged homes and businesses,' the prime minister tweeted.
