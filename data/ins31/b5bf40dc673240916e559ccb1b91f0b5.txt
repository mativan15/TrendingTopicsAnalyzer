There is an important new development emerging in the freelance revolution. Naming the trend is still a challenge, but let's describe it as a greater emphasis on the primacy of freelancer success. A working title might be 'freelancer first.' Whatever the name, what we are seeing is a shift of importance to the longer-term success of the freelance movement. It is more focus on the experience and success of individual freelancers. It seeks to empower full-time freelancers, and increase the confidence and competence of part-timers, the 35-40% of employees who have 'side-hustles' in addition to full-time employment and are eager but hesitant to go full time.
