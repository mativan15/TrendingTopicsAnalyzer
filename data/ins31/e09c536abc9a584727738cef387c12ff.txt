Fritz Jorgensen/iStock Editorial via Getty Images For the first time in weeks, the tech sector wasn't overwhelmed by what has been an ongoing circus involving Elon Musk and his $44 billion acquisition of Twitter (TWTR). In fact, Musk actually made his most noise this past week with his day job, as the Tesla (TSLA) Chief Executive said he expects the electric carmaker's top executives to get back and start working at least 40 hours a week at Tesla (TSLA) offices. Musk laid down the law, so to speak, when he said in an email to Tesla (TSLA) employees that company leaders would no longer be allowed to work full-time from "some remote pseudo office" if they wanted to keep their jobs.
