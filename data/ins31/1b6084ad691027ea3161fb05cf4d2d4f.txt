Carl Laemmle, Adolph Zukor, Louis B. Mayer and Harry Warner were Jewish immigrants who made Hollywood big. They are among the founding fathers of the US film industry.

Adolph Zuker was born into a poor Jewish family in the Austro-Hungarian Empire, present-day Hungary. At age 16, he left for the US, where the young man first made a living by sweeping floors and other trades. He worked for a furrier until a cousin gave him the idea of entering the film business.
