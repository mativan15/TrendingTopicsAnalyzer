For years, the World Economic Forum gathering in Davos has been lampooned as a 'strange annual gathering of the very rich, the very powerful, and the very clueless' in the words of Michael Hirsh writing for Foreign Policy magazine in 2019. Hirsh captured the key irony of Davos: 'The people whom the forum asks to address the problems of global capitalism are often the very people who represent the problems of global capitalism.'After a two-and-a-half year pause, Davos was back this week, with a new element: A big and growing industry notionally interested in tipping the balance of power, or at least decentralizing some of that wealth.
