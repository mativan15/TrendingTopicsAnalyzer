MaryAnn Garza, 37, puts a show of support at her restaurant in Uvalde, Texas, after a gunman killed 21 people at a nearby school.

As I sit here, in virtual shock and dismay over two mass murders in 10 days, it is easy to feel broken and useless.

I don't need to go over what happened, other than two individuals that should never have been allowed to purchase guns — no, assault weapons — went out and literally hunted down innocent Americans, in part because our democracy and our country are at a tipping point.
