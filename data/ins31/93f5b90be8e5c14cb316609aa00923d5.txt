Prosecutors Strike at Heart of Zuma-Era Graft in South Africa

2022-05-27 10:48:07

(Bloomberg) -- South African law-enforcement officials arrested a former chief executive officer of the state ports and freight rail company and senior finance officials in the most significant step yet to bring those implicated in the looting of government coffers during former President Jacob Zuma's rule to book.

Most Read from Bloomberg

Siyabonga Gama, who was fired as Transnet SOC Ltd. CEO in 2018, appeared on charges of fraud, corruption and money laundering at a magistrates' court hearing in Johannesburg on Friday, along with Garry Pita, the company's former acting chief financial officer, and three other men.
