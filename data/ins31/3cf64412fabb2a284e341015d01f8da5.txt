Published: 13:00 EDT, 17 October 2022 | Updated: 13:00 EDT, 17 October 2022 LONDON Oct 17 (Reuters) - Britain's new finance minister Jeremy Hunt on Monday reversed the bulk of Prime Minister Liz Truss's economic growth plan of unfunded tax cuts, sparking a rally in battered UK assets. But UK borrowing costs and mortgage rates remain well above where they stood before the plan's Sept. 23 announcement, while some 80 billion pounds ($91.22 billion) has been wiped off the value of London's blue-chip FTSE stock index.
