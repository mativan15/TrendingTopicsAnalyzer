"Italy chose us. We will not betray [the country] as we never have" – this was the victorious cry of Giorgia Meloni, the woman who is set to become Italy's first-ever female Prime Minister after September's election. But this historic feat is not the only reason that the eyes of the world are now on Rome.The Meloni win represents another seismic shift – a far-right political figure now leads the fourth-largest economy in Europe.It is the first time someone of this ilk has taken power in Italy since World War 2, when fascist Benito Mussolini occupied the halls of power.
