Oracle employees say new location-based hiring restrictions are even impacting the cloud unit, which is the one place the typically frugal company was always willing to spend big for talent
businessinsider.com
news
2022-10-21 18:33:25

When database giant Oracle conducted layoffs — first in August and again earlier this month — the cuts impacted thousands of employees globally. Its marketing and customer experience (CX) divisions were among the hardest-hit units, but the company's cloud division, Oracle Cloud Infrastructure (OCI), appeared to remain untouched. But as Oracle tightens spending in a bid to cut as much as $1 billion in costs, not even OCI is immune, Insider has learned.Led by exec Clay Magouyrk, OCI is Oracle's big bet into cloud computing, where it faces stiff competition against heavyweights like Amazon Web Services, Google Cloud, and Microsoft Azure.
