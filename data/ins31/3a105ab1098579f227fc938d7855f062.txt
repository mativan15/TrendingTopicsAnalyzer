How Trump's ‘love letters' with Kim Jong Un could cost him
washingtonpost.com
politics
2022-10-19 16:07:33

In a presidency chock full of peculiarity, it ranked near the top of the list. It was Donald Trump's relationship with North Korean leader Kim Jong Un, whom Trump once threatened with 'fire and fury,' but with whom he later forged a relationship he lightheartedly compared to a 'love' affair.But there's increasing evidence that Trump's pride in his correspondence with Kim could cost him legally, experts say.Not only were Trump's letters with Kim apparently so treasured that he opted to show them off and took them with him when he left the White House — among other highly sensitive government documents — but now The Washington Post reports that Trump in late 2019 and early 2020 appeared to acknowledge these letters were indeed highly sensitive government documents.
