(Bloomberg) -- Prime Minister Liz Truss's government issued a stark warning to Conservative MPs not to back an opposition motion on fracking, as party managers struggle to control mutinous lawmakers.

The opposition Labour Party has called on Tory MPs to 'put country over party' and back its attempt to ban the extraction of shale gas by forcing the introduction of a draft law to do so.

It's a clear attempt by Labour to expose divisions in Truss's ruling Tories at a time when the premier is under intense pressure after being forced to backtrack on the bulk of her economic plans.
