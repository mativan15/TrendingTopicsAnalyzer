Sturgeon told she's 'gambling with people's lives'
express.co.uk
business
2022-10-17 09:32:13

Nicola Sturgeon has been accused of "gambling with people's livelihoods" after she insisted Scottish independence was "essential to build an economy that works for everyone".At Bute House later this morning, the First Minister will present the third independence paper outlining economic and currency plans for an independent Scotland.Ahead of the speech, Ms Sturgeon said the country had an "abundance of skilled people, innovative businesses, and natural resources".But the SNP leader warned: "Scotland's economy is one of the best performing in the UK - however, the UK economy, particularly post-Brexit, is now lagging behind many EU and international comparators.
