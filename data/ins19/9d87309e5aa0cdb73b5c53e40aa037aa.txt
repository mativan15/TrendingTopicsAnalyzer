When South Carolina voters cast their ballots in November, they will see two questions that will ask whether lawmakers should keep more state-collected tax dollars in rainy day accounts in case of economic downturns.

Voters will be asked whether the general reserve fund should equal 7% of the previous year's revenues, and whether the capital reserve fund should equal 3% of the previous year's revenues.

So why are voters being asked about how much money should be saved?

The state budget process is complex.
