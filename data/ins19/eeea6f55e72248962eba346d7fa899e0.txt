Published: 09:56 EDT, 1 June 2022 | Updated: 09:58 EDT, 1 June 2022 President Joe Biden will announce on Wednesday $2.1 billion in funding to strengthen the food supply chain and he also will meet with manufacturers to address the shortage of baby formula.  The funds are being directed toward projects that will increase food production and access, according to the Department of Agriculture.Specifically, the money includes $900 million for food processing workforce training and supply-chain infrastructure, $550 million for small food businesses and reducing food waste, $375 million for organic and urban agriculture projects, and $370 million to boost public access to healthy food, according to Reuters.
