Australian companies to face fines of $50m for data breaches
theguardian.com
news
2022-10-21 19:00:48

Companies that fail to adequately protect people's data could face fines of $50m or more under new legislation to be introduced next week.After Optus and Medibank reported significant breaches of customer data, including sensitive health information, the Albanese government was now moving to increase penalties for serious or repeated breaches of customer data.The attorney general, Mark Dreyfus, who has had cybercrime added to his portfolio, will introduce the legislation that would increase penalties for serious or repeated data breaches from $2.
