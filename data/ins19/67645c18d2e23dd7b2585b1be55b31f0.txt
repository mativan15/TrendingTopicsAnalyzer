Within its first 100 days in office, Jersey's government has done many of the things it said it would.

Its £56m mini-budget was approved by the States Assembly, the minimum wage will rise to £10.50 and free sanitary products are now available in some public places.But Chief Minister Kristina Moore now accepts the wording of some of her commitments were "over ambitious".
