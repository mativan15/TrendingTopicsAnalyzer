FOR the past dozen years, billions of lire and some of Italy's best brainpower have been lavished on the rocky lands and people of the Mezzogiorno. Centuries of poverty and exploitation have given Southern Italy a crushing inheritance of ignorance, suspicion, and apathy, but there now exists a proved blueprint showing how to tackle the worldwide problem of the have-nots.While the Italian North, centered in Milan and Turin, booms with unprecedented prosperity, the South remains Italy's poor relation.
