More than 200 Gopuff workers were let go last week as the company continues to adjust to an economic downturn and a slowdown in the startup funding environment.The cuts hit Gopuff's customer service team, full-time employees as well as temporary workers, according to people familiar with the matter. Gopuff has outsourced most of its customer service operations to the Philippines, these people said. They asked not to be identified discussing private topics.In a statement to Insider, Gopuff confirmed the layoffs, saying it affected less than 250 workers.
