Published: 23:07 EDT, 1 June 2022 | Updated: 23:10 EDT, 1 June 2022 The Barefoot Investor is urging Australians to put more into their superannuation with cost of living pressures expected to worsen.Retirees living on their own need to have $46,494 a year set aside to at least live comfortably, with the elderly copping the biggest jump in expenses in more than a decade.Scott Pape, a bestselling author also known as the Barefoot Investor, said those under 45 needed to switch to a growth-orientated fund, which were often riskier but delivered higher returns.
