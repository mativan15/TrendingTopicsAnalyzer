24K-Production/iStock via Getty Images By Scott Glasser U.S. equities have been at or near bear market levels in recent trading sessions, as tightening monetary conditions and fears of persistent inflation drove the S&P 500 Index to its lowest level since February 2021. A seventh straight week of losses marked the worst losing streak for stocks since 2001. The NASDAQ Composite and similar growth indexes entered a bear market in April as long-duration technology and related growth assets bore the brunt of sharply rising interest rates.
