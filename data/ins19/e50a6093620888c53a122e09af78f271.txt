Meg Rothwell obituary
thetimes.co.uk
news
2022-10-19 23:01:00

As one of the first female 'cadets' in the Foreign Office, Meg Rothwell was no stranger to sexism. When she was posted to Helsinki in 1976 she discovered that a fair amount of government business seemed to be conducted in the sauna, and that most government officials were male. Undeterred, she made an application to get on the next rung of the diplomatic ladder, that of first secretary, only to be stalled repeatedly by the ambassador to Finland. When he finally appointed her first secretary it was on condition that she also run the Diplomatic Service Wives' Association, a role traditionally reserved for the wife of the head of the chancery.
