Not all food has gone up in price — here's how to shop smarter and save while still eating well
thestar.com
news
2022-05-30 00:00:00

Temilade Fashesin has spreadsheets filled with food prices. For the last four years, the entrepreneur from Etobicoke has recorded her grocery bills — noting the price of individual products at specific stores, along with fluctuation in cost over time. In the past year, the price of food has increased at rates she has never witnessed.'When I told my friends that I have all the prices of all the things that I buy at each grocery store, they sometimes think that I'm crazy. But I'm seeing that almost everything is ridiculously more expensive,' Fashesin says.
