Hasbro's stock took a hit after the toy maker reported weak third quarter results, and CEO Chris Cocks warned that consumers are showing resistance to higher toy prices. Hasbro HAS had indicated at an investors day two weeks ago that the quarter would be weaker than originally forecast. But the results released today, and the accompanying conference call, raised concerns that toys - a darling category during the pandemic - will see sales drop due to inflation.
