Foreign banks appear to be raising dollars abroad and stuffing them into their U.S. branches. That's the takeaway from this chart, which was created using the Federal Reserve's weekly release of data on bank balance sheets from 2000 through Oct. 5 of this year.

One possible explanation for this behavior is that the foreign banks are taking advantage of the risk-free 3.15 percent interest rate that they can earn on reserves at the Fed, Marc Chandler, a managing director at Bannockburn Global Forex, wrote in an email.
