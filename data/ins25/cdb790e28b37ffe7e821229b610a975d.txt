They are the faces of the new Italian legislature. On Friday, October 14, Lorenzo Fontana, 42, elected from Verona (North) and close to Matteo Salvini (League, far right), was elected to the presidency of the Chamber of Deputies. A consecration for this hardline member of party, a traditionalist Catholic known for his opposition to same-sex marriage and his proximity to Russia. In 2019, he explained that he wanted to make the League "a hinge between Trump and Putin." Mr. Fontana's election sparked strong protests from the opposition.
