Slash Film It's rare to see the final movie in a mainstream series take as many huge and potentially divisive swings as "Halloween Ends" does, but however you feel about the movie, there's something admirable about trying to do something more. As /Film's Bill Bria explains, "Halloween Ends" makes the sequel trilogy's mission statement crystal clear. Director David Gordon Green's films aren't (wholly) concerned with nostalgia or fanservice, but rather the cyclical of trauma, violence, and evil, and how each of those things perpetuates itself.
