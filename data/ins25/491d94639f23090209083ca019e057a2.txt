Whenever stories bubble up about the disastrous human consequences of the United States' lack of universal healthcare – people dying because they can't afford live-saving drugs, or pregnancies that cost $30,000 – Australians love taking the high moral ground. We're adamant that our commitment to a publicly funded safety net, in areas like health, education and social services, is baked into our national psyche. Unlike those freedom-loving, low-taxing, hyper-capitalist Americans we look after our own, we claim.
