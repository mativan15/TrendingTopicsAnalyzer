Published: 15:50 EDT, 17 October 2022 | Updated: 18:09 EDT, 17 October 2022 The Brazilian Football Confederation is calling on courts to hand out criminal charges against fans whose behavior sparked a series of riots at two soccer games this weekend. A first division match between Ceará and visiting Cuiabá at Castelão Arena in Fortaleza on Sunday was suspended with the teams drawing 1-1 after fans brawled in the upper ring of the stadium.At the 36th minute of the second half, Ceará supporters tore down parts of the wire perimeter fencing and charged onto the pitch.
