When Sarah Hillier was first diagnosed with complex post-traumatic stress disorder and Type 2 bipolar disorder in 2017, she wasn't sure how she was going to earn a living.

Acting on medical advice, she'd left her job as a call centre manager. She needed time to focus on treatment and therapy for her mental health. But though Hillier received sickness benefits while on medical leave, those benefits dried up after a couple of months.
