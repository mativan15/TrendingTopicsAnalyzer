Western analysts say Russian President Vladimir Putin's increasingly haphazard, vicious, and unpredictable actions in Ukraine speak of a leader losing control and options.Though backing further and further into a corner, Putin is not entirely bereft of friends and allies, with Hungary's Prime Minister Viktor Orbán one such example.Orbán, who has long-standing ties to Putin, has repeatedly questioned the efficiencies of sanctions against Russia and its impact on gas prices, while often echoing the Kremlin's talking points.
