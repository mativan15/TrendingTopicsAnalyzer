Today, the UK's Competition and Markets Authority (CMA) has ordered Meta to sell Giphy, the GIF library, deeming its ownership anti-competitive. Meta is obeying, and promptly looking at paths to divest. This is the same CMA that has been extremely against Microsoft's pitch to buy Activision Blizzard, seemingly siding with Sony's complaints about the purchase, and even if their arguments don't always add up and sometimes even their facts are wrong, it shows the very real possibility that yes, this is a regulatory body that could potentially kill this $70 billion deal.
