 Former Congress president Rahul Gandhi will now have to appear before the Enforcement Directorate (ED) on June 13 for questioning in a money laundering case involving the party-owned National Herald newspaper, sources said on Friday.The fresh date follows a request from Mr. Gandhi's office to reschedule his June 2 summons as he is out of the country and is expected back on June 5.Both Mr. Gandhi and Congress president Sonia Gandhi have been summoned by the central agency after a fresh case in the National Herald newspaper-Associated Journal Limited matter was registered under the Prevention of Money Laundering Act (PMLA).
