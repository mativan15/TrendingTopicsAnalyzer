2020 and 2021 were the years of the SPAC. Called special purpose acquisition companies, these investment vehicles helped hundreds of companies go public quickly in the post-pandemic stock boom. A lot of these stocks put out huge investor presentations saying they would grow revenue in the triple-digits for the foreseeable future, garnering expensive valuations in the process. One of these stocks was Latch (LTCH -5.00%), a smart-lock technology company for apartment buildings. But investors have soured on the stock as it has failed to meet its growth projections.
