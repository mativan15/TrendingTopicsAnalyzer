Govs. Ron DeSantis (R-Fla.) and Greg Abbott (R-Texas) are not 'marginal actors' in 'the conservative movement,' notes Paul Matzko at The Dispatch. So their recent anti-speech moves against Disney and Big Tech suggest the 'old fusionist detente between social and business conservatives is breaking down.' Yes, 'the political bent of companies like Disney has changed,' but 'the pesky thing about principles is that they must be universally applied.' The govs 'have adopted a war-time mentality' with 'no principle so high that it cannot be violated' and 'no right so inviolate that it cannot be abridged.
