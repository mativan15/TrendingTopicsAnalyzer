Main Street Cities across the country are taking down Confederate monuments, which stand as a daily reminder of the country's racist roots. But when it comes to dismantling lingering symbols of white supremacy, Texan artists and other organizers are taking a different approach: They're reclaiming and repurposing a building once used by the Ku Klux Klan (KKK).
