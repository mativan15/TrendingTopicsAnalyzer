Manitoba Premier Heather Stefanson speaks in Victoria, on July 12.CHAD HIPOLITO/The Canadian PressThe Manitoba government says it is to spend up to $40-million toward the expansion of North America's largest tri-modal inland port.Premier Heather Stefanson committed funding that would go toward water and sewer service infrastructure for CentrePort's expansion in Winnipeg.The port provides access to rail, truck and air cargo operations at a site in the Rural Municipality of Rosser and in Winnipeg.The company has estimated $60-million is needed for the infrastructure upgrades.
