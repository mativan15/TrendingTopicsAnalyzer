Kakao co-CEO Namkoong Whon apologizes after the company's services crashed over the weekend at a press conference held Wednesday morning at the company's office in Pangyo, Gyeonggi. [PARK EUN-JEE] Kakao co-CEO Namkoong Whon resigned on Wednesday after the company's services crashed over the weekend when a fire broke out at a data center in Pangyo, Gyeonggi. Whon made the announcement at a press conference Wednesday at the company's office in Pangyo, near where the blaze broke out. Whon and co-CEO Hong Eun-taek issued an apology for the crash that affected a broad range of services including KakaoTalk, Kakao Pay, Kakao Bank and Kakao Games.
