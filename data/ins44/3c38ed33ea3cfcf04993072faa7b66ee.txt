How to create a more resilient power grid to avoid blackouts
thehill.com
news
2022-06-01 17:30:00

A recent report finds that the U.S. power grid is well prepared to maintain reliable electric service during 'normal' conditions. The problem is, extreme weather fueled by escalating climate change has become the new normal, and the resulting power outages will put more lives and livelihoods at risk. Last year, a winter storm in Texas left millions without power for days; a few months later in the Pacific Northwest, a 'heat dome' made millions more swelter in homes that had never before required air conditioning.
