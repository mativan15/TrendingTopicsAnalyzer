Firefighters work after a drone fired on buildings in Kyiv. Photo / APAuthoritarian regimes in Moscow and Tehran have in common international isolation, domestic crisis and conflict with the West. The Iranian-made drones that Russia sent Monday to divebomb Ukraine's capital delivered the most emphatic proof yet that Tehran has become a rare, increasingly close ally to the Kremlin, offering both weapons and international support that Russia sorely lacks. There is no deep love between Russia, newly a pariah for attacking another country, and Iran, for decades one of the most strategically isolated nations in the world.
