Amid the to-and-fro cannabis gold rush, Tilray Brands (TLRY 2.05%) is a contender to be the top dog. Though its shares are down by more than 85% over the past three years, the next 10 years might be different. The company will need to grow massively as more international marijuana markets open up via legalization and as increasing consumer interest drives pressure to innovate with products.
Can Tilray rise to the occasion? And if so, how much can investors expect it to grow? Let's examine its recent performance and sketch its trajectory through 2032 to find out.
