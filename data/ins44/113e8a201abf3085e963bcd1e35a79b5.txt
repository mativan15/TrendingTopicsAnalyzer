Today we're returning to a management topic that organizational psychologist Adam Grant and I discussed earlier this month: how to better train managers to oversee roles that didn't exist just a few years ago. If you haven't caught on, this is a bit of an obsession of mine. As companies face new challenges and new risks, they're, in turn, creating new jobs to address those issues. Take the head of remote work, for instance, a now-popular role designed to smooth the transition to hybrid work. The proliferation of new functions has made it increasingly common for managers to be in charge of operations and tasks they have never performed.
