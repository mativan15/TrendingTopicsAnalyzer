Market relief after tax cuts scrapped
thetimes.co.uk
news
2022-10-17 11:00:00

London's stock markets started the week on the front foot on relief that the government will scrap almost all of the tax measures announced in last month's growth plan.The FTSE 100 continued to steadily rise, with housebuilders up, as Jeremy Hunt decided to keep changes in stamp duty in place. Barratt Developments, which last week warned that the outlook for the year looked less certain as high mortgage rates hit sales, recovered 12 points, or 3.4 per cent, to 358¾p.
