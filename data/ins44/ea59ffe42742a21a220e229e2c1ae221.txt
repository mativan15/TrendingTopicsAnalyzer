Cryptocurrency Fraud Is Growing Fast And Consumers Must Be Protected
newsweek.com
news
2022-10-19 11:45:01

The cryptocurrency revolution began with the launch of Bitcoin in 2009, fueled by a rejection of pre-existing, centralized banking institutions in the wake of the economic crisis of 2008. Thirteen years later, cryptocurrency markets have grown to an assessed value exceeding $900 billion, but they have also created opportunities for new forms of fraud to emerge, such as that faced by Cy, a 52-year-old San Francisco man who was conned out of more than $1 million.Cy is far from alone, as the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) has projected that consumers are on track to lose more than $1 billion this year to cryptocurrency scams.
