(Bloomberg) -- Luiz Inacio Lula da Silva returned to his industrial roots in Sao Bernardo do Campo to reboot his bid for Brazil's presidency.He chose the satellite city on Sao Paulo's outskirts for a rally kick-starting his campaign for the runoff. Known as the cradle of Brazil's labor movement, it's here that he emerged as a political force in the 1970s, founding the metalworkers' union, organizing strikes and leading marches.'This is the street where we traditionally carry out our demonstrations, ever since the founding of the Workers' Party,' the two-times former president told supporters on Oct.
