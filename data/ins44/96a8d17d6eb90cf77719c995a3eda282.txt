Published: 11:23 EDT, 2 June 2022 | Updated: 11:23 EDT, 2 June 2022 June 2 (Reuters) - The Russian rouble eased towards 62 against the dollar on Thursday, stabilizing in a relatively narrow range after sharp and uncontrolled moves last week due to an imbalance of supply and demand on the Moscow Exchange. The market focus was on a new round of U.S. sanctions against Russia for what it calls a "special military operation" in Ukraine, with targets including 17 individuals, 16 entities, seven vessels and three aircraft.
