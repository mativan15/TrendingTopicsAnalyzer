Moussa81/iStock via Getty Images All indications are that sports betting is not a casualty of consumers pulling back on spending due to macroeconomic pressures. Early September gaming revenue reports out of New York, Indiana and Iowa have looked strong enough to keep analysts optimistic. The first full month of football, as well as a surge of interest in baseball pennant races and Aaron Judge's home run chase, helped push New York's sports betting handle back over $1B for the month and gross gaming revenue was a record $143M.
