The Conservative Party could find itself going "backwards" if Boris Johnson returned to Downing Street, Dominic Raab has suggested.

Mr Raab, the former deputy prime minister and a supporter of Rishi Sunak, noted he had stood by Mr Johnson and insisted he could eventually make a return to frontline politics.

But expressing his concern that the privileges committee could become a distraction, Mr Raab told the Today programme: "I just can't see in practice how the new prime minister, in office latest next Friday, could give the country the attention [and] the focus it needs and at the same time be giving testimony and answering all of those questions.
