Shoppers are watching their spending on food purchases due to high inflation.Photo: ed jones/Agence France-Presse/Getty ImagesListen to article(2 minutes)Retailers cut staff in May as they struggled with bloated inventories and the squeeze from high inflation, while employers more broadly staffed up ahead of summer, including strong hiring at hotels, restaurants and entertainment venues.The retail sector shed 61,000 jobs in May, an outlier in the labor market that added 390,000 workers to payrolls in May, the Labor Department said Friday.
