As the daughter of restaurant owners, I recall trips to the cash and carry being a regular feature of my childhood. Back then it felt like a club no one wanted to belong to, reserved for tired-looking business owners who longed to be anywhere else.The cash and carry was always dully lit, a warehouse with freezers the size of family cars. It was boring, the monotony broken only by registering the sheer randomness of the items you could buy (horrendous shell suits sitting next to Christmas decorations, opposite plastic slides for the garden).
