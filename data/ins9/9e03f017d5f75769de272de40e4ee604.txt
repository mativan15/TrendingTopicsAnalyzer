Sask. Premier Scott Moe looks to follow Quebec's lead in push for more provincial autonomy
ctvnews.ca
politics
2022-10-16 12:00:00

OTTAWA - In a push for more provincial autonomy to develop its natural resources, Saskatchewan Premier Scott Moe says he may follow Quebec's lead and make unilateral changes to the Constitution. Moe released a white paper this week — called ‘Drawing the Line: Defending Saskatchewan's Economic Autonomy' — detailing how he's prepared to fight what he calls federal government intrusion and interference into provincial jurisdiction of natural resources. The premier states the next step is to table legislation to 'clarify and protect constitutional rights belonging to the province,' with further details expected in his upcoming throne speech on Oct.
