Scott Heins/Getty Images News When the third quarter results came out, media and minority investors immediately slammed Barry McCarthy, Peloton's new CEO, for being unable to stop the cash bleeding. The truth however is that he had little time to do anything. From the day he assumed his leading position, to the day the Q3 period came to an end, only 1.5 months had passed. What did everyone expect? Magic? What the relevant results mirrored was Foley's legacy. An ever expanding inventory, a $400 million Ohio investment plan (cuts were made when McCarthy took over), a surreal stock based compensation schedule (for a company about to run out of money), a level of cash burning that would have inevitably ‘kneeled' Peloton it in just a couple of quarters… There was no touch with reality.
