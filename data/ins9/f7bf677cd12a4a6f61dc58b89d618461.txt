A scene in the first season of HBO's Succession condenses many of the dilemmas that the fashion industry has forced upon men in recent years. The heir Kendall Roy, played by Jeremy Strong, attends a meeting to try to reach an agreement with a young tech company. In the car on the way to the start-up's office, the aspiring business mogul changes his footwear, switching out his custom-made dress shoes for a pair of luxury athletic sneakers. But he still doesn't fit in: surrounded by workers in New Balance, his discomfort grows as the seasons progress.
