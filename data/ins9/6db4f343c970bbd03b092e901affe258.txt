Chief Justice asks jury to act 'impartially' as they retire to deliberate in Bruce Lehrmann rape trial
abc.net.au
politics
2022-10-19 00:25:35

The jury has retired to consider its decision in the trial of Bruce Lehrmann, the man accused of raping Brittany Higgins inside Parliament House in 2019.Defence lawyers for accused rapist Bruce Lehrmann have wrapped up their case in the ACT Supreme CourtThe prosecution finished giving its closing submissions yesterdayMr Lehrmann has pleaded not guilty to the charge of raping Brittany HigginsThe case ran for 12 days and heard evidence from almost 30 witnesses.Before they retired, Chief Justice Lucy McCallum told the jury they must act "impartially" and "dispassionately" as they go through the facts of the trial to reach a decision.
