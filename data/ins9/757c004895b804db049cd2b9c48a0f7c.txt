DjordjeDjurdjevic/E+ via Getty Images Inflation was low when I wrote the article It's Time For Inflation Hedges: Consider Gold, Mining Stocks And Farmland. Two years have gone by, and inflation certainly accelerated, from 1.4% to 8.2%. But how have the possible inflation hedges performed? The article's conclusion was, "There is no asset that is obviously a slam dunk for an inflation hedge, but gold, mining stocks and farmland should be considered." Farmland did turn out well, but "no slam dunk" was the wisest part of the article.
