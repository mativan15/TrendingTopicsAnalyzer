By Reuters Published: 07:43 EDT, 3 June 2022 | Updated: 07:43 EDT, 3 June 2022 By Rajendra Jadhav and Bharat Gautam June 3 (Reuters) - Gold discounts widened in India this week as demand faltered due to a rise in prices and a winding up of wedding season, while consumers remained wary of purchasing bullion in top consumer China amid a gradual relaxing of coronavirus restrictions. Indian dealers offered a discount of up to $9 an ounce over official domestic prices this week - inclusive of the 10.75% import and 3% sales levies, up from the last week's $5 discount.
