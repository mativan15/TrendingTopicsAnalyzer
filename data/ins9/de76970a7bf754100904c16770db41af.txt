This column ends in Australia, where politics has been shrivelling to a specialised branch of marketing; from whose 'communications' gurus modern British politicians have for decades sought advice on winning elections; and where in a federal election last weekend such advice hit the wall and a government fell: fell on merits rather than on message.But the column starts in the office of our chancellor, where this week a cabinet minister did something. At last! A plan! Why do I read the chancellor's proposals for helping households through energy price-rises with such a sense of relief? It's not, after all, as if these handouts are necessarily wise.
