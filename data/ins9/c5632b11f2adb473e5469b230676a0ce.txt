More than any other statewide race, the contest for Colorado attorney general this year seems to come down to one overwhelming question: Do voters want the office to be filled by someone who has a very focused approach to reducing crime?

Democrat Phil Weiser, who has held the office since early 2019, has overseen a wide range of legal issues, from studying potential effects of mergers on business competition to defending Colorado water rights to securing $520 million for the state in a national opioid settlement.
