ALL that the Johnsons had was the earth. Very often it seemed as if it were all they had ever had.It was true that they also had possessions, — a plough, a two-wheeled cart, tools, a bony brown mare which slowly dragged the plough and the cart about their rough four-acre plot, — but without the earth these things were useless. It was true that they also had a son.The Johnsons' son was named Benjy, and it was more than thirty years since they had surrendered to the idea that he was not right in his head.
