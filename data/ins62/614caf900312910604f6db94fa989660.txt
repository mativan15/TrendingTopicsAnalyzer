Chipmaker Micron's venture arm to invest $200 million in deeptech startups
indiatimes.com
news
2022-06-02 14:00:00

NEW DELHI: MIcron Ventures, the corporate venture capital (VC) arm of Micron Technology Inc., Thursday said it will be investing $200 million in deeptech startups with its Fund II. 20% of the invested capital in the new Fund II will be aimed at startups led by women and other underrepresented groups, an official statement said. Since its inception, Micron Ventures said it has made investments in 25 startups, yielding 'strong financial returns and multiple unicorn companies within the portfolio'. It said that 25% of the invested capital in Fund I has gone to startups led by underrepresented groups, surpassing the company's initial 20% target.
