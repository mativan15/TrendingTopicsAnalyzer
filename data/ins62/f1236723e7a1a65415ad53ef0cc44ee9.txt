China's Party Congress and 'covid zero' policyA security personnel waits to check for visitors' green QR health codes at the entrance of a shopping area, in Shanghai, China, 18 October 2022 (issued 19 October 2022). As China holds its 20th National Congress of the Communist Party of China (CPC) this week, many anxiously dwell on the hope that after the event, the country would relax it's 'covid zero' policy, a strategy that has dominated everyday life for close to three years. Chinese and foreigners alike look forward to 'day zero', which the high profile meeting has come to symbolise, in a country disconnected from the rest of the world and where health codes and PCR tests reign.
