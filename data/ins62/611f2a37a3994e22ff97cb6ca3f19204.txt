While the tech industry has seen many products that have become tremendous, world-changing successes—such as the iPhone or Microsoft's Windows software—it's also had its share of 'flops.' Even the giants haven't been immune (remember Apple's wireless charging mat or Amazon's Fire phone?). High-profile product failures from the industry's biggest names can be looked at both as cautionary tales and motivation for other, smaller tech companies. Building on the lessons learned from these examples could help a company roll out the next world-changing tech innovation.
