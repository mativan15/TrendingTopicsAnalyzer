Inflation soars to 10.1% amid rising food costs
thetimes.co.uk
news
2022-10-19 06:14:00

Inflation rose to 10.1 per cent in September from 9.9 per cent in August, adding pressure to the Bank of England to continue raising interest rates to bring down prices.Darren Morgan, director of Economic Statistics at the ONS, said: 'The rise was driven by further increased across food, which saw its largest annual rise in over 40 years, while hotel prices also increased, after falling this time last year.'September's CPI rate will be used as part of the Work and Pensions Secretary's annual benefits uprating review.
