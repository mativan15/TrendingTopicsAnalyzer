Fredrick E. Vars (University of Alabama - School of Law) has posted The Slayer Rule: An Empirical Examination (ACTEC L.J. (forthcoming Spring 2023)) on SSRN.  Here is the abstract: Elmer Palmer murdered his grandfather. The undisputed motive was money. The grandfather's will included a large gift to Elmer, which the grandfather was poised to eliminate. Elmer acted first. Under the law at the time, Elmer would inherit despite having intentionally killed his grandfather: the existing will controlled.
