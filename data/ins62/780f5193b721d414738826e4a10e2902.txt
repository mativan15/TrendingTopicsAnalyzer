The clocks will this month be reverting back to Greenwich Mean Time (GMT), which means there'll be going back a full hour as we head into the darker winter monthsThe change will allow for more sunlight in the mornings (Stock Photo)At the end of October, early risers can look forward to an extra hour curled up in bed, with clocks reverting back from British Summer Time (BST) to Greenwich Mean Time (GMT). Each year, on the last Sunday in October, the clocks will go back one hour at 2 am, allowing for more sunlight in the mornings as we enter the darker months ahead.
