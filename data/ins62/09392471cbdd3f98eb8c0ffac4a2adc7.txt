As newly resigned British Prime Minister Liz Truss begins packing up for her move out of No. 10 Downing after 44 days in office, assuming she had even unpacked in the first place, the world is left with perhaps the most spectacular flameout in contemporary political history, at least in the West.

Recency bias is a thing, sure, but has anyone else reached the pinnacle of power then lost it, entirely by virtue of their own bad decisions, in less than seven weeks? That has got to be some kind of record.
