Yen Sinks To 150 Per Dollar, Lowest Since 1990
ibtimes.com
news
2022-10-20 07:57:37

Analysts say the yen will continue to slide as long as the Bank of Japan's policies differ from the Federal Reserve's
The falling yen hit 150 per dollar for the first time since 1990 on Thursday, driven down by the contrast between Japanese monetary easing and aggressive US interest rate hikes.The currency has plunged from February levels of around 115 as the Bank of Japan sticks to its longstanding ultra-loose policies, designed to encourage sustainable growth in the world's third-largest economy.
