By Reuters Published: 02:14 EDT, 21 October 2022 | Updated: 03:44 EDT, 21 October 2022 By Boleslaw Lasocki Oct 21 (Reuters) - Finnish forestry firm Stora Enso warned on Friday that fourth-quarter profit for its packaging business, its biggest division, would be hurt by soaring energy costs, sending its shares sliding. Its stock fell 6% in early trade despite the company having posted a better-than-expected 29% jump in third-quarter operating profit. European manufacturers are grappling with sharp rise in energy prices caused by Russian curbs on natural gas.
