REUTERS/Toby Melville/Pool/File PhotoLONDON, Oct 18 (Reuters) - Prime Minister Liz Truss has apologised for threatening Britain's economic stability after she was forced to scrap her vast tax-cutting plans and embark on a programme of "eye-watering" public spending cuts instead.Truss watched silently in parliament on Monday as her new finance minister Jeremy Hunt tore up the radical economic agenda the prime minister had proposed less than a month ago, and which triggered a bond market rout so deep that the Bank of England had to intervene to prevent pension funds from collapsing.
