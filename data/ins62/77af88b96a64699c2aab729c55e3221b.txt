Climate change disproportionately impacts people of color and low income communities. The Inflation Reduction Act provides a way to change that.
Since the passage of the Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) in August, critics and supporters alike have been parsing what it will mean for our social and economic future. This is most true for climate advocates, who see both the benefits of the economic incentives that will drive action on climate change, while also being wary of the 'business as usual' economics that have harmed communities and hampered innovation.
