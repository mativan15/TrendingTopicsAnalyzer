Hibbett 1Q Profit Sinks Without Stimulus Spending
marketwatch.com
news
2022-05-27 10:52:00

By Dean Seal Hibbett Inc. missed earnings expectations for the fist quarter after sales dropped without the spending boost from government stimulus money that consumers received a year earlier. The sporting-goods retailer on Friday posted net income of $39.3 million, or $2.89 a share, for the quarter ended April 30, compared with $84.8 million, or a record $5 a share, in the prior year. Analysts polled by FactSet had expected earnings of $3.05 a share. Net sales declined 16% to $424.1 million, ahead of analyst expectations of $418 million, according to FactSet.
