Former employee of short-term lender explains how it snared consumers in debt traps
typepad.com
news
2022-05-30 17:45:00

New Mexico recently enacted a 36% rate cap on consumer loans. Among the remarks that motivated them to do so was the following: Thank you Madam Chair, Members of the Committee. My name is Will Hancock and I'm a student at the University of New Mexico School of Law. I had the displeasure of working briefly for a short-term loan company in Albuquerque. I completed the training and left shortly thereafter because I felt I was actively ruining people's lives through predatory practices. While I was there I saw countless customers come in seeking to pay off their loans.
