When he was 8 years old, John Carpenter picked up the violin. His father, a violinist himself and a music teacher, was his tutor. 'The only problem,' Carpenter cackles on the phone from his office in Los Angeles, 'was I had no talent.' That might be a bit of a misstatement. Young Carpenter went on to become a legendary horror director, writing scores for many of his most iconic movies. His eerie synths are now as much a part of his legacy as his visuals.Still, he never imagined he'd become what he is now: a full-time professional musician.
