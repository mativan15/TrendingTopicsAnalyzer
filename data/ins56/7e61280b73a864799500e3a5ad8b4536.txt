This story is part of Mysteries of the Brain, CNET's deep dive into the human brain's infinite complexities. 

I'll never forget the last real conversation I had with my late mother-in-law, Grace. She'd had Alzheimer's disease for a number of years, and making a call on her smartphone was getting tough for her. So I was surprised to see her name pop up on my screen, calling me on a random Thursday night in the fall of 2021. The author's mother-in-law, Grace Peters, on her 84th birthday in 2020. She died just a few weeks after turning 85, in 2021, and spent her last year battling Alzheimer's.
