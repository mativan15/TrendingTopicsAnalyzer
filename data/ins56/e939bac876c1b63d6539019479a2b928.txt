By Colin Kellaher Shares of Rimini Street Inc. jumped more than 10% Wednesday after the provider of enterprise software products and services boosted its stock-buyback plans and prepaid part of its debt. The Las Vegas company, which has about 87 million shares outstanding and sports a market capitalization topping $600 million, said its board authorized the repurchase of up to $50 million of stock over the next four years from a prior authorization of up to $15 million over two years. Rimini Street also said it prepaid $5 million of debt under its term loan, leaving a loan balance of about $81.
