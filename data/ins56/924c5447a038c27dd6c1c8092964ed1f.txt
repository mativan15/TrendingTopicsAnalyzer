111 Million Buildings in the U.S. Rely on Gas, but a 19th Century Mansion May Provide a Solution
entrepreneur.com
news
2022-10-19 07:00:00

The United States' existing stock of 111 million buildings — roughly two-thirds of which will still be standing in 2040 — pose a daunting challenge to decarbonization efforts.

Geothermal cooling and heating is among the technologies that can negate the need for natural gas, but it can prove costly and complex in dense urban areas. A historic home on Chicago's near South Side, however, provides a blueprint for how it can be done.

Glessner House, completed in 1887, was a radical departure from ornate Gilded Age mansions of the era with its relatively simple exterior design.
