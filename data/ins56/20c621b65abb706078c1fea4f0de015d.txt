Visits to the dentist are important for your oral health, but they can also be expensive. It's estimated about one third of Canadians do not have any form of dental insurance. The last thing you want to hear is that you need a filling, a crown, or a root canal, but if your dentist recommends a procedure do you always need to follow through with it? When should you consider getting a second opinion? Consumer Reports took a look at some common dental procedures to help you figure out if and when you really need them.
