COVID-era ‘baby boom' marks the first major reversal in declining fertility rates since 2007
msn.com
science
2022-10-17 20:30:00

If think you're seeing babies everywhere, there's good reason. A new paper confirms that there was an increase in births last year, primarily due to the COVID-19 pandemic.'We … found that the COVID pandemic resulted in a small ‘baby bump' among U.S.-born mothers,' according to a paper circulated by the National Bureau of Economic Research on Monday. Combining the 2020 fertility decline and the 2021 baby bump, the pandemic led to an increase in births among U.S. mothers, of around 46,000 children, the study concluded.
