A blue-collar worker who retired early thanks to his real estate portfolio explains how he used a self-directed IRA to buy property and scale up his portfolio
businessinsider.com
news
2022-10-18 17:32:25

When thinking about individual retirement accounts (IRAs), assets like stocks and bonds probably come to mind. Those assets — plus mutual funds and exchange-traded funds (ETFs) — are typically held in these types of accounts. It's possible, however, to buy real estate in an IRA. That's exactly what one property investor did to scale up his portfolio.Mark, who prefers not to share his last name for privacy reasons, built a 25-unit portfolio in less than five years, which Insider verified. He did it on a police officer salary that never exceeded $52,000 a year.
