REUTERS/Lisi NiesnerBERLIN, June 3 (Reuters) - Germany's Bundestag lower house of parliament on Friday approved the creation of the 100 billion euro ($107.2 billion) special defence fund that Chancellor Olaf Scholz announced in response to Russia's invasion of Ukraine.The money is destined to top up the regular defence budget of around 50 billion euros over several years to help re-build Germany's military that has suffered years of neglect following the end of the Cold war.
