Indiewire Animation, like social progress, takes a long time. It's been thirteen years since Henry Selick's last film, 'Coraline,' premiered to critical adoration and major box-office success. Despite a few Oscar nominations and becoming the third-highest-grossing stop-motion film of all time, it still took over a decade for Selick to get another movie made.Thanks to co-writer Jordan Peele and his Monkeypaw Productions, Selick was able to birth another animated fantasy with 'Wendell & Wild,' a twisted comedy about the dangers of playing with life and death.
