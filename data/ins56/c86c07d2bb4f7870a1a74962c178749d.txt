Google, Temasek Back India's ShareChat With $300 Million At $5 Billion Valuation

ShareChat's latest funding round gives the seven-year-old company a $5 billion valuation. SOPA Images/LightRocket via Getty Images 
Mohalla Tech, the Bangalore-based parent of ShareChat, has raised nearly $300 million from Google, Indian media conglomerate Times Group and the Singapore government's Temasek Holdings, according to a report from Reuters. The newest funding round values the social media unicorn at nearly $5 billion. The deal is set to be announced as early as next week. ShareChat's app, which is available in 15 languages, has 180 million monthly active users, and more than 32 million creators, according to their website.
