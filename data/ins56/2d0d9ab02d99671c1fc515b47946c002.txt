Japan, US to stage joint drills for remote island defense
nhk.or.jp
news
2022-10-21 10:45:00

Japan and the United States plan to stage drills in November to simulate remote island defense in which joint logistics bases will be set up for the first time. Defense Minister Hamada Yasukazu said on Friday the Japanese Self-Defense Forces and the US military will hold the drills from November 10 to 19 in southern Japan. This will be 16th active Japan-US exercise to date. The drills will involve 36,000 personnel, 30 vessels and 370 aircraft. The aim is to simulate a response to ballistic missile attacks and defense of Japan's remote islands.
