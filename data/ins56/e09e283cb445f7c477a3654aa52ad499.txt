To the list of economic headaches plaguing consumers and retailers these days, consider an old truism: what goes up must come down. The price of gas stings, but the auto manufacturing backlog caused by the pandemic means the car you're fueling could (at the moment) be worth near or even more than what you paid for it. Average prices for used cars surged last year by nearly 30%. Similarly, an affordable house in much of the US has been a unicorn.
