You've Got 2 Weeks to Lock in the 9.62% Rate on I Bonds: How to Buy Them

Series I savings bonds provide a rate of return based on inflation, and the current rate is as high as it's ever been. But you've only got a few more weeks to lock in that interest rate for the next six months -- the current inflation rate on I bonds expires Oct. 28, 2022.Series I savings bonds pay more when inflation goes up. Inflation has slowed in recent months, but the projected rate for the next six-month period is 6.47%, very good on its own for a low-risk investment.TreasuryDirect, the site that sells I bonds online, struggled with demand earlier in 2022 -- computer systems crashed and callers were on hold for hours.
