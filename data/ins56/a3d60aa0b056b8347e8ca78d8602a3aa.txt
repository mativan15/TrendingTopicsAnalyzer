'Saturday Night Live' (1975- )This long-running sketch-variety series may be TV's most influential show, despite its unevenness. Yes, the sketches range from classic to awful, and they always have; but 'SNL' has nonetheless consistently served as a training ground for some of the best American comics and comic actors, many of whom (from John Belushi and Will Ferrell to Jimmy Fallon and Tina Fey) go on to dominate the world of entertainment. It also provides the kind of political satire that, when it hits the nail on the head, can affect public opinion.
