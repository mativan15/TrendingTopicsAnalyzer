U.S. adds 390,000 jobs in May and shows economy is still at cruising speed
marketwatch.com
news
2022-06-03 12:00:00

The numbers: The U.S. added a stronger-than-expected 390,000 new jobs in May, signaling the labor market and broader economy are still going strong despite some fraying around the edges. Economists polled by The Wall Street Journal had forecast a smaller increase of 328,000 new jobs after reports leading up the government's employment survey had hinted at slower hiring.
