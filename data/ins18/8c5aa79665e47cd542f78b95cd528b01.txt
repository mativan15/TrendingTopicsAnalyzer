By Bernd Debusmann JrBBC News, WashingtonImage caption, A conceptual image of a Taser-equipped drone.The US company behind Tasers plans to produce stun gun-armed drones it claims will help stop school shootings.The Arizona-based company, Axon, says that the high-tech solution is necessary amid a "fruitless" debate on gun policy in the US.The announcement, however, earned a stern rebuke from the company's own ethics board.Nearly 30 school shootings have taken place in the US this year.Axon - formerly known as Taser International - said that it has formally begun developing a miniature, lightweight Taser that can be deployed on a drone or robot, as well as "targeting algorithms" to assist operators safety aim the device.
