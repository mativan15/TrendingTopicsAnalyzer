Student-loan borrowers who combined debt with their spouses can now legally separate their balances — but they need to take action by the end of October to get federal relief
businessinsider.com
news
2022-10-19 16:24:12

For over a decade, federal law prohibited student-loan borrowers who combined their balances with a spouse to separate the loans, blocking them out of federal debt relief.President Joe Biden changed that by signing into a law a bill that would allow those borrowers to separate their loans — but they now have less than two weeks to make use of a key federal benefit before it expires.The spousal joint consolidation loan program gave married couples the option to combine their balances, with the intent of a more affordable monthly payment with a single interest rate.
