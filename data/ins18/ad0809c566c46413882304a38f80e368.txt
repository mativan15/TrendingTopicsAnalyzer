GROVELAND, Fla. — On a muggy summer morning, Donna Brodus Forrest stepped into an old, nearly forgotten cemetery hidden in a thicket of trees, in pursuit of a family mystery.For much of her life, Forrest had heard stories about her grandmother, Viola Brodus — a laborer who was born just a few years before the century turned, died at 41 and was buried in the Old Groveland Cemetery in 1938. Before Forrest's father died eight years ago, he shared another detail: the possible location of his mother's grave.
