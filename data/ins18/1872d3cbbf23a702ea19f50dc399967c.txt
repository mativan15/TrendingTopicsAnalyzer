NEW DELHI: Shares of farm machinery and construction equipment maker have fallen nearly 14 per cent from their early April highs, more than Sensex's 8 per cent slide during the same period. Ace investor Rakesh Jhunjhunwala recently tendered 3.57 per cent stake or 47,19,362 shares of the company in a recent open offer by the new co-promoter Kubota. The ace investor held a 5.68 per cent stake in the company as of February 18.

The tractor maker's March quarter results were also forgettable, with profit falling 28 per cent on a 32.
