Bank of England chief David Roberts in conflict of interest over mentoring job
thetimes.co.uk
news
2022-10-19 23:01:00

The chairman-elect of the Bank of England has been caught up in a conflict of interest after revealing he has been privately mentoring a senior unnamed City business leader.David Roberts, who formally takes over as chairman of the Bank on October 24, revealed in letters to MPs that he originally had planned to continue mentoring the executive until the end of 2022, but had changed his mind.Roberts wrote in a letter dated October 14: 'Given the recent period of market volatility — and the actions taken by the Bank in response to emerging financial stability risks — I have chosen to end this professional relationship with immediate effect.
