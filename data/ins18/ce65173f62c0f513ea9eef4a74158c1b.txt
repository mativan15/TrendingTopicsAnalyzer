[Photo/Agencies] UNITED NATIONS - More than 2.8 million people have been impacted by Nigeria's worst floods in a decade, with 1.3 million displaced and hundreds of lives lost, said UN Secretary-General Antonio Guterres on Friday, expressing his sadness at the devastation. The secretary-general extended his deepest condolences to the government of Nigeria and to the affected families, reiterating the United Nation's continuing commitment to supporting the Nigerian government "in this challenging time.
