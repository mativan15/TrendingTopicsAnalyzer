Ontario proposes to double fines against home builders, sellers who unfairly cancel an agreement
theglobeandmail.com
news
2022-10-20 15:08:03

A construction worker at a new housing development in Ottawa, on Oct. 14.Sean Kilpatrick/The Canadian PressOntario is planning to double the maximum fines for new home builders and sellers who unfairly cancel a project or purchase agreement.The proposed changes to the New Home Construction Licensing Act would, if passed, increase penalties to a maximum of $50,000, up from $25,000.As well, individual developers who repeatedly violate the New Home Construction Licensing Act would face fines of $100,000, up from $50,000 and corporations would face fines of $500,000, up from $250,000.
