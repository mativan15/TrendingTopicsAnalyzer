Small business: An unqualified success . . . founders who skipped final exams
thetimes.co.uk
news
2022-06-02 23:01:00

One person who won't be waking up with nightmares about the Leaving Cert this week is the tech entrepreneur Brendan Morrissey. That's because he never sat it. The Kilkenny man, who is dyslexic, left school at 15.'I didn't understand school at all but went on to create an edtech company, which is quite ironic,' he says.eSchools is used by 26,000 schools around the world. It's just one of 23 businesses he owns. In all he employs 350 staff, in nine offices globally.As well as software products for the music and entertainment business, he has also developed iHelp, a mental health platform that he plans to give away free of charge to youngsters.
