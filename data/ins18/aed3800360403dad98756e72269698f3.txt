By Reuters Published: 00:19 EDT, 18 October 2022 | Updated: 00:19 EDT, 18 October 2022 BENGALURU, Oct 18 (Reuters) - Indian shares jumped to three-week highs in early trade on Tuesday, as rate hikes fears were soothed after the country's central bank said inflation looked set to ease and as risk sentiment improved globally on Britain's fiscal policy U-turn. The NSE Nifty 50 index rose 1.02% to 17,488.25 as of 0354 GMT and the S&P BSE Sensex advanced 1.04% to 59,016.61, set to add to two straight sessions of gains.
