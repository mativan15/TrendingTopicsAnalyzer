The financial documents you get in the mail are sort of a necessary evil. After all, they probably contain information you need, so as much as you may not appreciate the clutter they cause, it's easy to see why financial institutions send them out.At some point, though, you may be inspired to do a cleanout and free up some space in your office filing cabinets. The question is, which documents do you keep versus save? It can be hard to know. But Suze Orman says these six documents are worth keeping for at least three years.
