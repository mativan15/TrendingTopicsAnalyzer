Stan Guilfoyle.Inheriting his interest in politics from his mother who was active in the AWNL – the Australian Women's National League (one of the groups that formed the Liberal Party). Stan was a foundation member of the Liberal Party. Stan became an influential member of the Victorian Liberal Party especially on the financial side in the 1960s.Charged by Menzies to find a 'home' for the party (that is, a head office) Stan took on the task and found Centenary Hall in Exhibition Street which the state government were keen to sell.
