(Minoru K/Shutterstock)BURLINGTON, MA — Police will visit Burlington businesses that hold liquor licenses to conduct random alcohol compliance checks during the first week of June, Chief Thomas Browne said Friday.The checks are meant to curb underage drinking and the sale of alcohol to minors. Underage buyers typically volunteer with police to visit local businesses and try to buy alcohol. If their purchase is successful, the business fails the check and can be subject to a fine or suspension.
