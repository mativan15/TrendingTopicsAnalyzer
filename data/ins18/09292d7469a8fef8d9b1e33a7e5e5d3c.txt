Peter Higgs in 2008.Photo: Alamy Stock PhotoBy Andrew CrumeyJune 3, 2022 10:06 am ETListen to article(3 minutes)In July 1964, the 35-year-old physicist Peter Higgs remarked, 'This summer I had the only really original idea I've ever had.' Forty-eight years later, Mr. Higgs was in Geneva for the announcement that his idea had been experimentally verified. The subatomic particle known as the Higgs boson hadn't actually been seen, but its influence on subatomic debris in the Large Hadron Collider at CERN in Switzerland was unmistakable.
