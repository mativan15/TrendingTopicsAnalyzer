Across the sub-region, social protection programs have been essential to enable households to cope with high food prices and localized shortages. The poorest households are the most vulnerable since they spend the largest share of their income on food.In Somalia, "the Baxnaano program came to us at a right moment,' relates Ms. Nishey Mohamed Kheyre, a mother of eight, living in the Bakool region. 'Our livelihood was mainly dependent on farming, but we have been impacted in recent years by bad harvests and locust infestation.
