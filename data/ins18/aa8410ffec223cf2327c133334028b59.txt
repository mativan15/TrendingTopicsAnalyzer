By Reuters Published: 05:32 EDT, 30 May 2022 | Updated: 05:32 EDT, 30 May 2022 By Iain Withers LONDON, May 30 (Reuters) - The U.S. dollar resumed its backward slide on Monday as risk appetite across markets tentatively strengthened, supported by more encouraging economic data and bets that the Federal Reserve will tighten policy at a slower pace. The dollar index - which tracks the greenback against six major rivals - is on track for its first monthly drop in five, as the safe haven currency loses steam after a breakneck start to the year.
