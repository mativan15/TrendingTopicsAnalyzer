During the pandemic, consumers rediscovered forgotten vegetables, unfashionable tinned food and the frozen food aisle, partly out of convenience (they lasted longer in homes when people were stuck inside), partly because they had more time than before to think about food but also because, in times of uncertainty, people want comfort food. And now, with a war taking place in Europe, the cost of living crisis and the need to save money, it's happening again—a lot sooner than people might have hoped—and people are once more rediscovering old-school ingredients.
