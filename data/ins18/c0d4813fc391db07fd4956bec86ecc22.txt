Retired banker has 1k plants of 400 varieties in his Agra house, making it a tourist spot
indiatimes.com
news
2022-06-04 18:30:00

AGRA: In congested Shahganj, the house of a retired banker has become a point of attraction for not just local residents but also foreign tourists from countries such as Germany Japan , and Bangladesh Former State Bank of India employee, Chandrashekar Sharma, calls his 300-year-old residence a 'greenhouse' as it is home to over 1,000 plants of 400 different varieties planted across a 6,300 sq ft area.A large part of the outer walls of his property is covered by a vertical garden. Interestingly, he has grown lotus plants, rare in the Agra region, at his residence.
