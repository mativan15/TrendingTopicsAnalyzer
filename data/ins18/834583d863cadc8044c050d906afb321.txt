REUTERS/Florence Lo/Illustration/File PhotoORLANDO, Fla., Oct 20 (Reuters) - The yen's daily slide to new multi-decade lows against the dollar and the global bond market rout are pushing the Bank of Japan's 'yield curve control' (YCC) close to breaking point.Were the BOJ to abandon or even modify its implicit 0.25% cap on the 10-year Japanese government bond yield, the yen's probable surge would lob a grenade into world markets already shell-shocked by a dire 2022.
