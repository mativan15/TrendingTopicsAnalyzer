United States considers producing weapons with Taiwan amid rising tensions with China

The US government is considering a plan to jointly produce weapons with Taiwan, a business lobby has said, with the intention to speed up arms transfers to bolster Taipei's deterrence against China. It is yet to be determined which weapons will be considered, according to the US-Taiwan Business CouncilA US state department spokesperson said all options were being looked to transfer defensive capabilities to TaiwanTaiwan's Foreign Ministry described Taiwan-US relations as "close and friendly"US presidents have approved more than $20 billion in weapons sales to Taiwan since 2017 as China has ramped up military pressure on the democratically-governed island Beijing claims as its own territory.
