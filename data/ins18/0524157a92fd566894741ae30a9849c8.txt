Published: 05:52 EDT, 20 October 2022 | Updated: 05:52 EDT, 20 October 2022 The AA's chief has said more cycle routes will benefit British drivers as they will reduce congestion and save them money - as the Government debates cutting walking and cycling scheme budgets.The motoring organisation's president Edmund King said that motorists taking fewer short car trips and cycling instead would reduce road congestion and spending on home fuel costs.'Every driver is a pedestrian and most cyclists are drivers, therefore it is in everyone's interest to level up walking and cycling infrastructure,' said Mr King.
