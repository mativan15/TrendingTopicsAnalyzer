Vanguard ‘not confident' U.S. Treasury rates have peaked after painful bond-market losses. Why ‘risk-reward profiles' still appear more attractive across fixed income
marketwatch.com
news
2022-10-20 13:00:00

U.S. Treasury rates may have yet to peak after surging this year, but higher yields have made bonds more attractive in tumultuous markets as investors face a likely recession next year, according to Vanguard Group.'It's been tough being a fixed-income investor for the last few years,' with investors struggling a couple years ago to find yield as many parts of the bond market had negative rates, said Jeff Johnson, Vanguard's head of fixed-income product, by phone. 'Today, the good news is that yields are much more attractive, but the process of getting there has been pretty painful and it's involved a big decline in bond prices.
