helen.salisbury{at}phc.ox.ac.ukFollow Helen on Twitter: @HelenRSalisburyThere has been much disquiet in GP circles about the treatment of Manjula Arora, firstly by her employers and then by the General Medical Council and the Medical Practitioners Tribunal Service.1 How this dispute about whether or not Arora had been promised a new laptop reached the GMC is a mystery to most of us. What is clear is that, in any functioning organisation, the issue would have been swiftly resolved at local level.The tribunal's report is distressing to read.
