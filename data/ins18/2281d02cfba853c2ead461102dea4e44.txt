As Federal Reserve Chairman Jerome Powell ponders how much latitude he has to hike interest rates, realities in Tokyo suggest the answer is not much. In March, Powell's team hit the monetary brakes for the first time in three years. That 25 basis points move came at a moment when inflation is rising the fastest since the early 1980s. It fueled expectations are there's lots more tightening where that came from.
