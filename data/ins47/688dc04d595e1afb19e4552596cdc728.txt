Colleges Are Now a Breeding Ground for Mediocrity, Not Meritocracy
newsweek.com
news
2022-10-17 11:04:01

The decision of New York University not to renew the contract of one of the most distinguished professors of organic chemistry is a sign of the times. Academic standards in general have been consistently lowered over the past several decades. That should come as no surprise, because standards for admission to colleges and universities have been lowered as well. To the extent that meritocracy is replaced by other factors, such as identity politics, the standards will continue to diminish.It is not surprising that universities will have to lower academic standards for performance if they lower academic standards for admission.
