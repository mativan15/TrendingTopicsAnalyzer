Marion Davies, a Hollywood star whose career has been overshadowed by "the greatest film ever made" decades after her death, is back in the spotlight.The actress, who made her mark during the silent film era and the first decade of "talkies," is the subject of a new book titled "Captain of Her Soul" by film writer Lara Gabrielle. It chronicles Davies' rise to stardom as a sought-after comedienne, as well as her tragic final years. For the book, Gabrielle tracked down some of Davies' surviving confidantes who were eager to shed new light on their pal.
