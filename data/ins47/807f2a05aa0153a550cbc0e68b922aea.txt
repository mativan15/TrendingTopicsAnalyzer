Published: 15:59 EDT, 17 October 2022 | Updated: 15:59 EDT, 17 October 2022 INDIANAPOLIS (AP) - Indianapolis coach Frank Reich and general manager Chris Ballard spent the spring and summer insisting the Colts receiving group would be strong, deep and productive. Yes, they expected it to take time for a young, mostly unproven group of receivers to grow into new roles and jell with another new quarterback. In Week 6, the contingent finally demonstrated what it could do - together. Rookie receiver Alec Pierce and rookie tight end Jelani Woods each caught one touchdown pass.
