'It hurts': Restaurants tightening belts, reducing serving sizes as food costs skyrocket
cbc.ca
food
2022-06-01 21:03:00

Ahmed Abdulkadir used to stock his restaurant without giving his shopping list a second thought, but now a simple stop at the store is painful.The prices of staples from rice to cooking oil have skyrocketed, leaving the restaurateur standing in the grocery aisle asking himself, "OK, do I need this?""It hurts," he said Wednesday morning, the banging and clanging of prep work ringing out behind him at Safia Restaurant, the eatery his family runs on St. Laurent Boulevard. "Literally it hurts going to the store knowing that you're spending $1,500 a day.
