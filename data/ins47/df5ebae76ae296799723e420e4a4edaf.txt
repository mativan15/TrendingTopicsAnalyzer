[THIS is a gloomy picture, and not the bright one we should like to publish. It is printed as a rudely awakening account of conditions which point to schools and more schools as the single road to salvation. It deals with extreme conditions, but it is the honest recital of a man born to other surroundings, who has not inherited an understanding of the negro, yet has studied him at first hand, in the section where the problem is singularly perplexing, and, to a not unsympathetic stranger, often seems quite hopeless.
