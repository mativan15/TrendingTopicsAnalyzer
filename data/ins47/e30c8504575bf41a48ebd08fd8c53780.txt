John Fetterman, improving from stroke, can work 'full duty in public office,' doctor says
azcentral.com
news
2022-10-19 17:11:19

Pennsylvania Lt. Gov. John Fetterman is recovering from his stroke and his health is continuing to improve, according to a letter from his doctor that the Democratic Senate candidate's campaign released Wednesday.Fetterman suffered a stroke in May two days before the Keystone State held its Democratic primary election. He said the stroke was caused by a clot from his heart being in an A-fib rhythm for too long.University of Pittsburgh Medical Center's Dr. Clifford Chen wrote in the Oct. 15 letter that Fetterman's physical exam was normal.
