More than 150 Ontario communities have scrapped in-person ballot boxes. What will that mean for voter turnout?
cbc.ca
news
2022-10-20 19:42:00

Municipal election day is quickly approaching in Ontario, and this year many communities are turning to tech-only voting methods, with 151 municipal governments across the province not holding in-person ballot boxes in favour of either online voting, phone voting or a combination of both methods.The Association of Municipalities of Ontario says there will be 417 municipal elections held in the province on Oct. 24, and more than half — 217 — have decided to use some form of online or phone voting.
