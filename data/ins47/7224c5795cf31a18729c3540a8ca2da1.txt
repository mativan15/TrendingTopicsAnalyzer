Tourists could soon be banned from an internationally popular mountain hike in New South Wales in a controversial move that has been compared to the closure of Uluru in 2019. The NSW government says it is up to the local Indigenous community to decide if the mountain permanently closesTweed's mayor says the government is avoiding responsibility over the controversial topicShe is calling for a gradual closure like the approach used at UluruThe state government has announced the future of Wollumbin National Park will be guided by cultural beliefs through the development of a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) with local Aboriginal custodians.
