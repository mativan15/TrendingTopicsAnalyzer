A woman has told how all her childhood memories were wiped out after life-saving surgery to remove a brain tumour.Weronika Fafinska, 23, was diagnosed with a grade 2 diffuse astrocytoma brain tumour in February 2014 when she was just 14.In 2012, Weronika went deaf in her right ear and an MRI scan later revealed she had a brain tumour.She had most of the tumour removed during surgery in February 2014, but when she came round she did not even recognise her parents who were sitting at her bedside.Weronika, from Edinburgh, said: 'In the car going home, I was sick three times because, in my mind, I had never been in a car before.
