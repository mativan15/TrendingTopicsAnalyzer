Drug use and addiction are popular themes in movies and television, but they often get things very wrong. Here are eight common myths about drugs you'll see on the silver screen. 1. Rehab goes for 28 days In the movie 28 Days, Sandra Bullock is given a choice between prison and 28 days in a rehab centre. The 28-day program, popular in the United States, actually has nothing to do with the optimum treatment period. Health insurance companies in the US are only prepared to fund 28 days in rehab. So the 28-day rehab model was developed around funding, not effectiveness.
