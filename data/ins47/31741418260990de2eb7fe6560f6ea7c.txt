Fed policymakers: September rate hike a question of how big, not if
indiatimes.com
economics
2022-06-03 03:00:00

The Fed slashed rates to zero in March 2020 as the Covid-19 pandemic slammed the US economy causing millions of layoffs.The Federal Reserve is likely to continue tightening monetary policy beyond the half percentage point interest rate hikes expected at each of its next two meetings, two policymakers signaled on Thursday, with the only question being how much. "Market pricing for 50 basis points potentially in June and July, from the data we have in hand today, seems like a reasonable path," Fed Vice Chair Lael Brainard told CNBC.
