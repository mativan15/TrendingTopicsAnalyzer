REUTERS/Lucy NicholsonWASHINGTON, June 2 (Reuters) - Consumer advocates on Thursday urged U.S. regulators to investigate video game maker Electronic Arts Inc (EA.O) for the misleading use of a digital "loot box" that "aggressively" urges players to spend more money while playing a popular soccer game.The groups Fairplay, Center for Digital Democracy and 13 other organizations urged the Federal Trade Commission to probe the EA game "FIFA: Ultimate Team".
