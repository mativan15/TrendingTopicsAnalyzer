If you are puzzled, exasperated or suspicious about the ubiquity of the ultra-rare Japanese beef called Kobe that now seems to appear on every steakhouse and high-end restaurant menu in America, you're not alone. For more than a decade I've been watching the import of true Kobe beef by the U.S. meat industry and restaurateurs to find it had leapt from a minuscule amount to what seems like quite an easy thing to order every day of the year, if willing to pay a high price.
