THIS excellent book belongs to a species that is too seldom met with in American literature. The English produce almost innumerable examples, and many good ones. There is no place in the Empire so remote that it has n't its illuminating volume, soberly documented and bemapped, and with all in it that is worth knowing about the politics, sorceries, and table manners of the local brown brothers. Our own agents of the enlightenment seem to be far less literate than their British colleagues. Most of them, I dare say, can read and write after a fashion, but what they write is buried in government files.
