A new book takes a harsh look at the first 'celebrity CEO,' GE's Jack Welch — but the author's argument lacks nuance

2022-05-30 10:30:00

Jack Welch, "the first celebrity CEO"who led General Electric from 1981 to 2001, has long had a full-throated takedown coming. During his tenure, the company became the most valuable in the world. At his well-attended 2020 funeral, JP Morgan's Jamie Dimon spoke for the crowd when he described Welch as a "legendary leader" who had "really set the standard as a CEO."  The strength of this conventional wisdom was due in no small part to Welch's aggressive marketing of his brand in his post-retirement years, through books, business ventures, and relentless television appearances.
