A woman argued with an elderly passenger on a train after she asked her if she could have her seat - as she refused and told her she should've booked her ownThe woman had booked a first-class seat on the train (stock photo) Getting a seat on a train can sometimes feel like winning the jackpot, especially if you're travelling at peak times or your journey is going to be particularly lengthy. But, etiquette rules still apply when it comes to older travellers, those with disabilities, or those who are pregnant, as a healthy person is often expected to give up their seat in these circumstances - especially if they're sat in one of the allocated "priority" seats that many trains provide.
