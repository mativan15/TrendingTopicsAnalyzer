Burnout and poor working conditions are pushing this paramedic to throw in the towel

Cody Lucente of Happy Valley-Goose Bay says exhausted paramedics have been known to do jumping jacks on the roadway to stay away during overtime shifts. (Rafsan Faruque Jugol/CBC)After more than seven years of service, a central Labrador paramedic is leaving his job because of what he describes as an unbearable workload that has had a deep impact on his profession. "I cannot handle it anymore. I've become very burnt out. I've reached my breaking point," said Cody Lucente, who is based in Happy Valley-Goose Bay.
