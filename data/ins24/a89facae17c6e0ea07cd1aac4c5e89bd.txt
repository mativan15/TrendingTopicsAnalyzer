The economic recovery process that was underway was weighed down by the localised restrictions due to Omicron variant and higher commodity prices impacting margins of India Inc. The economy slowed down in the January-March period to 4.1 per cent, lowest in a year, as a result but maintained the sequential momentum. Strong government spending came to the rescue once again and along with an investment push kept the month-on-month growth largely intact. Companies, especially the FMCG ones, were forced to pass the input price pressures on to the consumers.
