Kelly Sullivan Meta Platforms (NASDAQ:META) has lost more than 60% of its value this year, making it one of the hardest hit stocks as it grapples with both souring market sentiment and internal operating challenges. Despite its robust chequebook, rising concerns over the company's decelerating growth, user decline, and capital intensive metaverse vision have been viewed as major drawbacks. However, the long-term gains that we had alluded to in a previous article may be finally starting to show. Meta's yearslong investment in AI/ML developments are starting to payoff, as advertisers start to show interest in its new products.
