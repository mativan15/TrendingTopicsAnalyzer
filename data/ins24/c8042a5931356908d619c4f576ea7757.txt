(Patrick Butler/Radio-Canada)On the back of higher than expected revenue from oil — and taxes — Newfoundland and Labrador is projecting a 2022 budget surplus of nearly half a billion dollars.The provincial government's fall fiscal update Wednesday shows a surplus of $479 million, rather than the $351-million deficit projected in the 2022 budget earlier this year — a reversal of $830 million.Finance Minister Siobhan Coady said the revenue increase is fortunate but doesn't necessarily mean smooth sailing ahead.
