Quoting the central government order, he said seatbelts for drivers as well as passengers have been made compulsory for M1 category vehicles.BENGALURU: All occupants of car and sports utility vehicles in Karnataka must wear seatbelts, failing which they will have to pay a fine of Rs 1,000 per person. Police said the order issued by the state government on Tuesday will come into effect immediately.An order from the central government dated September 19 this year has made fastening of seatbelts mandatory for all the occupants of cars.
