Alan Rickman's voice was a purr, but it masked sharp claws. This was evident in the memorable baddies he played and now you can hear it in the feline prose of these selected diaries. 'John Major said, ‘You have given us so much enjoyment.' ‘I wish I could say the same of you,' was the unstoppable reply. He had the grace to laugh.' You suspect not everyone did. Rickman didn't suffer fools and his intelligence keeps chafing against his fellow professionals – their self-absorption, their vanity, their unresponsiveness.
