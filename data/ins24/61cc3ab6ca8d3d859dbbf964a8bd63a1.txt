BENGALURU, Oct 19 (Reuters) - Indian chemicals-to-sugar maker DCM Shriram Ltd (DCMS.NS) reported a 19.2% fall in second-quarter profit on Wednesday, hurt by challenges in its vinyls business and higher excise duty, power and fuel costs.The New Delhi-based company's consolidated profit fell to 1.28 billion rupees ($15.4 million) in the three months ended Sept. 30, from 1.59 billion rupees a year ago.Its total expenses jumped 36% in the quarter.Register now for FREE unlimited access to Reuters.comSurging inflation and cost pressures are threatening DCM Shriram's margins after a rebound from several pandemic-hit quarters.
