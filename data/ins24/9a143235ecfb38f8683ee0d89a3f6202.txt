While inflation will hit consumers, high commodity prices and a strong jobs market mean Chalmers will reveal an extra $100 billion in income tax revenue.LoadingIn the March budget, Treasury forecast the government would collect a record $2.2 trillion in taxes over the forward estimates out to 2025-26. Of that, $1.6 trillion would come from income taxes, which include personal, company and superannuation taxes.Most of the extra cash will flow into the current financial year and 2023-24.Chalmers cautioned that the extra revenue was largely 'temporary', due to high commodity prices, adding that the government would not spend all the windfall.
