Tom Mauser has seen this all before.After the Columbine massacre of 1999, there was a sense in the air, like there is now, that change needed to happen. Lawmakers convened at the federal level, led by New Jersey's Democratic Sen Frank Lautenberg, determined to close the so-called 'gun show loophole': a provision in federal law that allows for gun sales to occur between private sellers and buyers, including at weapons shows, without a background check occurring.But like so many firearm restrictions to come up in Congress over the past two decades, the effort failed.
