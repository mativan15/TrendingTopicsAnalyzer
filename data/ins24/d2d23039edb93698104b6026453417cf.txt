3:45 PM ETThe recent Women's European Championship had millions of Germans and many millions more across Europe glued to their television screens. In Britain, the final between England and Germany, which was played in front of a record crowd of 87,192 inside Wembley Stadium, drew an average of 11 million television viewers and a 66% audience share. The tournament felt like a breakthrough moment for women's football in Europe, but will it lead to sustainable change and possibly true equality in football?Germany already experienced a wave of excitement in 2011 when the country hosted the Women's World Cup, but the excitement died down as soon as the final whistle was blown.
