By Reuters Published: 21:49 EDT, 17 October 2022 | Updated: 21:49 EDT, 17 October 2022 TOKYO, Oct 18 (Reuters) - Bank of Japan Governor Haruhiko Kuroda said on Tuesday he had no intention of stepping down, when asked by an opposition lawmaker on whether he would take responsibility for failing to achieve his 2% inflation target and hurting the economy with a weak yen. Speaking in the same parliament session, Prime Minister Fumio Kishida defended the BOJ's policy, saying the government will play its part by ensuring wages rise enough to compensate households for the rising cost of living.
