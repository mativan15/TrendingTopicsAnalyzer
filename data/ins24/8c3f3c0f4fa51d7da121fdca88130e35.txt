'it's time to do it myself,' says Marlene Clow, who is growing her own food for the very first time, at Stratford's community garden. (Sara Fraser/CBC)At 53, Marlene Clow has become a first-time gardener at her nearby community garden in Stratford, P.E.I., because the price of food in stores has skyrocketed. Community gardens across P.E.I. have seen demand increase steadily the past few years, first during the pandemic as people enjoyed renewed interest in getting their hands dirty, and this year in large part because of the high price of food.
