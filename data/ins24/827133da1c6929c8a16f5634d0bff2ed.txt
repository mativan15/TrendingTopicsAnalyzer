We saw it most of the season — the splits to haul in a wide throw, the great picks of balls in the dirt, the back-to-the-plate running catches of pop-ups in shallow right — Vladimir Guerrero Jr. looked great on defence a whole lot of the time in 2022 and he's been rewarded by being one of five Blue Jays named as a finalist for the Rawlings Gold Glove Award along with third baseman Matt Chapman, right-fielder Jackie Bradley Jr.. pitcher José Berrios and utility player Whit Merrifield.

It's a big honour for Guerrero, and one no one could have seen coming two years when, after being shifted across the diamond from third base to first, the then-21-year-old struggled badly with his new position.
