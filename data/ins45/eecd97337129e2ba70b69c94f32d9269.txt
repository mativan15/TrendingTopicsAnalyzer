There's a toxic three-word phrase that people with low emotional intelligence use a little too often. To explain why it's such a bad choice, it helps to examine a separate, one-word term that most people have never even heard of.Let's tackle the lesser-known term first. It's a word about words, actually: "litotes." What are litotes? They're an ironic figure of speech the posits the denial of an opposite, instead of a simply making a straightforward statement.This will make much more sense by simply providing examples: "He isn't terrible-looking," instead of, "He's good-looking.
