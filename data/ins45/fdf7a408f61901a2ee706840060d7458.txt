REUTERS/Dado Ruvic/IllustrationAMSTERDAM, Oct 18 (Reuters) - Supply chain snags, spending cuts by customers and U.S. trade curbs on China are likely to be in focus at ASML Holding NV's (ASML.AS) quarterly results on Wednesday, which should benefit from strong past orders of its equipment to make computer chips.Europe's largest technology company is working through a 33 billion euro ($32.5 billion) order backlog that stretches into 2024.
