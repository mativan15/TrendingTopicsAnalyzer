3 ballot issues bring Colorado's liquor wars to voters in November

2022-10-17 14:21:00

This fall, Colorado voters will have a chance to rewrite state beer, wine and liquor regulations in the most significant way since the state ended the prohibition on liquor stores operating on Sundays in 2008.

For 14 years, efforts to expand alcohol sales have produced the noisiest fights at the state Capitol. Each session, different segments of the liquor industry clash — and, most often, proponents have been defeated.

The three initiatives appearing on ballots in November not only are straining emotions between liquor store owners and grocery chains — and pitting one giant national liquor store chain against most of the rest of the state — but are drawing contributions in totals that dwarf what most statewide candidates are raising.
