Pay Dirt: College Athletes Cashing In
iheart.com
news
2022-05-31 15:00:00

College sports has come a long way from Knute Rockne and "Win One for the Gipper." These days, college athletics is big business for the schools and the players. And now, players are getting their piece of the pie after decades of toiling under rules of amateurism. Most states, including Texas, now allow players to make money off of their name, image and likeness, or NIL. This has resulted in what some critics dub the Wild West of college sports, with players signing million-dollar endorsement deals, while schools and coaches compete to lure players through these coveted deals.
