By Kevin PeacheyPersonal finance correspondent, BBC NewsImage source, Leeds Museums and GalleriesImage caption, Mystery still surrounds the Cridling Stubbs hoard, held by Rachael Dilley, Leeds City Museum's curator of exhibitionsIn 1967, in a field near the village of Cridling Stubbs in North Yorkshire, a pot containing more than 3,300 Roman coins was unearthed.The hoard of copper coins from the 300s AD were still in the jar, buried upright, with a makeshift lid on top."We do not know for certain why they were buried there, and why nobody ever came back to find them," says Kat Baxter, Leeds Museums and Galleries' curator of archaeology and numismatics.
