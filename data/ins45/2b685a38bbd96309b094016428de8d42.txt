Tech companies added fertility features just in time for the culture war
theverge.com
news
2022-10-20 18:24:16

Tech companies picked an interesting time to start including women's and reproductive health in their products. For years, they sidestepped the issue: Apple didn't include period tracking in its health app until 2015. Fitbit only added 'Female health tracking' in 2018. For products that claim to help people understand their bodies, most spent years sidestepping the basic biology that affects half the population.But over the past year or so, companies have started juicing up their menstrual tracking and fertility features, and there's been more investment in so-called femtech.
