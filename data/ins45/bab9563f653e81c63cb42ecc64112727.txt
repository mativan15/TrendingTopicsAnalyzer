The cost of integrity: reports of bad council behaviour are costing taxpayers and could change their vote
cbc.ca
news
2022-10-20 18:34:00

Many candidates running in this municipal election across northeastern Ontario are carrying baggage from the integrity commissioner investigations that have dominated some council agendas over the last four years. No community has felt the impact more than Elliot Lake, which has had more integrity commissioner reports than the largest cities in the province.The two leading mayoral candidates — incumbent Dan Marchisella and city councillor Chris Patrie — have both been called out violating the municipal code of conduct.
