The Globe's front-page story about the continued lack of options for housing children during a crisis seemed to put the blame on the Department of Children and Families ('DCF taking desperate measures, workers say,' Oct. 20). While the paper clearly deserves credit for continuing to monitor this issue, the article lacked potential remedies. As someone with more than 40 years' experience in child welfare, including working in residential treatment centers in Massachusetts and later in my career for a national child advocacy organization, I would like to share a few thoughts.
