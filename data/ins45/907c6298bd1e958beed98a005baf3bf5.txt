France is Banning Video Game Words Because They Aren't French Enough
gizmodo.com
tech
2022-05-31 16:00:00

French officials are saying 'adieu' to popular English video game vocabulary in an effort to preserve their rarified language. On Monday the country's Ministry of Culture reportedly announced it would effectively ban popular gaming jargon like 'pro-gamer,' 'streamer' and 'esports,' and replace them with explicitly French alternatives, according to an AFP report spotted by The Guardian. Those variations range from relatively straightforward translations—'joueur professionnel' for 'pro gamer,' for example'—to more comically convoluted creations like 'joueur-animateur en direct,' which translates roughly to live player host for 'streamer.
