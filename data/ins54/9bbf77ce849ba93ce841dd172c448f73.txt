Britons should wash their hair 'at night' to save hundreds on bills

Energy bills: Exact hours of the day you shouldn't be drying your hair to save hundreds (Image: Getty)Although the hairdryer is not the appliance which consumes the most energy in the house, people use it on a daily basis. According to USwitch, using a hairdryer for eight minutes uses up to 1.8 kilowatts and the cost of electricity currently stands at 34p per kWh, according to the energy regulator Ofgem.This means that in the UK, the average cost of using a hairdryer for eight minutes is 6p a day, but this can add up if it is used for longer and on a daily basis.
