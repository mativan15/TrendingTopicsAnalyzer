Published: 11:20 EDT, 19 October 2022 | Updated: 13:19 EDT, 19 October 2022 The Home Office is effectively running 'unregistered children's homes', a watchdog warned after figures revealed thousands of lone migrant children have been living in hotels after arriving in the UK. Chief inspector of borders and immigration David Neal gave the Government department until the end of the year to come up with a plan to stop using hotels to house migrants after he found 'limited evidence of progress on a concrete exit strategy' from the policy.
