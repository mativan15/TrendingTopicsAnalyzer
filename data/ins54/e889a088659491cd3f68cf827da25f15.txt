Los Angeles To Fully Electrify, Add Solar Power To Nine City Building

news
2022-05-27 23:00:00

Photo: Getty ImagesLOS ANGELES (CNS) - The Los Angeles City Council allocated about $28 million Friday to fully electrify nine city facilities and equip them with solar generation and battery storage capability as part of an effort to decarbonize public buildings in Los Angeles. The buildings are the Las Palmas Senior Citizen Center, the Silver Lake Branch Library, the Balboa Sports Complex, the Benjamin Franklin Branch Library, the Cypress Park Branch Library, the Old Fire Station No. 39, the Green Meadows Recreation Center, the Valley Plaza Recreation Center and the Los Angeles Zoo solar photovoltaic system.
