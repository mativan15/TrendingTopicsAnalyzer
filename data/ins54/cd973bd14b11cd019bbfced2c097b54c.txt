By Till Dengel, SAP When it comes to efficiency, today's global supply chain has never been better. In the last 20 years, the size of the container ship has doubled – and the largest ships sailing today can haul 24,000 containers (TEU)—equal to the capacity of a freight train 44 miles long. But then again, ships of this size are getting stuck in the Suez Canal. The result is a supply chain disruption. With disruptions increasingly common (climate change, wars, pandemics), companies now realize that what sets them apart from the competition is not only efficiency, but also the ability to deliver.
