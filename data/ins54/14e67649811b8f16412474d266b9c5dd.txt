This essay was adapted from Meg Conley's newsletter, homeculture. Subscribe here. I asked for a purse for my fourth birthday. I wanted one that I could drape from my wrist when I walked with my mom as she pushed my sister's stroller. I thought purses were for holding treasures. The treasures I had. A rock that almost looked like a crystal. A little Lucite charm I found in my grandma's jewelry box. An old coin my grandpa gave me on my last visit to New Mexico. A Rainbow Brite pencil. The treasures I'd find on walks and errands.
