Renmark levee bank upgrades need to be fast-tracked, say Murray River irrigators
abc.net.au
news
2022-10-20 01:29:43

Irrigation groups say the South Australian government must complete works on vulnerable levee banks in Renmark within weeks to prepare for incoming flows.Two of Renmark's levee banks are designed to withstand daily flows of 130GL Engineers are assessing the banks todayPrevious costings have put the upgrades into the millions of dollarsUp to 120 gigalitres of water a day is forecast to come down the Murray River by early December — the highest level of flows since the 1970s.As engineers and flood planners today visit the sites of concern, irrigators have called on Renmark Paringa Council and private landholders to cooperate with the state government to get the upgrades swiftly completed.
