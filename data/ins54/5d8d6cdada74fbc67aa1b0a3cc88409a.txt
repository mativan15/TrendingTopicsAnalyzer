Pep Guardiola said coins were thrown at him during Manchester City's match at Anfield - with Liverpool pledging to ban for life anyone guilty of hurling the missiles.The Manchester City manager revealed he was targeted after his team had a goal disallowed during the second half, with Liverpool going on to win the game 1-0.
Guardiola was not hit by any of the objects but the Merseyside club promptly opened an investigation into the matter.It came as Liverpool condemned "vile chants relating to football stadium tragedies" from the away supporters' end during Sunday's Premier League match.
