Published: 07:31 EDT, 16 October 2022 | Updated: 07:37 EDT, 16 October 2022 The UK economy is now alongside Italy and Greece in terms of risky bets for lenders and politicians are not being honest about the problems facing the nation, a former Bank of England chief warned today.The central bank's former deputy governor Sir Charles Bean said it is 'disingenuous' of the Government to say the market turmoil is 'all a global phenomenon'.He hit out today after Jeremy Hunt replaced Kwasi Kwarteng as chancellor in a bid to calm panicky markets.
