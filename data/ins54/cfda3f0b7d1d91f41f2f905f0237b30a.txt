Team TOI PlusTIMESOFINDIA.COMUpdated: Oct 18, 2022, 21:03 ISTOn February 7, 1856, Lord Dalhousie, then governor-general of India, ordered the annexation of the kingdom of Awadh. The reason? King Wajid Ali Shah was accused of misgovernance. Shah was deposed and the rich kingdom was taken over by the British. If the East India Company was still around, there's every chance that the Britain of today would be annexed on the same ground of misgovernance. UK Prime Minister Liz Truss took office in circumstances that were less than ideal.
