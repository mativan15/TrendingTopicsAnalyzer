Published: 13:44 EDT, 21 October 2022 | Updated: 13:44 EDT, 21 October 2022 By Rajesh Kumar Singh CHICAGO, Oct 21 (Reuters) - U.S. carriers including Delta Air Lines and United Airlines are betting big on American consumers' unquenched thirst for travel across the Atlantic by adding more flights to Europe. U.S. airlines are responding to consumers emboldened by a more powerful U.S. dollar and more flexible work arrangements that allow them to work from wherever they want, extending the travel season into the fall, industry officials said.
