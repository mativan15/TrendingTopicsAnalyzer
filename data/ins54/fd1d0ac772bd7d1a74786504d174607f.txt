By Reuters Published: 14:09 EDT, 20 October 2022 | Updated: 14:09 EDT, 20 October 2022 Oct 20 (Reuters) - Wolverhampton Wanderers caretaker manager Steve Davis will remain in charge of the team until the end of the year after taking over from Bruno Lage, the Premier League club said on Thursday. Davis has guided Wolves to one victory in three league games and they are 18th in the standings with nine points from 11 matches. "The move provides continuity and stability within the first-team group until a permanent head coach is appointed," Wolves said in a statement.
