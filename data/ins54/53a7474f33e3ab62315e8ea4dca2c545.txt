The Toronto municipal election is anything but dull. How could it be when it's a battle for an opposition to the status quo?

While Mayor John Tory and other sitting councillors seem to be coasting to victory with little effort, relying on name recognition and incumbency advantage, they're really riding a tide of civic decline that few are willing to address.

After eight years of Tory's austerity politics, and four years of raucous but similar Rob Ford politics before him, Toronto desperately needs an effective opposition to the endless Toryism of ribbon-cutting announcements that are later undercut by either not fully funding projects or diminishing city divisions by holding budgets as inflation soars.
