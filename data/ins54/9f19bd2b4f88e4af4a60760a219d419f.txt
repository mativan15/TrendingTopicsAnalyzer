Trade, supply chains get fillip in new push

By ZHONG NAN | CHINA DAILY | Updated: 2022-05-27 09:05 Containers await shipping at a port in Xiamen, Fujian province. [Photo by Wang Xieyun/China Daily] China will roll out fresh measures to remove barriers in its foreign trade and bolster the stability of its supply chains, the Ministry of Commerce said on Thursday. One of the measures will seek to alleviate the pressure of international logistics. The government will facilitate smooth domestic transportation of foreign trade goods, and help COVID-affected foreign trade companies resume production as soon as possible, said Gao Feng, spokesman of the ministry, in an online news briefing.
