Even as recession worries intensify in Canada, the central bank is likely to go ahead with another supersized interest rate hike next week after data showed underlying inflation was stubbornly persistent despite aggressive tightening, analysts said. Money markets are betting on another 75-basis point move at the Bank of Canada's Oct. 26 meeting, taking its policy rate to a 14-year high at 4.0%. That eye-watering pace of tightening - and the promise of more to come - has economists, consumers and businesses increasingly certain Canada's economy will slip into a recession sometime in the next 12 months.
