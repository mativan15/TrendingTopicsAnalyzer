It can be head-spinning to keep up with the sudden trends taking hold in the workplace: Workers are "quiet quitting." They're also being "quiet fired." Bosses were scared of workers spontaneously quitting their jobs to join the Great Resignation; now companies are panicking about "cyberloafing" workers. Managers and bosses are scrambling to try out various advice, tactics, dubious solutions, and mind tricks to combat these nefarious developments. If all of this seems unnecessarily confusing, that's because it is.
