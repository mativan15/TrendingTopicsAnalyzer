Prime Minister Elisabeth Borne discusses with lawmakers after triggering Article 49.3 of the Constitution, in Paris, on October 19, 2022. JULIEN MUGUET FOR "LE MONDE" Even though it does not happen that often, there is always well-rehearsed drama surrounding the use of Article 49.3 of the French Constitution, which allows for the government to force a bill through Parliament without a vote. The prime minister steps up to the podium and makes a succinct statement under jeers from the opposition and applause from the standing majority.
