After Friday's French Open semifinal, Spanish newspaper Marca reported that Rafael Nadal would miss Wimbledon later this month as he continues to recover from rib and foot injuries. Nadal himself acknowledged the pain he was feeling, telling reporters: 'I would be happy with my life with a new foot. Win is beautiful, but life is much more important than any title.' It might have been a surprise, then, that it was his opponent—third-seeded Alexander Zverev—who retired from the match with an injury, and that Nadal emerged with a victory.
