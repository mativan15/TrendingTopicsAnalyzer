Sir, Timothy Young KC (letter, Oct 18) makes a plea for greater expert advice within government and the civil service. There may be good reason why this is lacking. When I was a scientist in the civil service there were several occasions where my knowledge was deemed unpalatable.In the early 1980s I was asked to take up a post which meant supporting the case for the retention of tetraethyl lead in petrol. Pointing out that there was clear evidence that this was extremely harmful especially to the young, I was firmly told that it was policy to support the (British) firm making this noxious additive.
