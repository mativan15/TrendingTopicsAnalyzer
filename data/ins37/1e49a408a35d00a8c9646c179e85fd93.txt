All that nuclear talk: Is the unthinkable suddenly possible?
csmonitor.com
world
2022-10-17 18:24:35

Since the bombing of Nagasaki in 1945, the world has observed a taboo on the use of nuclear weapons. It became unthinkable.But now, Russian President Vladimir Putin has begun raising the prospect that he might use nuclear arms – albeit smaller-scale, tactical battlefield weapons – to defend Russian-held territory in Ukraine. And North Korean leader Kim Jong Un has shown signs of shifting his nuclear doctrine away from 'no first use.' Since the bombing of Nagasaki, the world has resolutely avoided the use of nuclear weapons.
