The Premier League returns this midweek with a slew of fixtures, as the season continues at a hectic pace ahead of November's World Cup in Qatar.The top two teams in the table, Arsenal and Manchester City, are not in domestic action due to the Gunners having to play their rearranged fixtures with PSV in the Europa League on Thursday.It means those below them can catch up, and there will be extra focus on Tottenham as they travel to Manchester United knowing that a victory at Old Trafford would send them top.
