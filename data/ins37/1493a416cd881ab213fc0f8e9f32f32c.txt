A little over 30 years ago — the period in which the Booker Prize-winning Seven Moons of Maali Almeida is set — four separate militant groups were driving their boots into Sri Lanka's belly. There was the LTTE, the Tamil separatist force which, according to the book's protagonist, is 'prepared to slaughter Tamil civilians and moderates' to free their people from the state. Also, in the north was the Indian Peace Keeping Force, known colloquially as the Indian People Killing Force, who'd 'burn villages to fulfil their mission'.
