The Australian Labor Party is Australia's oldest political party, and Anthony Albanese is only its seventh prime minister. He would do well to recall the experiences of his predecessors. Incoming Labor prime ministers have invariably faced immediate and serious economic challenges, some of them bequeathed by conservative governments that styled themselves as superior economic managers. In October 1929, Labor leader James Scullin defeated the conservative Stanley Melbourne Bruce, 12 days before Wall Street began the great crash that set off the Great Depression.
