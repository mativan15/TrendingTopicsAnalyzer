This is why elavating Africa's towns into cities is not a good idea

world

Everywhere one looks in Africa, they'll notice a new planned city cropping up that promises to be a utopia not plagued by the challenges the continent's cities currently face.Kenya's Konza Tecnocity which promised to be the biggest smart city south of the Sahara is still struggling to prove to investors why they should inject capital in it nine years after it was conceived.In 2010, Nigeria's Eko Atlantic City promised to house 250,000 citizens on land reclaimed from the sea but it is still empty.
