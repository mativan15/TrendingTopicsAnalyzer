How Local Hero came to rescue of Scotland's film and TV sector
thetimes.co.uk
news
2022-10-15 23:01:00

It's one of Scotland's most beloved and enduring films, a winning fiction of big business and simple lives, featuring a heart-swelling soundtrack and the most famous telephone box in the country.Michael Douglas wanted to be in it; Robin Williams came close; as did Henry Winkler, aka the Fonz. Yet it was the magical light of the Scottish Highlands, not the blaze of Hollywood's spotlights, which became the true star of Bill Forsyth's most whimsical yarn.Now a new book exploring the legacy of the 1983 celluloid classic Local Hero has identified the director's 'metaphorical fingerprints' on the contemporary Scottish film industry.
