The company behind Parler, an alternative social media platform that primarily caters to the far right, announced Monday that musical artist Kanye 'Ye' West had agreed to purchase the site, days after Twitter and Instagram locked the rapper's accounts over a string of antisemitic remarks.West's planned acquisition of the platform exemplifies a deeper problem with the way the flow of information exists online—it is subject to the whims and persuasions of a select group of ultra-wealthy people, with few meaningful mechanisms of accountability available to keep them in check.
