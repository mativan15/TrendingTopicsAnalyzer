By Reuters Published: 22:55 EDT, 16 October 2022 | Updated: 22:55 EDT, 16 October 2022 SHANGHAI, Oct 17 (Reuters) - China and Hong Kong stocks sagged on Monday, after Chinese President Xi Jinping talked up national security in a speech on Sunday, while dashing hopes of any changes in growth-hitting zero-COVID policies and property sector curbs. But China-listed shares' premium over their Hong Kong peers hovered near seven-year highs, as regulators announced several market-friendly policies over the past days in an apparent effort to stabilise the domestic market.
