AgenciesThe test results show that some chemicals have gone beyond the prescribed limit. A storm is brewing in the teacup. And it could impact consumption of tea in the country as well as in the export markets. Packet tea players have rejected a good volume of first flush teas they had bought from the auctions from April to middle of May for the domestic market on account of the chemical content not conforming to FSSAI standards. Federation of All India Tea Traders Association (FAITTA), the apex trade body of the packet tea players which includes Tata Global Beverages, and others, tested teas at an independent firm, Eurofins Analytical Services India.
