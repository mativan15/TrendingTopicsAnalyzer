Deep smartphone penetration and cheap internet rescued payments from card-and-cash dominance, seized access to investment from the gatekeepers of D-street, and released credit from the ivory towers of institutional banks. For the first time, access to these services is right in the hands of the people.
Users from tier-2 and tier-3 cities and younger demographics are leading their adoption from the front. There's no doubt about the fact that digital literacy in India has skyrocketed. But has financial literacy followed suit?
Evidently not.
