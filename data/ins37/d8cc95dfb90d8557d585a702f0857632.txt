Bowel cancer DNA test to spare thousands pointless chemotherapy

THOUSANDS of bowel cancer patients could be spared chemotherapy every year with a new DNA blood test.
Around 14,000 Brits a year go through chemo for bowel disease, the cancer suffered by Sun writer Dame Deborah James.
Around 43,000 Brits are diagnosed with bowel cancer every year in the UKCredit: Getty - Contributor
Dame Deborah James has documented her terminal bowel cancerCredit: bowelbabe/Instagram
Many face grim side effects including vomiting and diarrhoea, mouth ulcers and tiredness.
A trial found high-tech screening can halve the number of people who need the gruelling treatment after surgery - without affecting survival.
