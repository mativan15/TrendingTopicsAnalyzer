Photo / Eric Thayer, The New York TimesMark Zuckerberg, Meta's chief executive, has restructured his company so that he no longer has a top deputy. For more than a decade, Mark Zuckerberg and Sheryl Sandberg began and ended each week by meeting together. The symbolism of the ritual was clear. It was intended to signal that Zuckerberg, the CEO of Meta, and Sandberg, the chief operating officer, were in lockstep with each other at the top of the company.
