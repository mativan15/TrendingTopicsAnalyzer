Aussie baby formula company Bubs has nearly doubled in value after it agreed over the weekend to help the US plug a gaping shortfall.Shares in ASX-listed Bubs Australia jumped by as much as 80 per cent when the local market opened on Monday, following news it struck a deal to supply 1.25 million tins of formula to the US government.US President Joe Biden himself gave the company a shout out on Twitter when announcing the deal on Saturday.The haul en route to the US represents 27.5 million bottles of infant formula and comes as parents in the world's biggest economy struggle to feed their children.
