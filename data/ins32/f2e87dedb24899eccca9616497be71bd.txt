By Reuters Published: 16:54 EDT, 18 October 2022 | Updated: 16:54 EDT, 18 October 2022 RABAT, Oct 18 (Reuters) - The Moroccan government has prepared a draft budget envisaging economic growth at 4%, a fiscal deficit at 4.5% of GDP and inflation at 2%, Finance Minister Nadia Fettah Alaoui said on Tuesday. The economy is expected to slow to 0.8% growth this year following the worst drought in decades while inflation- mostly due to external factors- is expected to surge to 6.3% this year, according to most recent central bank data.
