Prosecutors launch crackdown on failed cryptocurrencies, investigating signs of fraud, Ponzi scheme



Changes in Bitcoin price are shown on an electronic board at Bithumb`s customer service center in Seoul on May 24.(Yonhap)South Korean authorities and major cryptocurrency exchanges are cranking up the heat on controversial 'stablecoin' terraUSD and its sister luna over its price fall fiasco, with the prosecution launching a crackdown on the case, reports said Sunday.Major crypto exchanges here are planning to form a joint body that would help them counter crises like the collapse of the terraUSD and luna, which caused some 280,000 Korean investors to see almost $38 billion wiped out from their balance sheets in the first week of the price fall, according to reports.
