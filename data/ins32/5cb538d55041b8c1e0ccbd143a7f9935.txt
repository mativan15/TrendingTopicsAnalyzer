Liz Truss announced today (October 20) that she is stepping down as Prime Minister and leader of the Conservative Party.
Ms Truss spent just 44 days in Number 10, though made waves in such a short time with a number of debacles leading to members of her own party calling for her to resign.
Former PM Boris Johnson and Rishi Sunak are rumoured to put their hats in the ring for the upcoming leadership race, but eyes are also focused on Thérèse Coffey.
She became the first woman to act as Deputy Prime Minister alongside role on alongside becoming Secretary of Health, a position she has said she is ‘honoured' to take.
