primeimages/iStock via Getty Images People are changing their attitudes about what they do with their money and investments. Failing to define their changed concerns about the future, they are not happy. They appear to fear more fundamental changes than just a relatively simple, shallow and quick, cyclical recession. Without fully defining the cause of their fears, they are moving their cash and investments around slowly. Third-quarter statements for leading commercial banks are showing increased deposits and the purchase of fixed-income securities and loans.
