Published: 01:14 EDT, 20 October 2022 | Updated: 01:14 EDT, 20 October 2022 SHANGHAI, Oct 20 (Reuters) - China shares fell on Thursday morning, while Hong Kong stocks hit levels last seen during the 2008-09 global financial crisis, as overnight Wall Street losses and a resumed slide in the yuan dented fragile investor confidence. ** Hong Kong benchmark Hang Seng slumped 2.4% by the lunch break, to 16,110.98 points, the lowest level since May, 2009. The Hang Seng Tech Index dropped 3.4%, after Nasdaq-listed China stocks tumbled 7.
