By Reuters Published: 17:09 EDT, 21 October 2022 | Updated: 17:09 EDT, 21 October 2022 OUAGADOUGOU, Oct 21 (Reuters) - Burkina Faso's new military leader Ibrahim Traore on Friday named jurist and author Appolinaire Joachim Kyelem de Tambela as interim prime minister, Traore said in a statement. Army captain Traore seized power in a coup on Sept. 30 and was officially designated transitional president of the West African country on Oct. 14. He was sworn in on Friday, after which he named Kyelem de Tambela as the prime minister of the interim government that will oversee the transition to elections scheduled for July 2024.
