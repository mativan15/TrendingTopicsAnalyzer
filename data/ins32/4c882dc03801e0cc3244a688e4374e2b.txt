The government has scrapped most of the key elements of its mini-budget after just over two weeks - but why did it have to respond to the turmoil in the financial markets and why does this matter to mortgage payers and everyone else?The government's job is to decide how much to spend on the things we need like the NHS, defence and education but also how to pay for them - which it does using taxes and by borrowing money.The government had announced help for people with their energy bills but the mini-budget on 23 September didn't explain how it would be paid for.
