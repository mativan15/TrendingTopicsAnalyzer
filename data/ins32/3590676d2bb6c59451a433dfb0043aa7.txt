Published: 16:24 EDT, 18 October 2022 | Updated: 16:24 EDT, 18 October 2022 By Stephen Culp NEW YORK, Oct 18 (Reuters) - Wall Street stocks closed higher and Treasury yields dipped on Tuesday as upbeat earnings and better-than-expected factory data stoked a risk-on rally. Building on Monday's broad gains, the S&P 500 led the major U.S. stock indexes higher to end the session up nearly 1% or more, with sectors across the board advancing. Meanwhile benchmark Treasury yields were last lower, having oscillated throughout the day.
