Investors had low expectations for Electronic Arts (EA -0.97%) during the early months of 2022. Its share price declined by as much as 15% this year over fears of a slowdown in consumer spending, poor reports from competitors like Activision Blizzard (ATVI 0.71%), and worries about how the company's comparative results would look as it lapped periods when the pandemic was boosting the amount of time people spent playing video games.
EA assuaged all of these worries with the strong fiscal Q4 2022 earnings report it released on May 10.
