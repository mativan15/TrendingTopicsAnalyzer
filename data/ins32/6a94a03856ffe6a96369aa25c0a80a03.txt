Even though it's the road team that aims to earn a split in the first two games in a best-of-7, it's the San Diego Padres who should be feeling pretty good about their chances in the NLCS against the Philadelphia Phillies. That's because, while the games were in San Diego, the Phillies had their two in-form aces, Zack Wheeler and Aaron Nola, on the mound. Getting a split against the 1-2 punch of Wheeler and Nola puts the series back on script for San Diego, even if home-field advantage now belongs to the Phillies.
