Key Takeaways Every month, the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics releases the Consumer Price Index (CPI) data which is then used to calculate inflation numbers. As inflation numbers go up, consumer purchasing power goes down, and consumers are forced to spend more money for the same items. Inflation data is used to determine economic policies which then impact everyone as rate hikes make the cost of borrowing money more expensive. 
 The most recent inflation data came out on October 13 and made it clear that while inflation has eased a bit, it's still soaring with no clear end in sight.
