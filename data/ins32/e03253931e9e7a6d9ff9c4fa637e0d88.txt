Amazon has tapped Hawaiian Airlines to operate a fleet of cargo planes starting in the fall of 2023, the carrier announced Friday.

Hawaiian Holdings, Inc., the parent company of the airline, said in a statement it has entered into an agreement with Amazon.com, Inc. and its subsidiaries to fly Amazon's A330s 'to move cargo between airports near the online retailer's operations facilities.'

Per the agreement, Amazon will provide the aircraft, while Hawaiian will provide the crew and maintenance services.
