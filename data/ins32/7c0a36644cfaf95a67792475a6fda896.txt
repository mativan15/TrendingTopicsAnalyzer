Venezuela's Refugee Crisis Should Not Be Ignored
newsweek.com
news
2022-10-18 12:00:01

I arrived in the United States in 2000 to attend Vanderbilt University. I was 17. As a Venezuelan American with a U.S. passport, it was a no-brainer for my family.Hugo Chavez, a charismatic leader who vowed to fight poverty and curb the power of the elite, had been elected president. His socialist rhetoric stoked fear among many middle to upper class Venezuelans, my family included. Invoking what happened to Cuba as a proxy for Venezuela's future, my father encouraged me to head north. And so I did.
