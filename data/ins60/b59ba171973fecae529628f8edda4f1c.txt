Yen hits 30-year low against dollar
thetimes.co.uk
news
2022-10-20 11:00:00

The Japanese yen fell to its weakest level against the dollar since the country's economic crash in 1990, raising expectations that the government will be forced to prop up the currency again.The yen fell below 150 to the dollar for the first time since August 1990 today, despite attempts from the country's authorities to intervene in the foreign exchange markets to halt the slide.Japan's currency has been battered by the country having the last negative interest rate regime in the world as other central banks have aggressively tightened monetary policy to quell inflation.
