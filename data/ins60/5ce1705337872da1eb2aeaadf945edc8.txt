Not so long ago, this city's name carried the same weight of grandeur and elegance. From 1847 to 1937, the Fall River Line steamship service connected Manhattan and Boston, carrying the Vanderbilts, Astors, Rockefellers, Roosevelts and several U.S. presidents in high luxury aboard ships whose opulence rivaled the world's finest hotels.

'Some of the wealthiest and best-known people in the country were passing through Fall River,' says journalist and author John Henry.
