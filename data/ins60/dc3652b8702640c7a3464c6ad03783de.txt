21st Century Monetary PolicyThe Federal Reserve from the Great Inflation to COVID-19 By Ben S. Bernanke Norton. 480 pp. $35 - - - Federal Reserve Chairman William McChesney Martin spoke for generations of monetary policymakers when he famously declared in 1955 that the job of the central bank was to take away the punch bowl "just when the party was really warming up." But in recent years, this puritanical approach to managing the ups and downs of the economy had fallen into disrepute. Citing structural changes in the economy and the lessons learned from the two most recent recessions, central bankers have come to believe that they must "do whatever it takes" to pull an economy out of a serious downturn and keep the cheap money flowing until the economy has fully recovered - and then some.
