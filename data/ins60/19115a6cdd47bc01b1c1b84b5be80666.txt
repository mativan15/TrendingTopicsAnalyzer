It was early 2012 and for the ambitious men of the Chinese Communist Party (CCP), possibility was in the air. After a decade under Hu Jintao as president and Wen Jiabao as premier, it was time for a changing of the guard.Due to term limits and a mandatory retirement age of 68, both men were preparing to stand down. The rules also meant that seven of the nine members of the all-powerful Politburo Standing Committee were on the way out. To win a seat on the committee meant you were immediately vaulted into the upper echelon of Chinese power.
