Edmonton released a multi-pronged plan Monday in response to outcry from Chinatown residents and demands from Alberta's justice minister for action to address ongoing crime and social disorder issues. (Natasha Riebe/CBC)The City of Edmonton has firmed up steps aimed at preventing crime and disorder in Chinatown as calls for action continue to grow less after two men were killed in the community.Mayor Amarjeet Sohi's office confirmed the new measures Monday — a few days after Alberta Justice Minister Tyler Shandro gave the city two weeks to come up with an action plan.
