Published: 12:07 EDT, 21 October 2022 | Updated: 12:10 EDT, 21 October 2022 A man who wrongly served 21 years in prison for the rape and murder of girls aged eight and ten was today cleared in one of Norway's 'most serious miscarriages of justice.'The charges against Viggo Kristiansen, now 43, were dropped by the Norwegian prosecutor's office after a re-examination of the evidence.   His false conviction for the rape and murder of Stine Sofie Sorstronen, eight, and Lena Slogedal Paulsen, 10, has been labelled one of the worst miscarriages of justice in the country's history by the media.
