By Reuters Published: 06:49 EDT, 21 October 2022 | Updated: 06:49 EDT, 21 October 2022 By Rama Venkat BENGALURU, Oct 21 (Reuters) - Indian shares ended higher for a sixth straight session on Friday, as strong corporate earnings, including lender Axis Bank, countered weakness in global equities over rate hike fears. The benchmark indexes also posted their biggest weekly gains since late July. The NSE Nifty 50 index settled 0.07% higher at 17,576.30, while the S&P BSE Sensex finished 0.18% up at 59,307.
