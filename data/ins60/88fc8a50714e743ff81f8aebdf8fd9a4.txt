The federal cabinet has signed off on contentious new workplace relations legislation, which the Albanese government says is intended to help low-paid employees, particularly women, negotiate higher pay packets and better conditions.The Albanese government plans to introduce its new workplace relations bill into parliament next week, hoping to pass it this yearThe bill will include a ban on pay secrecy clausesSome business groups claim proposals to include multi-employer bargaining in the bill are 'at best, half-cooked'Workplace Relations Minister Tony Burke plans to introduce the Secure Jobs, Better Pay Bill into parliament next Thursday, following the government's first budget on Tuesday.
