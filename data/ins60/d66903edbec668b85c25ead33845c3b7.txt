The Virtue Bubble Is About to Burst. Good Riddance.

There are costs to living a virtuous life; It requires going without. This is true psychologically, because sacrifice gives virtue meaning. But it's also true mathematically. You pay a price when you constrain who you will buy from, who you will work for, and what you will invest in. When times are good, the costs of a virtuous life may not seem too high, but eventually the bill comes due.That reckoning is now. We may be seeing the end of the virtue economy bubble. And that's not necessarily bad, because the virtue economy wasn't making the world a better place.
