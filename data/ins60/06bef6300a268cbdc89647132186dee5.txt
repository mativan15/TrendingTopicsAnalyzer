The sleek stainless steel cars that Via Rail Canada uses for its cross-country trains and on some other routes have gained an embarrassing visual indication of their very advanced age. Under orders from the federal transport minister, empty coaches now sit at the front and the back of the trains to act as safety buffers during collisions.

Image

Passengers having a drink within the curved rear windows of bobtailed observation cars at the back of the transcontinental Canadian trains will eventually again get to watch the rails recede into the distance, rather than stare at an empty coach.
