Brian Villani, 26, tall and in khakis, extroverted, both opinionated and earnest, shares a garden-level apartment with two roommates in greater Boston that's outfitted with the material culture of young adulthood: big overstuffed couch, multiple gaming systems, oversize posters, a clutter of plastic kitchenware. He commutes by train to a job he's held for years at a corporate mail room downtown, a job he loves—'I pick up all the packages, and all my vendors know me,' he says. He lives close—'but not too close,' he says wryly—to his parents and has an abiding passion for sports, especially the art of play-by-play announcing.
