Published: 11:02 EDT, 27 May 2022 | Updated: 11:02 EDT, 27 May 2022 DUBAI, May 27 (Reuters) - Iranian protesters chanting anti-government slogans took to the streets of several cities including southwestern Abadan where a building collapse blamed on widespread corruption killed at least 24 people, news agencies and social media said. Demonstrators vented their anger at Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, chanting "Death to Khamenei", late on Thursday as they stormed the gate of Abadan, according to videos posted on social media on Friday.
