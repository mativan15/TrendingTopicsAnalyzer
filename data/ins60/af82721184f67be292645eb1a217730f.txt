Goldman Sachs CEO: 'It's time to be cautious' (NYSE:GS)

Alex Wroblewski/Getty Images News Goldman Sachs CEO David Solomon struck a note of caution in the wake of the financial giant's Street-beating quarterly results, saying that economic headwinds will likely impact both consumers and the markets in the coming quarters. "It's time to be cautious," the head of Goldman Sachs (NYSE:GS) told CNBC. "[Most economists] are predicting slow-to-no growth in the U.S. They are predicting negative growth across other developed economies in Europe." For Goldman's business, Solomon said the recent volatility represented opportunity because it meant the firm's clients were "resorting their position given the fact that the world's changed.
