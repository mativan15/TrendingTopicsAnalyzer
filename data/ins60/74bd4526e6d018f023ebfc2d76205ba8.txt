Slovenia's parliament has voted into office a new, center-left government, replacing a right-wing one that had pushed the moderate European Union nation toward populism

FILE - Robert Golob, the leader of the Freedom Movement party, speaks after meeting with President Borut Pahor, in Ljubljana, Slovenia, April 26, 2022. Slovenia's parliament has voted into office a new, center-left government, replacing a right-wing one that had pushed the moderate European Union nation toward populism. Lawmakers voted 53-28 Wednesday, June 1 for the Cabinet of Robert Golob, head of the liberal-green Freedom Movement party and a former business executive who only recently entered politics.
