25% of Americans are delaying retirement due to inflation, survey finds
cnbc.com
news
2022-05-31 17:49:35

A customer pumps gas into their car at a gas station on May 18, 2022 in Petaluma, California.Americans' finances are being squeezed as inflation pushes up prices on things such as rent, groceries and gasoline.As a result, one-quarter of Americans will have to delay their retirement, according to the BMO Real Financial Progress Index, a quarterly survey conducted between March 30 and April 25.Putting off retirement plans is mostly due to disrupted savings from increased prices, the survey found. Because of higher prices, 36% of survey respondents said that they reduced their savings, and 21% specifically are putting away less for retirement to keep up with growing costs.
