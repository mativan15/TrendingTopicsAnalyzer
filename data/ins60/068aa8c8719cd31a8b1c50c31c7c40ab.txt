Save for a glimpse through a window or briefly open door, many of us never come into direct contact with the capital's most exclusive homes.Though our collective notions of luxury are shifting, these tend to tick a few tried-and-tested boxes — think serious square footage, slick amenities and prime locations in a handful of choice neighbourhoods.The same week that saw the resurfacing of London's most expensive home — which achieved its dizzying £210 million price tag in 2020 — gave us more market volatility, made plainly conspicuous by a fluctuating pound.
