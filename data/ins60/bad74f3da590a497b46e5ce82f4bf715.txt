‘It's driven by fear': Ukrainians and Russians with Jewish roots flee to Israel
theguardian.com
sport
2022-10-16 10:00:33

A visit to Jerusalem's only boxing club is like stepping into the past: the former bomb shelter in a working-class neighbourhood of Jerusalem is a riot of old-school paraphernalia, and a CD of 1970s disco provides the soundtrack for training sessions.This is the kingdom of Gershon Luxemburg, better known as Grisha, a fit 78-year-old who barks instructions while chewing menthol gum. Born in Uzbekistan, the former Russian champion came to Israel as a young man; today he trains many new Russian-speaking émigrés.
