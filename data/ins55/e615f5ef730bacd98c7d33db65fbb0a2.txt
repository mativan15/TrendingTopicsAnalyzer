Foxconn might have made its name globally as the manufacturer of Apple's iPhone, but the Taiwanese company is targeting a new future; electric vehicles. At the company's annual Tech Day on Tuesday, executives unveiled the next three vehicles in that plan, including the first Taiwan-made electric pickup.At its annual shareholder meeting in May, chief executive Liu Young-Way had already underlined just how seriously the company takes its ambitions. He said he wanted the company to account for 5% of the global market within three years, per the Washington Post, a market equivalent to $31 billion.
