Shares of Electronic Arts (EA -0.35%) gained 17.5% in May, according to data from S&P Global Market Intelligence. The surge started with a robust earnings report. Later on, the momentum was boosted by reports about a potential buyout.

Image source: Getty Images.

So what
EA's fiscal fourth-quarter sales rose 36% year over year, landing at $1.83 billion. Earnings jumped from $0.26 to $0.80 per share. The results were better than management guidance across the board, with higher revenue and lower expenses than expected.
