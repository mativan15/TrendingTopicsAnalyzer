Pay for female CEOs rose 26% in 2021, but ranks remain small
go.com
news
2022-05-27 00:00:00

NEW YORK -- Pay packages for the women who run S&P 500 companies jumped in 2021 as the economy recovered and stock prices and profits soared.Median pay for the women occupying the corner office rose to nearly $16 million, according to the annual survey done by Equilar for The Associated Press. Still, experts say there's much more to be done to improve gender diversity in the corporate ranks and close the pay gap between men and women.Jane Stevenson, vice chair, Board & CEO Services at organizational consulting firm Korn Ferry said while it is good that women CEOs' pay rose, there is still a lot to be done.
