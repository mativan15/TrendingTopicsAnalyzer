It was a desire to revive the humble flapjack that inspired Sally Jenkin and Carol Myott to start a business eight years ago from their kitchens in Tavistock, the picturesque West Devon market town on the edge of Dartmoor.'We both like good home cooking and felt the flapjack had been victim of so much horror,' said Jenkin, 59, who was fed up with the flavourless, long-life lumps of sugar sold in supermarkets and service stations.Flapjackery was born — and the two entrepreneurs have not looked back since.
