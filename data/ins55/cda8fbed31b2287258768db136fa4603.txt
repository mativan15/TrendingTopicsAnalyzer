Ironically, the person running a company can be one of its greatest strengths and also one of its weakest links. Where employees and investors see a stalwart leader, a hacker may see a lucrative attack vector that's an easy inroad to an organisation.These high-profile people—executives, the board of directors, and other leaders—are privy to sensitive information that cybercriminals lust after which makes them primary targets for bad guys online.FBI statistics (opens in new tab) show that defrauding CEOs is a '$12 billion scam.
