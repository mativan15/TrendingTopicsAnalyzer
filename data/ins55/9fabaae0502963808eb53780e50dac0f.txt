Just ahead of half-term for many British families, Spain has suddenly announced the ending of Covid-19 restrictions.The nation, which is the most popular overseas destination for British holidaymakers, has lagged well behind most of the rest of Europe in removing vaccination and testing rules.At present all travellers to Spain from countries outside the EU who are over the age of 12 must submit proof of one of the following:full vaccination, including a booster if the original course was completed more than 270 days previouslyrecovery from Covida negative Covid test, either antigen or PCRBut for arrivals from one minute past midnight on Friday 21 October, all the requirements will end.
