Back Issues: Inspector of public nuisances a role not for the faint-hearted
stuff.co.nz
news
2022-06-03 17:00:00

Barely a week goes by when Stuff does not report on a ‘'public nuisance'' of some kind – boy racers, rubbish ‘fly tippers', dangerous dogs, litterbugs, water polluters, producers of excessive noise. Wherever people live in close proximity there is potential at the very least to annoy others, and at worst, to endanger health and safety. When this happens, rules are enacted. Someone is appointed to enforce these rules. And in the newly-formed borough of Palmerston North in 1877, this person was the ‘'inspector of public nuisances''.
