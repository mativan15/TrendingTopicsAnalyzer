Why the Furious Critics of Baseball's Wild Playoffs Are Wrong
slate.com
entertainment
2022-10-19 09:45:00

The Major League Baseball postseason is a ritual exercise in volatility. No other sport has the combination of a 162-game regular season and a playoff system that allows a team to reach the summit with as few as 11 more wins across three series (or, now, 13 wins across four series). NHL and NBA teams play half as many regular-season games (82) and need 16 more playoff wins to top their sports. The NFL demands three or four extra games on top of a 17-game regular season. Compared with other sports, small playoff samples relative to the regular season make baseball weird and open championships up to teams beyond any given year's top echelon.
