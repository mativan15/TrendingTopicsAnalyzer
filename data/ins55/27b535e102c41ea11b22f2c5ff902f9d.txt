By Reuters Published: 13:40 EDT, 17 October 2022 | Updated: 13:40 EDT, 17 October 2022 By Angelo Amante ROME, Oct 17 (Reuters) - Giorgia Meloni, expected to be named Italian prime minister, and her coalition partner Silvio Berlusconi had a cordial meeting on Monday and aim to quickly form a "cohesive" government, their parties said. Tensions have been running high over the allocation of cabinet posts between Meloni, whose Brothers of Italy party led a rightist alliance to victory in a Sept. 25 election, and Berlusconi, who heads the smaller Forza Italia party.
