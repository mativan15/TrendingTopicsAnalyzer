Shutter2U/iStock via Getty Images Investment Thesis There are some stocks that we consider speculative. These stocks have aggressive growth rates and are tagged with premium growth multiples. They are also likely to be GAAP unprofitable, given their primary focus on growth. But, they could at least report free cash flow (FCF) or adjusted profitability. Then some stocks are highly speculative, or we call them hype stocks. They have negative FCF, negative adjusted profitability, and obviously negative GAAP profits.
