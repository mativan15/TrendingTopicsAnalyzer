Advocates push for University Hospital funding
politico.com
news
2022-05-27 00:00:00

Advocates are pushing for New Jersey's only public hospital to receive some healthcare of its own.As the state continues to recover from the overwhelming Covid-19 pandemic and is flush with cash due to higher than expected tax revenues — and billions in federal Covid dollars — Newark's University Hospital may be primed with its best shot at acquiring desperately needed funding for infrastructure upgrades and construction of a new campus.Health Professionals and Allied Employees, the state's largest union of registered nurses and health care professionals, and Senate Majority Leader Teresa Ruiz (D-Essex) have been leading the charge to sufficiently finance the state's sole public hospital, which also counts Essex County Executive Joseph DiVincenzo Jr.
