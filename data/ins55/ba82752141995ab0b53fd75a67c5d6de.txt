Can Britain escape the 'moron risk premium'?
economist.com
finance
2022-10-20 10:50:11

After a month of being exciting for all the wrong reasons, Britain's bond market is at last settling down. All it took was an emergency bond-buying programme from the central bank, the defenestration of a chancellor, the installation of a sensible successor, the humiliation of a prime minister and the shredding of a vast unfunded tax-cutting package that set the fiasco in motion. At the height of the chaos, Britain's five-year borrowing costs were higher than those of Italy and Greece, two countries that have difficult relationships with their lenders.
