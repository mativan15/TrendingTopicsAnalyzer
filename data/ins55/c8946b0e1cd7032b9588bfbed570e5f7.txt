By Reuters Published: 03:39 EDT, 19 October 2022 | Updated: 03:39 EDT, 19 October 2022 BERLIN, Oct 19 (Reuters) - German exports to Britain this year could grow for the first time since 2015, statistics office data showed, but the DIHK business association said the numbers did not mark a turn for the better, especially in light of Britain's current turbulence. From January to August, German companies sold goods worth 48 billion euros ($47.37 billion) to Britain, an increase of about 11.4% compared with the same period in 2021, showed data made available to Reuters.
