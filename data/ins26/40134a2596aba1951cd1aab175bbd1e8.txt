Medicine has made huge progress in the fight against cancer. In the past three decades, the average person's risk of dying from cancer in the United States has decreased by 32%, thanks to factors like earlier detection and advances in drug treatment.  Still, while increasing survival rates are encouraging, cancer remains prevalent. According to the American Cancer Society, cancer is the second most common cause of death in the U.S., after heart disease. And scientists continue to look for ways to turn the tide against cancer.
