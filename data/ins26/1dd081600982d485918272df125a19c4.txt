An inquiry into Canada's worst mass shooting has released audio of a call that lead to political interference claims in the massacre investigation.

The head of the RCMP, the federal police force, was accused of pressuring local officers to help advance the Trudeau government's gun control plans. The accusation stemmed from notes from the call first released in June as part of the inquiry.Commissioner Brenda Lucki has denied any meddling in the investigation.On Thursday, the Nova Scotia mass casualty commission posted "partial audio recordings" and transcripts of the conference call between Ms Lucki and local RCMP personnel.
