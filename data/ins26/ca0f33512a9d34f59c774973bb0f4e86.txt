For more than 60 years Britain's housing policy has failed. Insatiable demand has been fuelled by years of stagnation, government inaction, failure in policy and vision and failure in leadership that has always prioritised local political expediency over national and local needs. Most of what the government has done has only worsened the problem: shortfalls in housebuilding, the long-standing buy-to-let subsidies, the Help to Buy and Right to Buy schemes, the tinkering with planning rules, the failure to stop foreign investors parking cash in London, the stamp duty holidays and so on.
