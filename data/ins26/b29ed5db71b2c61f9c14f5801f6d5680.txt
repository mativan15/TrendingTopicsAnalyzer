Published: 16:49 EDT, 17 October 2022 | Updated: 16:49 EDT, 17 October 2022 Pressure is building on UK Prime Minister Liz Truss, who sacked her finance minister last week following his controversial mini-budget The pound surged against the dollar Monday as Britain's fourth finance minister in as many months sensationally ripped up a tax-cutting budget that had spooked markets. Chancellor of the Exchequer Jeremy Hunt axed almost all of the debt-fueled tax cuts unveiled last month by the new government of Prime Minister Liz Truss, reassuring markets that had tanked and pushing the pound to an all-time low against the dollar.
