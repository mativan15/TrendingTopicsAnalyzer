GameStop sales climb along with shares even as loss widens
foxbusiness.com
news
2022-06-02 04:52:35

GameStop Corp. sales rose as more people returned to its stores last quarter, but its loss widened, damping investors' hopes for signs of a turnaround for the videogame retailer. The Grapevine, Texas-based company posted on Wednesday $1.38 billion in net sales for the quarter through April 30, up from $1.28 billion a year earlier. On an adjusted basis, the retailer reported a loss of $2.08 a share, compared with a loss of 45 cents a share over the same period a year ago. Three analysts polled by FactSet expected net sales of $1.
