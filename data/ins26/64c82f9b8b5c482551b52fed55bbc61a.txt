Netflix plans to crack down on password sharing. Future Publishing
Netflix has been saying for years that password sharing—when someone without a Netflix NFLX account uses a friend or family member's account to access the streamer's content—is a problem. During this week's earnings call, the company outlined a new plan to crack down on password sharing. It claims that 100 million households worldwide, and 30 million in North America, are sharing passwords, but it also made that claim while sustaining its first decline in subscribers in a decade and fighting a rush of new competitors.
