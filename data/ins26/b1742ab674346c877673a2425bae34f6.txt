By Reuters Published: 11:59 EDT, 21 October 2022 | Updated: 11:59 EDT, 21 October 2022 By Gertrude Chavez-Dreyfuss and Saqib Iqbal Ahmed NEW YORK, Oct 21 (Reuters) - The battered yen jumped against the dollar on Friday, triggering speculation that Japanese authorities may be in the market to stem a slide in their currency. The yen rose as high 147.10 per dollar in late morning U.S. trade, up more than four yen from a 32-year peak of 151.94 yen. It was last down 1.1% at 148.46. "It looks like the Ministry of Finance is intervening here.
