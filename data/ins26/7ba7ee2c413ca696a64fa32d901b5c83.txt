China is having a field day with former UK Prime Minister Lizz Truss' resignation.On Weibo, China's heavily censored version of Twitter, the hashtag "Prime Minister Truss resigns" is flush with comments of glee and ridicule. As of Thursday, it had been seen more than 360 million times.But the jeering goes deeper than race-against-lettuce memes. On Weibo, it's not just Truss and her economic plan that are the butt of the joke — the mockery also extends to the very political system that governs the West.
