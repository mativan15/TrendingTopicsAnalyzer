Colorado Republicans' goal in House races: 'Let's narrow the gap'
bizjournals.com
business
2022-10-18 20:59:00

Short of a historical landslide, Republicans are not going to be able to win back control of the Colorado House of Representatives on Nov. 8.

But if the party can flip even four seats, the way that policy is considered in the body could look very different from the past two years.

Republicans have essentially been a non-entity in the House for the past four years, when they've been at a 41-24 deficit in members. While they've put up fights against policies like increased oil-and-gas regulation or more rigorous rules around employer-employee relations, such major business-affecting bills have passed with few if any changes from the chamber.
