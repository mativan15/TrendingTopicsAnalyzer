When India's first commercial flight took off in 1911 within Allahabad, little had the world hoped for any remarkable uptake of this sector in a price-sensitive, nascent market like India. However, over time, the rising Indian middle class embraced this mode of transport, and thus, with economies of scale kicking in, fares dipped. This defined the Indian civil aviation growth story until 2016.
Air travel for aam admi
In the year 2016, this sector saw a tectonic change. Small no-frills airports sprouted in places like Darbhanga, Jharsuguda and Kishangarh that earlier didn't have a single trace on India's aviation map.
