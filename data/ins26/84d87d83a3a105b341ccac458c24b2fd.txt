Life could have been very different for Rosie Galligan. When the Harlequins second-row runs out to make her Rugby World Cup debut against South Africa in Auckland on Sunday, an arduous journey through some punishing yet character-forming experiences will be complete.Three years ago she faced the grim possibility of a double leg amputation due to a bout of meningitis. Her impressively swift recovery from that episode, over several weeks, was promptly followed by a career-threatening injury. She was dropped in a lineout which caused her left ankle to shatter and it required several pins to be reconstructed.
