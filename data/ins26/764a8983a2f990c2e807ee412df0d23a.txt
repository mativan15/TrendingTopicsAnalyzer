Published: 02:04 EDT, 21 October 2022 | Updated: 02:04 EDT, 21 October 2022 By Mike Dolan LONDON, Oct 21 (Reuters) - The Bank of England has the uneviable task of convincing markets they are wrong about how high UK interest rates need to rise, without pulling the rug from under sterling again in the process. In all the chaos of UK government decision-making and political leadership over the past month, the BoE was initially sidelined and then handed a mop to clean up the mess. Given the detritus, it's done well to at least partly clean up shredded financial market confidence and restore a semblance of credibility to policymaking.
