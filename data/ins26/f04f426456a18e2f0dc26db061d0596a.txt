Today in 1892, the Sierra Club was formed by John Muir and others in San Francisco, to promote the conservation of nature. Today in 1929, the first 'talking' color movie, 'On With The Show,' opened in New York. Today in 1934, the Dionnes were born in Callander, Ontario. They were the first quintuplets to survive infancy. Today in 1936, Alan Turing submitted "On Computable Numbers" for publication, in which he set out the theoretical basis for modern computers. Today in 1940, an airtight steel and concrete time capsule with over a thousand artifacts was buried under Ogelthorpe University in Atlanta, Georgia.
