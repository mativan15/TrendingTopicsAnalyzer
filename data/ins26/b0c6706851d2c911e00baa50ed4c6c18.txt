The process by which the next Tory leader, and therefore prime minister, will be elected is not that different from the previous one, albeit with a higher threshold among MPs and a significantly condensed timetable. Will it produce a better result for the country?Whoever emerges faces multiple overlapping challenges. They must unite a political party that has descended into factionalism and threatens to become unmanageable. They must stabilise an economy that was pushed to the brink by a disastrous mini-Budget.
