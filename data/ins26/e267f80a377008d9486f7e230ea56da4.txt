Voters Stick to Pandemic-Era Habits, As Early Turnout Surges
nytimes.com
news
2022-10-22 09:00:30

Days into early voting in the 2022 midterm elections, states across the country have seen a surge of voters casting ballots at in-person voting sites and by mail, the latest sign that the 2020 election ushered in a transformation in the way Americans vote.

Through the first five days of early voting in Georgia, in-person turnout is up 70 percent compared with turnout in the 2018 midterm elections, according to the secretary of state's office. In North Carolina, absentee ballot requests are up 114 percent compared with requests in 2018, according to the board of elections.
