How the Kremlin Infiltrated Russia's Facebook
wired.co.uk
news
2022-06-01 11:00:00

In December 2021, two months before the invasion of Ukraine, the new CEO of Russia's second-most-popular social media network sat next to an immaculate Christmas tree and introduced himself to staff in a video message. For the occasion, Vladimir Kiriyenko—a man usually only pictured in suits—donned dark jeans with New Balance trainers. Throughout the one-hour video, he mostly avoided eye contact with the camera, instead directing his comments about the company's future to Ochir Mandzhikov, VK's vice president of communications.
