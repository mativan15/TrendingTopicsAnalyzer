Inflation is clocking in at the highest levels in four decades. According to a September 2022 release from the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the Consumer Price Index is up 8.3% over the last 12 months. And the biggest price increases have come in items people need most, including shelter, food and medical care.

But the pain at the checkout counter is just the beginning of the damage we're sustaining. A less obvious but potentially even more costly impact over the long run is the distortion that inflation causes in the information that people and businesses use to plan their pursuits and chart the course of our economic future.
