Texas GOP moved quicker to protect internet trolls than school children

If Texas Republicans were as vigorous about protecting school kids from gunfire as they have been about protecting internet trolls from social media bans, the 21 people massacred in a Uvalde, Texas, last week might still be alive. On Tuesday, the Supreme Court temporarily blocked a Texas law that prohibits social media companies from banning users based on their political 'viewpoints.' The decision came through the Supreme Court's 'shadow docket,' the mysterious method of issuing "emergency' rulings that the court's conservative justices have frequently used to restrict civil rights.
