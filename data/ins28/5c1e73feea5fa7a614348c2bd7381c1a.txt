LONDON - Liz Truss said on Thursday she was resigning as British prime minister just six weeks after she was appointed.A leadership election will be completed within the next week to replace Truss, who is the shortest-serving prime minister in British history. George Canning previously held the record, serving 119 days in 1827 when he died.Given the divisions in the party there is no obvious candidate and any replacement would face a country likely heading into a recession.
