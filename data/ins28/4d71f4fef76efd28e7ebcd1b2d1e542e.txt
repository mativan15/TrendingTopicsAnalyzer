Credit card annual fee too expensive? Here's how to downgrade it and avoid lowering your credit score

This story is part of CNBC Make It's One-Minute Money Hacks series, which provides easy, straightforward tips and tricks to help you understand your finances and take control of your money.If your credit card annual fee is starting to feel like it's not worth your while, don't close the card: consider downgrading it instead.Downgrading a credit card means switching to another card from your same issuer that has a lower fee, or none at all, usually in exchange for fewer points and rewards.Closing a card can hurt your credit score because it lowers your credit limit, which in turn increases your credit utilization rate.
