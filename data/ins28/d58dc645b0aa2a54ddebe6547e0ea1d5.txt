 Much like taxes, annuities just aren't very popular.
No one likes paying taxes to Uncle Sam, and a mere 17% of American households led by someone between 40 and 85 owns an annuity, according to industry group Limra's 2022 Consumer Sentiment Survey.
That makes any conversation about both annuities and taxes enough to make your head spin. But here's the thing: A better understanding of annuity taxation could offer you big financial benefits.
