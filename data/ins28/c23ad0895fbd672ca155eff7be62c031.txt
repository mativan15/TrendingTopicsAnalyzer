Like millions of Americans, I found myself out of work during the pandemic. At first, I was laid off from my waitressing job. Then I began working for myself as an online fitness coach, but running the business required 10-hour workdays and I was only making $1,000 to $2,000 a month. So in July 2021, I decided to pivot. I took my entrepreneurship experience and started to use my TikTok and Instagram accounts to market wealth-building tools and online programs. By October, business had taken off and my TikTok following grew to more than 40,000 followers.
