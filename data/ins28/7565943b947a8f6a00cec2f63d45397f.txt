(Image: Getty)Ah, the great outdoors. Breathing in fresh air. Swimming in streams. Getting your hands a bit muddy – it's all good. There are few folks who come back from spending time in nature without feeling its positive effects. No wonder some call it the "Natural Health Service".And yet, for many of us, enjoying nature doesn't happen as often as it should. Even when we do lace up our walking boots for a jaunt in the country, it's only for a couple of hours before heading back to computers and commuting and weekend boozing.
