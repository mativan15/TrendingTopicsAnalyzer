Jay Inslee says more federal money is needed to finish the job of cleaning up the Hanford Nuclear Reservation, a former nuclear weapons production siteRICHLAND, Wash. -- Washington Gov. Jay Inslee, who has recently criticized the slow pace of cleaning up the Hanford Nuclear Reservation, toured the former nuclear weapons production site on Thursday and said more federal money is needed to finish the job.Hanford created more than two-thirds of the nation's plutonium for nuclear weapons, including the atomic bomb dropped on Nagasaki, Japan, at the end of World War II.
