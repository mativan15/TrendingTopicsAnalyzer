After two years of lockdowns and social distancing the flu has returned with a vengeance.Lab-confirmed cases of the flu were among the worst rates on record in 2017, resulting in 29,000 hospital admissions and 745 deaths across Australia.When the pandemic arrived, the flu all but disappeared as better hygiene measures were adopted and lockdowns limited the population's movements.But now the easing of COVID restrictions means people are out and about, and this has led to a surge in flu cases.Now health authorities are pleading with Australians to get a flu shot to ease the growing pressure on hospitals.
