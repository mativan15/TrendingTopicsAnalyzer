Death of teenage boy highlights Australia's lack of resources for treating gaming disorder, coroner finds
theguardian.com
news
2022-10-19 04:35:55

The psychological dangers of online gaming have not been adequately addressed by Australian authorities, as highlighted by the death of a teenage boy in regional Victoria, a coronial investigation has found.Victorian coroner Paresa Spanos released her findings into Oliver Cronin's death on Wednesday, saying the tragedy underscored a lack of resources for recognising and treating gaming disorder.The coroner heard Oliver, 13, was a well-liked student with a large circle of friends who had not suffered depression before his gaming addiction took hold.
