Sovereignty and self-determination should be negotiable—and not under the barrel of a gun.By Timothy William WatersTyler Comrie / The AtlanticOctober 20, 2022, 7 AM ETWhen U.S. Secretary of State Antony Blinken talks about defending the 'rules-based order,' he is referring to the post–World War II liberal, internationalist, market-based system that, bolstered by human rights, seemed to reach its apotheosis with the end of the Cold War. One of that system's foundational principles is the rejection of war as a way to change borders.
