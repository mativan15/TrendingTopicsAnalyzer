When the stock market sinks, not all stocks sink with it. Some manage to do quite well during a downturn, whether it be because they cater to cost-conscious consumers, such as Dollar Tree (DLTR -3.16%), or because they operate in the recession-resistant healthcare industry, such as McKesson (MCK -0.51%). It could also be because they have built-in diversification that insulates them from a downturn, such as industrial supplier Carlisle Companies (CSL -4.09%).
All three of these companies are easily beating the S&P 500 index, which has declined roughly 25% so far this year.
