Chris Wellisz profiles Harvard's Melissa Dell, who pioneers new ways of unmasking legacies of the past

Melissa Dell needed a break from the archives in Cusco, Peru. So she climbed onto a bus to visit the remote mountainous area she was studying and spent a few weeks talking to the descendants of the people who had toiled in the silver mines under Spanish rule. They wore traditional Quechua garb and lived in wooden dwellings with dirt floors and no windows.
