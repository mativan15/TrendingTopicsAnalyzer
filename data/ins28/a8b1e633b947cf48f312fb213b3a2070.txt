Were it not revealed in the very first chapter of the novel, it might seem unlikely that Allie's most fervent admirer is a white supremacist named Matthew Strong. And were every subsequent chapter not titled in relation to what Strong does to her ('Twenty-Four Days Before My Abduction,' 'Five Days Before My Abduction,' etc.), her eventual entrapment within a restored plantation home might seem like a bizarre twist in the career trajectory of a scholar, whose greatest dramas are typically about securing tenure.
