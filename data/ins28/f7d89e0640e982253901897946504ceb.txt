Slash Film If there's one thing Alicent Hightower certainly is, it is conflicted. To like Rhaenyra or not to like. To hate Rhaenyra or not to hate. In "House of the Dragon," HBO's breathtaking adaptation of George R.R. Martin's "Fire and Blood," Alicent is the show's most conflicted character. And that's saying something, because there are many.While "House of the Dragon" is relatively faithful to its source material, it takes the initiative to inject secrets, altercations, and personal struggles between characters that causes the souring of their relationship, making these one-dimensional historical figures seem more human.
