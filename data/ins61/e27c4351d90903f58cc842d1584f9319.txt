Shenzhen gives $4.5 million in e-CNY to boost consumption

By Chen Liubing | chinadaily.com.cn | Updated: 2022-05-30 16:57 A staff member of the Bank of Communications Beijing Branch instructs a visitor to open a digital RMB wallet on a mobile phone on June 16, 2021. [Photo/Xinhua] The city of Shenzhen in South China's Guangdong province is giving out digital RMB red envelopes valued at 30 million yuan ($4.51 million) through on-demand services platform Meituan to boost consumption and business recovery. Consumers in Shenzhen could log on to the Meituan App and sign up for a raffle starting at 10 am on May 30, with winners obtaining e-CNY to spend on more than 15,000 offline e-CNY pilot merchants and the Meituan platform, news portal Economic View reported on Monday.
