Delhi suffers from poor air quality as min temp falls 2 degrees below season's average

According to the Met office, the national capital's minimum temperature on Monday morning was 17.5 degrees Celsius, which is two degrees below the season's average. The weatherman has forecast mainly clear skies for the day and the maximum temperature expected to settle at 32 degrees Celsius, they said. The relative humidity at 9.30 am was 96 per cent. The Central Pollution Control Board's 24-hour average Air Quality Index (AQI) data revealed that at 9.05 am, Delhi's air quality was in the poor category at 241.
