Starmer leads calls for Truss to decline £115,000 allowance granted to former PMs

Keir Stamer has said Liz Truss should down turn the the allowance of up to £115,000 a year she will be entitled to as a former prime minister.The Labour leader told ITV's Good Morning Britain: 'She should turn it down. I think that's the right thing to do.'She's done 44 days in office, she's not really entitled to it, she should turn it down and not take it.'Despite having the shortest tenure of any prime minister in history, Ms Truss is able to claim the money from the Public Duty Costs Allowance (PDCA).
