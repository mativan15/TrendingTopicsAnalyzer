Image caption, With no mains gas, people in rural areas have to rely on wood, coal and oil to heat their homesPeople living in rural areas of Cumbria have said they are worried by escalating fuel costs.More than two thirds of homes in the Eden District are not on mains gas - the highest proportion in England.Residents instead have to rely on oil, wood and coal to stay warm, but prices are rising, and oil and solid fuel costs are not capped in the way gas is. Alice Bondi, who lives on Alston Moor, said she may have to use her savings to pay for fuel.
