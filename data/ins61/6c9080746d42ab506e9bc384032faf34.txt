Xi Jinping aims for major leap in China's economy by 2035
seekingalpha.com
finance
2022-10-17 08:18:23

Kevin Frayer/Getty Images News China President Xi Jinping stood firm on his policies in a two-hour speech at the 20th National Congress of the Communist Party of China. Xi said that per-capital GDP would rise to the level of a "medium-developed country" in a "giant new leap" by 2035. While he did not give a specific numbers, economists told Bloomberg that would mean doubling GDP and per-capita income, with an average GDP growth rate of 4.7%. Standing firm: Xi focused on economic development that he said would not sacrifice national security.
