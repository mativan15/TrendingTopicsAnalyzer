Slash Film As far as horror movie scores are concerned, few are as iconic as John Carpenter's synth-driven masterpiece for "Halloween." The movie's main theme — that ominous tinkling of the piano — is so powerful that not only does it bring to mind scenes of a menacing Michael Myers stalking Laurie Strode (Jamie Lee Curtis), but it has also solidified itself as essential October listening whether you're a fan of the "Halloween" franchise or not.The soundtrack is iconic not just for its killer sound, but also because of how it came to be.
