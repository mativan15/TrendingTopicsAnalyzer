How Susan Kare Designed User-Friendly Icons for the First Macintosh

Susan Kare designed pictorial symbols that enabled non-technical users to operate a computer, a great contrast to previous screens with 'command line' interfaces that required knowing code. Photo of Susan Kare by Ann Rhoney; Apple Macintosh Computer Icons by Susan Kare / Photo Illustration by Shaylyn Esposito If it wasn't for needlepoint, the computer graphics we have come to know and love today might have looked a lot different. Pioneering designer Susan Kare was taught by her mother how to do counted-thread embroidery, which gave her the basic knowledge she needed to create the first icons for the Apple Macintosh 35 years ago.
