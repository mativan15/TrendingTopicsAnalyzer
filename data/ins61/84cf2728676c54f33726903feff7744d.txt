Tullow and Capricorn strike it rich in Africa-focused tie-up
thetimes.co.uk
news
2022-06-01 23:01:00

Tullow Oil is to buy rival producer Capricorn Energy in an all-share deal valued at £657 million. The enlarged group will have a stock market value of £1.4 billion and has been recommended by the boards of both companies. It will create an oil and gas firm with the bulk of its resources in Africa.Tullow has interests in countries such as Ghana, Gabon and Ivory Coast, while Capricorn has producing assets in Egypt and licences in Mauritania. It is estimated the group will have one billion barrels of resources on its acreage once the deal completes.
