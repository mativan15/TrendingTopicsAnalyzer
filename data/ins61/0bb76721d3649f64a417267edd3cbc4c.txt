7 Tips for College Students to Save Money on Food
fool.com
news
2022-10-15 12:32:38

The increasing cost of living is impacting everyone, and that includes college students. The U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics' Consumer Price Index Summary noted in September 2022 that the cost of food as a whole was up 11.4% over the previous year, with grocery costs up 13.5%. And digital education blog, My eLearning World, recently calculated that during the 2022-2023 school year, college students can expect to pay an average of $294.06 on groceries and $369.36 on eating out per month. That's an average monthly total of $663.
