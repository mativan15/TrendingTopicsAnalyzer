Published: 13:02 EDT, 27 May 2022 | Updated: 13:02 EDT, 27 May 2022 By Siddharth Cavale and Arriana McLymore NEW YORK, May 27 (Reuters) - Major U.S. retailers that recently scrambled to restock shelves amid product shortages disclosed this week that their stores are now packed with too much merchandise, and some are even doing what was unthinkable just a few months ago: discounting unsold goods. It's a sign of possible more trouble to come for retailers amid soaring inflation and higher gas prices.
