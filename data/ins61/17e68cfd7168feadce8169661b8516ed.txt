WARNING: This story contains distressing details.Canada's top court will decide Thursday morning whether to hear an appeal by survivors of a Catholic-run residential school near Fort Albany, Ont., in an ongoing dispute with the federal government over the impact of withheld documents on compensation claims.The group of St. Anne's survivors, unidentified in court documents but represented by author and former chief Edmund Metatawabin, have been trying to have their cases reopened for years.They contend there were "procedural and jurisdictional gaps" in the lower courts' handling of the case — which has unfolded under the residential schools class-action settlement — and asked the Supreme Court to intervene in a March 1 filing.
