What to Make of the Poll That Showed Independent Women Swinging Republican

On Monday, a New York Times/ Sienna College poll indicated that 49 percent of likely voters would back their district's Republican congressional candidate—a supposed swing toward red in a close race for control over the House. But perhaps the most headline-generating result was that the poll also found that women who identified as independent voters now backed Republicans by 18 points–when in September they favored Democrats by 14 points.
