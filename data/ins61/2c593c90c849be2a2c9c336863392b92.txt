Imagine gazing up in the Sistine Chapel with a ceiling 6,500 light-years away.Astronomers have called this tiny portion of the Eagle Nebula in the Serpens constellation (pictured above) the "Pillars of Creation." Some say the name comes from a phrase in a 19th century sermon on the condescension of Christ. In it, a pastor described the dichotomy of a powerful God who created everything and the form of a delicate newborn baby. But this cloud of gas and dust in space has always borne a resemblance to an older masterwork — and an even older Biblical icon — that of Michelangelo's Creation of Adam.
