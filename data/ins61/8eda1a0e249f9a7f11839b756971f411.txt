The stories of their far-flung interests from banking to philanthropy and wine are well known. In this engaging family biography, Natalie Livingstone ('The Mistresses of Cliveden,' 2015) reveals that the women of the family were as accomplished as the men (despite the remark of a Rothschild who once described his wife as 'an essential piece of furniture').
