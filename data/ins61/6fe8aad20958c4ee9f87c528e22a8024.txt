Published: 06:01 EDT, 19 October 2022 | Updated: 06:16 EDT, 19 October 2022 A children's home has been ordered to improve after extraordinary failings saw a manager buying a tattoo gun for one child - which they used to permanently ink another – and staff being slow to stop a young resident making a flamethrower.The centre, which cannot be named but is registered in Leicester, was today revealed to be the site of a series of worrying incidents.At one point – just three months ago – staff were threatened by a child who had made his own weapon to menace them.
