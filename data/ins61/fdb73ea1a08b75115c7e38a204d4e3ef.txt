James Forsyth, 66, Swaffham Prior, Cambridgeshire, bought the 1830s windmill and hopes it will be functional by 2023, cutting out his £200-a-month electricity billJames Forsyth with his windmill restoration project.A villager is grinding out the energy bill crisis and generating his own electricity by restoring an 1830s windmill - and he'll even sell excess power back to the grid. James Forsyth, 66, lives at a corn mill site in Cambridgeshire that has been defunct since 1925. The retired boat builder is undertaking an ambitious project to restore a defunct windmill that he says will cut out his £200-a-month electricity bill.
