June 3, 2022, 6:48 PM UTC / Source: TODAYBy Alexander KacalaWhen I first started going to Fire Island in 2016, I didn't really know what to expect of the iconic gay destination 50 miles east of New York City, accessible only by ferry. I had heard sordid stories from some older gay men on the pleasures that awaited me there: days by the beach and nights by the bar. But the sandbar is cloaked in mystery for anyone who hasn't had the opportunity to set foot on its shores.There are no cars?The streets are boardwalks?You don't really drink tea at tea?After one weekend, I fell in love, and every summer since — excluding a pandemic one here or there — I've spent countless weekends in a share in one of the more infamous houses in the Pines, the island community comprised of predominantly gay men.
