By Carys NotleyBBC Wales newsImage caption, Geoff Thomas says "a delay in renewing projects would be a travesty"When 66-year-old Geoffrey Thomas suffered a stroke five years ago, he thought he would never work again. Until a scheme run by a local charity helped him into a job. "Now I've got pride back as well" said Geoff, from Bedlinog near Merthyr Tydfil, But an umbrella body for charities says projects supporting some of Wales' most vulnerable people are under threat because UK ministers have been too slow to set up a new funding scheme.
