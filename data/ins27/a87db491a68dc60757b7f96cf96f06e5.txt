Anniversaries invariably cause one to look back and reassess the future, jubilees especially so. The Queen has been such a constant in most of our lives it is sometimes hard to grasp quite how much Britain has changed since 1952.When she came to the throne, government brokers still wore top hats to work and at 3.15pm a waiter would ring a bell on the stock exchange floor to allow the gentlemen to smoke. It wasn't until more than 20 years into her reign that a woman (apart from herself) had ever been allowed on to the floor of the exchange itself.
