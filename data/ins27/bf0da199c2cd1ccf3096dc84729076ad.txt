PTIAn exhibit of Tata Advanced Systems Limited (TASL) C-295 transport aircraft during the 12th edition of DefExpo, in Gandhinagar.Aircraft manufacturer Airbus says it has received approval from India's aviation regulator for the C295 aircraft programme. The Indian government, in September, signed a Rs 21,00 crore deal with Airbus Defence and Space to procure 56 C295 transport aircraft. The Directorate General of Aeronautical Quality Assurance (DGAQA) approved the European aviation major's plan to replace the Indian Air Force's ageing Avro-748 planes.
