It's been a turbulent year worldwide for stocks and shares investors, as they've grappled with a backdrop of higher interest rates, soaring inflation, Russia's invasion of Ukraine and China's stringent Covid lockdowns.
The London stock market, in relative terms, has ridden out the storm better than other leading financial centres such as the US.
Take the FT-SE 100. In the year to date (end of May 2022), the stock market index of the UK's largest companies has gained about 1%. Hardly an earth-shattering performance, but compared with events in the US, it's almost a cause for celebration.
