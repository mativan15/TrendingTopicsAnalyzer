By Australian Associated Press Published: 23:11 EDT, 17 October 2022 | Updated: 23:11 EDT, 17 October 2022 Singapore has offered to help the Australian government in responding to the Optus data breach after almost 10 million people had their data stolen. Optus is owned by Singaporean telecommunications conglomerate Singtel. Singaporean Prime Minister Lee Hsien Loong said while Optus was an Australian company operated out of Australia, Singtel was taking the incident seriously and offering its support.
