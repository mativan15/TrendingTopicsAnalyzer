Political donations rule overhaul still leaves Tasmania with 'the weakest laws' in the country, critics say

The Tasmanian government has unveiled long-awaited legislation to overhaul the state's political donations rules, but opponents say they would still be the weakest laws in the country. Under the bill, political donations of more than $5,000 would need to be declared within seven days during election cycles, or six-monthly otherwiseIt is much higher than the $1,000 figure recommended by experts and opposition partiesBut Michael Ferguson said the government believed the threshold was appropriateThe bill was unexpectedly tabled in state parliament on Tuesday, more than four years after former premier Will Hodgman promised to look at reforms.
