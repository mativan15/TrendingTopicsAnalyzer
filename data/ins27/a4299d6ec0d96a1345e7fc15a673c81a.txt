This Halloween we want to celebrate Disneyland's original macabre monument the Haunted Mansion, which for many has served as a rite of passage as a first friendly introduction to horror when visiting the theme park. It definitely was for me growing up in Southern California—I can still remember my first few rides, hiding my face in my parents' arms and peeking through my fingers on the Doom Buggy until I was brave enough to embrace the silly spooks in their graveyard jamboree.So my chat with Imagineer Kim Irvine, daughter of Leota Toombs—the Imagineer who is the model and namesake of the fortune-telling floating head in the Haunted Mansion's seance room—feels like years in the making.
