Fashionistas are stunned, retailers are frightened, but it doesn't appear that anyone is fighting against politicos who propose (what many feel) is a historic case of industry over-regulation by government. Surely the politicos mean well, and everyone wants to support made-in-USA, protect the environment, and be opposed to forced labor - but the numerous financial penalties, mountains of paperwork, and disruptions included in new and proposed legislation will eventually translate into a diminished fashion industry or (potentially) a slow Lingchi death by 1,000 cuts.
