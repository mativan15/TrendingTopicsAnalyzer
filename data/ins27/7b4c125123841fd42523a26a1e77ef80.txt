(Bloomberg) -- Bitcoin has spent more than two weeks trading below a key threshold of $20,000 for the first time since breaching that level in late 2020, indicating a lack of optimism among traders as the asset class's trademark volatility dissipates in the face of rising interest rates.The largest cryptocurrency by market value edged lower for a fourth trading session, dropping less than 1% to $18,969 as of 9:33 a.m. in New York.
