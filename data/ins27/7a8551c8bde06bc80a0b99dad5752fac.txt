America has never lacked for self-confidence. Our unwavering belief in our national superiority bleeds through into our sports — baseball's championship is the World Series, despite the fact that exactly two countries participate. And who needs a silly little World Cup when you already have a Super Bowl?The problem for America is that the rest of the world's sports fans and sporting organizations don't share the same adulation for all things American. In most cases, that's fine with American audiences — billions may play cricket around the world, but it's microscopic in America.
