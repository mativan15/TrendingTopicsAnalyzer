Published: 12:45 EDT, 17 October 2022 | Updated: 12:45 EDT, 17 October 2022 London's top stock exchange, the pound, and Government bonds have all rallied on the day the Chancellor reversed the majority of his predecessor's key fiscal policies in one sitting. Markets were buoyed by Jeremy Hunt's emergency statement in which he ripped up tax-cutting plans to shed billions off the UK Government's debt book. The FTSE 100 jumped up by 1.57% at one point on Monday afternoon and closed firmly in the green, boosted by gains across utilities and housebuilders.
