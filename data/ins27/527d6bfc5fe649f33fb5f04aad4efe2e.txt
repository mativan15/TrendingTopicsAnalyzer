The United States has been experiencing a transformation in the way people recognize diversity and inclusion. In recent years, many have prioritized engaging with and correcting the cultural barriers maintained across multiple areas of their daily lives.Millennial and Gen Z workers—who make up a growing percentage of the workforce—increasingly look for a diverse and inclusive work environment with ethical leadership practices, according to a Gallup survey [1]. In response, many companies, Coursera included, have aligned their cultural priorities accordingly.
