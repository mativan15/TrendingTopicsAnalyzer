It is hard to get excited after looking at Danaher's (NYSE:DHR) recent performance, when its stock has declined 9.9% over the past month. However, stock prices are usually driven by a company's financial performance over the long term, which in this case looks quite promising. In this article, we decided to focus on Danaher's ROE.

Return on equity or ROE is a key measure used to assess how efficiently a company's management is utilizing the company's capital. Simply put, it is used to assess the profitability of a company in relation to its equity capital.
