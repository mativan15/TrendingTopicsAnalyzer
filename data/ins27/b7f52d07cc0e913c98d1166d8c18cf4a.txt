Published: 13:11 EDT, 20 October 2022 | Updated: 13:11 EDT, 20 October 2022 A SpaceX engineer is currently fighting for his life in a hospital after he was critically injured while working on a Raptor engine at Elon Musk's California facility - and the company was only fined $18,475 for two safety violations related to the incident.Francisco Cabada, who has been with SpaceX for more than a decade, was conducting a routine pressure check on a Raptor engine in January when he was struck in the head by a fuel controller cover that broke off immediately after he initiated the venting.
