Jeremy Hunt's statement on medium-term fiscal plan: key points at a glance
theguardian.com
sport
2022-10-17 10:26:35

Jeremy Hunt says the government will reverse almost all of the tax measures announced in the mini-budget that have not been legislated for so far.The measures on stamp duty and national insurance will remain.The chancellor will scrap plans for cuts to dividend tax rates, reversal of off-payroll reforms, the new VAT free shopping scheme for non-UK visitors and the freeze on alcohol duty rates.Hunt says the basic rate of income tax will remain at 20% and do so 'indefinitely' until economic circumstances allow it to be cut.
