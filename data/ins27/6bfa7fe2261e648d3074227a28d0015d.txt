Investors signing up to partake in the $44 billion takeover bid for Twitter are betting on one big thing: Elon Musk. Twitter has spent years as an underperforming Silicon Valley punching bag, but investors big and small are now scrambling for a piece of it, a trend reflected by pitches bouncing around Silicon Valley hawking new ways to get involved. Investors are driven by optimistic revenue projections, their own strategic interests and fierce loyalty to a tech executive seen by many as infallible.
