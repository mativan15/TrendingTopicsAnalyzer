Register now for FREE unlimited access to Reuters.comMILAN, June 1 (Reuters) - Italian energy services group Saipem (SPMI.MI) said on Wednesdayit had agreed to sell its drilling onshore business to British-based oil drilling contractor KCA Deutag (KCAD) for $550 million.As part of the deal - which takes the Italian company further in the achievement of its capital structure and liquidity objectives - Saipem will also get a 10% stake in the combined entity, it said in a statement.Saipem operates its drilling onshore business outside Italy, with a focus in the Middle East and Americas and a portfolio of 83 proprietary land rigs, it added.
