More Video Katie Linsell, Bloomberg News (Bloomberg) -- Asos Plc's new chief executive officer laid out an extensive plan to reduce stock, slow automation and cut capital expenditure in an effort to survive as consumers retrench. Jose Antonio Ramos Calamonte, who took the helm at the British fast fashion chain in June, said Wednesday that the restructuring measures will result in a non-cash write-off of £100 million ($113 million) to £130 million and that free cash flow this fiscal year will be zero at best.
