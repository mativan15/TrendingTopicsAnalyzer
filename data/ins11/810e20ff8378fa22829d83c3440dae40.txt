When you're cooking dinner at home, running late isn't a huge issue. Just don't tell everyone that dinner is ready and that they have to come to the table now because it isn't and they don't. But when you're dining out, you're already at the table. And if the food comes at a glacial pace, it's like being frozen in time. Gaps between courses stretch into chasms and you find that you can actually spend less time eating than you do waiting for something to eat.
