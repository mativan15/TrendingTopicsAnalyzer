Image caption, The Greater Manchester Clean Air Zone was due to come into force on 30 May but it was paused in FebruaryMore than £60m has been spent on Greater Manchester's Clean Air Zone (CAZ) despite the controversial scheme still never having gone ahead.The plan to charge the most polluting vehicles on the city's roads was paused following a public backlash earlier this year.Mayor Andy Burnham said the scheme became "unworkable" post-pandemic.But it means the city is now facing a shortfall because it is tied into contracts with no revenue from charges.
