By Reuters Published: 01:05 EDT, 16 October 2022 | Updated: 01:05 EDT, 16 October 2022 Jonathan Marchessault scored twice and goaltender Adin Hill made 31 saves as the Vegas Golden Knights defeated Seattle 5-2 Saturday night, spoiling the Kraken's home opener. Shea Theodore added a goal and an assist, and Keegan Kolesar and Reilly Smith also scored for the Golden Knights, who earned their third win in as many games under new coach Bruce Cassidy. Jack Eichel had two assists. Justin Schultz and Jaden Schwartz scored in the final 6:41, with Andre Burakovsky assisting on both goals, as the Kraken avoided the shutout in suffering their first regulation loss in three games this season.
