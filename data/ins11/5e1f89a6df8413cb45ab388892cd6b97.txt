Apple's new entry-level iPad just got more expensive
washingtonpost.com
tech
2022-10-18 23:45:02

Apple surprised many industry observers by not raising the price of its latest iPhones this year.But just about everything feels at least a bit more expensive lately with inflation. Is it any surprise that your next tablet may be a little pricier, too?On Tuesday, the Cupertino, Calif., company revealed a handful of updated products, the most notable of which is a new version of its entry-level tablet — the one it simply calls 'iPad,' no modifiers involved. And here's what might be the biggest change: the new model costs $449 to start — that's $120 more than the basic iPad it's eventually meant to replace.
