Published: 15:18 EDT, 16 October 2022 | Updated: 15:30 EDT, 16 October 2022 The amount of British taxpayer money being used to provide Albanian translators for suspected criminals has more than doubled in the last five years, new figures show.The Ministry of Justice (MoJ) revealed that a record £1.2 million was spent in the last year alone providing bilingual staff to communicate legal proceedings to Albanians accused of committing crimes in the UK.This is a rise of 35 per cent in the last year and double the £507,000 figure recorded five years ago.
