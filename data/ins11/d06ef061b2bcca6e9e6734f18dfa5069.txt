Germany may need an additional $39 billion to save Uniper collapse: report
marketwatch.com
news
2022-10-20 11:01:00

The German government may pump another €40 billion ($39 billion) in soon-to-be nationalized German gas importer Uniper to save it from collapse, according to a report from German newspaper Handelsblatt. Uniper's UN01, +0.84% shares dropped as much as 5% to €3.62 on Thursday morning, paring back to €3.76 later in Frankfurt trading. Last month, Berlin announced that it would bring Uniper, which imports around one third of Germany's gas, into state ownership with a rescue deal worth €29 billion. The federal government agreed in September it would buy out Finnish-state energy group Fortum Oyj's FORTUM, +0.
