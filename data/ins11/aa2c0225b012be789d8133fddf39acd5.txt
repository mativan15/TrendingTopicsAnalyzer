Searching for unmarked burial sites is a painstaking process that not all Indigenous communities could be immediately ready for after the remains of more than 200 children were found at a former residential school in British Columbia, says an anthropologist who has done similar projects on the Prairies.John Ulan/The Canadian PressIt was one year ago when the Tk'emlúps te Secwépemc First Nation announced they had found 215 potential unmarked graves around the former Kamloops Indian Residential School.
