Stargazers have something new to look for as a giant explosion rocked space and has been nicknamed BOAT, an abbreviation for Brightest of All Times. Said to be a gamma-ray burst, astronomers believe that the explosion happened either because of a supernova or the death of a star. Chile's Gemini South telescope recorded the explosion. The NOIR Lab of the National Science Foundation operates the Gemini South telescope.
 
• The event is named GRB 221009A

• The explosion happened 2.4 billion light-years away

• It was first captured on the morning of October 9, 2022

• The Gemini Multi-Object Spectrograph and the FLAMINGOS-2 imaging spectrograph are analysing the explosion One researcher, Brendan O'Connor, stated that the explosion was the brightest gamma-ray burst recorded, as its afterglow broke all records.
