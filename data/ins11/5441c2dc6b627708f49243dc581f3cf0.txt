Pakistani stocks jump over 2% as government ends fuel price freeze
indiatimes.com
economics
2022-05-27 07:00:00

AgenciesReuters reported earlier on Thursday that the IMF and Islamabad had reached a deal to release over $900 million in funds, once Pakistan removed the fuel subsidies and hiked prices, according to a Pakistani source directly involved in talks in Qatar that concluded on Wednesday.Pakistani stocks jumped more than 2% shortly after opening on Friday, reacting positively to an overnight decision by the government to end a months-old freeze on fuel prices. Pakistan late on Thursday announced it would hike fuel prices so that it can resume receiving aid from a $6 billion package signed with the International Monetary Fund (IMF) in 2019.
