EU warns it may shut down Bitcoin miners to steer more energy to households and factories
fortune.com
news
2022-10-19 09:48:55

With Europe in the grips of an energy crisis, European policy makers are once again questioning the value of supporting a regional Bitcoin industry. 'The energy consumption of cryptocurrencies has increased by 900% in the past five years and has more or less doubled compared to two years ago, reaching around 0.4% of worldwide electricity consumption,' the EU governing body, the European Commission, said Tuesday, as it released its action plan for how to digitalize the bloc's energy system. Although the most ambitious component of the plan is establishing a region-wide data sharing initiative so that national power suppliers and government can coordinate on distribution, the most eye-catching aspect of the blueprint is its vision for cryptocurrencies.
