People Come to Office to Connect, Not for Policy
indiatimes.com
economics
2022-10-20 02:30:00

The world is experiencing a once-in-a-lifetime change in 'how, where and why we work,' and it is 'not going back to 2019,' according to Microsoft chairman Satya Nadella. Companies 'need to accept that and find a new path forward,' he said in an exclusive email interview to ET's Priyanka Sangani & Surabhi Agarwal. The India-born executive, widely credited with transforming the iconic software maker into a $198-billion tech powerhouse with focus on mobile and cloud, said investment in both digital infrastructure and skilling is equally important in the current macroeconomic scenario.
