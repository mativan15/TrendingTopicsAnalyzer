CDC advisers' vote to put COVID shots on ‘vaccine schedule' ignites new fight in midterm campaigns
washingtontimes.com
news
2022-10-21 13:00:00

The recent federal endorsement of COVID-19 vaccines for children won't turn into school mandates overnight but the routine decision injected a fiery new debate into the late-October campaigns.

Everyone from Health and Human Services Secretary Xavier Becerra to Florida Gov. Ron DeSantis' administration agrees that a unanimous advisory-panel recommendation to add shots to the 2023 immunization schedule won't make the vaccine mandatory for children to attend school — unless a state decrees it so.

But GOP candidates, especially those trailing in the polls, are seizing on the vote as an issue of 'medical freedom.
