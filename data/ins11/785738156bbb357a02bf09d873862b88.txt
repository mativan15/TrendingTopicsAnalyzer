LA Local NewsBy City News ServiceMay 31, 2022Photo: Getty ImagesLOS ANGELES (CNS) - Paramedics Tuesday evaluated six workers at a legal cannabis operation in the South Los Angeles area who entered the business after it had been sprayed with a pesticide overnight, authorities said. The incident was reported in the 300 block of West Martin Luther King Jr. Boulevard at about 8 a.m., said Brian Humphrey of the Los Angeles Fire Department. "Six adult workers have been medically evaluated by LAFD after they experienced brief respiratory irritation upon entering a legal cannabis operation, which they were opening for daily business," Humphrey said.
