When a loved one dies, most people follow a general plan for informing others of the loss and honoring the death: They call friends and family members, host a funeral, commission an obituary for a local paper, and attempt to process the sadness and hard feelings.But after the coronavirus pandemic led thousands of people to confront these types of losses at once, Rehan Choudhry saw an opportunity for a way to remember those who died. Choudhry's wife, a news anchor for CBS, was working on making video memorials for each of the earliest victims of COVID-19 in New York City, and he was struck at how the families of the victims reacted to the videos.
