Crypto Scammer with 452K Followers Stole Photos, Advertised on Instagram
snopes.com
news
2022-05-26 18:37:53

Since July 2021, a cryptocurrency scammer with the fake name 'Melissa Jackson' (@melissa_jacks0n) was able to build up an Instagram following of 452,000 users. The account featured stolen photographs of an online marketing educator named Amy Porterfield. We reached out to Porterfield to alert her of the unauthorized usage of her pictures. Source: Instagram In addition to building up a large following (some of which may have been fake accounts) and stealing photographs to pretend to be someone named 'Melissa Jackson,' the crypto scammer was also allowed by Meta, Instagram's parent company, to pay to advertise on the platform.
