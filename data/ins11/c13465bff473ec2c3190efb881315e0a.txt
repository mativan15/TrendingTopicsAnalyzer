Nearly a quarter of Los Angeles County households experienced food insecurity over the last 12 months — up 7 percentage points since the end of 2021, according to a new study released by USC Dornsife's Public Exchange. Food prices nationally have gone up 9% in 2022, according to the U.S. Department of Agriculture. (Image source: iStock.) More than 800,000 Los Angeles County households — nearly a quarter of the total (24%) — experienced food insecurity over the 12 months ending July 2022, up from 17% in 2021.
