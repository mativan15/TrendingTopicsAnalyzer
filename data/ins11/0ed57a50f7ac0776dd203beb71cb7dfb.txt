Maryland lawmakers secure funds to cover cost of COVID-19 tests for public school students and staff

By CBS Baltimore Staff October 18, 2022 / 5:10 PM / CBS Baltimore BALTIMORE -- The Democratic members of Maryland's congressional delegation have banded together to secure $67 million in federal emergency funding for the state's Department of Health and Mental Hygiene, according to congressional staff.The money will be used to pay for more than 1.3 million in COVID-19 tests, which were administered to Maryland public school students and staff between July 20, 2021, and May 31, 2022, congressional staff said.
