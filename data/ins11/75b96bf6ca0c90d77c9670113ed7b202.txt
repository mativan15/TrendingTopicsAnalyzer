Regulator IRDAI Wednesday permitted insurers to introduce health and almost all general insurance products without its prior approval, a move aimed at ensuring a ‘a fully insured India'.The Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India (IRDAI) extended the ‘Use and File' procedure for all health insurance products and almost all general insurance products in line with the reforms agenda taken up towards ‘having a fully insured India', the regulator said in a statement."This is a stepping stone towards improving the ease of doing business in the insurance sector by moving from the current regime requiring prior approval for launching the products to a regime where products could be launched without any prior approval," it said.
