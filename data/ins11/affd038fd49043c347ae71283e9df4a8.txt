Published: 14:59 EDT, 21 October 2022 | Updated: 14:59 EDT, 21 October 2022 By Stephen Culp NEW YORK, Oct 21 (Reuters) - Wall Street surged and benchmark Treasury yields hit pause on Friday following reports that the Federal Reserve might consider less aggressive inflation-curbing tactics after November. All three major U.S. stock indexes gathered momentum as the session progressed, remaining on track to post gains from last Friday's close, capping in a week marked by a mixed bag of earnings, soft economic data and political turmoil in the UK.
