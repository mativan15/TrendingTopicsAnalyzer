Published: 14:03 EDT, 3 June 2022 | Updated: 14:03 EDT, 3 June 2022 By Ben Klayman and Joseph White June 3 (Reuters) - Tesla CEO Elon Musk's "super bad feeling" about the economy could be the auto industry's "canary in the coal mine" moment, signaling a recession for an industry whose bosses have shown no signs of concern. Musk said the electric carmaker needed to cut about 10% of its workforce in an email to executives seen by Reuters. He later told staff that white-collar ranks were bloated and he would keep hiring workers to make cars and batteries.
