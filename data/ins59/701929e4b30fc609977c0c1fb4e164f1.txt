Sensory Politics: In Literature, Relatability Matters
psychologytoday.com
news
2022-10-16 23:54:19

The day I found the word 'relatable' in a dictionary, I stopped telling my students it didn't exist. I apologized. For years, I had objected to this term that readers use to define literary characters more according to their own feelings than to identifiable artistic techniques. Those of us who teach and study literature at universities cling to the illusion that our analyses differ radically from those of book clubs, where readers discuss characters as though they were real people. Thirty-five years of teaching have convinced me that the difference is small.
