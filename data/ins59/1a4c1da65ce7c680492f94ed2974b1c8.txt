Labor elder statesman Kim Beazley's appointment to the council of the Australian War Memorial is being closely considered by the federal government, with a decision due after current member and outgoing chairman Brendan Nelson steps down in mid-November.Beazley is a former Labor leader, defence minister, governor of Western Australia and ambassador to the United States and is seen by many in the parliamentary Labor Party as the perfect person to take the council spot.Kim Beazley could replace Brendan Nelson on the council of the Australian War Memorial.
