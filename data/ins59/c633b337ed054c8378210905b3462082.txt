Liz Truss has insisted she is "completely committed" to keeping the pensions "triple lock" after Downing Street said only yesterday that the pledge was under review. Ian Blackford, the SNP's Westminster leader, asked the Prime Minister to "get permission to make another U-turn and commit to raising the state pension at the rate of inflation". She replied: "I honestly don't know what the honourable gentleman is talking about because we have been clear in our manifesto that we will maintain the triple lock and I am completely committed to it, so is the Chancellor.
