Michael Vi/iStock Editorial via Getty Images As one of China's earliest technology standouts, TCL Electronics Holdings Ltd. (OTCPK:TCLHF) (1070.HK) has always been ambitious. The company shot to global fame in 2003, when its parent, TCL Technology, struck a $560 million deal to take over the TV assets of France's Thomson SA, making it the world's largest TV maker. A year later it formed another joint venture by taking over the cellphone operations of France's Alcatel. Neither of those tie-ups was particularly smooth, with the Thomson deal unraveling in three years, and the Alcatel deal in just one.
