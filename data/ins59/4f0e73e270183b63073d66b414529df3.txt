When an aging couple operating a struggling Moroccan dress shop hire a dashing young apprentice, some of the first words out of his mouth are 'I work fast.' That also describes the approach of 'The Blue Caftan' director Maryam Touzani, who sets up its straightforward premise so quickly that you'd be forgiven for thinking you had the entire film figured out within five minutes. A closeted gay tailor, who fights with his wife about money, begins mentoring a young man who's more beautiful than any item in his shop.
