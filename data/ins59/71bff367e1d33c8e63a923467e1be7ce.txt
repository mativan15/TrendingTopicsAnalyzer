New York is home not only to landmarks to private-sector excess like the supertall skyscrapers on Billionaire's Row, but to obscure landmarks to public-sector excess like a $4.7 million bathroom at Ferry Point Park in the Bronx. Whether government is fixing sewers, building libraries or schools or jails, or even just installing playground equipment, it all takes way too long and costs way too much.

It's not that mayors haven't tried to speed things up — pols love ribbon-cuttings — but that entrenched systems and the people who benefit from inefficiency undermine the best-intentioned overhauls.
