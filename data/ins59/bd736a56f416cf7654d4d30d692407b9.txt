Media caption, FM Nicola Sturgeon said Scotland would continue to use the pound sterling while the Scottish pound is introduced in a "careful and responsible phased approach".An independent Scotland would keep the pound and move to its own currency when the "time is right".Nicola Sturgeon outlined the view at a briefing giving updated arguments for what could happen if the country voted for independence.The first minister said a timetable for creating a Scottish currency would not be set, however, use of sterling would be as "short as practicable".
