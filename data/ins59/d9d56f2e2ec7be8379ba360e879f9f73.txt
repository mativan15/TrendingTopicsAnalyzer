Scott Galloway wants to be the most influential thought leader in the history of business. When asked why, he offered his usual self-criticism: mostly narcissism, a desire to be relevant, fear of death, a drive for economic security. 'Mostly selfish reasons,' he summarized. Upon reflecting further, however, he noted, 'I also feel as if I have something to say about technology and monopoly power and unchecked corporate interests. They've always existed, but I think it has gotten worse.
