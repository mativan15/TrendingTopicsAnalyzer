After many months of global dominance by the Omicron subvariant BA.5, an alphabet soup of newer Omicron strains is now rising in the U.S. and abroad as SARS-CoV-2 continues to rapidly evolve. Though BA.5 still accounts for more than 60 percent of new U.S. COVID cases, that percentage has been steadily dropping for weeks. But unlike past progressions of variant competition and dominance in the country and world, there isn't one single variant that has been supplanting BA.
