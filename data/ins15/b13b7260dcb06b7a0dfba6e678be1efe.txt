Dirty Dick's

LOCATED JUST A STONE'S THROW from Liverpool station, this watering hole has an unusual moniker with a famous literary connection. When William Barker ran the establishment during the 19th century, the place was called The Old Jerusalem. One day, Barker decided to rename the establishment after a local character with a reputation for his filthy hygiene. 
The disheveled gentleman in question was Richard Bentley—or Nathaniel, no one seems to be quite sure. Although originally from a respectable family, Bentley suffered an emotional breakdown after his fiancee died on their wedding night.
