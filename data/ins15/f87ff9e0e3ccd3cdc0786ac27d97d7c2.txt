As Croatia is sailing through euro adoption and is poised to join the currency area on Jan. 1, political bickering in another European Union member is complicating its path.

Bulgaria's government approved a plan this week to take on the euro in 2024, with Prime Minister Kiril Petkov arguing that it will help the EU's poorest member to lure investment, boost the economy and improve living standards in the bloc's poorest member.
