Evan McMullin, who is running for Senate in Utah to replace Mike Lee, speaks at a rally in Salt Lake City on November 10, 2021. LAURA SEITZ / AP Utah has never seen anything like it: a contested Senate race. Independent candidate Evan McMullin, a former Republican, is neck and neck with incumbent Mike Lee, one of Donald Trump's lackeys, in the November 8 midterm elections. In this deeply conservative state – more than 65% Mormon but which never fully embraced the 45th president – Mr. McMullin's performance has revived the hopes of the "Never Trumpers," the lifelong anti-Trump Republicans, to form a coalition to "save American democracy from authoritarianism," in the candidate's words.
