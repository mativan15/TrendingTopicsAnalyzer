While President Xi Jinping is already China's most powerful leader since Mao Zedong, the career path of a key ally will show how much the Communist Party is becoming a one-man show.

Li Qiang, the party chief of Shanghai, sealed off the financial hub for two months earlier this year following one of the biggest outbreaks to breach Xi's strict Covid Zero policy. The city saw food shortages and clashes between citizens and health workers, which generated an unusual outpouring of anti-government posts on social media.
