Syrian authorities have found a mass grave in the historic town of Palmyra with bodies of victims of the militant Islamic State group, which controlled the area years ago. The mass grave was discovered near the second-century Roman amphitheatre, according to SANAThe remains of 12 people buried there have been taken to hospital morgues for identificationPalmyra is a UNESCO world heritage site and once linked Persia, India and China with the Roman empireSyria's state news agency SANA said the mass grave was discovered near the second-century Roman amphitheatre.
