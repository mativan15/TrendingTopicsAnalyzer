By Australian Associated Press Published: 22:40 EDT, 18 October 2022 | Updated: 22:40 EDT, 18 October 2022 Julian Assange's lawyer warns the WikiLeaks founder will die behind bars unless an urgent political fix is secured. Held in the UK's high-security Belmarsh prison for more than three years, the Australian journalist is battling an array of health conditions including a mini stroke, severe depression and suicidal ideations, while he recently caught COVID-19. He's waiting on a UK High Court appeal against his extradition to the US, where he faces espionage charges.
