Usually, midterm elections that are just three weeks out are relatively high up on investors' list of concerns as uncertainty swirls around future regulation and fiscal policy.And usually, the political clarity that has come after the elections are over have boosted stocks in a significant way. In the 12 months following midterm elections, the S&P 500 has historically risen 17%. Goldman Sachs But things are different this year, according to Goldman Sachs. Factors like inflation, tightening monetary policy and the risk it poses to the economy, and geopolitics are dominating the minds of investors — so the outcome of November's election likely won't impact the broader market in the same way it has in the past, the investment bank said in a note to clients last week.
