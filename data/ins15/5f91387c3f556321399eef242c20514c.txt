Aluminium has for years been characterised by chronic oversupply thanks to China's relentless build-out of primary smelting capacity.LONDON - These are turbulent times for the global aluminium market.Aluminium has for years been characterised by chronic oversupply thanks to China's relentless build-out of primary smelting capacity.Now, however, buyers in Europe and the United States are paying up record high premiums to get hold of physical metal.The Chinese aluminium juggernaut has run out of momentum and smelters in Europe are powering down as a rolling energy crunch takes a rising toll on the region's producers.
