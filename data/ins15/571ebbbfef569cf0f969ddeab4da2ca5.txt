By Reuters Published: 21:24 EDT, 19 October 2022 | Updated: 21:24 EDT, 19 October 2022 Cleveland point guard Darius Garland exited the Cavaliers' season opener against the host Toronto Raptors on Wednesday due to a left eye injury. Garland appeared to be poked in the eye before exiting with 3:51 to play in the first half. The team's medical personnel were checking on him as he left, and the Cavaliers ruled him out for the remainder of the game. In 13 minutes before leaving, Garland scored four points on 2-of-8 shooting.
