﻿Harvard Business Review published its first issue 100 years ago with a mission to help leaders put the world's best management thinking into practice. To mark our centennial, we asked eight current and former CEOs from some of the world's top companies to describe the ideas that have propelled their own careers and organizations. Two themes emerged: first, the need for constant innovation and out-of-the-box thinking as markets become more dynamic; second, the importance of purpose and a vision that encompasses all stakeholders.
