What drew a California investor to a $1M deal on Henry Johnson Boulevard

2022-10-16 23:00:00

An eight-unit apartment building with a ground-floor laundromat in Albany's West Hill neighborhood sold twice in four months for more than $1 million.

The initial purchase in May — and the quick resale in September — illustrates how a rental property in a low-income neighborhood can attract strong interest from some commercial real estate investors.

As with any business deal, it all came down to the numbers.

In this case, the financials made sense to 54-year-old Ross Boon of San Francisco, who started buying rental properties a decade ago to supplement his income at a tech job.
