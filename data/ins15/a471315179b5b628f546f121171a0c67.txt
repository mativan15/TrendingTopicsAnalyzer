Allan Akins/iStock via Getty Images The short-term equity risk premium is back to levels not seen since before the Financial Crisis and cash is piling into bonds, BofA Securities says. The U.S. 2-year Treasury yield is now 280 basis points higher than the S&P 500 dividend yield (SDY), the widest spread since 2007 and investors are "flooding to short-duration bonds (NASDAQ:SHY)," strategist Michael Hartnett wrote in the weekly Flow Show note. The risk to equities is that this continues via rotation from stocks (NYSEARCA:SPY) (QQQ) (DIA) (IWM) (IWB)," Hartnett said.
