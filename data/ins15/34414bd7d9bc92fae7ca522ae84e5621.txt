European VCs Urge Tech Start-Ups to Slash Costs and Extend the Runway
nbcsandiego.com
business
2022-06-01 11:00:00

Following a bumper 2021 that was full of IPOs and mega funding rounds, some of the most valuable start-ups in Europe are now laying off significant numbers of staff and drastically scaling back their expansion plans. Swedish fintech giant Klarna, which became Europe's most valuable start-up last June when it was valued at $46 billion, announced last week that it is planning to lay off about 10% of its global workforce. Elsewhere in Europe, the rapid grocery delivery boom is coming to a grinding halt.
