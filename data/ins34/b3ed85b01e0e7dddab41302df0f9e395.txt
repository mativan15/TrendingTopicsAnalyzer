Dollar Tree, Dollar General Stocks Up As Inflation Pushes More Consumers to Stores

Both Dollar Tree and Dollar General saw their stock prices pop up following the companies' positive financial guidance, as four-decade high inflation pushes consumers to their stores.

On May 26, Dollar General shares rose 13.71% to close at $222.13, while Dollar Tree shares jumped 21.87% to close at $162.80, CNBC reported.

'We are in the midst of a very challenging time for consumers, as many are living paycheck to paycheck,' Rick Dreiling, Dollar Tree executive chairman said in an investor call on May 26, according to a transcript of the call.
