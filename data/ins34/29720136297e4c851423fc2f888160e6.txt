A New South Wales MP is urging the state government to create a personal shark deterrent device rebate scheme and remove shark nets from the state's beaches.The most recent report on the NSW's shark meshing program found a total of 376 marine animals caught in nets, but only 51 were target sharksHeathcote MP Lee Evans says the government should follow WA's lead, which offers a $200 rebate for shark personal protection devicesPrimary Industries Minister, Dugald Saunders says he wants the councils to survey beachgoers about removing the shark netsHeathcote MP Lee Evans has used a notice of motion in parliament to call for the adoption of a model similar to the $200 rebate offered in Western Australia.
