It wasn't quite 10 p.m. on Broadway a few Thursdays ago, but as throngs waited to cross the street, it was impossible to tell the weekend hadn't yet arrived.

Nashville's multimillion dollar tourism industry was built on the image of Music City, supported by Lower Broadway and its famed honky-tonks.

Recently, a growing coalition of downtown residents, business leaders and stakeholders have expressed concern for Lower Broadway's changing atmosphere as comparisons to the Las Vegas strip mount.
