Liudmila Chernetska Retail, particularly retail that deals with fashion, can be a rather tricky business. In addition to being highly competitive, margins are small, and volatility can be commonplace even during milder economic periods. At the same time, however, a retailer that has excess cash and that generates positive cash flow can only trade so low before it becomes an attractive prospect. A great example of this can be seen by looking at The Cato Corporation (NYSE:CATO), a fashion retailer with a variety of brand names across its 1,312 locations.
