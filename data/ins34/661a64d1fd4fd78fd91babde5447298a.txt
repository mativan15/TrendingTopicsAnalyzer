This Diwali, traditional sweet makers expect brisk business
indiatimes.com
news
2022-10-18 06:15:00

Prayagraj : Rising demand of traditional Diwali sweets like gatta (sweet cubes), batasha (sugar candy sweet) and khilone (sugar toys) after a gap of two years have bought smiles back on faces of traders ahead of the festival of lights.Makers claimed that the demand had seen a dip of up to 50% in Prayagraj and adjoining markets in past two years due to the pandemic.Traders at Mutthiganj said demand of such sweets have gone up in the region despite rising prices of sugar, coal and other raw materials.
