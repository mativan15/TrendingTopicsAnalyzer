Published: 15:06 EDT, 17 October 2022 | Updated: 15:10 EDT, 17 October 2022 Fishermen across the Pacific northwest face financial ruin after the Alaska Department of Fish and Game canceled the state's lucrative snow crab fishing season in the Bering Sea. The news will also have a huge bearing on the restaurant industry as price increases and shortages become inevitable. Alaska provides 60 percent of the seafood consumed in the United States.The decision came after an ADF&G study found that billions of snow crabs had disappeared from the region.
