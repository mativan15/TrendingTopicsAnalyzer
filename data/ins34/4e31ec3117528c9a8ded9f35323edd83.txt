Denver's Ping Identity stops trading on stock exchange as it wraps up sale to private equity firm
bizjournals.com
business
2022-10-18 20:42:00

Ping Identity (NYSE: PING), a cloud-based cybersecurity company based in Denver, announced Tuesday that its sale to the private equity giant Thoma Bravo is complete.

Thoma Bravo acquired Ping through an all-cash transaction for about $2.8 billion. The transaction was approved Monday by Ping's stockholders. At a special meeting, more than 68 million stockholders voted in favor of the deal, while about 16,000 disapproved of it.

With the sale, Ping Identity will become a privately held company. Ping's stock had ceased trading as of Tuesday and will be delisted from the New York Stock Exchange.
