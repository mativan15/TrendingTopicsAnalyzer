Nigeria's Tinubu: Will remove fuel subsidy, deregulate gas prices if elected
reuters.com
news
2022-10-21 18:07:16

LAGOS, Oct 21 (Reuters) - Nigeria's presidential frontrunner Bola Tinubu on Friday promised to remove a fuel subsidy if elected next February, adding that his government would ramp up oil production and deregulate midstream gas prices within six months.Nigerians will elect a successor to President Muhammadu Buhari, who steps down next year after serving the two presidential terms mandated by the constitution.The 70-year-old Tinubu, from Buhari's ruling All Progressives Congress, is a former state governor and senator of Lagos, the country's commercial capital.
