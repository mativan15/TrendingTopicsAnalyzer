This is the daily notebook of Mike Santoli, CNBC's senior markets commentator, with ideas about trends, stocks and market statistics. With one eye on the Treasury market and another on corporate results, the S & P 500 is sticky around the 3,700 level for a third straight day. Bond yields are up small, as mixed economic data and stretched technical conditions invite predictions of a potential near-term top, with some familiarly hawkish Fed speak as a counterweight. Philadelphia Federal Reserve President Patrick Harker helped undercut a morning rally with a message – repeated endlessly for weeks by Fed folks – that it's too early to slow or stop tightening.
