By Reuters Published: 02:03 EDT, 1 June 2022 | Updated: 04:33 EDT, 1 June 2022 June 1 (Reuters) - TomTom will slash hundreds of jobs due to improvements it has made in automating its mapmaking activities, the Dutch digital navigation company said on Wednesday, adding that the cuts would amount to around a tenth of its global workforce. "Regrettably, this will have an intended impact on approximately 500 employees in our Maps unit," the company said in a statement. "The full assessment of the financial implications of the reset of the Maps unit is ongoing.
