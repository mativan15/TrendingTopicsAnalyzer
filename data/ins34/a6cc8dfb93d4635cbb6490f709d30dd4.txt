Here are three ways Heineken is investing in Africa

2022-05-31 07:00:00

Global players are seeing potential on the continent as it rebounds from the pandemic. Google, which opened its first artificial intelligence centre in Ghana in 2019, has pledged to invest at least $1 billion over the next few years. Microsoft is also investing in Africa with the Microsoft Africa Development Center (ADC) and the Microsoft Africa Research Institute (MARI) in Nairobi, Kenya. The ADC is planning to invest $100 million in new tech within the first five years, while MARI uses AI cloud tech to improve productivity.
