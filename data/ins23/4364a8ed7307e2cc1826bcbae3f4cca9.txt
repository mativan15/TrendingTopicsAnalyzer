'Return to the office,' they say. 'Our company culture depends on it.' I'm pretty sure it's all a lie. The likelihood of us ever returning to full-time, in-office work as it was pre-pandemic feels increasingly slim. And even if by some (other) divine act we do, the culture that's so oft heralded, was never really there to begin with. For years, long before COVID had us hole up at home, the tech startup influence on the workplace made us believe the epitome of so-called office culture was a ping-pong table, beer on tap, free catered lunches, and the not-your-dad's-office-party office party.
