Steve Maharey is a director. He was formally an academic, politician – a Labour party cabinet minister – and vice-chancellor. OPINION: The consensus seems to be that the local government elections told the story of an appetite for change. Maybe. The majority did not vote. And of those who did vote, a large proportion knew little or nothing about the people they voted for. Such is the nature of local government elections where the golden rule is that name recognition is the key to getting elected.
