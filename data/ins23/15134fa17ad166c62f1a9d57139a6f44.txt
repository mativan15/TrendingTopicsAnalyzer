Meta Platforms (FB 1.83%) surely hasn't had the ideal start to 2022. In early February, the social media giant delivered a weak fourth-quarter 2021 earnings report after experiencing its first-ever decline in daily active users on the Facebook platform. All around, growth to wrap up 2021 was patchy -- Apple's iOS privacy update, coupled with the company's transition to short-form video (Reels), continued to place pressure on its top line.
To add fuel to the fire, Snap warned investors earlier this week that the macroeconomic environment has worsened more than anticipated.
