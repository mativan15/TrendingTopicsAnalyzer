Published: 09:07 EDT, 18 October 2022 | Updated: 09:07 EDT, 18 October 2022 Liz Truss looks to be on the verge of yet another U-turn after Downing Street refused to stand by her vow to keep the triple lock on pensions.The PM's spokesman pointedly insisted the government is 'not making any commitments' when asked about the key policy.The dodging will alarm pensioners who rely on the long-standing promise to increase payments by the highest out of inflation, wages, or 2.5 per cent.It comes as new Chancellor Jeremy Hunt warns that no departments will be exempt from an 'eye-watering' £40billion squeeze expected in his Halloween Budget.
