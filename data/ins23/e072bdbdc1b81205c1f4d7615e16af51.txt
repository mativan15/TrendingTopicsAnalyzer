The idea that public land on the harbour can benefit the public is generally ridiculed in Sydney by those in the know. Lip service may be paid by politicians, and there may be a token garden thrown in. But those scrapings from the developer's luncheon table are dwarfed by obscene towers that destroy the beauty of the foreshore that at one stage belonged to all citizens of this state. During the Depression the maritime foreshore of Barangaroo was known as The Hungry Mile. It's still an appropriate epithet.
