Indian Health Ministry Questions Global Hunger Index Methodology; Calls Its Data Biased, Unethical and Lacking Validity

(SL Shanth Kumar/TOI, BCCL/Mumbai )The Global Hunger Index (GHI) is an erroneous measure of hunger and suffers from serious methodological issues, the Union Health Ministry sources said on Tuesday, after India was ranked 107 out of 121 countries in this year's rankings.Three out of four indicators used for calculation are related to the health of children and cannot be representative of the entire population, the sources said. The fourth indicator, the 'Estimate of Proportion of Undernourished Population (PoU)' for India, is 16.
