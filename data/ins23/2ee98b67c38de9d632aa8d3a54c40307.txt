Published: 06:31 EDT, 18 October 2022 | Updated: 06:31 EDT, 18 October 2022 Britain is set to default on the welfare state in 20 years' time if it continues to spend beyond its means, shocking OBR and Government research finds.The National Insurance Fund, which is financed by taxpayers and pays for benefits and pensions, will run dry by 2043 without a deluge of extra funding by the Treasury.Strains on Britain's coffers will be exacerbated by a declining birth rate and ageing population, inflationary shocks, geopolitical tensions and the triple-lock state pension.
