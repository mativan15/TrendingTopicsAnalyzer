An iconic Berkeley street could eliminate cars forever
sfgate.com
news
2022-06-02 11:00:00

In late 2021, UC Berkeley students Brandon Yung and Sam Greenberg approached the Berkeley Transportation Commission with a bold proposal.The urban studies majors wanted to remove all private vehicles from the northern portion of Telegraph Avenue that dead-ends into the college campus. They envisioned a walkable community space that also honored the avenue's history of local activism. The iconic four blocks, between Bancroft and Dwight ways, have been ground zero for free speech and anti-war protests.
