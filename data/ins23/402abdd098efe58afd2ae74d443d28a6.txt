Power-hungry data centres find their perfect home in Iceland
thetimes.co.uk
news
2022-10-15 23:01:00

The former Keflavik US naval air base on the southwestern tip of Iceland is no longer home to military planes and soldiers. Today, it hosts warehouses storing giant servers, stacked high, which expel hot air — making the cages around them warm to the touch.The rows and rows of machines store vast amounts of data for big tech companies and large corporations, serving billions of people. This is the heart of the internet — except it doesn't beat, it whirs.Dominic Ward, chief executive of the operator Verne Global, has granted The Sunday Times rare access to the former Nato site, where security is so tight it still feels like an active military base.
