1. Growth, feed efficiency and feed intake of offspring
from selected and wild Japanese flounder
(Paralichthys olivaceus)
Hiroshi Y. Ogataa,*, Hiromi Okua
, Takeshi Muraib
a
Fish Nutrition Division, National Research Institute of Aquaculture, Nansei, Mie 516-0193, Japan
b
National Fisheries University, Honmachi, Shimonoseki 759-6595, Japan
Received 26 June 2001; received in revised form 4 September 2001; accepted 4 September 2001
Abstract
Feeding and starvation trials were conducted to investigate the nutritional trait differences
between the offspring from broodstock under selective improvement for growth for two generations
and wild-caught broodstock of the Japanese flounder.
