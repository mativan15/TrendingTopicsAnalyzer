Published: 09:22 EDT, 4 June 2022 | Updated: 09:22 EDT, 4 June 2022 An ex-KGB agent accused of poisoning Putin critic Alexander Litvinenko in London has died of Covid.Dmitry Kovtun, 56, was alleged by Britain to have laced the former spy and British citizen's green tea with polonium in 2006.Kovtun and associate Andrei Lugovoy had met Mr Litvinenko earlier on November 1, 2006, the day he fell ill, at Mayfair's Millennium Hotel.Traces of radioactive substance polonium-210 were found on the aeroplane seat Kovtun took home from London to Hamburg.
