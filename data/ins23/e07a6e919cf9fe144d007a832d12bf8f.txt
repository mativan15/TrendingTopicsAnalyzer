Thanks to a little-known loophole, lower federal courts have regularly written the government a blank check to search homes and seize firearms from lawful gun owners without a warrant. One of those owners, Wayne Torcivia, is now calling on the U.S. Supreme Court to close that loophole once and for all. Torcivia's case dates back to April 6, 2014, when three officers from Suffolk County, New York showed up at his house in Ronkonkoma right after midnight. They were responding to what they were told was a 'a violent, domestic dispute of a 17-year-old female and an intoxicated father.
