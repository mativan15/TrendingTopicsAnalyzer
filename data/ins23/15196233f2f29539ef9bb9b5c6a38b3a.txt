By Reuters Published: 12:27 EDT, 27 May 2022 | Updated: 12:27 EDT, 27 May 2022 JOHANNESBURG, May 27 (Reuters) - South African prosecutors said on Friday that several former executives at state-owned ports and freight rail company Transnet had been arrested for alleged corruption during former president Jacob Zuma's time in power. The National Prosecuting Authority's Investigating Directorate (ID) and police arrested the former executives on Friday morning, after which they appeared in court, the ID said in statements.
