Since the pandemic, thousands of executives and remote working professionals have migrated to South Florida from other parts of the nation. That influx of wealth and humanity sparked another migration: out-of-state companies and firms looking to establish a presence in South Florida. Usually, such companies were content with opening a branch office and facility while keeping their headquarters in other major cities such as New York, Chicago, or Los Angeles. But more companies now are opting to relocate their headquarters to Miami-Dade, Broward, or Palm Beach counties.
