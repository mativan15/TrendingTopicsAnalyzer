By Reuters Published: 04:34 EDT, 18 October 2022 | Updated: 04:34 EDT, 18 October 2022 SEOUL, Oct 18 (Reuters) - South Korea's finance ministry said on Tuesday the government plans to introduce 30-year treasury bond futures for trading in the local bond market in the first quarter of 2024. The move is aimed at meeting "huge demand" for longer-term bond futures from financial institutions seeking to effectively manage interest rate risks, the ministry said in a statement after meeting with various stakeholders.
