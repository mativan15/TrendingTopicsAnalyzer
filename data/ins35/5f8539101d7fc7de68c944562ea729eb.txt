Stock prices have fallen sharply on recession fears this year, causing the Nasdaq Composite to slip deep into a bear market. In fact, the tech-heavy index has fallen nearly 36% from its November peak, marking its largest decline in the last decade.
Many individual stocks are in the same boat. Nvidia (NVDA -6.13%) and Microsoft (MSFT -2.42%) have seen their share prices fall 66% and 33%, respectively. Neither stock has suffered a worse loss at any point in the past 10 years. But both companies are still backed by a solid investment thesis, making this a once-in-a-decade buying opportunity.
