Chancay, Peru; Calama, Chile; and Chaco, Argentina To a large extent, China's rise to economic dominance and deepening political influence in Latin America has been a quiet affair.Even the United States – first preoccupied with wars in the Middle East and then turning inward to the tune of 'America First' – seemed to hardly notice over the past two decades as its chief economic competitor and geopolitical rival dethroned Uncle Sam as top trading partner and go-to investor in much of his own backyard.
