Investor hopes for either a rate 'pause' or 25-basis-point hike in September are starting to slip away
marketwatch.com
news
2022-05-31 16:12:00

Financial-market participants are focusing their attention on where Federal Reserve policy makers are most likely to take their main policy rate in September, with hopes slipping that they could back off of half-point increases after delivering similar-size hikes in June and July.Remarks by Fed Gov. Christopher Waller, who said on Monday that he supports hiking target rates by half-percentage-point increments for several meetings until he sees signs of inflation coming down, set the tone for markets on Tuesday following the three-day Memorial Day weekend.
