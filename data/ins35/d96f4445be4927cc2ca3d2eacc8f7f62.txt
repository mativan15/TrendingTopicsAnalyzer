Millions on Universal Credit and benefits set to lose up to £716 a year
thescottishsun.co.uk
news
2022-10-19 09:20:52

THOUSANDS of low-income workers could lose up to £716 a year if benefits don't rise in line with inflation.
Inflation rose by 10.1% in September, thanks to soaring food and energy prices, according to the Office for National Statistics.
Hairdressers will lose out the most in benefits are uprated with wage growth figures
Inflation is what goods and services are worth in a country.
And for many years benefits have been raised each April in line with September's inflation figures.
But the government has failed to commit to uprating benefit payments rising in line with inflation.
