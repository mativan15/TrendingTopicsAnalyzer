United States WNBA star Brittney Griner, who has been locked up in Russia since February, turned 32 Tuesday, an occasion that has led her family and online supporters to again raise calls for her to be returned home to the United States.Griner's wife, Cherelle Griner, has been part of the launch of a #WeAreBG campaign. She shared a video message on her personal Instagram account about the initiative on Monday, the eight-month anniversary of when the Phoenix Mercury player was taken into custody at a Moscow airport and charged with possession of vape canisters containing cannabis oil.
