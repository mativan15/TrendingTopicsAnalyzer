Why patenting your million-dollar idea might not be worth your time and money

A patent doesn't automatically guarantee you protection from predatory competitors.

Lede: If you're an ambitious entrepreneur with plans for a transformative product bouncing excitedly around your head, you might think getting it patented is a must to shield your intellectual property (IP) from any similar-minded (and dastardly sticky-fingered) competitors.

That's not necessarily the case.

Securing a patent for a piece of IP is an arduous and often expensive process that some young ideators — depending on the industry they work in — may not need to undertake.
