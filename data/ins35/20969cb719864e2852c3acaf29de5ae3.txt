Published: 17:16 EDT, 1 June 2022 | Updated: 17:32 EDT, 1 June 2022 Not so long ago, walking through womenswear at Marks & Spencer felt less like shopping and more like being on The Crystal Maze. Crammed with stock of every style and colour, jumbled onto rail after rail, you almost needed a machete and compass to navigate your way. If I'd had to sum up the M&S approach to fashion for the past two decades, I'd have said ‘confused' (if I was being kind) and ‘utterly shambolic' (if not). As a clothes addict and former editor of Glamour and The Mail on Sunday's You Magazine, fashion matters to me.
