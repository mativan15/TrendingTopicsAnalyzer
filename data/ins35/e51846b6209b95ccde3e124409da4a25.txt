India should reduce tariffs on alcoholic beverages in high growth areas: ICRIER
indiatimes.com
economics
2022-06-03 04:45:00

ThinkStock PhotosIn FY20, India exported wine worth $7.2 million and imported wine worth $19.55 million.NEW DELHI: Economic policy think-tank ICRIER has suggested that India should reduce custom duty on alcoholic beverages and offer phased tariff liberalisation in high growth areas like wine to gain market access for its exporters. Presently, among alcoholic beverages, tariffs are the highest for wine, with the price of imported wine ranging between 200 and 400 per cent of the global average price.
