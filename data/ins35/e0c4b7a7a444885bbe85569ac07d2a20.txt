Published: 09:03 EDT, 29 May 2022 | Updated: 10:29 EDT, 29 May 2022 PITTSBURGH (AP) - The victory party took on the feel of an evangelical worship service after Doug Mastriano won Pennsylvania's Republican gubernatorial primary this month. As a Christian singer led the crowd in song, some raised their arms toward the heavens in praise. Mastriano opened his remarks by evoking Scripture: "God uses the foolish to confound the wise." He claimed Pennsylvanians´ freedom would be "snatched away" if his Democratic opponent wins in November, and cast the election in starkly religious terms with another biblical reference: "Let´s choose this day to serve the Lord.
