In the age of Twitter, where audiences' reactions on the platform on the day of a movie release is now being construed as the yardstick for word-of-mouth, it is becoming increasingly tough for filmmakers to engage the audience with their work. It has become tougher in the case of thrillers having a delicious twist at the end. PS Mithran believes that the concept of ‘twist' has become obsolete. He drops truth bombs by saying that today's audiences are only concerned about two things: whether the film is engaging or whether it is giving them a new experience.
