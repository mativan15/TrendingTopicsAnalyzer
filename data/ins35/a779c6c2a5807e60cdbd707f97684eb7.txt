Cisco CEO Chuck Robbins. Mint/Getty Images Cisco had a good year amid the market downturn, according to CEO Chuck Robbins. But it encountered some bumps along the way. After three quarters of steady earnings, the $157 billion hardware giant ended the 2022 fiscal year with a Q4 revenue of $13.1 billion. The firm saw record highs of annual product orders and backlog in more than a decade, according to its latest earnings report.Disruptions initially led to lower earnings in previous quarters. Yet, overall sales for networking hardware has been high as customers put in orders early to avoid delays caused by unpredictable supply chain disruptions, according to Cisco.
