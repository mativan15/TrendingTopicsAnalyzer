Paris Police Ready For Living Costs Protest As Fuel Strike Drags On
ibtimes.com
science
2022-10-16 04:15:38

The dispute has forced many filling stations to close and had a knock-on effect across all sectors of the economy
Nearly three weeks into a strike that has forced filling stations across France to close, police in Paris were preparing for protests Sunday against soaring living costs.Left-wing opponents of President Emmanuel Macron's administration have organised the demonstration, which they say is also in protest against government inaction over climate change.Jean-Luc Melenchon, leader of the left-wing France Unbowed (LFI) party, had planned the march well before the current strike, but organisers are hoping to pick up some of the momentum from the current industrial unrest.
