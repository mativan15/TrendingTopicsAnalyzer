SINCE the UK is a democracy, that means that the public or members of a party vote and elect their Prime Minister, and then he or she is appointed by the King.
The monarch has an important role to play in the country's ruling, so what powers over Parliament does the King hold?
The King and future monarchs have power over future dissolvements of ParliamentCredit: Getty Historically, the monarch is able to dissolve Parliament and does so at the end of each term. 
This usually happens every five years but can be brought forward by royal proclamation.
