Published: 19:00 EDT, 3 June 2022 | Updated: 19:08 EDT, 3 June 2022 Cancer patients should be prescribed exercise classes on the NHS to boost their chances of survival, according to experts.Research shows staying active significantly reduces the risk of complications and helps patients tolerate treatments including chemotherapy, radiotherapy and surgery.Cancer patients who do aerobic exercise such as walking, running, swimming or gardening report a better quality of life, less fatigue and reduced anxiety and depression, the study suggests.
