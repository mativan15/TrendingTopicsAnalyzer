Unlicensed miners work in a tin producing area in Toboali, on the southern shores of the island of Bangka, Indonesia, in April 2021.   © Reuters October 19, 2022 16:46 JST | IndonesiaJAKARTA (Reuters) -- Indonesian authorities are preparing data and assessing current industry conditions to be ready in the event that the government decides to bring in a ban on tin exports, a senior mining ministry official said on Wednesday.The Southeast Asian country, which is the world's top tin exporter, has already moved to halt shipments of a number of other metals in order to develop more processing at home.
