Never ignore new Apple alert on your iPhone

YOUR iPhone has a handy feature designed to protect your privacy and protect you from spam attacks.
The feature is called Sign In with Apple – and you may have already used it.
Look out for this pop-up on iPhones, iPads or MacsCredit: Apple
Apps will only see the randomly-generated email that Apple has created – and not your real addressCredit: Apple
It's a way to sign in to websites with your Apple ID and, importantly, lets you hide your email.
The tool prompts you to give it a try with a pop-up that you'll see from time to time when browsing the web.
