It's on from this moment - an electrifying attraction - but she is now a suspect. For a man who's so proper, this woman is a challenge. Seo-rae is a femme fatale, but not in the old Hollywood style. She is reserved, wry, observant, rather than knowing. She falls hard for him - or is she just leading him away from her guilt?That's a very old idea in film noir - a cliche in fact - that might be held against the film's sexual politics, except that there is never a sense of inequality between them. They look at each other with the same hunger, despite their compromised positions.
