The enduring threat of Jair Bolsonaro

Brazil's president, Jair Bolsonaro, has had a big impact on the country's politics and society. In four years in office, he's won a cult-like devotion from his followers, undermining democracy and aggravating division in the process. Whether or not he wins this month's run-off in the presidential election, Bolsonarismo—the right-wing populist movement he's inspired—is set to endure. What does this mean for the future of Brazil?
