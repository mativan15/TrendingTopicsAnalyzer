* Indonesian 10-yr yield at 3-month high * Most equities advance; Singapore shares tick lower * Bank Indonesia seen hiking key rate by 50 bps - Reuters poll By Jaskiran Singh Oct 18 (Reuters) - Asian currencies strengthened against a subdued dollar and equities advanced on Tuesday as risk appetite got a boost from an upbeat start of corporate earning season in the United States and a reversal in the United Kingdom's financial policy. The U.S. dollar too took a breather from its recent rally with the index sagging to near a 1-1/2-week low, boosting regional currencies.
