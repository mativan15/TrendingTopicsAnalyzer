Charity calls for government funding as NSW South Coast homeless shelter shuts its doors
abc.net.au
news
2022-10-17 07:57:23

Last night, 12 homeless people in Nowra on the NSW South Coast had a warm bed and a meal at Safe Shelter Shoalhaven, but tonight they will be on the streets, with the shelter to close immediately. Safe Shelter Shoalhaven housed between 12 and 16 people every night in NowraIt was run by local charity Salt Care and funded by the communityOrganisers say they want to reopen the shelter, but will not be able to do so before a $250,000 target is reachedPeter Dover, the chief executive of Salt Care, a local church-led charity that has run the shelter for the past five years, said funding for the service had run dry.
