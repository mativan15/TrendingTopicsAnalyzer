By Reuters Published: 11:13 EDT, 31 May 2022 | Updated: 13:23 EDT, 31 May 2022 LONDON, May 31 (Reuters) - Two company directors behind a fraudulent green investment scheme in Brazil were convicted on Tuesday by a London jury of defrauding about 2,000 investors out of around 37 million pounds ($47 million). Andrew Skeene and Omari Bowers were found guilty of three counts of conspiracy to defraud and one count of misconduct in the course of winding up a company at London's Southwark Crown Court, the UK Serious Fraud Office (SFO) said.
