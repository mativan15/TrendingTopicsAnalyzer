The CEO of the notorious spyware vendor NSO Group apparently has a new plan to rebound from the company's ongoing legal and fiscal tailspin: start re-selling its noxious spyware to the very governments that got it into trouble in the first place. Beset by ongoing lawsuits, declining sales, scurrying investors, and unendingly negative press coverage, NSO Group has entered a financial spiral and seems so strapped that it's struggling to pay its own employees. The pivot by CEO Shalev Hulio entails selling its products to countries that have been deemed 'elevated-risk' clients, reports the Financial Times.
