ReutersFILE PHOTO: A sign of Foxconn is seen inside its office building in Taipei, Taiwan November 12, 2020. REUTERS/Ann WangTaiwanese tech giant Foxconn , best known for assembling Apple's iPhone, said on Tuesday it hopes to one day make cars for Tesla as it ramps up electric vehicle (EV) manufacturing in a strategy to diversify its business. Speaking at the company's annual Tech Day, Chairman Liu Young-way said Foxconn, the world's biggest contract electronics maker, wants to replicate its level of success in assembling consumer gadgets as it expands into building EVs for auto brands.
