L.J. __ (2022): Becoming a law professor in a law school has become a difficult endeavor. For most candidates, the roughly 50% reduction in open positions over the past decade is discouraging. In addition, nearly all successful candidates have completed another competitive credential in addition to their J.D. degree, like a federal clerkship, an advanced degree, or a faculty fellowship at a law school.
