Image caption, Bertie Matthews said there is no sign things will get better imminentlyA miller said his business has had to weather unprecedented price increases in recent months and there is no sign of when the struggles might end. Bertie Matthews' family has run Matthews Cotswold Flour in Oxfordshire for 110 years. He said companies like his normally see price hikes "once or twice a year", but they have had four so far in 2022.The war in Ukraine and inflation has meant the price of fuel has increased by 45%, paper by 50% and wheat by 60%.
