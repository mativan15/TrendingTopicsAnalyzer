LONDON: Liz Truss said on Thursday she would resign as British prime minister just six weeks after she was appointed.A leadership election will be completed within the next week to replace Truss, who is the shortest serving prime minister in British history. George Canning previously held the record, serving 119 days in 1827 when he died.1922 Committee chair Graham Brady set out some details on how the successor would be chosen and said he would give further information on the process later in the day.
