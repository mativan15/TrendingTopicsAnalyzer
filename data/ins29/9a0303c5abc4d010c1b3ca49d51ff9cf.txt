Train workers will turn off Opal machines from Thursday in an escalation over an ongoing wages and conditions dispute with the NSW government, the union representing the workers has confirmed.Ram, Trail and Bus Union NSW Secretary Alex Claassens confirmed on Monday that rail workers would switch off Opal machines from 3-7pm each day beginning this Thursday, providing commuters with free travel 'indefinitely' the union said.The RTBU said the industrial action resulted from the NSW government's refusal to offer fair wages and working conditions.
