He walks around Castle Island every day, a comforting routine that helps him appreciate the freedom he won in 2017 when a judge found prosecutors withheld a report that pointed the finger at someone else being responsible for the 1980 murder of Robert LaMonica in Braintree.On Thursday morning, two women stopped him during his daily loop. They had seen him on TV, the little guy who took on the government and won, and asked for a hug.Fred Weichel hugged them.His claim faced a heavy burden of proof.
