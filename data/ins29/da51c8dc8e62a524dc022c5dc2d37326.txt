Why teenage mothers in Zimbabwe struggle to get educated
economist.com
economics
2022-10-20 13:31:24

Brilliant Ndlovu has never really known childhood. Since the age of seven she has headed her household in Tsholotsho, a town in rural western Zimbabwe, after her parents went to work abroad. The oldest of five, she scraped a living growing crops while trying to keep up with her schoolwork. But in 2020 the covid-19 pandemic struck, coming shortly after a devastating drought. Farmers could not afford to pay child labourers like Ms Ndlovu. 'So I looked for a man to help support my family,' she recalls.
