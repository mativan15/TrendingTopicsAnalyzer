2022-06-01 15:26:00

By Colin Kellaher Levi Strauss & Co. on Wednesday affirmed its fiscal 2022 revenue and earnings guidance and raised its long-term sales targets. The San Francisco jeans maker said it still expects full-year adjusted earnings of $1.50 to $1.56 a share and an 11% to 13% rise in revenue to between $6.4 billion and $6.5 billion. Analysts polled by FactSet, on average, are expecting an adjusted profit of $1.55 a share on sales of $6.44 billion. Levi also said it is now targeting annual revenue growth of 6% to 8% from fiscal 2022 to fiscal 2027, up from a prior growth target of 4% to 6%, as it aims for fiscal 2027 revenue of $9 billion to $10 billion.
