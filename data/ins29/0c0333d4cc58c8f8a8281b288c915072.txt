TORONTO - Gains in the technology and utility sectors helped lead a broad-based rally as Canada's main stock index gained nearly 400 points in late morning trading and U.S. stock markets also rallied.

The S&P/TSX composite index was up 371.33 points at 18,697.68.

In New York, the Dow Jones industrial average was up 464.80 points at 30,099.63. The S&P 500 index was up 82.21 points at 3,665.28, while the Nasdaq composite was up 292.90 points at 10,614.29.

The Canadian dollar traded for 72.80 cents US compared with 72.
