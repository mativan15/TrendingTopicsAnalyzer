46% of seats in ITIs lie vacant. In a country looking at rapid ways to rebuild after the pandemic, the purpose of vocational education needs to change from merely preparing a student to earn a living to preparing them for vibrant careers Twenty-two year old Charan Babu S. graduated from the Government Industrial Training Institute (ITI) in Bengaluru in 2020 and bagged an apprenticeship in Hindustan Aeronautics Limited. He finds his job gratifying and enjoys servicing fighter jets. However, Charan, who studied in a private ICSE school, did not start out feeling this positive about his choice of joining an ITI.
