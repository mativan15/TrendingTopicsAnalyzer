Pensioner forced back to work by cost-of-living crisis

A worried pensioner has been forced to return to work aged 78 after 10 years of retirement, to cope with the rising cost of living.Maurice Taylor said he was concerned about prices going up and wanted to 'future-proof his finances'.He will start his job as a customer services advisor at premium taxi firm Addison Lee on Monday.Mr Taylor, a father of one, said: 'I haven't worked full time for ten years.'I had what seemed like a reasonable pension 10 years ago but it hasn't kept pace with the rate of inflation.
