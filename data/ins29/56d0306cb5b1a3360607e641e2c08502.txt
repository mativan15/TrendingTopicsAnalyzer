Published: 13:49 EDT, 21 October 2022 | Updated: 16:09 EDT, 21 October 2022 Meloni's party is anti-immigration and Eurosceptic but she supports Ukraine following Russia's invasion Far-right leader Giorgia Meloni was named Italian prime minister on Friday, becoming the first woman to head a government in Italy. Her post-fascist Brothers of Italy party -- Eurosceptic and anti-immigration -- won the September 25 legislative polls but needed outside support to form a government. Meloni's appointment is an historic event for the eurozone's third largest economy and for Brothers of Italy, which has never been in government.
