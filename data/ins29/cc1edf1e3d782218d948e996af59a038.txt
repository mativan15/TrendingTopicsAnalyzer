Published: 12:09 EDT, 21 October 2022 | Updated: 12:09 EDT, 21 October 2022 By Stephen Culp NEW YORK, Oct 21 (Reuters) - Wall Street advanced in morning trading and Treasury yields paused their uphill climb following reports that the Federal Reserve might consider less aggressive inflation-curbing tactics after November. All three major U.S. stock indexes shuffled between red and green before turning resolutely higher, and remain on track to post gains from last Friday's close in a week marked by a mixed bag of earnings, soft economic data and political turmoil in the UK.
