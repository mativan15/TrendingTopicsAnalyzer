CHANCELLOR SECURES SOME BREATHING SPACE Paul Kelso Business correspondent @pkelso When markets closed on Friday, after a dramatic day that saw a chancellor sacked and a totemic economic policy junked, the verdict was troubling: A sell off UK gilts had gathered pace before, during and after the Prime Minister's press conference, and the closing bell could not come fast enough. After Jeremy Hunt spent the weekend signalling a dramatic change in course to reassure the investors on whom confidence in the UK economy rests, the Treasury was plainly not going to take any chances on Monday morning.
