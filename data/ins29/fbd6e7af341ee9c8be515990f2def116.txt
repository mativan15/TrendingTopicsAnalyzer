The S&P BSE Sensex has given a negative return of 2.8% since the last Muhurat trading, making investors poorer.As per an analysis of stock exchange data on sectoral indices by broking firm Samco Securities, BSE Capital Goods yielded the highest at 12.5%, followed by BSE FMCG and BSE Auto with a gain of 11.4% and 11%, respectively.BSE Bankex saw a moderate gain of 1.2%.On the other hand, the BSE Smallcap index has shed 1.2% while BSE Oil & Gas is down by 2.8%. In the same period, BSE Midcap lost 4.
