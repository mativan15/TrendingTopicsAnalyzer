By Reuters Published: 02:04 EDT, 20 October 2022 | Updated: 02:04 EDT, 20 October 2022 SHANGHAI, Oct 20 (Reuters) - China shares rebounded after lunch break on Thursday, while Hong Kong stocks saw their losses trimmed, after a media report said China was considering easing COVID-19 restrictions. China is considering a cut in the duration of quarantine for inbound visitors from 10 to seven days, Bloomberg News reported on Thursday. Both China's blue-chip CSI300 Index and the Shanghai Composite Index clawed into positive territories in early afternoon trading.
