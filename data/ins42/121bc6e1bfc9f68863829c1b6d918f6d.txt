'I've survived two pandemics, two recessions ... and a flood.'As she cooked up some world weariness, Alida Solomon was also basking in the milestone her restaurant is readying to mark: 20 years in business. A unicorn by any measure in the fickle world of dining out, Tutti Matti, on Adelaide West, also remains a gauge of much local history.Widely considered the first to zero in on Tuscan food in Toronto (buoyed by Solomon's six years working in Montalcino at the hilltop Boccon di Vino), hers is the non-showy, trendphobic establishment that draws gobs of steadfast regulars, boasts the admiration of out-of-town supremos like Lidia Bastianich, and has also nabbed global accolades like the 2009 Leccio d'Oro for Osteria of the Year.
