Chesa Boudin is running out of time. San Francisco's district attorney has less than a week until a recall election that could cost him his job, and polls are showing his ouster is all but inevitable.So Team Boudin is in full November Mode, working out of a campaign headquarters that has little in the way of central air or even adequate plumbing. They're all relentlessly poring over data and papering neighborhoods with campaign literature begging people to vote 'NO on H,' a surprisingly difficult message to relay thanks to San Francisco's commitment to overly verbose ballot measures.
