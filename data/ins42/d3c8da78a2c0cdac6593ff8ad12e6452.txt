Direct: The Rise of the Middleman Economy and the Power of Going to the Source. By Kathryn Judge. Harper Business; 304 pages; $29.99 and £22In 2011 there was an outbreak of E. coli in Germany. Thousands of people fell ill. The authorities suspected that salad ingredients were to blame, but did not know which ones were contaminated with the bacteria. Their initial guess was Spanish cucumbers—and so European consumers avoided that country's fresh produce. Only later did the authorities find that salad sprouts grown in Germany were to blame.
