Pahrump, Nev. – The Audi RS 3 is one of those performance models that feels more like a passion project than a calculated business case. Now that the TT-RS has bowed out of the market, Audi's track-tuned compact sedan is the only car currently sold in North America that's motivated by a five-cylinder power plant. Given that, it would've made sense for the second generation RS 3 to have transitioned to a hopped up four-cylinder borrowed from the Volkswagen Group parts bin – after all, the S3 already gets its grunt from a 2.
