The U.S. spends twice as much on cancer care than average for high-income countries but mortality rates are only slightly better than average for these countries, according to a new study. 'There is a common perception that the U.S. offers the most advanced cancer care in the world,' said Ryan Chow, an M.D./Ph.D. student at Yale and lead author of the study. 'Our system is touted for developing new treatments and getting them to patients more quickly than other countries.
