Stocks open steeply down Monday following losses on Wall Street

Electronic display boards at Hana Bank in central Seoul show stock and foreign exchange markets Monday morning. [NEWS1] Stocks opened steeply lower Monday as the recent plunge on Wall Street spooked local investors. The benchmark Kospi dropped 29.1 points, or 1.32 percent, to 2,183.45 as of 9:17 a.m. On Friday, the Nasdaq Composite tumbled 3.08 percent and the Dow Jones Industrial Average fell 1.34 percent amid woes over higher inflation. The S&P 500 retreated 2.37 percent. In Seoul, Samsung Electronics shed 0.
