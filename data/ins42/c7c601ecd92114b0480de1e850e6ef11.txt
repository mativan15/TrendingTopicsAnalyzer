What the Fed's ‘QT' Means for That $2 Trillion Pile of Cash
washingtonpost.com
business
2022-06-01 14:00:37

In late May, investors flush with cash and left with nowhere else to put it parked a record of more than $2 trillion overnight at the US Federal Reserve. Starting June 1, the Fed will begin draining that plus $3.3 trillion of bank reserves from its nearly $9 trillion balance sheet to put all of this money in motion -- a process it called quantitative tightening.
