For the better part of a decade, International Business Machines (IBM 4.73%) has been working to remake itself as IT budgets increasingly shifted to the cloud. The company landed on a hybrid-cloud strategy. Large enterprises, IBM reckoned, would favor a mix of public cloud and on-premises hardware. Moving to a hybrid-cloud architecture would require specialized software, hardware, and expertise, all of which IBM could deliver.
While IBM's cloud business has been growing swiftly for many years, slow-growing legacy businesses were dragging the company down.
