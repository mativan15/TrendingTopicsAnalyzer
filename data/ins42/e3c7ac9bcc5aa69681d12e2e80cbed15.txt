Epsilon Carbon's arm plans to raise Rs 1,200 crore
indiatimes.com
energy
2022-10-21 20:00:00

Epsilon Advanced Materials, a subsidiary of coal tar derivatives manufacturer Epsilon Carbon, is looking to raise about Rs 1,200 crore to fund its operations, a top executive said. The company, which makes graphite anodes or negative terminals of lithium-ion cells used in electric vehicles (EV) and other consumer electronics, is seeking investor capital to expand this business. Epsilon Advanced Materials has invested about Rs 300 crore in the business so far and is now looking for external funding, said Vikram Handa, the managing director of Epsilon Carbon.
