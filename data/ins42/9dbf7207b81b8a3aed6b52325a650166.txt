Sri Lankan author Shehan Karunatilaka wins Booker Prize
dw.com
news
2022-10-18 00:15:00

Karunatilaka won the Booker Prize for "The Seven Moons of Maali Almeida," a political satire about Sri Lanka's decadeslong civil war.

The Booker prize is one of the most pretigious literary awards in the world
Sri Lankan author Shehan Karunatilaka won the Booker Prize on Monday for his novel, "The Seven Moons of Maali Almeida."
Karunatilaka accepted the award at a ceremony in London, saying he hoped the book would be read in a Sri Lanka that "learns from its stories."
Karunatilaka's book is set in the 1990s and is about a gay photographer who wakes up dead during the country's civil war.
