On the evening of Nov. 20, 1880, Louisa Gray went into labor in Hartford, Connecticut, and her husband, William, started to panic. More than the typical anxiety of watching a new human venture into the world, William had extra cause for concern. Just four years earlier, he had lost his first wife, Nellie, in childbirth. When Louisa started to experience complications, Gray's first thought was to head to a local factory. The telephone had come to Hartford two years earlier, and as in most cities at the time, phones were more common in businesses than homes.
