Central Bank research published this week shows the public expect average inflation of 10 per cent this year, but don't believe their earnings will rise anything like as fast. If this is even roughly correct, it will mean the first big fall in general living standards since the years after the financial crash. Managing this, in an era of populist politics, is going to be difficult, to say the least.
