Written by Rityusha Mani Tiwary
The weeklong National Congress of the Communist Party of China began in Beijing on Sunday (October 16). This five-year event defines the political-institutional character of the CCP due to its monopoly of Chinese politics. Against the backdrop of a complicated and chaotic international situation — post-pandemic slow global economic recovery, and the Ukraine-Russia conflict — this 20th session of the Party Congress has acquired more significance than usual for both China and the world.
