Published: 04:05 EDT, 17 October 2022 | Updated: 04:05 EDT, 17 October 2022 By Stefano Rebaudo Oct 17 (Reuters) - Euro zone government bond yields fell on Monday, taking cues from British gilts, but were still not far off their highest levels in more than 10 years with investors focused on inflation risks and increasing bond supply. Yields on UK government bonds dropped after new finance minister Jeremy Hunt, seeking to stem a dramatic loss of confidence among investors in Prime Minister Liz Truss's government, will announce tax and spending measures on Monday, two weeks earlier than scheduled.
