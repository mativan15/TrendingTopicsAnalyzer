It may take a couple of years for a new equilibrium to emerge in our work lives. Till then, expect a tough battle between managers and employees in determining the new norms of work. The debate on the future of work has brought attention to several pain points in a typical office-goer's life: Commutes, urban planning, workplace privacy, and office design. But the debate also raises larger questions about what it means to live and work productively.
In an 1898 research paper, The Instinct of Workmanship and the Irksomeness of Labour, the Norwegian-American economist Thorstein Veblen first drew attention to mankind's conflicted relationship with work.
