metamorworks/iStock via Getty Images Do politics matter for markets? We generally have a standard and perhaps somewhat annoying answer to that question, which is 'typically no, but sometimes yes.' Short-term market movements normally react to political developments only when those developments pertain directly to changes in interest rate policy or tax policy, since those are two variables that, when changed, have an immediate effect on cash flow valuation models. For the most part, our experience is that political events which may seem huge in Washington or Brussels or London resonate weakly, if at all, in financial markets.
