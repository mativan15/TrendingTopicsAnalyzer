by SUMNER H. SLICHTER1WITHIN two years after the end of fighting, Federal expenditures will drop from about 90 billion dollars to 25 billion dollars a year. This will represent the greatest and swiftest disappearance of markets in all history. Within a year after the fighting, about eight or nine million men will leave the Armed Forces, two million will leave civilian government jobs, and five million will be laid off by plants making combat munitions. Of eleven million additional men making war goods, perhaps half will be laid off.
