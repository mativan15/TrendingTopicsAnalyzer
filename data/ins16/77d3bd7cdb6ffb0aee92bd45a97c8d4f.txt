Danielle Supkis Cheek CPA, CFE, CVA is a VP at MindBridge and is a 5-time U.S. Most Powerful Women in Accounting. 
getty A common question posed to me by students considering a career in forensic accounting is, 'What is the hardest part of your job?' My answer always surprises them. Most people assume that the hardest part of forensic accounting is finding the money or some other technical aspect of the role. It is not. The hardest part of the job is getting used to being uncomfortable. There is cause for discomfort in this line of work for seemingly every type of person.
