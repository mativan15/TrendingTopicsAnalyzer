Many investors define successful investing as beating the market average over the long term. But its virtually certain that sometimes you will buy stocks that fall short of the market average returns. We regret to report that long term Noranda Income Fund (TSE:NIF.UN) shareholders have had that experience, with the share price dropping 51% in three years, versus a market return of about 47%. More recently, the share price has dropped a further 18% in a month.

Now let's have a look at the company's fundamentals, and see if the long term shareholder return has matched the performance of the underlying business.
