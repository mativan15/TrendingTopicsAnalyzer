Couples dreaming of an iconic Las Vegas wedding may feel all shook up if Elvis isn't allowed in the building.
The licensing company that controls the name and image of Elvis Presley has ordered Las Vegas chapel owners and operators to cease using the star's likeness in themed ceremonies, the Las Vegas Review-Journal originally reported.Authentic Brands Group (ABG), the company that licenses all Elvis-related products, sent a cease and desist letter on May 19 to numerous chapels in the state. The group also oversees several other major estates, including that of Marilyn Monroe and Muhammad Ali.
