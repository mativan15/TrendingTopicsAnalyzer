Published: 09:58 EDT, 2 June 2022 | Updated: 10:33 EDT, 2 June 2022 DETROIT (AP) - A judge declined to put a former Detroit police chief on the Republican ballot for Michigan governor, the third candidate to lose a court challenge after election staff said campaign petitions were marred by fraudulent signatures. It's a remarkable setback for James Craig, who has high name recognition in southeastern Michigan and retired as Detroit's top cop last year to make his first run for office.
