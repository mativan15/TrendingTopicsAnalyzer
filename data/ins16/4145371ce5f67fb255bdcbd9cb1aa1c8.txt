Published: 22:43 EDT, 2 June 2022 | Updated: 22:43 EDT, 2 June 2022 By Anna Mehler Paperny TORONTO, June 2 (Reuters) - A larger-than-life politician, Ontario premier Doug Ford beat back criticism of his handling of the coronavirus pandemic to win a second term for his party on Thursday, but he faces an affordability crisis and a strained health system. Media projected Ford's right-leaning Progressive Conservatives to win a big majority in Canada's most populous province shortly after the close of voting, in line with the party's lead in opinion polls.
