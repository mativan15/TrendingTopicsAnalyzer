Playing games with your pelvic floor could be a useful exercise for urinary incontinence
theconversation.com
science
2022-05-30 04:29:14

Many of us have heard of 'Kegels' or pelvic floor exercises, and probably have a vague sense we should be doing more of them. For many women, our social media news feeds are full of ads for the latest gizmos and gadgets for exercising our pelvic floors. There are brands with game-like apps including Perifit and Elvie, and there are Kegel balls for sale too. As technology advances and the need for pelvic floor rehabilitation after pregnancy, childbirth and menopause continues, the demand for innovation in these devices has increased.
