For Kanye West, the past month might as well have been a decade. Even before his dangerous and roundly condemned 'death con 3 ON JEWISH PEOPLE' tweet got him suspended from Twitter, he debuted a 'White Lives Matter' T-shirt alongside conservative pundit Candace Owens during his Paris Fashion Week presentation, instantly tarnishing his reputation within the industry. This week, after a noxious cycle of public appearances where Ye doubled down on his comments, repeating antisemitic talking points as well as the debunked claim that George Floyd's death at the hands of Minneapolis police was actually a result of a lethal dose of fentanyl, he apparently 'agreed' to purchase the far-right social media platform Parler (which is run by Owens' husband).
