Embarrassing confession time: I've always been a bit of a mess around blood and guts. When I was a teenager, I'd regularly feel light-headed at the sight, or even the description of it. Biology class dissections, history lessons about gruesome forms of capital punishment in the middle ages, a pig-butchering scene in Michael Winterbottom's otherwise dreary adaptation of Jude the Obscure that we had to watch for an English lit lecture: all would turn me white as a sheet of A4.In recent years I've largely managed to get over my squeamishness around a bit of spilled claret.
