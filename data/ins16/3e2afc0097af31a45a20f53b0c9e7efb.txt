Teenager conquers Hadrian's Wall trail in wheelchair

Image caption, Seeing the whole of Hadrian's Wall had been a long-held ambition of Brynn HauxwellA teenage wheelchair user who conquered the Hadrian's Wall trail said he hoped he has inspired others to be undeterred by obstacles.Brynn Hauxwell reached the end of the 84-mile (135km) course at Wallsend, North Tyneside, 10 days after starting from Bowness-on-Solway, Cumbria.The 17-year-old has raised funds for Ability Shetland, which introduced him to the special mountain trike he used.His mum, Kim, said her heart was "bursting with pride".
