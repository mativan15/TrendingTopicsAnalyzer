Women's matches should move to weekend

2022-10-19 12:05:41

Marissa Callaghan plays for Women's Premiership leaders CliftonvilleNorthern Ireland captain Marissa Callaghan says changing Women's Premiership fixtures from a Wednesday to the weekend will improve women's football "massively".Matches in the league are currently held midweek.In comparison, the majority of men's Irish Premiership games take place on a Friday night or Saturday afternoon."When you are working full-time and have a young son to look after, it is quite tough," said Callaghan.She added: "In my opinion, the game needs to change from a Wednesday evening to weekend fixtures.
