getty It's no secret that the way we approach work has fundamentally changed due to the pandemic and the 'Great Resignation.' Not only did the pandemic force a collective shift toward remote work and a huge reliance on technology, but also the Great Resignation pushed business leaders to take a more people-centered approach to meet employee demand and improve hiring and retention. While leaders across businesses have faced significant challenges, human resources professionals arguably took the brunt of the early days of the pandemic, as they were tasked with navigating a remote-first landscape and ensuring employees were safe and healthy.
