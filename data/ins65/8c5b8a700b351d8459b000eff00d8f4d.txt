REUTERS/Pascal RossignolOct 19 (Reuters) - Strikes at French oil refineries have given temporary relief to volatile crude markets in Europe but created delayed demand for future months when refined product supply is set to be tight.Over 60% of France's refining capacity – or 740,000 barrels per day (bpd) – has been offline since mid-September because of strike action over pay or unplanned maintenance.
