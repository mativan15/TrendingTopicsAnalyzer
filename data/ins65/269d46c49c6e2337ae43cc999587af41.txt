You probably already know that because of market-capitalization weighting, a broad index such as the S&P 500 can be concentrated in a handful of stocks. Index funds are popular for good reasons — they tend to have low expenses and it is difficult for active managers to outperform them over the long term.

For example, look at the SPDR S&P 500 ETF Trust which tracks the S&P 500 by holding all of its stocks by the same weighting as the index. Five stocks — Apple Inc. Microsoft Corp. Amazon.
