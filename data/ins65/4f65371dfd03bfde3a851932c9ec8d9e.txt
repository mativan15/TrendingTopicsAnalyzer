Today in 1009, the Church of the Holy Sepulchre in Jerusalem was destroyed by the Fatimid caliph Al-Hakim bi-Amr Allah, who hacked the Church's foundations down to bedrock. Today in 1767, the Mason-Dixon line was agreed upon, ending a boundary dispute relating to Maryland, Pennsylvania, and Delaware. Today in 1775, African-American poet Phillis Wheatley was freed from slavery. Today in 1867, the US took formal possession of Alaska from Russia. The land was purchased of a total of $7-million dollars (2-cents per acre).
