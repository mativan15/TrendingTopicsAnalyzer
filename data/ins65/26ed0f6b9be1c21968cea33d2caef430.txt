Germany and France should establish a new bloc without Warsaw and Budapest, Lech Walesa said © Getty Images / NurPhoto / Contributor Former Polish President and Nobel Peace Prize laureate Lech Walesa has suggested that the European Union should dissolve itself to create a new bloc without Poland and Hungary. In an interview with the Polish portal Interia about the European Commission finally approving the National Reconstruction Plan for his country, Walesa, who was president between 1990 and 1995, said that sending billions of euros in investments to Warsaw would be a 'failure' for the EU.
