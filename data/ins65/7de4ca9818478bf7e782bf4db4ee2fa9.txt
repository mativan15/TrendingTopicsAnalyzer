They say fortune favors the bold, but I think it favors the anxious (like me), particularly when it comes to event planning. And what is Thanksgiving but an event? The 'very detail-oriented' among us tend to plan things 'too far' in advance, but that extra bit of preparation usually pays off in the end. If what I'm saying has you nodding along, I have some suggestions for actions you can take now—in the middle of October—to ensure your Thanksgiving is as fabulous as you want it to be. Now is the time to test out recipes This is a fairly obvious one, but the morning of turkey day (or even the day before) is not the time to try out a new pie recipe, put a 'spin' on your classic rolls, or change up the stuffing.
