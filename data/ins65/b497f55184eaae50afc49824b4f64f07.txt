At Kidder he headed an industry task force that resulted in the creation in 1970 of the Securities Investor Protection Corp., the body that protects investors against loss from failing brokers.The SIPC was a federally legislated response to chaotic conditions in the late 1960s, when swelling trading volume overwhelmed lagging back-office technology; stock markets had to curtail trading hours, closing entirely on Wednesdays for months. Today, the industry body has become so embedded in the financial system that many brokers no longer bother to advertise their membership and its coverage of customer accounts.
