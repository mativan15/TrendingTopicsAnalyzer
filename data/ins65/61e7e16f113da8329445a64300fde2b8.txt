Published: 10:26 EDT, 31 May 2022 | Updated: 10:28 EDT, 31 May 2022 Boris Johnson has warned Britain's civil servants they must endure the same cost-cutting as families up and down the country as he defended his pledge to axe 91,000 of their jobs.The Prime Minister, in a letter to civil servants, insisted there was no longer a need for Whitehall to have the same 'colossal presence' in people's lives.Although he apologised for the 'concern and uncertainty' caused by his promise to slash thousands of roles, Mr Johnson doubled down on his plans to return the civil service to the size it was in 2016.
