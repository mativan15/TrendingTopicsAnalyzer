Getty The four-day workweek has been a trendy topic for a while. All over the world, we are starting to question the 40-hour workweek, which has been the standard in the Western world since the Industrial Revolution. In 1940, the U.S. adopted a 40-hour workweek. A shorter workweek would be a way to increase productivity, improve mental health and even fight climate change. This trend has been accelerated by the 2020 pandemic, which sharpened issues around well-being, burnout and work-life balance.
