The new book 'Showroom City' examines how High Point, North Carolina, pivoted from furniture production to exposition — and the sacrifices it made along the way.

By

In the heart of North Carolina, there's a small city dominated by an unexpected industry: the business of showing furniture. High Point, which sits about 100 miles west of Raleigh, is known as the 'furniture capital of the world.' Not because it produces furniture — as it once did in staggering numbers decades ago — but because it displays it.
