Strawberries sold in grocery stores across the U.S. and Canada have been linked to a possible hepatitis A outbreak.The Food and Drink Administration (FDA) and the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) are investigating the outbreak and urging people to not eat any FreshKampo- or HEB-branded strawberries purchased between March 5 and April 25.Where were these contaminated strawberries sold?The strawberries were sold and distributed on a national level and ended up in stores across the country.
