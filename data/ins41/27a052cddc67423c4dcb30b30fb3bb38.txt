Boston has awarded $12.8M in Covid relief to 1,037 small businesses
bizjournals.com
business
2022-10-19 16:10:00

The second wave of Boston's Small Business Relief Fund provided nearly $13 million combined to more than 1,000 small business owners throughout all the city's neighborhoods.

Mayor Michelle Wu announced Wednesday that the program, funded through the federal American Rescue Plan Act, helped 1,037 small and diverse businesses bounce back or grow during the pandemic with grants of up to $10,000.

As of Wednesday, the city has awarded $12.8 million, with the largest portions going to businesses in Dorchester, the downtown area, Roxbury, Jamaica Plain, Hyde Park and Back Bay.
