Pandemic Layoffs Were a Route to Longer-Term Joblessness and Retirement for Many, CNBC Survey Finds
nbcconnecticut.com
business
2022-06-02 20:30:00

Millions of workers were laid off during the pandemic and ended up returning to work at different companies and in different jobs, according to the new CNBC All-America Workforce survey. The survey also found 57% of those who retired during the pandemic said they did so after being laid off. The special workforce survey reached 1,200 currently employed people, 450 people who retired during the pandemic, and 400 people not in the workforce but also not retired. Call it the Great Reshuffle. The new CNBC All-America Workforce Survey finds that millions of workers were laid off during the pandemic and ended up returning to work at different companies and in different jobs; many started working from home and don't expect to go back.
