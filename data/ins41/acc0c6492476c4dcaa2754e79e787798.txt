The Government has won a vote against a Labour motion to ban fracking. Labour set out to introduce a draft law to ban fracking, and Tory MPs were warned prior to the vote that a Labour vote in the Commons in support of banning fracking would be treated as a 'confidence motion' in Liz Truss.Truss reassured MPs with concerns that there would be a 'robust' process to ensure that local communities consented before fracking went ahead.Prior to the vote, Conservative deputy chief whip Craig Whittaker wrote to Tory MPs, saying: 'This is not a motion on fracking.
