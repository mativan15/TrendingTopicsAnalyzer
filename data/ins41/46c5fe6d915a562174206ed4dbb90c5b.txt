Dow slumps 300 points as stocks turn lower for week after strong May jobs report
Copyright 2022, MarketWatch, Inc.
finance
2022-06-03 11:35:00

U.S. stocks fell Friday after better-than-expected May payrolls data reinforced expectations for a series of interest rate rises by the Federal Reserve in coming months. The technology sector was leading the way south as Treasury yields rose and a report that Tesla Inc. may be considering job cuts amid CEO Elon Musk's misgivings about the economic outlook. The Dow Jones Industrial Average DJIA, -1.11% fell 332 points, or 1%, to 32,916. The S&P 500 SPX, -1.74% dropped 69 points, or 1.7%, to 4,108.
