Ye, the billionaire music entrepreneur and fashion designer previously known as Kanye West, is having quite a few months. He's unleashed hateful rhetoric against Jews, worn clothing that mocks the Black Lives Matter movement and used social media to harass his ex-wife and her then-boyfriend — a trifecta of nauseating behavior. Add persistent chaos in his own management team — one member characterized the game of corporate musical chairs as a 'reorganization,' but still — and you have a case of a billionaire polluting his own brand with hate, mockery and harassment.
