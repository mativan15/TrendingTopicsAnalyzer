Las Vegas is known as the Entertainment Capital of the World for a reason: It has something to keep everyone entertained – even hardcore gamers.

Obliterating the notion that people shouldn't just stay inside playing video games while on vacation, HyperX Arena Las Vegas inside Luxor Hotel & Casino is a mecca for gamers. The first dedicated esports arena on the Strip, the 30,000-square-foot, state-of-the-art arena and production facility puts any gamer's home setup to shame with a 50-foot LED video wall, 120 PC and console stations, telescopic seating for spectators, and weekly events like Knockdown! Fridays where players can register to participate in a variety of fighting games, such as 'Super Smash Bros.
