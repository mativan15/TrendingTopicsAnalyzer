By ZHU WENQIAN | CHINA DAILY | Updated: 2022-06-04 08:23 Visitors try out ready meals during a food expo in Fuzhou, Fujian province. WEI PEIQUAN/XINHUA COVID-19 restrictions mean dining at restaurants has become a distant memory for some cities in China. Diners are eager to buy ready-to-cook, ready-to-heat or ready-to-eat meals that taste similar to those served at restaurants. Given the convenience of such meals that require minimum preparation, a niche has developed for ready meal retailers, market insiders said.
