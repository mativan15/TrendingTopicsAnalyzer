As investors get more defensive, they may look at stalwart stocks -- businesses that will be fine regardless of whether the U.S. plunges into a recession. Two companies that come to mind when discussing stalwart businesses are Costco Wholesale (COST -2.85%) and Home Depot (HD -2.26%).
Both have been around long enough to weather multiple recessions and economic downturns. But which one is better-positioned for the economic future that awaits us? Let's find out.
Home Depot is more prone to discretionary spending
Both businesses are consumer-facing retail chains.
