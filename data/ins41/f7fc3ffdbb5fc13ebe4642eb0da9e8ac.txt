Published: 09:28 EDT, 2 June 2022 | Updated: 09:28 EDT, 2 June 2022 By Anisha Sircar and Devik Jain June 2 (Reuters) - U.S. stock indexes were set for a mixed open on Thursday as shares of Microsoft fell after the company lowered its profit forecast, offsetting relief from a pullback in oil prices that eased worries about rising inflation. Microsoft fell 2.3% in premarket trading after the company cut its fourth-quarter forecast for profit and revenue, citing a hit from unfavorable currency movements.
