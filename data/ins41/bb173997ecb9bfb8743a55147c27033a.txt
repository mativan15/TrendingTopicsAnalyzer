Sir, The principal flaw in the Conservatives' leadership election rules is not so much that they are undemocratic, as Lord Grabiner says (letter, Oct 15), but that they are unconstitutional. Constitutional precedent provides that the sovereign should invite the person who can command a majority in the House of Commons to form a government. Giving the final say to members risks the membership electing a leader the parliamentary party doesn't want, with the wrong choice imposed against the better judgment of MPs by the threat of deselection.
