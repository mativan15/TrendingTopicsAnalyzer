David Sheppard in LondonOil prices rose above $120 a barrel on Monday as tightness in refined fuel markets and concerns over supplies from Russia propelled prices to the highest level in two months.Brent crude, the international benchmark, hit a two-month high of $120.50 a barrel in early trading, up 1 per cent ahead of the July contract's expiry on Tuesday. US benchmark West Texas Intermediate rose by a similar amount to above $116 a barrel. The Brent contract for August delivery was up 0.5 per cent at $116.
