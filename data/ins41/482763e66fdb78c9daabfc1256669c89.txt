Despite feeling snubbed at a recent international forum, Brazilian President Jair Bolsonaro, the leader of South America's largest economy, will meet next month with President Biden, the leader of North America's largest economy. The two embattled leaders are scheduled to meet at the Summit of the Americas in Los Angeles next month, the first such meeting in the United States since it was held in Miami in 1994 during efforts to create the Free Trade of the Americas, which eventually failed.
