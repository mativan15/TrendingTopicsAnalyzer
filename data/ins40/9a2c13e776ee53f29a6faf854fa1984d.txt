By Reuters Published: 14:34 EDT, 20 October 2022 | Updated: 14:34 EDT, 20 October 2022 The Calgary Flames signed backup goaltender Dan Vladar to a two-year contract extension worth $4.4 million on Thursday. Vladar, 25, is 1-0-0 this season after allowing three goals on 29 shots against Edmonton last Saturday. For his career, Vladar is 16-8-3 in 29 games (25 starts) for the Boston Bruins (2020-21) and the Flames. He has a 2.88 goals-against average and .902 save percentage. The Flames acquired Vladar from Boston in July 2021 for third-round pick in the 2022 draft.
