CNN — Fads come and go in fashion. (Are skinny jeans no longer a thing?) The same is true of the stock market.Investors occasionally get too wrapped up in the latest big consumer or corporate trend, only to see companies get crushed when fickle tastes change — or even worse, a trend goes mainstream and there's too much competition.Take a look at Boston Beer, for example. The Sam Adams brewer, which also owns the hard seltzer brand Truly, is struggling as the popularity of alcoholic seltzer appears to be fading fast.
