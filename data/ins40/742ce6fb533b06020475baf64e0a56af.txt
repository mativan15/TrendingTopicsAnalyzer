One of the great accusations levelled at T20 cricket lies in its alleged inability to endure.Games are spectacular but rarely memorable; one explosion of sixes soon indistinguishable from the next, which is usually only a day or two away. Series can be briefly thrilling, but are swiftly over and forgotten, the joys of victory passing quickly and, perhaps above all else, defeats delivering few consequences and leaving no lasting mark.Having been on the end of narrow ones at each of the last two T20 World Cups, it is a suggestion Jos Buttler refutes.
