New Cars Are Still Selling for Over MSRP. Here's How to Avoid Sticker Shock
nbclosangeles.com
business
2022-10-15 15:00:00

September was the 16th consecutive month that car buyers paid over MSRP (sticker price) for new autos, with the average price paid for a new car at $48,094, up 6.1% year over year. Automakers including GM, Hyundai, and BMW are still seeing healthy demand. But as new car supply increases and the economy weakens, auto consumers may want to think twice about overpaying for cars. With demand for new cars still outpacing supply at many dealerships, buyers may be asking themselves if they need to pay over the average manufacturer's suggested retail price, or the sticker price, to get the car that they want.
