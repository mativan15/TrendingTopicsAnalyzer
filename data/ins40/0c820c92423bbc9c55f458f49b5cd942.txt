Observations from hospital beds offer glimpse into what death means to Koreans



'What a Thousand Deaths Have Taught Me" by Kim Yeo-hwan, Forest BooksDeath might be the only thing that comes to everyone indiscriminately, but discussing it has long been considered taboo in South Korea.'I wouldn't dare talk about death with my parents, let alone imagine their death,' would probably be the train of thought in most Korean people's minds.Perhaps the sudden deaths brought on by the prolonged COVID-19 pandemic and a growing number of discussions on what is considered a "dignified death" allow us to contemplate the issue.
