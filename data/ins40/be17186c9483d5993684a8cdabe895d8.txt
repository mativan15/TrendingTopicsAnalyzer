The new export duty levied on steel by the Centre has put steelmakers in a dilemma of choosing between margins and relationship with customers as they ponder whether to continue exports even at a loss, said Seshagiri Rao, the joint managing director of . "Exporting after paying 15% duty is definitely a loss-making proposition. But we have to look at the long term," said Rao. "If we don't supply to our customers that we have developed over the last decade, we will lose them." Rao believes the duties to be a temporary measure by the government to arrest inflation.
