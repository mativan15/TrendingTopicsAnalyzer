The British government is facing calls to summon China's ambassador to the UK over allegations a Chinese diplomat oversaw the attack of a Hong Kong man staging a peaceful protest against President Xi Jinping.

Alicia Kearns, a lawmaker in the ruling Conservative Party and head of parliament's Foreign Affairs Committee, wrote on Twitter that any official who had beaten protesters 'must be expelled or prosecuted.
