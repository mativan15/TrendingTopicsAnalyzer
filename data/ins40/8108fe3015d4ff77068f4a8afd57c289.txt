Brandon Bell (Note: This article was in the newsletter on October 20, 2022.) The response to the AT&T (NYSE:T) third quarter report by the market shows just how overblown the worries were about the future of the company. Mr. Market tends to assume that a bad quarter will be followed by more bad quarters (especially with the history of this company). Then there is the additional part of the market that figures a stock has to be bad if it goes down. Combined these two groups and additional worries really made a bargain level stock for those that had the nerve to take advantage of the situation.
