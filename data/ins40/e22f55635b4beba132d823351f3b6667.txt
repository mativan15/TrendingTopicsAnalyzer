James Grimmelmann (Cornell Law School; Cornell Tech) & Christina Mulligan (Brooklyn Law School) have posted Data Property (American University Law Review, Forthcoming) on SSRN.  Here is the abstract: In this, the Information Age, people and businesses depend on data. From your family photos to Google's search index, data has become one of society's most important resources. But there is a gaping hole in the law's treatment of data. If someone destroys your car, that is the tort of conversion and the law gives a remedy.
