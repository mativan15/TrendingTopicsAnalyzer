Hundreds have been injured and 50 killed during anti-government protets in Chad. Protesters demanding a transition to democratic rule in the central African country took to the streets on Thursday and were met with a crackdown by police and security services who reportedly fired on demonstrators.Prime Minister Saleh Kebzabo branded the incident an 'armed insurrection', but human rights group said that unarmed civilians were massacred by security forces in the capital, N'Djamena, and several other cities.
