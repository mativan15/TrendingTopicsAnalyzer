Riding high off a record year of growth, many cryptocurrency investors filed tax returns on mind-boggling gains this spring—some in the range of 20 to 30 times their original outlay. But now it's time to settle up with the IRS, and there's a problem: The money isn't there anymore.The crypto market endured its worst crash in years this month, wiping out more than $400 billion in value in a matter of weeks. Message boards were flooded with comments from people saying they'd lost their entire life savings, and others who worried they could lose their homes.
