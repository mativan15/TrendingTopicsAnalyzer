As you may have heard, this country has a new ruler. Jeremy Hunt now sets the government's course; Liz Truss is merely his barely-human shield. But very few of this week's tributes to the 'real prime minister' mention that precisely 18 people voted for him to take that job. That is the grand total of MPs who backed Hunt in this summer's Tory leadership contest, from which he was ejected in the very first round. Out of eight contenders, he came eighth. 'Who voted for this?' Truss was asked when she unveiled her mini-budget.
