By Reuters Published: 21:54 EDT, 18 October 2022 | Updated: 21:54 EDT, 18 October 2022 Oct 19 (Reuters) - Gold prices inched lower on Wednesday as the dollar steadied, while the U.S. Federal Reserve's commitment to tightening monetary policy also weighed on zero-yield bullion's appeal. FUNDAMENTALS * Spot gold was down 0.1% at $1,650.75 per ounce, as of 0125 GMT. * U.S. gold futures were up 0.1% at $1,657.30. * The dollar index was a tad bit higher in early Asian hours, having bounced off its lowest level since Oct.
