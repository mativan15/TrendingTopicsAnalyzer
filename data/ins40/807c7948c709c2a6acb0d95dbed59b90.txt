Fintech's historic era of hyper growth is over. Here's who stands to struggle as rates rise and investment slows.
businessinsider.com
tech
2022-05-31 10:00:00

As the Federal Reserve hikes interest rates in the face of mounting inflation, a pandemic-fueled run of easy money is coming to an end — and some fintechs are closer to the line of fire than others.Ahead of this spring, investment in fintech startups of all stripes had soared to historic levels. In 2021, private market funding to these companies reached $132 billion globally, according to CB Insights — up nearly 170% compared to 2020. But with borrowing becoming more expensive, public and private fintech companies alike stand to lose as investor appetite wanes.
