Op-Ed: How previous financial trauma keeps Latinos in the U.S. from investing
yahoo.com

2022-06-03 10:05:25

A U.S. flag waves outside the New York Stock Exchange in January. (John Minchillo / Associated Press)

Many Latinos living in the U.S. suffered through acute economic upheaval in their countries of origin. Our troubled experiences influence our financial behavior and our economic outlook. It's like we have financial PTSD.

Our instinctive reaction is to save money in places that feel safe — under the mattress or, at best, in a checking or savings account — rather than investing it to build wealth.
