The Queensland Government will double the size of its Housing Investment Fund by injecting $130 million each year into the state's housing sector.The Queensland Housing Summit hosted in Brisbane on Thursday welcomed government, private and public sector stakeholders to discuss the housing crisis facing the state.Premier Annastacia Palaszczuk announced the government would be increasing its funding from $65m to $130m to help with the commencement of new homes in Queensland.'This increased investment means we are now targeting an additional 5,600 new social and affordable homes by 2027,' she said.
