Justice DY Chandrachud: Career highlights, landmark judgments & other things about India's new CJI

2022-10-17 14:45:00

Justice DY Chandrachud, the new Chief Justice of India, will succeed outgoing CJI UU Lalit on November 8. He will be sworn in as India's 50th CJI on November 9. Chandrachud was named successor by the outgoing CJI on October 11. Justice Chandrachud will serve as the CJI for two years, while his predecessor only got a brief tenure of 74 days.

During his glittering career, Justice Chandrachud has been part of a number of landmark judgments, including those on Ayodhya title dispute, decriminalisation of homosexuality, adultery, privacy, entry of women into Sabarimala etc.
