Poland Wrecks Bond Market as Inflation Fight Takes Back Seat

(Bloomberg) -- Poland is painfully finding out what happens when investors start to fear that policy makers are turning their backs on inflation to keep the economy humming for next year's election.

Most Read from Bloomberg

Its government bond yields are rising faster than anywhere else in the world. In the past month, the 10-year yield jumped by a whopping 2.3 percentage points to a two-decade high of 8.53%.

The selloff accelerated after a surprise central bank decision to keep rates unchanged despite surging inflation.
