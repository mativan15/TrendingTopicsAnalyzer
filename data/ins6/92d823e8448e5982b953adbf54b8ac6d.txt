In simpler times, 'player power' in sports reflected the wider society's industrial relations. When players banded together against their employer, it was to demand fairer pay and conditions. The origins of that pay – cancer money (cigarette sponsorship), pollution money (oil sponsorship), chronic illness and domestic violence money (alcohol sponsorship) – were not up for discussion.
