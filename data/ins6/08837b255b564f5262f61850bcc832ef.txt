Published: 10:56 EDT, 19 October 2022 | Updated: 10:56 EDT, 19 October 2022 An unopened, first generation iPhone from 2007 has been sold at an auction for more than 50 times its original price.With a starting bid of $2,800 (£2,500), the 15-year-old device was expected to bring in at least $30,000 (£26,700), but ended up fetching a whopping $39,000 (£34,700), according to LCG Auctions.The first iPhone was introduced by Apple founder Steve Jobs in January 2007 at MacWorld San Francisco, with the slogan 'Apple reinvents the phone'.
