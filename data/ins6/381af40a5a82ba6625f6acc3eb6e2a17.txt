Stu Sjouwerman did not want to repeat history when it came to his fifth startup.

The Dutch-born serial entrepreneur hit what seemed like the pinnacle of success in 2010 when he sold his company Sunbelt Software and walked away with an eight-figure payout.

But after the congratulatory calls and general fanfare faded, Sjouwerman was left with the bittersweet feeling many founders have come to know.

"You see a whole bunch of zeros in your bank account, and the next day you feel like shit because you have no game to play, no company," he said in a 2019 interview with the Tampa Bay Business Journal.
