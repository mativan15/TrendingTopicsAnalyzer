19 October 2022 by Matt Mulcahey 
Filmmaker Magazine Pronouncements of finality by slasher franchises have always been amusingly premature. Final Chapters. Final Fridays. Final Nightmares: You can't keep a good (or at least a profitable) slasher down for long. While Halloween Ends—the 13th film in the franchise—probably won't be the last we ever see of Michael Myers, it certainly does feel like the conclusion of director David Gordon Green's chapter of the story. Set four years after Halloween Kills, the new entry finds Myers long missing from Haddonfield and Jamie Lee Curtis's Laurie Strode attempting to move on with her life.
