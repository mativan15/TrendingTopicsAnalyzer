Published: 19:24 EDT, 16 October 2022 | Updated: 20:00 EDT, 16 October 2022 A rise in screen time has seen children as young as six suffer from an excruciating eye condition which usually affects adults, experts have warned.Dry eye disease occurs when tears aren't able to provide adequate lubrication for the eyes.It normally affects people aged 50 to 60, and if left untreated can be extremely painful with symptoms including gritty, sore, red and sensitive eyes.Sufferers have compared the feeling to that experienced when chopping onions, with a constant sensation of burning.
