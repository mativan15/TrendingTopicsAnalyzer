REUTERS/KhamHANOI, Oct 19 (Reuters) - Vietnam's government has asked its central bank to help local fuel traders have better access to foreign currencies to pay for fuel imports, the Ministry of Industry and Trade said late on Wednesday.The ministry, in a document sent to the State Bank of Vietnam, said the move would help fuel trading firms "quickly make their purchases of fuel products for the domestic market," it said in a statement.
