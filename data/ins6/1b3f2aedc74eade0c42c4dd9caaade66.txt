Banks Brace For Tax Hit To Help Plug UK's Budget Gap

General view of signage at a branch of Lloyds bank, in London, Britain October 31, 2021. 
British banks are bracing for a potential tax hit to their profits as the government scrambles to plug an estimated 40 billion pound ($45 billion) hole in its finances.A source familiar with the British government's plans said new finance minister Jeremy Hunt was reviewing the current surcharge on bank profits and would confirm the level when he delivers his medium-term fiscal plan on Oct. 31.Governments across Europe have been weighing whether to slap new taxes on banks to help pay for state support packages for citizens struggling with soaring food and fuel prices, with both Spain and Hungary proposing one-off levies.
