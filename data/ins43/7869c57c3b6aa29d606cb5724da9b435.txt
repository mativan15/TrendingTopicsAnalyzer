(Bloomberg) -- Hello again.Here are a few snippets to ease you into what promises to be yet another lively week in UK politics and markets.The big U-turn: The Truss honeymoon is over before it even began. The UK starts the week with its fourth Chancellor in less than a year. Liz Truss sacked Kwasi Kwarteng on Friday — who served just 38 days — after their mini-budget roiled markets and tanked support for the Conservative party. Truss's rocky start led the Economist magazine to declare that her premiership would have the shelf life of a head of lettuce and the country's fiscal mess became the butt of jokes at the IMF meeting in Washington.
