The last two years have highlighted the importance of managers. It's ever clearer that their jobs are not just about managing productivity, but about supporting sustained performance, wellbeing, and inclusion in the more complicated context of flexible work arrangements.

So Nicolai J. Foss and Peter G. Klein's new book Why Managers Matter, released this week, is timely. Foss and Klein, professors at the Copenhagen Business School and Baylor's Hankamer School of Business respectively, mount an extensive argument for the importance of managers in the new world of work.
