Published: 15:39 EDT, 18 October 2022 | Updated: 15:39 EDT, 18 October 2022 By Saqib Iqbal Ahmed NEW YORK, Oct 18 (Reuters) - The U.S. dollar edged higher against a basket of currencies on Tuesday, shaking off some of the weakness of the previous session, but a revival in risk appetite in global financial markets kept a lid on its gains. "Yesterday's risk-on vibe looks to be continuing into today's session," said Michael Brown, head of market intelligence at payments firm Caxton in London. "A lack of any major headlines, coupled with some semblance of fiscal stability in the UK, appear to be the culprits," he said.
