Insurer MS Amlin fined after watchdog's repeated warnings ‘ignored'
thetimes.co.uk
news
2022-10-20 23:01:00

A Lloyd's of London insurer has been fined almost £10 million by the Bank of England after weak internal controls led to the firm suffering losses of nearly £550 million in a year.The Bank's Prudential Regulation Authority said MS Amlin's London market business had failed to heed warnings that the watchdog had sounded in the run-up to 2017, when the two Lloyd's syndicates managed by the firm made combined losses of £545 million.The unit, MS Amlin Underwriting Limited, has been fined £9.7 million by the regulator for its failings between 2014 and 2019.
