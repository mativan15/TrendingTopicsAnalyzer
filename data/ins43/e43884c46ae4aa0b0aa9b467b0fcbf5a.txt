Japanese government data shows real wages falling and consumers cutting back on spending.   © Reuters YOHEI MATSUO and JUNYA HEMMI, Nikkei staff writersOctober 21, 2022 05:09 JST | JapanTOKYO -- Food prices in Japan rose at the fastest pace since July 1991 during the past week, Nikkei data shows, as producers scramble to pass on the impact of a softening yen and high commodity costs.The seven-day average was up 4.5% on the year as of Tuesday, according to CPINow data. The trend threatens to hamper the economy's nascent recovery from the coronavirus pandemic, with poorer households hit the hardest.
