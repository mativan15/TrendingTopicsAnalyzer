View: Charitable institutions' tax benefits were bound to garner controversy
indiatimes.com
economics
2022-10-20 19:30:00

We live in times when conducting charitable activities is regarded as an essential tool for addressing the inequities in society. It would seem, at first blush, that 'doing charity' is fundamentally opposite to earning profits and making gains by carrying out trade, commerce or business. Tax laws provide exemption from tax to recognised charitable institutions and organisations engaged in providing education and medical facilities. While the laws permit them to earn profits incidental to the carrying out of their activities, the revenue authorities understandably would not want them to be engaged in business activities while being sheltered from taxation of income.
