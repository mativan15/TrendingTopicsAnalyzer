Ex-judge's findings on Hockey Canada's use of National Equity Fund alarms financial watchdog
theglobeandmail.com
news
2022-10-15 01:21:21

When Hockey Canada came under fire for using registration fees to settle sexual assault claims without disclosing to parents and players how their money was being used, executives told hearings in Ottawa that its National Equity Fund was also used for health and wellness programs.Among the items Hockey Canada said it funded were various forms of counselling and treatment for players. But in conducting a review into Hockey Canada's governance, including oversight of the National Equity Fund (NEF), retired Supreme Court Judge Thomas Cromwell couldn't find records of these things in the financial statements.
