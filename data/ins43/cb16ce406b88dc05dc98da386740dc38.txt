Photo: Reuters / Toru Hanai SoftBank Group Corp trimmed borrowing from its main bank Mizuho Financial Group Inc by 4.5% in the year ended March to 812 billion yen ($6.38 billion), a filing showed.Borrowing by the Japanese tech conglomerate from JPMorgan Chase & Co fell 23% to 637 billion yen and from Goldman Sachs Group Inc fell 39% to 364 billion yen.The disclosure ahead of the group's annual general meeting on June 24 comes after SoftBank reported a record investment loss at its Vision Fund unit due to weakness in tech stock valuations.
