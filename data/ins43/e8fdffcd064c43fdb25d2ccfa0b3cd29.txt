Buy now, pay later took the lending industry by storm in 2021, with Affirm Holdings (AFRM 7.89%) among the companies affected. The company went public in January 2021 and surged to an eventual high of $176 a share. However, fears over a potential recession and "buy now, pay later" becoming "buy now, pay never" have quickly cooled investor interest in less-proven lenders like Affirm.
The stock price tumbled 92% from its highs and is currently clawing its way up from recent lows. CEO Max Levchin recently spoke at an event, and his commentary revealed a potential game-changer for the company and its investors.
