A family with two children earning less than £24,700 will lose out by over £600 if benefits increase by earnings instead of inflation at 10.1 percent, according to the research by Child Poverty Action Group and Action for Children. The charity highlighted that 'only a fraction of this' will be offset by lowering the level of National Insurance that is paid.
