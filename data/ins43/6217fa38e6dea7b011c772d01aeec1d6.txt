microgen/iStock via Getty Images Crude oil and natural gas prices have moved appreciably higher from the 2020 lows. In April 2020, nearby NYMEX crude oil prices fell to the lowest level in history when the expiring contract fell below zero to negative $40.32 per barrel. The Brent futures dropped to $16 per barrel, the lowest price this century. Natural gas reached a quarter-of-a-century low of $1.44 per MMBtu in June 2020. Since then, prices have exploded higher, with oil and gas reaching the highest prices since 2008.
