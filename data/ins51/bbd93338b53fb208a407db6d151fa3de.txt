When Georgia college students return to their campuses in the fall, they could be in for more spirited intellectual debate than they're used to.

The state Board of Regents, which oversees the state's 26 public universities, voted Tuesday to change their institutions' free speech policies to bring them in line with newly-signed state legislation.
