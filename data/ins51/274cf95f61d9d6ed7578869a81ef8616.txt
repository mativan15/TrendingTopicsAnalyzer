Deutsche Bank Trims Investment Banking Staff Amid Slowdown

(Bloomberg) -- Deutsche Bank AG laid off dozens of origination and advisory staffers within its investment-banking division globally as fears of a recession stymie dealmaking, according to a person with knowledge with the matter.

The firm communicated the job cuts across all levels on Wednesday, with a focus on junior ranks, said the person, who asked not to be identified discussing personnel changes. The reductions are in line with past years' efforts to keep a lid on costs, the person added.
