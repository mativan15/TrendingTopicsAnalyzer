Parents often wish to invest money in their child's name to demarcate and help build a corpus which can come handy in meeting the long-term goals, like education or marriage. They often invest small amounts received as gifts from friends and relatives on birthdays, ceremonies or contribute money themselves regularly in the child's name. 
Can a child invest in a mutual fund scheme? Do mutual funds allow investments in the name of a child? How does it work?Mutual funds accept investment in all schemes that they run in the name of a minor, whose age is less than 18 years.
