Adam Jeffery | CNBC CNBC's Jim Cramer on Wednesday told investors that they should be buying stocks based on the company's financial performance, rather than on whether they like the company's products. "If you don't know how the companies you own shares in will survive an economic hurricane, or even a [Federal Reserve] tightening or two, then just use the product," the "Mad Money" host said.
