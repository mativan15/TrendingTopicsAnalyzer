On the internet, nobody knows you're a dog, or so goes the old joke. But these days, they not only know you're a dog, they know what breed, when you last got wormed, and what's your favorite treat. Trackers and advertisers follow your web surfing, data brokers scrape public records to create detailed (and saleable) personal profiles, and hackers do their best to parlay small lapses in privacy practices into full-blown identity theft.It's nearly impossible to maintain total anonymity and still connect to the internet, but there are things you can do to limit your exposure, from connecting through a VPN to hiring a service that deletes your data from legitimate data aggregators.
