When the pandemic shut down the world in March 2020 and people withdrew to work or study from home, OC Transpo's ridership vanished almost overnight.The transit agency has been hoping to climb back out of that crater ever since. This city has seen more than its share of transit woes — from light rail problems that escalated into a public inquiry to regular complaints about buses that don't show up — but the pandemic seems to have created structural problems the incoming city council will need to confront.
