KPS/iStock via Getty Images Ramen noodle restaurant chain Yoshiharu Global (YOSH) has updated terms for its proposed $17M initial public offering. The California-based Japanese food restaurant group said it now plans to offer 3.8M Class A common shares priced between $4 and $5 apiece, which would raise approximately $17M if priced at the midpoint. Underwriters would be given a 45-day option to buy up to 563K additional shares at the IPO price. EF Hutton is serving as lead bookrunner.
