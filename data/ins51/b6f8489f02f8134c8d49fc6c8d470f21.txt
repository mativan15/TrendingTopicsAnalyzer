(Bloomberg) -- Traders in UK government bonds helped topple Liz Truss. Now they're setting their sights on the next goal: ensuring her successor will stick to the fiscal discipline required to shore up the country's fragile finances.

Should word get out that the party's considering a new prime minister who embraces fiscal largesse or any other unorthodox position, the market reaction, they warn, will be as swift and severe as the wild four-week-long rout that followed Truss's ill-fated proposal to slash taxes and boost spending.
