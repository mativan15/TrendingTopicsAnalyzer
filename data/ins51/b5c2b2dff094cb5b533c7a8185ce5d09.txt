Liz Truss will be the new Anthony Eden in our political vernacular
telegraph.co.uk
news
2022-10-17 12:30:00

Amid the pejorative language being used to describe the chaos that the Conservative Party, by its own actions, has wrought in the country and the economy, it is worth noting one phrase in particular: 'In office but not in power'.
So familiar has this form of words become, so apposite to current circumstances, that its origins may have been lost in time, particularly to those too young to remember the early 1990s.
It was coined by Norman (now Lord) Lamont in the speech he made from the back benches of the Commons following his sacking as Chancellor of the Exchequer by John Major.
