Collider.com Through the words of both marvel and peril, J.R.R. Tolkien used his expertise in linguistics to develop and enrich the lands of Middle-Earth in his novels. Drafting maps and describing in great detail the high mountain peaks and tunneling caves, rolling hills, and crashing oceans. Trees and rock, earth and life sprung from so many of Tolkien's explanations of the beautiful, but often treacherous, landscapes and cities his characters would visit through their many adventures.
