In Maoist politics, 'self-criticism' occupies a central place. Authoritarian despots in communist regimes often use it to engineer purges, but, occasionally, it is a productive exercise used to make a departure from a set of policies. No one expected the most ideologically Maoist leader after Mao Zedong to engage in any self-criticism.
