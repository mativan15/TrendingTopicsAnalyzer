Gen Z is reportedly using TikTok over Google to get answers to everyday questions. The social media app makes it easy to find entertaining videos offering tips and advice for just about everything -- including money.More than one-third of people in Gen Z turn to TikTok for financial advice. While more young people seeking financial advice indicates a positive trend for financial literacy, the problem is that some of this advice might not be accurate. And some may even result in legal trouble. 
We spoke with an attorney and a credit counselor who've seen firsthand what can happen when people listen to bad money advice on TikTok.
