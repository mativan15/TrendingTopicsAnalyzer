The Sherman Act to-Morrow
theatlantic.com
news
2022-05-28 19:00:00

IN the March issue of the Atlantic, in a paper entitled ‘The Sherman Act To-day,'I made an effort to point out some of the baneful effects of our so-called antitrust laws, due almost wholly to the perverted economic philosophy that has been set up as a basis for the administration of these laws. Chief among those effects was a direct responsibility for that prevailing ‘profitless prosperity' so amply disclosed by official statistics, and for furnishing the incentive for a large part of the increasing tendency toward ‘horizontal' combinations of capital that would otherwise be unnecessary.
