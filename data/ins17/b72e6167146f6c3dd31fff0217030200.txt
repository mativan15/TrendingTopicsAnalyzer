Navin Fluorine International, a part of the commodity chemical space, is down by about 5% from its September high but technical setup suggests that the stock could bounce back from crucial support levels to hit fresh record highs in the next few months. The stock with a market capitalisation of more than Rs 22,000 crore hit a 52-week high of Rs 4,847 on September 15, but failed to hold on to the momentum and took support above Rs 4,300 levels
