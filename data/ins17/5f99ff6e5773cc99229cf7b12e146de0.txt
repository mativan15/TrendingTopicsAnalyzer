By Colin Kellaher F.N.B. Corp. agreed to buy fellow bank-holding company UB Bancorp for about $117 million in stock in a deal that bolsters F.N.B.'s presence in North Carolina, it said Wednesday. Pittsburgh-based F.N.B. will issue 1.61 shares, worth $19.56 based on Tuesday's closing price of $12.15, for each share of UB, F.N.B. said. The deal represents a 10.2% premium to Tuesday's closing price of $17.75 for Greenville, N.C.-based UB, which has 15 banking offices and about $1.2 billion in total assets.
