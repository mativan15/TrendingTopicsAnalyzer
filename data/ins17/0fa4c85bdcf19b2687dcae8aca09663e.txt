Billionaire activist Nelson Peltz speaks at the WSJD Live conference in Laguna Beach, Calif. on Oct. 25, 2016.Mike Blake/ReutersBillionaire activist Nelson Peltz will join the board of Unilever, the consumer goods giant said on Tuesday, heaping pressure on the maker of Dove soap and Hellmann's mayonnaise as it reviews its strategy.Shares in the London-listed group jumped 7% as it said Peltz, whose hedge fund Trian has built up a 1.5% stake in Unilever, will join as a non-executive director from July and serve as a member of the board's compensation committee.
