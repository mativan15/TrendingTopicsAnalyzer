By Huw Jones

LONDON, Oct 20 (Reuters) - Stock markets drifted lower on Thursday as bond yields climbed ahead of the next round of hefty central bank interest rate hikes starting next week, with an update from electric carmaker Tesla rattling Wall Street.

Crude oil prices rose on tight supplies, and the yen tumbled past the key psychological level of 150 against the dollar to hit a 32-year low, prompting fresh threats of intervention from Japan's finance ministry.
