Volkswagen CEO Herbert Diess attends the Munich Auto Show on Sept. 7, 2021.WOLFGANG RATTAY/ReutersVolkswagen plans to continue operations at its plant in China's Xinjiang region despite reports of human rights violations there, the chief executive said in comments published by the Handelsblatt newspaper on Monday.The German car maker, which has a joint venture with China's biggest automaker SAIC Motor, has since 2013 run a plant in Urumqi, the capital of the western region where Western nations and rights groups say ethnic Uyghurs face torture and detention.
