Paterson Police Sergeant Convicted of Conspiracy to Violate Civil Rights and Filing False Police Report
justice.gov
news
2022-05-27 02:10:05

NEWARK, N.J. – A federal jury convicted a Paterson Police Department sergeant today of conspiring to violate civil rights and filing a false police report, U.S. Attorney Philip R. Sellinger announced. Michael Cheff, 51, of Oakland, New Jersey, was convicted following a five-day trial before U.S. District Judge Katharine S. Hayden on one count of conspiracy to deprive persons of civil rights and one count of falsification of a police report. According to documents filed in this case and evidence at trial: Eudy Ramos, Daniel Pent, Jonathan Bustios, Matthew Torres, and Frank Toledo were police officers with the Paterson Police Department.
