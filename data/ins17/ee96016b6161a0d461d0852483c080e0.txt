The Supreme Court on Friday rejected a plea seeking reconsideration of a July verdict where it upheld the key provisions of the Prevention of Money Laundering Act (PMLA). Two-judge bench comprising Chief Justice of India UU Lalit and justice Bela M Trivedi dismissed the plea which challenged the constitutional validity of Section 37 of the Act that stipulates the power of the chairperson of the PMLA Authority to transfer cases.
