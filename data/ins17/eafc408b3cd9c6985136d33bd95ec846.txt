The extent of the cuts, which have not been previously reported, help explain why Twitter officials were eager to sell to Musk: Musk's $44 billion bid, though hostile, is a golden ticket for the struggling company - potentially helping its leadership avoid painful announcements that would have demoralized the staff and possibly crippled the service's ability to combat misinformation, hate speech and spam.The impact of such layoffs would likely be immediately felt by millions of users, said Edwin Chen, a data scientist formerly in charge of Twitter's spam and health metrics and now CEO of the content-moderation start-up Surge AI.
