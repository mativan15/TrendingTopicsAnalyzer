(Bloomberg) -- Goldman Sachs Group Inc.'s David Solomon is embarking on his third major reorganization in just four years as chief executive officer -- undoing some of the signature moves he made as recently as 2020 and spotlighting a new class of his most senior lieutenants.

Most Read from Bloomberg

The Wall Street giant plans to once again combine its expanded asset-management and private-wealth businesses into one unit run by Marc Nachmann, according to people familiar with the matter. Goldman will also fuse its investment-banking and trading operations under one group run by Dan Dees, Jim Esposito and Ashok Varadhan.
