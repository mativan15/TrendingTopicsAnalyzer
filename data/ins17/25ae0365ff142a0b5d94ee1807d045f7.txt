Two Essentials for a Stable and Secure Relationship
psychologytoday.com
news
2022-10-20 13:43:02

Most people seek stability and security in their relationships, but few achieve it. Secure relationships are generally associated with lower levels of stress, higher levels of comfort, and greater ability to negotiate differences and problems. Commitment and not expressing anger are two characteristics of intimate relationships that support security and stability. Unstable/insecure relationships are transactional: They are characterized by the last interaction. Any frustration or dissatisfaction is seen as the current status of the relationship as a whole.
