What's missing?If we look at the traditional way of doing things, an insurance company generally looks at your age, past driving records (accidents, traffic violations), medical history, and specially credit score to come up with an insurance premium. That means, drivers with poor credit score have to pay higher prices. Forbes estimates, drivers with poor credit pays nearly $1,180 more per year compared to drivers with good credit.What's missing is a smarter insurance eco-system, that looks at your driving over time, and provides insurance, discounts, rewards based on your driving skills and usage.
