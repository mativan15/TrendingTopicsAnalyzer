San Antonio's First NewsOct 20, 2022Photo: Getty ImagesA newly filed lawsuit is accusing Google of collecting a massive database of voice and facial features without users' consent. Texas Attorney General Ken Paxton says that's against the law. The complaint suggests that Google Nest smart home products are actively listening and recording what they hear. That includes children and visitors, who may not want to be recorded. The suit goes on to claim Google is using the information to improve its artificial intelligence algorithms.
