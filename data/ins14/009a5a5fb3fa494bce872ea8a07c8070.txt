nimis69 Sometimes, what looks like a value stock may in fact just be a mediocre business that deserves to trade at a low price. One telltale sign of this is that, even though shares are already priced at a low level, the company's stock will still move more or less in tandem with a broader market. A great example of this can be seen when looking at MRC Global (NYSE:MRC), a global distributor of pipes, valves, and fittings dedicated to the diversified energy, industrial, and gas utility end markets.
