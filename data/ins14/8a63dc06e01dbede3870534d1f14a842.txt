For decades, the story of Betsy Ross, the patriotic widow who sewed the first American flag (thirteen alternating red and white stripes and thirteen white five-pointed stars arranged in a circle against a blue background) and presented it to General George Washington in her Philadelphia parlor, was a staple in American classrooms. It turns out the legend doesn't hold up to scrutiny, and most historians believe it originated in the somewhat-embellished tales that an elderly Ross told to her family.
