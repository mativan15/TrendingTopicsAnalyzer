Tesla Inc on Wednesday posted record third-quarter revenue but still missed Wall Street estimates as the electric carmaker led by billionaire Elon Musk delivered fewer vehicles than expected, while spending on new factories and new battery production squeezed margins.The company did not set out year-end delivery targets and failed to deliver a hoped-for stock buyback program, disappointing some with its quarterly report.
