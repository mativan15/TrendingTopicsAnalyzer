If it comes down to a putt on the 18th hole to win this week's US Open, former world No.1 Lydia Ko is adamant the record $14m purse, and $2.5m winner's cheque, won't be on anyone's mind.But Australia's world No.4 Minjee Lee, a major champion already, said there was no denying the 'huge step' forward the historic prizemoney increase has been not just for this week's event but for women's golf.Lee is leading a five-player Australian charge at the second major of the year at Pine Needles in North Carolina along with fellow major champ Hannah Green, Sarah Kemp and rising stars Grace Kim and Gabi Ruffels.
