RMT boss says more UK workers should push for strikes

Image caption, Mick Lynch said some unions were "limp" in their negotiationsThe leader of one of the UK's biggest transport unions has said he hopes workers in many industries follow rail staff in backing nationwide strikes.RMT general secretary Mick Lynch told the BBC's Nick Robinson that employees deserved protection from poor conditions and "rampant" inflation.His union has voted heavily in favour of industrial action, which could start as early as next month.The government has called the move "hugely disappointing".
