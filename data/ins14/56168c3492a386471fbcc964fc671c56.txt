When a tech team launches a new project, it's always with the hope that they're setting out to create a great product that will delight users. However, not every tech project is destined to reach the finish line. Sometimes, a tech company must cut its losses and pull the plug on a project, no matter how much initial enthusiasm it generated or how personally invested the team might be. It's essential for tech leaders to learn (and accept) the clear, objective signs that a project—even one they've personally championed—simply isn't viable.
