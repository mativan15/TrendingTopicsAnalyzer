New study shows Gen Z and millennials are struggling financially
ctvnews.ca
economics
2022-05-30 19:27:18

According to Rev. Kyle Wagner, the food bank and community fridge at Christ Church in Dartmouth, N.S., are both being used on a daily basis 'We probably have close to 70 clients each week here,' said Wagner, who added the people who need help come from a variety of ages, including thosein their 20s and 30s who are employed with careers. 'We are seeing younger and younger people come, people who are working and the working poor.' According to a new study released by Deloitte, 46 per cent of Gen Z members -- those born between 1995 and 2003 and 47 per cent of millennials -- peopleborn between 1983 and 1994 -- are dealingwith financial stress and living paycheque-to-paycheque.
