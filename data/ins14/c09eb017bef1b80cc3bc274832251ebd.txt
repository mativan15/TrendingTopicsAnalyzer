How Mdou Moctar's Music Went Viral Via Bluetooth
wired.com
news
2022-06-01 19:17:44

Depending on where you are in the world, life after the death of physical music looks very different. In West Africa, the post-CD landscape was populated by market stalls selling SIM and micro-SD cards preloaded with tracks. Eventually, pals started sharing songs directly by Bluetooth, phone to phone. After WhatsApp began to spread in the 2010s, you didn't have to be in physical proximity to send your buddies the bangers. It wasn't links being shared, though, but highly compressed MP3s, which stood a better chance of getting to their destination.
