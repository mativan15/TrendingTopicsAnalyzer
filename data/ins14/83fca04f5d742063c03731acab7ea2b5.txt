Image source, GoogleImage caption, The building dates back to 1900 and was formerly a shoe and hosiery factoryA council wants to buy a £5.5m block of flats to create new affordable homes.Leicester City Council has outlined plans to purchase the Zip Building so it can add 58 flats and bedsits to its housing stock.The three storey block, in Rydal Street near the Leicester Royal Infirmary, will be bought using £2.4m generated from selling other council homes under Right to Buy laws.The authority will rent the flats out to people on its housing waiting list.
