The School for Good and Evil review – Netflix's Harry Potter rip-off is a disaster

JK Rowling has done a lot in recent years to lower the public's estimation of her professional achievements, but Netflix's charmless fantasy The School for Good and Evil teaches the object lesson that conjuring a phenomenon on par with Harry Potter isn't as simple as scribbling on a cocktail napkin.Novelist Soman Chainani and writer-director Paul Feig – a man doing his darnedest to erase all memory that he once possessed the power to make us laugh – set out to reproduce that generational smash with another tale of misfit teens spirited away to a magical cliffside academy weirdly attached to its flawed organizational system.
