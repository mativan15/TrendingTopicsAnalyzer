Op-Ed: After Davos, Going ‘Long' on Optimism in an Anxious World
nbcchicago.com
business
2022-05-29 14:50:30

DAVOS, SWITZERLAND – A hedge fund investor told me she attends the World Economic Forum each year "so that I'll know what to short." In a world awash with geopolitical and economic pessimism, the dominant mood at this year's Davos, her argument is that it might be time to go "long" on optimism. One can quibble with her premise that Davos is a place more for conventional wisdom than investable solutions. As the world's leading convener of global and business elites for most of the past half-century, the WEF often has been ahead in identifying trends, including the Fourth Industrial Revolution, and in generating positive change, such as CEOs' increased focus on social responsibility.
