The National Green Tribunal on Friday told the forest officials of Uttarakhand that it would direct the Union government to form a committee to probe the matter of ‘illegal tree cutting' in Jim Corbett National Park.The apex green court had earlier taken cognisance of The Hindu's report on felling of trees at the Corbett Tiger Reserve. The report had quoted a Forest Survey of India (FSI) report saying that over 6,000 trees were illegally cut to facilitate the Pakhru tiger safari project at the tiger reserve.
