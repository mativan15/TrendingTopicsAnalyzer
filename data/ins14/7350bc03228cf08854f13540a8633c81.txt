As more rail strikes get under way in Great Britain, the government has announced that from next year 'minimum service levels' will be imposed to avoid a compete shutdown of transport links.Ministers will introduced a bill in Parliament as early as this week to ensure a certain number of trains, Tubes or buses continue to run even during industrial action. But will it work – and, if so, how?We have just had the first weekend in October without a national rail strike, but various regional disputes are happening.
