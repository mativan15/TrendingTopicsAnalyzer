Justin Sullivan/Getty Images News Dell Technologies (NYSE:DELL) shares rocketed up by almost 10% in pre-market trading, Friday, as Wall Street threw its weight behind the computing giant following its upbeat outlook and better-than-expected first-quarter earnings report. Late Thursday, Dell (DELL) Chief Financial Officer Tom Sweet said on a conference call that the company expects its second-quarter earnings, excluding one-time items, will be between $1.55 and $1.70 a share, with revenue in a range of $26.
