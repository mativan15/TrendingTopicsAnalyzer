Microsoft parts ways with Covington & Burling
politico.com
news
2022-06-01 00:00:00

With Daniel LippmanMICROSOFT PARTS WAYS WITH COVINGTON & BURLING: Covington & Burling has ended its lobbying work for Microsoft after more than two decades representing the software giant in Washington, according to a termination filed Tuesday. Covington has lobbied for Microsoft since at least 1999, the earliest date lobbying disclosures are available online. Disclosures show that during the past 23 years the company has paid Covington & Burling more than $10 million to lobby on issues like net neutrality, privacy, national security, immigration, tax and telecommunications.
