BMS seeks affiliation with ITUC, two Indian trade unions oppose it

The Bharatiya Mazdoor Sangh (BMS), a trade union supported by the Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh (RSS), is facing stiff opposition from two Indian unions — the Indian National Trade Union Congress (INTUC) of the Congress and the Hind Mazdoor Sabha (HMS) of the socialists — in its bid to become an affiliate of the International Trade Union Confederation (ITUC).Both INTUC and HMS, besides the Self Employed Women's Association (SEWA)and the Confederation of Free Trade Unions of India (CFTUI), are Indian affiliates of ITUC, an international body of independent trade unions from 187 countries.
