Apple, Google, and Microsoft have big plans to eliminate the password.Working with a standards group called the FIDO Alliance, all three companies are backing a system in which your phone or computer signs you into all your online accounts automatically, using on-device face detection or fingerprint recognition to verify your identity. Think of it like having a password manager, but without the actual passwords.News of the password's potential demise has yielded a lot of enthusiastic coverage in recent weeks, and for an understandable reason: Too many people use the same weak passwords everywhere, leaving themselves open to serious security risks.
