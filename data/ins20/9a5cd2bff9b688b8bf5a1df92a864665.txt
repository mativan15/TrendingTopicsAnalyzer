By Reuters Published: 23:44 EDT, 20 October 2022 | Updated: 23:44 EDT, 20 October 2022 BENGALURU, Oct 21 (Reuters) - Indian digital payments firm PhonePe is in talks to raise funds in a round led by General Atlantic at a valuation of $12 billion, financial news website Moneycontrol reported on Friday, citing people aware of the matter. U.S. private equity group General Atlantic is likely to lead the round with an investment of $450 million to $500 million, the report said. The round would make Walmart Inc-backed PhonePe the most valuable Indian financial tech firm, surpassing Razorpay and Paytm, a unit of One 97 Communications, the report added.
