Published: 01:04 EDT, 19 October 2022 | Updated: 01:04 EDT, 19 October 2022 By Lewis Krauskopf NEW YORK, Oct 19 (Reuters) - Some gauges of the stock market's health are showing that the latest rally in U.S. equities may be the start of a sustained move higher, though many investors are hesitant to jump on board until there are signs inflation is cooling. Few can blame them for being skeptical. The current gain - which has seen the S&P 500 bounce about 6.5% last week's fresh intraday low for 2022 - comes on the heels of several rebounds throughout the year that eventually crumbled.
