By Associated Press Published: 18:55 EDT, 20 October 2022 | Updated: 18:55 EDT, 20 October 2022 NEW YORK (AP) - Bolivia's former interior minister pleaded guilty on Thursday to conspiracy to launder bribes that a Florida-based company paid him in exchange for helping it secure a contract to provide tear gas to the Bolivian government, said the U.S. Department of Justice. Arturo Murillo, 58, who was arrested last year in the U.S., received at least $532,000 in bribe payments. He served on former interim Bolivian President Jeanine Áñez's government.
