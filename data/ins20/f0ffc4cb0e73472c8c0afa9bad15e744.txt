Publishing in the Dark
theatlantic.com
news
2022-05-29 19:15:00

IF SUCH a thing as Book Publishers' Row existed, there would undoubtedly arise from it, above the thunderous rolling of the giant presses and the raucous shoutings of Book Salesmen hawking their wares, a thin crying or keening, as of strong men in pain. This occupational moan would faintly express the mental anguish of Publishers and Editors, unable to estimate in advance the public's reaction to the books they are about to market. In their sanctums they chant to one another the grievous refrain that publishing a new book is as hazardous as producing a play on Broadway.
