Actor Colin Farrell, left, filmmaker Martin McDonagh, center, and actor Brendan Gleeson pose for a portrait to promote The Banshees of Inisherin on Oct. 11, 2022, in New York.Matt Licari/The Associated Press'Time be flyin',' it's said in Martin McDonagh's The Banshees of Inisherin. It's a sentiment shared by McDonagh and his two stars, Colin Farrell and Brendan Gleeson, who have re-teamed 14 years after McDonagh's pitch-black feature debut, In Bruges.'It feels like not two days of passing,' McDonagh said, shaking his head, on a recent fall day in New York while Farrell and Gleeson, sitting beside him, eagerly agree.
