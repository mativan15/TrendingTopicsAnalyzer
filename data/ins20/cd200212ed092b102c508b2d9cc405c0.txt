ReutersLast night, despite the fact that many Conservatives had moral objections to fracking, Conservative whips insisted that all members of the party vote against Labour's motion. This led to turmoil in Westminster. Cabinet ministers have been charged with coercing, intimidating and even "manhandling" colleagues into supporting the government's position on fracking.

They would face the threat of having the whip taken away if they disobeyed this.
The Conservative Chief Whip was Wendy Morton, but she has reportedly resigned.
