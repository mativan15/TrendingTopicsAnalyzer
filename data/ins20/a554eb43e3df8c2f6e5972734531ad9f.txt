Buncombe County Commissioners voted Oct. 18 to accept nearly $2.25 million in grants to help prevent crime and help victims of crime.

ASHEVILLE - Buncombe County's efforts to prevent crime and help victims is getting a federal infusion of nearly $2.25 million following two grant approvals that passed Board of Commissioners in a unanimous Oct. 18 vote.

In a budget amendment for the county's Justice Services Department, commissioners greenlighted grant money from the Bureau of Justice Affairs for $750,000 and the United States Department of Justice grant of nearly $1.
