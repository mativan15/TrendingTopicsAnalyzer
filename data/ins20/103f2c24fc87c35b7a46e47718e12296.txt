Still unable to pay off what he borrowed more than a quarter-century later, he said, he owes the federal government more than $63,000 for a degree he never received.'For me to be retrained, I had to take out student loans,' Chaney, 56, said. 'I had two babies and a disabled wife at the time, and I didn't have the choice.'Millions of Americans like Chaney borrowed to finance an education they didn't complete. The reasons vary — the coursework didn't fit their skills, illness, or family circumstances foiled their academic careers — but the result is the same: debt, sometimes in the tens of thousands of dollars, but no degree.
