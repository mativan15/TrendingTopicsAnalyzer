Off the top of his head, Shehan Karunatilaka can think of at least three plot lines for a potential novel from the massive people's protests that the economic crisis in Sri Lanka triggered earlier this year against its political leadership: 'There can be one in a petrol queue. There are so many characters in there, definitely a potential drama; Aragalaya (Sinhalese word for struggle) went on for three months. You can do an aragalaya love story. And because it's so well-documented, your research is already there, you can do a story about a family.
