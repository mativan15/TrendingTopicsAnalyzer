UK financial markets will get a fresh opportunity to pass judgment on Prime Minister Liz Truss's economic program on Monday without the Bank of England around to offset any turmoil.

Rarely has a developed economy's political fortune been so caught up in market gyrations. Traders' brutal rejection of the premier's fiscal plan have already sent the pound to record lows and forced Truss to sack Chancellor Kwasi Kwarteng, leading to the rollback of some of her marquee tax cuts.
