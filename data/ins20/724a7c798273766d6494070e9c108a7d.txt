By Reuters Published: 09:49 EDT, 21 October 2022 | Updated: 10:04 EDT, 21 October 2022 By Uditha Jayasinghe COLOMBO, Oct 21 (Reuters) - Sri Lanka's parliament on Friday passed a constitutional amendment aimed at trimming presidential powers, beefing up anti-corruption safeguards and helping to find a way out of the country's worst financial crisis since independence. Sri Lanka has struggled for months to find enough dollars to pay for essential imports such as fuel, food, cooking gas and medicine.
