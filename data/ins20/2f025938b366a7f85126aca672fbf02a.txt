Here are five things in technology that happened this past week and how they affect your business. Did you miss them? 1—IBM's Lotus Software has been revived to compete with Microsoft's long technological reign. The 40-year-old program Lotus 1-2-3 was reverse engineered to be 100 percent usable on Linux platforms. This program was popular in the 1980s for offering spreadsheet calculations, database functionality, and graphical charts. However - since Microsoft's introduction of GUI-based products in the 1990s - the IBM program became inferior.
