By Reuters Published: 13:04 EDT, 18 October 2022 | Updated: 13:04 EDT, 18 October 2022 By Nell Mackenzie Oct 18 (Reuters) - The U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission aims to shrink hedge fund fees so that more money winds up in the hands of the asset managers and pension funds that invest in them, one of its top officials said on Tuesday. William Birdthistle, director of the Division of Investment Management at the SEC, said the U.S. watchdog remained committed to increasing transparency and competition in the industry.
