Bo Hopkins, a prolific actor known for his roles in the seminal Oscar-winning drama 'Midnight Express' and George Lucas' 'American Graffiti,' has died at 80.

His wife of 33 years, Sian, confirmed that Hopkins died Saturday morning at Valley Presbyterian Hospital in Van Nuys, California, as a result of a heart attack suffered on May 9, per the Hollywood Reporter.

Hopkins racked up over 130 acting credits over his half-century-long career and was most known for playing prominent supporting roles in major studio films between the late '60s and late '70s.
