Published: 23:30 EDT, 19 October 2022 | Updated: 23:30 EDT, 19 October 2022 A lack of nightlife, excessive airfare prices and the emergence of cheaper alternatives have seen Australia become an afterthought for international travellers - with the country's tourism board launching a new ad campaign in a desperate attempt to attract foreigners.Despite borders reopening to the world in February, the interest in heading Down Under remains vastly lower than pre-pandemic levels.There were 326,000 international arrivals during July, 2022 - 59 per cent lower than the same month in 2019 (790,360).
