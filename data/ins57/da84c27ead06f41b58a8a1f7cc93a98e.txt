By Reuters Published: 13:55 EDT, 17 October 2022 | Updated: 13:55 EDT, 17 October 2022 PARIS, Oct 17 (Reuters) - Some 28% of Frances's petrol stations still faced supply problems as of 1400 GMT on Monday against about 30% on Sunday, due to weeks-long refineries strikes, the French energy minister said on Monday. Agnes Pannier-Runacher told RTL radio the government, which has ordered more fuel depot staff back to work to try to restore petrol supplies, was doing all it can "so that the situation improves on the eve of the Autumn school holidays," that start on Saturday.
