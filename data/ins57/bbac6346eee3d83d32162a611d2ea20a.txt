Jupiter Fund Management hit by £600m client exodus
thetimes.co.uk
news
2022-10-20 17:00:00

Falling markets, client defections, mutterings about substandard performance for high fees — it has been a grisly time for mainstream fund managers, and no one embodies that struggle more clearly than embattled Jupiter Fund Management.The London-based company, once a high-flying business with a £3 billion valuation, was yesterday forced to signal it was roughly halving the dividend as it reported another net £600 million of client business walked out the door in the three months to September.Quarter after quarter, Jupiter has been reporting net client outflows, which amount to £8 billion over the past seven quarters.
