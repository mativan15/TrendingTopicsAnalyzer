An Atlantic PortraitBY CHARLES RUMFORD WALKERITwo weeks before John Gilbert Winant went to England as our Ambassador he flew to Concord, New Hampshire, to address ‘the largest crowd to assemble in Representative Hall within memory, estimated at one thousand persons.' Half of these persons were New Hampshire legislators; the other half were friends and neighbors — with a sprinkling of enemies, too — from all over the state, who had come to hear his farewell address.The man who had three times been their Governor told them: ‘No compliment you could have paid me could mean more to me than to meet with you in these Legislative Halls before leaving for England.
