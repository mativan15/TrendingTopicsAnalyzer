Dame Esther Rantzen has congratulated the BBC on reaching 100 years of broadcasting, adding: 'You are enormously valuable not only to Britain, but to journalism around the world.'The corporation is marking a century since it was founded on October 18 1922, under its original name, the British Broadcasting Company.Dame Esther, 82, presented BBC One's That's Life! for 21 years, created and hosted Hearts Of Gold in the late 1980s on the same channel, and later presented her own chat show, Esther, on BBC Two.
