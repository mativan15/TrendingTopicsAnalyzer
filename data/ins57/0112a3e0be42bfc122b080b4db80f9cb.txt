What Bills Do You Pay?

INVARIABLY, inexorably, the second of January arrives, bringing with it the usual delayed Christmas cards and the usual stack of bills for all those beautiful, necessary, extravagant, vital things you bought in December — underwear, eggs, toys, furs, groceries, porcelain, doctor's house visit, coal, poultry, club dues, milk, millinery, and the gold inlay for Junior's tooth.What do you do? Sit right down and write checks for them all? Or do you add the disconcerting totals, flush, and find you've gone and slipped over the limit again? If so, what bills do you pay first?Does it go something like this? ‘H'm-m, that underwear was mostly gifts, and I've got to get some for myself next month — I'd better pay it now.
