Sunak's Tax Helps Consumers But Misses the Energy Mark
bqprime.com
business
2022-05-29 07:03:48

(Bloomberg Opinion) -- The UK government's 25% windfall tax on oil and gas company profits, along with the accompanying investment allowance that will let them avoid most of it, will do little to address the country's pressing energy needs.Speaking in parliament on Thursday, Chancellor of the Exchequer Rishi Sunak, didn't dare utter the words 'windfall tax' — something the opposition parties had been urging him to impose for months —  referring to it instead as a special levy. It amounts to the same thing.
