Bradford noise-detecting camera to crack down on boy racers

news
2022-10-18 05:14:22

Image source, ReutersImage caption, The government says road noise pollution costs the country £10bn a yearA noise-detecting traffic camera is to be trialled as part of a crackdown on "boy racers", the government has said.The camera is fitted with microphones to record vehicle noise and provide digital evidence to police.It will be used in Keighley for two weeks before moving to Bristol, Great Yarmouth and Birmingham.The Department for Transport says it is part of a £300,000 scheme to reduce the social impact of road noise, which it says costs the country £10bn a year.
