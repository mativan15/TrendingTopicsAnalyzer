Grain of truth

Retail cereal inflation, at over 11.5 per cent year-on-year in September, is at its highest since end-2013. This, even as total stocks of wheat and rice in government godowns have depleted to a five-year low. Such a scenario was unimaginable at the start of the year when the same warehouses were overflowing. In 2021-22, not only an unprecedented 105.6 million tonnes (mt) of grain got channeled through the public distribution system, the country also exported a record 28.4 mt of the two cereals. The debate, until quite recently, was on how to achieve both cropping and dietary diversification: Weaning away farmers from paddy, wheat and sugarcane and ensuring that the poor had greater access to foods rich in proteins, vitamins, minerals and fibre than just carbohydrates and calories.
