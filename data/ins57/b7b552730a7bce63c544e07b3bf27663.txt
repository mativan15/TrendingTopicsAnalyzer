Gen Z is stuck in between two crises not of their own making — a typical tale for those born between 1997 and 2012 — and they can't decide which one is more pressing.A new Wall Street Journal article delves into one of the debates that Gen Z jobseekers are coming up against: Whether they look for a role that pays well, or aligns with their social values. As inflation rises, it's become murkier.Personal values have long been a priority for Gen Z as they enter the workforce en masse. Nearly half of Gen Zers surveyed in a Workmonitor global study by Randstad of 35,000 workers said that they wouldn't accept a role at a company that doesn't align with social and environmental views.
