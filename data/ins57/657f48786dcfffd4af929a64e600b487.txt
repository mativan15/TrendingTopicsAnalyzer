Women's World Cup draw to set the stage for 32-team soccer tournament next year

2022-10-21 12:48:40

Canada will learn its path at next year's Women's World Cup early Saturday with coach Bev Priestman and Canada Soccer general secretary Earl Cochrane in the audience for the draw in Auckland, New Zealand. Former players Carli Lloyd, Alexi Lalas, Gilberto Silva and Ian Wright are among those taking part in the ceremony, which decides the eight groups for the expanded 32-team field for the soccer showcase slated to run July 20 to Aug. 20 in Australia and New Zealand. The top two in each pool will advance to the round of 16.
