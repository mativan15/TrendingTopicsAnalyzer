People experiencing mental health problems who fall victim to fraud are less likely than those without such conditions to get their money back, according to Which?The consumer group surveyed more than 1,000 fraud victims and found that six in 10 (58%) with a diagnosed mental health problem got all or some of their money back.Nearly seven in 10 (68%) fraud victims with no mental health condition were refunded.People with mental health conditions were around twice as likely to not have enough money for essentials (29%) and go into debt (26%) as a result of fraud compared to victims with no mental health conditions (15% and 12% respectively).
