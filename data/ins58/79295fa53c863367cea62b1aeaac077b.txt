AgenciesInvestors are consumed with big-picture considerations,' said Steve Sosnick, chief strategist at Interactive Brokers. 'It's just not human nature to be able to necessarily zag when everyone's zigging -- to be thinking in terms of bottom-up clarity, when the top-down is moving around so dramatically.'Day-to-day odds-making on whether the Federal Reserve will cause a recession has become the only thing that matters in equities. One day feast, next day famine -- obsession with a single input, the economy, has driven measures of correlation among individual stocks to the highest since the coronavirus crash.
