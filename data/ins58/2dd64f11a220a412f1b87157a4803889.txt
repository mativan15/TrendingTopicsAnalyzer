By Reuters Published: 16:53 EDT, 3 June 2022 | Updated: 16:53 EDT, 3 June 2022 By Fergal Smith TORONTO, June 3 (Reuters) - Canada's main stock index fell on Friday, pulling back from its highest level in nearly one month the day before, as robust U.S. jobs data bolstered expectations for aggressive interest rate hikes by the Federal Reserve. The Toronto Stock Exchange's S&P/TSX composite index ended down 241.08 points, or 1.2%, at 20,790.73, after posting on Thursday its highest closing level since May 4.
