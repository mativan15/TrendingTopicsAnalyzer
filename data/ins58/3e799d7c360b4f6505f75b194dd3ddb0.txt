Published: 02:23 EDT, 31 May 2022 | Updated: 02:23 EDT, 31 May 2022 By Tom Westbrook and Xie Yu SINGAPORE/HONG KONG, May 31 (Reuters) - Asian shares clawed back earlier losses on Tuesday as signs that China's economic pain may be gradually abating amid easing COVID-19 curbs overshadowed broader investor concerns about a global inflation shock. Also lifting sentiment in the region were details of Beijing's new policy support, which includes cash handouts for hiring graduates and support for internet companies' offshore listings.
