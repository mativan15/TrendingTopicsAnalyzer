Collider.com Though this October does belong to Michael, as he's basking in one of his many final films Halloween Ends, might we take a moment to pause and reflect on another silent killer? In 1974, a visionary by the name of Tobe Hooper unleashed his debut feature film onto the world, changing the face of horror indefinitely. The Texas Chain Saw Massacre rose from the bowels of the mid-seventies to reinvent a genre that, while still on the fringes of cinema, was otherwise romanticized in films like Psycho, Rosemary's Baby and even Night of the Living Dead.
