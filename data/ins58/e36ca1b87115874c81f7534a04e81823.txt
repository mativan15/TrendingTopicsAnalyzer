(Bloomberg) -- Thailand's parliament will put to vote an initial draft of the $93 billion annual budget on Thursday, posing a key test to coup-leader-turned Prime Minister Prayuth Chan-Ocha whose ruling coalition is coming under some strain from internal divisions.

The fate of the budget hangs in balance as some smaller parties try to extract concessions and distance themselves from Prayuth's government, which has struggled to revive the pandemic-battered economy ahead of elections that must be called by March next year.
