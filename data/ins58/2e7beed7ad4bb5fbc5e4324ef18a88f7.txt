Number of zero-hours contracts up 75% since ‘review' promised a decade ago
msn.com
news
2022-10-19 23:11:00

The use of zero-hours contracts has soared by 75 per cent since the government pledged to review the controversial practice nearly a decade ago, a new analysis shows. 
 

 © EPA Labour Party Conference in Liverpool


And there has been an 86 per cent leap in the number of workers aged 35-49 placed on the arrangements – forcing more families to 'rely on insecure work', Labour is protesting.Its study of official figures is revealed as Labour vows to ban zero-hours contracts – which put staff on standby, with no guarantee of any minimum work hours – as part of a New Deal for Working People.
