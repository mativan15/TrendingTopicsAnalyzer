Nurses union rejects latest pay offer from WA government including staff-to-patient ratios
abc.net.au
news
2022-10-17 23:58:11

The Australian Nursing Federation has rejected an offer on pay and conditions from the WA government on the eve of launching a campaign of escalating industrial action.The union says the latest offer will bring no relief to excessive workloadsThe offer was detailed in a newspaper before being sent to the unionThe WA government has rejected nurses' demands for a 10pc pay riseNurses and midwives have been calling for better pay and nurse-to-patient ratios.The latest deal on the table from the state government does not change the proposed pay offer, giving nurses either a $60 per week increase or a three per cent annual pay rise, whichever is greater, plus a one-off $3,000 cost of living payment.
