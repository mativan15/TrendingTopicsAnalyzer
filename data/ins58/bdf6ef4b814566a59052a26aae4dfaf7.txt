The Independent's petition calling for a general election to be held in the UK following recent political furore has surpassed 200,000 signatures.As of 6.25pm on Thursday, after Liz Truss quit as prime minister following just 45 days in office, the petition had been signed by more than 200,400 people – up by some 50,000 signatures in just one hour.It comes after weeks of chaos in Downing Street as Ms Truss's serial U-turning saw her fail to maintain control of her own party – bedlam puntuated by reports of 'manhandling' and 'bullying' during last night's fracking vote.
