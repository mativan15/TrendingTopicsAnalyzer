Egypt signs €8 billion deal with Siemens for high-speed rail system
dw.com
news
2022-05-29 00:00:00

The German company said it has sealed the biggest order in its history to build the world's sixth-largest rail network. Tracks will connect cities along the Nile with the Red and Mediterranean seas.

Siemens CEO Roland Busch called the ordoor the biggest in the company's history
The German industrial group Siemens said on Saturday evening that it had agreed on a deal to construct 2,000 kilometers (1,243 miles) of high-speed rail lines across Egypt.
The deal, which is worth €8.1 billion ($8.7 billion), is the biggest order in the Munich-based company's 175-year history.
