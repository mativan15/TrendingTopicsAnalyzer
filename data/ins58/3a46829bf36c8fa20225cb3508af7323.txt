REUTERS/Maja Smiejkowska/FilesLONDON, Oct 21 (Reuters) - The Bank of England's stockpile of government bonds has incurred the first loss for the public finances since the central bank launched its quantitative easing programme in 2009, data showed on Friday.Rising borrowing costs led to a 156 million-pound ($174 million) net loss in September from the portfolio of gilts held by the BoE, according to data from the Office for National Statistics.
