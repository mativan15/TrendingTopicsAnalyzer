By Reuters Published: 12:35 EDT, 17 October 2022 | Updated: 12:35 EDT, 17 October 2022 MEXICO CITY, Oct 17 (Reuters) - Mexican President Andres Manuel Lopez Obrador outlined goals to turn a northwestern agricultural state into a green energy center on Monday, ahead of a visit later this month by U.S. climate envoy John Kerry. The plan focuses on expanding solar energy capacity and lithium production in the state of Sonora, where Lopez Obrador said he hoped to meet with Kerry, possibly at a solar plant under construction in the coastal city of Puerto Penasco in the state of Sonora.
