How midlife ‘peakers' are resisting the return to the office

2022-05-27 05:00:00

Kevin Ellis, chief executive of accounting and consulting giant PwC has previously said that 'there is no substitute for being with people face to face to test views and make the right judgement calls'.
'Decisions around how much a business is worth or whether its accounts add up are not ones where you want to be second-guessing views on a screen,' he added. 
For those higher earners still at home, working through the screen seems enough to keep them content, however.
In 2010, research by Nobel prize-winning economist Daniel Kahneman and his Princeton colleague Angus Deaton found that people tend to feel happier the more money they make - but only up to a certain point.
