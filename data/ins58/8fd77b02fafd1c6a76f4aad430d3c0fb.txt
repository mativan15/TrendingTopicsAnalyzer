Like many retail stocks this year, Best Buy (BBY 2.49%) recently reported poor first-quarter numbers and remained pessimistic about the rest of the year. Surprisingly, the shares bounced after the report. Meanwhile, fellow retailers Walmart (WMT 2.13%) and Target (TGT 4.33%) reported better first-quarter numbers, and shares of those companies cratered. Wall Street's expectations helped fuel this strange reaction -- but the analysts who drove those share gains or losses seem to be missing the point.
