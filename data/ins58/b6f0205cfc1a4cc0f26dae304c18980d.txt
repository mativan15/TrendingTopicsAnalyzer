By Reuters Published: 01:55 EDT, 17 October 2022 | Updated: 01:55 EDT, 17 October 2022 The Green Bay Packers have lost two straight games to fall to 3-3 this season, but their struggles to score points have Aaron Rodgers suggesting that the coaching staff "simplify some things" on offense. The Packers have scored 107 points in their six games, the fewest output in any six-game stretch with Rodgers at the helm, according to ESPN Stats & Information. "I just think that based on how we've played the last two weeks, I think it's going to be in our best interests to simplify things for everybody -- for the line, for the backs, for the receivers, especially with (Randall Cobb's) injury.
