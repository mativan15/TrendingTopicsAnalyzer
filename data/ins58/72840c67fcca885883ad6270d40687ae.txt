After high school graduation, teens enter a world of adulthood where they are expected to make major financial decisions that will affect the rest of their lives. Every part of their lives will be affected by their understanding of personal finance: college, debt, first jobs, travel, entertainment, rent, buying a home, starting a family, changing careers, etc. Although this is a necessary life skill, shockingly a personal finance course is only required to graduate in 23 states.
