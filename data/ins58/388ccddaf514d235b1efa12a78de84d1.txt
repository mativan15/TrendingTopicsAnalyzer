By Reuters Published: 22:37 EDT, 29 May 2022 | Updated: 22:37 EDT, 29 May 2022 TOKYO, May 30 (Reuters) - Japan's Nikkei rose on Monday to its highest level in more than one month, led by machinery and technology stocks, as Shanghai's stimulus measures and decision to ease COVID-19 curbs tempered fears of a sharp slowdown in the world's second-largest economy. The Nikkei share average was up 1.8% at 27,263.37, as of 0136 GMT, a level not seen since April 21. The broader Topix was up 1.59% at 1,916.
