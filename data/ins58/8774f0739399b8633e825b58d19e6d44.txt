JHVEPhoto Introduction Canadian banks have long been perceived as stable businesses, where little volatility was to be expected. This was well demonstrated during the financial crisis of 2008, when Canadian banks experienced only a small decline in earnings, quickly followed by a recovery. They are banks that have had largely constant earnings growth and tend to be valued close to intrinsic valuation. They are however also banks that again appear to have fallen below fair valuation, and some that investors should consider.
