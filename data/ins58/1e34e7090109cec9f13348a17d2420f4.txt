Published October 18, 2022 2:57pm EDT Twitter has reportedly frozen its employees' equity award accounts as the clock ticks down for Tesla CEO Elon Musk to close his $44 billion acquisition of the social media company. According to Bloomberg, an employee FAQ page has been updated to notify workers that they will be unable to access or trade shares via its Equity Award Center. Sources told the outlet the change was made "in anticipation of the closing of the pending acquisition of Twitter by an entity controlled by Elon Musk.
