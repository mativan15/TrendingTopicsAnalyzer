Shop prices growing at fastest rate in more than a decade as food inflation worsens
sky.com
economics
2022-05-31 23:08:02

Shop prices grew at the fastest rate in more than a decade during May, spurred on by rapidly-accelerating food inflation.The figures come from the latest BRC-NielsenIQ Shop Price Index, which looks at baskets of food and non-food items and measures the change in price over time.
It found that retail price inflation was 2.8% in May - the highest since July 2011 - and experts have said the situation for shoppers will get "worse before it gets better".Helen Dickinson, chief executive of the British Retail Consortium, said: "Retail prices edged up further as commodity, energy and transport costs continued to climb.
