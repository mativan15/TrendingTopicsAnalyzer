THOMAS BLACK: The high-flying private aviation set sees clear skies ahead
businesslive.co.za
news
2022-10-18 16:19:31

While the superrich have the option to pay cash for aircraft and remain immune to the economic slowdown so far, demand for preowned planes at the lower end of the market — jets that cost less than $15m — is cooling off, said Janine Iannarelli, founder of the aircraft brokerage Par Avion.It is not just the price of the jet and higher interest rates that factor into a purchase decision. An owner has to constantly shell out for pilots, fuel, maintenance, hangar space and other expenses, which are now all experiencing the effects of inflation.
