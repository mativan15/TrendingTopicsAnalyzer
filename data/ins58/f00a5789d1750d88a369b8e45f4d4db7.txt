AgenciesRepresentative imageWomen representation in India Inc boardrooms has improved in recent times but the Covid-19 pandemic has slowed down the pace of growth and much more ground has to be covered to ensure gender parity at workplaces, a new report shows. Women now hold 18% of board seats in India, up from 13% in 2017 and 6% in 2013, said an EY report, 'Diversity in the boardroom: Progress and the way forward', shared exclusively with ET. Almost 95% of Nifty 500 companies have at least one female board member compared to 69% in 2017, it said.
