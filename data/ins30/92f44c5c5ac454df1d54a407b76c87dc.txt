China's Foreign Ministry says its diplomatic missions abroad have the right to 'take necessary measures' to maintain security after British police opened an investigation into the assault of a Hong Kong protesterLONDON -- China's Foreign Ministry said Tuesday its diplomatic missions abroad have the right to 'take necessary measures' to maintain security after British police opened an investigation into the assault of a Hong Kong protester who alleged he was dragged into the Chinese Consulate in Manchester and beaten up during a demonstration.
