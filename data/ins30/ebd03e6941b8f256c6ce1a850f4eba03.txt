By ZHU YUAN | China Daily | Updated: 2022-05-30 07:39 Law enforcement work the scene after a mass shooting at Robb Elementary School in Uvalde, Texas. [Photo/Agencies] The protests outside the National Rifle Association annual conference, which opened in Houston three days after the Uvalda school shooting, highlighted the deep divide in the country on the question of gun control. The protestors were vehemently voicing their desire to be free from the threat of gun violence, while those attending the event were stubbornly clinging to the Second Amendment as support for their desire to own guns.
