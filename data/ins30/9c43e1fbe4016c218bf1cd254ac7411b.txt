We raised £3,000 for our first-home deposit selling old junk and axing holidays

2022-06-02 07:23:38

SOMETIMES simply cutting back isn't enough while saving up for a house deposit - which is why Tom and Frank had a thrifty side hustle selling their old junk too.
The first-time buyers rifled through their unwanted gadgets and furniture and sold them on eBay to boost their savings by over £800.
A Help to Buy loan meant Tom and Frank could take out a smaller mortgage
The couple sold their old gadgets and furniture to raise £800 for their deposit
They also cut back on clothes and meals out to boost their savings
Along with their decision to ditch holidays for three years to save £2,000, these two money saving tricks helped the duo save up nearly half the deposit needed for their £134,995 first home.
