Published: 11:54 EDT, 19 October 2022 | Updated: 11:54 EDT, 19 October 2022 By Tim Reid Oct 19 (Reuters) - The fight to become Arizona´s next attorney general in November´s midterm elections smashed fundraising records this week. One major reason: the normally backwater contest has potentially big implications for U.S. democracy, election experts said. Arizona is a kingmaker state in U.S. presidential elections, and under Arizona law the attorney general must witness the certification of the election result, has the power to challenge certifications in the courts if they violate state law, and must approve the rulebook that governs how elections are run.
