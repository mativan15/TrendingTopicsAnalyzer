Cement is all around us. It's the key ingredient in concrete, and demand is soaring thanks to a global infrastructure and building boom driven by expanding economies. According to the World Economic Forum, many of these projects are much needed and will improve the quality of life and create jobs for millions of people. But there is also a troubling factor: the cement industry generates approximately 7% of global CO2 emissions. One way to reduce emissions in cement production is to substitute clinker, its main component, with materials that provide the same durability with half the environmental impact.
