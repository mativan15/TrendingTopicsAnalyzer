The first anniversary falls next week. Not, as Antonio Conte seemed to hope then, of his appointment as Manchester United manager, but of the well-sourced reports that he wanted to replace Ole Gunnar Solskjaer. echnically, of course, Solskjaer still had a job, though the 5-0 thrashing by Liverpool weakened his hold on it. Even Ed Woodward was starting to realise that perhaps the Norwegian was not the answer after all. A putative successor did not waste any time advertising his interest. Conte looked like the antidote and the antithesis of Solskjaer.
