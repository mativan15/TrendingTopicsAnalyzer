Buying a home should ideally help you improve your financial situation. If you buy the right property, you build equity as you pay down your mortgage and you end up owning a valuable asset. Ideally, your home's value will also rise, so you'll get richer just by owning it. Unfortunately, things don't always work out perfectly for home buyers. In some cases, you could jeopardize your financial security and your other goals if you make a mistake when purchasing your property. Ramit Sethi, author of I Will Teach You To Be Rich, warned about one common error you'll absolutely want to avoid if you don't want this to happen to you.
