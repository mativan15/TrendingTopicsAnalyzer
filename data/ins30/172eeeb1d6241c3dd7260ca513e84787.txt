A cyclist in New York is 25 times more likely to die than a cyclist in Vancouver and is about as likely to die as a cyclist in Auckland and Buenos Aires, according to a new study by the International Transport Forum, an intergovernmental organization.

'Cities should do more to protect pedestrians, cyclists, and motorcycle riders on their streets,' the report found. 'Every minute, someone in the world dies in urban traffic.
