Denver's productive training camp meshed into a promising preseason (3-2), and beginning on Wednesday at Utah, the games matter. The Nuggets' quest for, at minimum, a top-four seed begins against the Jazz, a team that realized it'd maxed out its potential and wasn't going to compete in the West.
The conference is too deep and will be too treacherous for most teams to survive. Friday's preseason opponent, Golden State, has every reason to believe it can repeat as champions, and Wednesday's opponent, the Clippers, have fully re-loaded around Kawhi Leonard and Paul George.
