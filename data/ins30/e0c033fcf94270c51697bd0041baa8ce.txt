Neeva becomes a third choice. So far Neeva has been closed to general users, with a long waiting list for applicants. But Ramaswamy tells me that it's coming to the UK – he hopes – by the autumn.
Those already in the Neeva club are enthusiastic, and I can see why. The absence of the tricksy ads was like being catapulted to an Alpine retreat – after years in the urban smog, you forget how fresh air feels.
Neeva itself offers a vivid illustration of why punters are poorly served today. On a mobile search for 'migraine headache' only three results are immediately visible on the smartphone's screen, and on Google, all three are advertisements.
