Continental Resources stock jumps after agreeing to be bought by founder in deal valuing company at $27 billion
marketwatch.com
news
2022-10-17 11:41:16

Shares of Continental Resources Inc. CLR, -3.18% rallied 8.3% in premarket trading Monday, after the oil producer announced an agreement to be acquired by Omega Acquisition Inc., which is owned by the company's founder Harold Hamm, in a deal that values the company at nearly $27 billion. Under terms of the deal, Omega will launch a tender offer to buy Continental's stock at $74.28 a share, which represents an 8.9% premium to Friday's closing price of $68.22. The bid is also 6.1% above what Hamm offered to pay in June, when he said he'd pay $70 a share to take the company private.
