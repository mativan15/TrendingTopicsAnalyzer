Visit our ETF Hub to find out more and to explore our in-depth data and comparison toolsPassively managed index funds have overtaken actively managed funds' ownership of the US stock market for the first time, data show.Passive funds accounted for 16 per cent of US stock market capitalisation at the end of 2021, surpassing the 14 per cent held by active funds, according to the Investment Company Institute, an industry body.The pattern represents a sharp reversal of the picture 10 years ago, when active funds held 20 per cent of Wall Street stocks and passive ones just 8 per cent.
