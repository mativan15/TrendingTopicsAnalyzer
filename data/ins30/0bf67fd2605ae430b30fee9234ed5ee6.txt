Wall Streets ends two-day rally on recession fears, UK inflation back to 40-year high

US stocks have turned lower after their recent bear market rally as the cost of government borrowing soared, and more warnings come from corporate giants about economic storm clouds on the horizon. In the US, Dow Jones index fell 0.3 per cent, to 30,424, the S&P 500 fell 0.7 per cent, to 3,695, and the Nasdaq Composite lost 0.9 per cent, to 10,681London's FTSE 100 fell 0.2 per cent, to 6,925, the DAX in Germany fell 0.2 per cent, as well, to 12,741, and the CAC 40 in Paris lost 0.4 per cent, to 6,041.
