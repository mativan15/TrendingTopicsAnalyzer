In a famous essay on baseball batting averages, the American scientist Stephen J Gould tackled one of the paradoxes of modern sport: if players, through increased professionalism, more efficient equipment and improved methods of training and nutrition, are getting better with every generation, why do the best not stand out statistically as much as before?The answer lies mainly in the general improvement of those at the lower end of the scale, which allows for fewer outliers at the top. 'The myth of ancient heroes — the greater distance between average and best in the past — actually records the improvement of play through time,' Gould wrote.
