Three years ago, while working as a software engineer at Google, Eugene Kirpichov happened to watch Al Gore's An Inconvenient Truth on a long flight. On the flight back, he watched the sequel. Afterward, he couldn't stop thinking about climate change. 'Every time I would meet someone who was a real [climate] expert, I would ask them, ‘Is it really that bad?'' he says. 'And they'd say, ‘Yeah, it's really that bad.''The more he learned about the problem, the more anxiety and despair he felt. Then the pandemic hit, adding a new source of anxiety.
