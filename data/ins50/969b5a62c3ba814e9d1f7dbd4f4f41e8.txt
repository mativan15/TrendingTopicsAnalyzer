French luxury is axiomatic. While Germany's luxury car brands had to expand into luxury from making taxis and trucks (Mercedes), bubble cars (BMW) and rebadged VW Polos (Audi), the French have always had a lock on luxe. Hermès. Dior. Chanel. Longchamp. Lacoste. Cartier. Veuve Cliquot. Whatever France touches turns to luxury.Except cars. It's an odd thing, but few take French luxury cars seriously, and it's all the fault of French car buyers. French car buyers are sensible people, and they've long since realised that you don't really need a car that's luxurious or expensive.
