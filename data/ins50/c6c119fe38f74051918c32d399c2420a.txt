Emma Kisby, UK and Europe CEO at Cogo getty Consumer apathy is often blamed for the fact that we are not moving to net-zero fast enough. But are consumers truly apathetic or simply overwhelmed? In my experience, usually, it's not that people don't care about the environmental impact of their actions. Research by Deloitte, in fact, shows that many consumers are taking small steps; for example, two-thirds are embracing cutting down on single-use plastics. The complexity of messaging around the climate crisis, however, is giving them "analysis paralysis," which means they panic and then opt for the safe or familiar options (or worse, do nothing).
