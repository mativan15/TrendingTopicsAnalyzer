Apple defends App Store policies, says it stopped $1.5B in 'fraudulent transactions'

DKart/iStock Unreleased via Getty Images Apple (NASDAQ:AAPL) has come under intense regulatory scrutiny over its App Store practices from lawmakers and companies around the world, but the company has defended the digital marketplace, noting it prevented "nearly $1.5 billion in fraudulent transactions" last year. In a release, the tech giant noted the prevention of the transactions, as well as preventing more than 1.6 million apps and app updates due to how it runs the store.
