Winter relief for flood-stricken northern NSW with government funding for roads, rates, services

The NSW government has splashed out more than $100 million for road repairs, rate relief, and non-government services in the state's flood-ravaged Northern Rivers region. Flood-affected ratepayers across seven local government areas will have their rates waivedThere is also $60 million for forestry roads and $13 million for non-government organisationsWhile the money is welcome, some locals still fear the winter months aheadMore than three months on from the start of the catastrophic flood event, many are still homeless and areas are still without power.
