Published: 10:57 EDT, 31 May 2022 | Updated: 10:57 EDT, 31 May 2022 By Suleiman Al-Khalidi AMMAN, May 31 (Reuters) - Jordan's only container port on the Red Sea has been capturing traffic to Syria, Israel and the West Bank as higher ocean freight costs give it an edge over closer but heavily congested Mediterranean ports, its CEO said on Tuesday. Crises from COVID-19 lockdowns to Russia's invasion of Ukraine have fed into a global supply chain crunch, with one study this month showing one-fifth of the global container ship fleet was currently stuck at various major ports.
