By Reuters Published: 00:59 EDT, 18 October 2022 | Updated: 00:59 EDT, 18 October 2022 SHANGHAI, Oct 18 (Reuters) - China stocks edged higher on Tuesday, with state firms seen taking measures to stabilise markets amid the ruling Communist Party's ongoing congress, while Premier Li Keqiang's vow to support growth also helped sentiment. ** The blue-chip CSI 300 Index rose 0.1% by the end of the morning session, and the Shanghai Composite Index added 0.2%. ** The Hang Seng Index climbed 1%, while the Hang Seng China Enterprises Index was up 1.
