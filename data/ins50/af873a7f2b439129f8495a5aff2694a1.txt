Both candidates in Brazil's presidential runoff election this month have offered relief for unpaid debts weighing on family finances and hurting economic growth.But economists and bankers say their solutions are minor band-aids compared to the impact of overextended consumers on Brazil's economy - and lenders themselves are finding new ways to renegotiate delinquent debts and lift that overhang from credit markets.
