There is nothing like a stint in an American hospital, where the medical expenses are the highest in the world, to give Irish medics an insight into how a private healthcare business works.Caroline Whelan had her induction into the US system in the late 1990s when, a year after graduating as an occupational therapist, she travelled with five friends to Florida and found a job working in a private hospital in Mississippi.'I learnt a lot clinically but I also learnt a lot from a business perspective.
