Take us off this knife edge, urge ailing firms
thetimes.co.uk
news
2022-10-21 23:01:00

The resignation of Liz Truss after only 44 days in office and another Conservative Party leadership election have increased uncertainty for businesses already facing a looming recession.There is a clamour for the next prime minister immediately to set out plans to deal with a dizzying range of problems, from higher energy bills and labour shortages to spiralling inflation and interest rates.Underlining dysfunctional relations with No 10 this week, John Allan, the chairman of Tesco and a former president of the CBI, one of Britain's leading business lobby groups, took the unusual step of talking up the prospect of a Labour government, while senior figures, including Lord Rose of Monewden, the Tory peer and chairman of Asda, publicly suggested that Truss's premiership should end.
