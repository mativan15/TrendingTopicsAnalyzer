Here's an uncomfortable thought experiment for the next time you're standing before your wardrobe, running your fingertips through your garments.Count five items containing cotton. Stop at the fifth garment. Now imagine an enslaved human being. Because by this point, you're statistically likely to own their work.Rayhan Asat thinks about this constantly. She's a U.S.-based lawyer who comes from an oppressed minority region of China where forced labour is systemic and which produces one-fifth of the world's cotton.
