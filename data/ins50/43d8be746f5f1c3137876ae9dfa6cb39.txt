The coal shortage has triggered an acute power crisis across India. Coal stockpiles, the fuel used to generate nearly 70% of India's electricity, have dwindled to the lowest in decades, causing nationwide power supply issues. As per April 2022 data, coal stocks at more than 100 thermal power plants in the country have fallen below the 25% mark. And, in more than 50 plants, the stock has fallen below the 10% mark.
So, the questions are: how did India, a power surplus nation since 2017, become power deficit? What factors have fuelled this crisis? And, what happens next?
