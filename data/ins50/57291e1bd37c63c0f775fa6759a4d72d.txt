There was one particular overture that became a siren call to brown internet children in the 2010s.'Waddup everyone it's your girlllllllll Superwoman!'It meant a new video had dropped by Lilly Singh, at the time a niche YouTuber known by the moniker iisuperwomanii, who dressed up like her Indian parents and put on skits in her childhood bedroom.In the midst of a largely white entertainment landscape, there she was, speaking Punjabi and wearing her mom's dupatta on a public platform. Her bits, titled 'My Parents Do This,' 'Sh-t Punjabi Mothers Say' and 'Types of Siblings,' were not only the driving force behind many late-night YouTube binges, they were also the beginning of a new era in diaspora comedy.
