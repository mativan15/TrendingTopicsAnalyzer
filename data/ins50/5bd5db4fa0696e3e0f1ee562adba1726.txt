REUTERS/Blair GableSept 20 (Reuters) - Futures for Canada's main stock index rose on Thursday buoyed by higher crude and metal prices, while investors weighed the possibility of a more aggressive pace of tightening by the Bank of Canada.Futures on the S&P/TSX index were up 0.2% at 7:25 a.m. ET. as their U.S. counterparts also edged up.Oil and metal prices were boosted by reports of China, the world's biggest crude importer, potentially easing its COVID-19 restrictions.
