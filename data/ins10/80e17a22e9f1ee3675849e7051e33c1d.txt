KARACHI, Pakistan, June 2 (Reuters) - Pakistan will slash fuel subsidies for a second time in a week in a bid to control the fiscal deficit and secure International Monetary Fund (IMF) bailout money, the finance minister said on Thursday.Pakistan and the IMF concluded negotiations last week on the resumption of the bailout programme, following which the lender stressed the need to end unfunded subsidies which were costing the cash-strapped country billions per month. read more Minister Miftah Ismail said petrol and diesel prices for consumers have been increased by 17% at the pumps starting on Friday, up 30 Pakistani rupees each per litre.
