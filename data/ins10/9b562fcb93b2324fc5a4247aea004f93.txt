Asian shares join global rebound as Fed hike fears ease, China tech boost

iStockThe United States will not block China from growing its economy, but wants it to adhere to international rules, Secretary of State Antony Blinken said on Thursday in remarks that didn't come as a surprise to investors and political analysts.Asian shares extended overnight global gains thanks to strong results from regional tech firms and U.S. retailers, while investors also took comfort from Federal Reserve minutes showing a pause to its rate hikes is on the cards later this year. The swing in sentiment left the dollar wallowing at one-month lows, with the euro rising to its highest since April 25.
