The pandemic highlighted fundamental differences across classes, races and genders in the United States.

For example, during the lockdown, many 'white-collar' employees were allowed to work remotely, while workers with manual labor and service-oriented jobs were still expected to report to work in person. And women — especially women of color — experienced higher rates of Covid-related job loss due to closures of daycares, schools and other female-dominated industries.

These disparities generated much hotly debated conversation.
