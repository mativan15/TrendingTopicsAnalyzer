Liz Truss's government is hurtling towards another winter of discontent with moves for coordinated industrial action expected to pass this week.It comes as the true scale of industrial action is revealed for the first time, with up to one in 16 employees – 1.9 million people – set to strike or be balloted for industrial action in the coming months, an analysis by The Independent has found.Essential public sector staff such as teachers, nurses and junior doctors could all down tools as they fight for pay rises in the face of soaring inflation.
