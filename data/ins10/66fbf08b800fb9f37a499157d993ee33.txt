5 healthy habits that make a child's observation stronger
hindustantimes.com
news
2022-10-17 06:43:27

For anyone, learning happens through active experiences. Many focus on learning by doing, especially for younger age groups. Learning by observing others, however, is equally impactful and leaves a lasting impression on a child's mind. For instance, they may learn a new sport or develop curiosity about it by simply observing players. On the flip side, children may pick up age-inappropriate words by noticing how adults act and talk around them. Observational learning helps children be more attentive and motivated, retain learned information for a longer period of time, and determine how well they explore and adapt to different situations, based on the cues they picked up from the environment.
