What Jamie Dimon Learned From Alan Greenspan

Look past the fracas about Jamie Dimon's shifting prognosis for the US economy — from cautious optimism a week ago to warning of an imminent 'hurricane.' What matters is that a titan of American finance is prepared to say what too few have over recent decades: Domestic conditions can look pretty good, until they're quickly undercut by adverse events abroad. (And this isn't the first time Russia has been a major player.)  Dimon, chief executive officer of JPMorgan Chase & Co., warned investors Wednesday to brace for an economic shock that's 'coming our way.
