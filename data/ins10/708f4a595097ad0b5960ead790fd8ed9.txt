ROME — As early as Friday, Giorgia Meloni is expected to receive a green light from Italian President Sergio Mattarella to form Italy's right-most government since the end of World War II. She's likely to succeed, getting her cabinet approved in a parliamentary confidence and going on to become Italy's first female prime minister. But most days recently — amid chauvinistic insults and leaked pro-Russia audio tape — it's been hard to tell if her far-right coalition is coming together or unraveling.
