An Unfinished Woman

Lillian Hellman, who grew up in New Orleans and New York, made her reputation as one of America's great playwrights before she was thirty. THE CHILDREN'S HOUR took New York by storm when it opened in 1934, ran for 691 performances on Broadway, and played in every major city in the United States. Her second triumph, THE LITTLE FOXES, which starred Tallulah Bankhead on the stage and Bette Davis in the film version, portrayed the selfishness and hypocrisy of a turn-of-thecentury Southern family and established Miss Hellman as a major American dramatist.
