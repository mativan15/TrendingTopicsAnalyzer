Opposition foreign affairs spokesman Simon Birmingham accused the government of sending out confusing messages on a sensitive topic, saying Labor was 'hiding from its own policy on Israel'.

Penny Wong clarified that the government had not changed its position on West Jerusalem. Credit:Alex Ellinghausen

Federal cabinet reached the decision on Tuesday morning, just hours after the government insisted there had been no change to its position on West Jerusalem.

Foreign Minister Penny Wong has announced Australia will no longer recognise West Jerusalem as the capital of Israel, reversing a controversial 2018 decision by the Morrison government.
