The shocking remarks come as Glasgow City Council, which Labour lost to no overall control in 2017, faced criticism over a lack of major events planned to mark The Queen's 70 years on the throne at the weekend. Councillor Leodhas Massie, of the Green Party, defended the authority's decision, stating public money should instead be spent on tackling the cost of living crisis.The councillor, who represents the Newlands and Auldburn ward, said: "To set aside even a penny of Glasgow City Council's budget for this during a chronic cost of living crisis would be sickeningly offensive.
