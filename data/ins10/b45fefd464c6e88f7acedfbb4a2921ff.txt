How the England T20 team rebuilt through 12 months of chaos
telegraph.co.uk
news
2022-10-18 08:00:00

In Abu Dhabi last November, England had to defend 57 runs from 24 balls to make the T20 World Cup final. Then, thunderous hitting from Jimmy Neesham and Daryl Mitchell got New Zealand to their target with an over to spare. Until that point, almost everything in England's World Cup campaign - besides a series of injuries - had gone to plan.
And so, with only 11 months between T20 World Cups, it was easy to think that England had no need to tweak much: welcome back their superstars Jofra Archer and Ben Stokes from injury, and hope to win the toss in knockout matches.
