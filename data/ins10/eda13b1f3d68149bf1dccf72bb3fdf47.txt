Katrina Shanks is chief executive of Financial Advice NZ. OPINION: Investing in volatile times can really feel like a minefield to those who are new to it or dabble just a bit. At such times, the questions are many: Will the markets drop more? Is this a good time? How do I pick a safer investment? Will I lose money? Of course, the volatility of markets is nothing new. You need only look at the past three years to see that. In 2019, the headlines were all about Brexit, North Korea, Donald Trump being impeached, protests in Hong Kong .
