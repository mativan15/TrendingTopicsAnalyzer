Auston Matthews has one goal in five games. Mitch Marner has one goal in five games. The power-play unit they run is struggling, managing just three goals in five games (two by William Nylander, one by John Tavares).

Yet, the Maple Leafs are 3-2-0 to start the season, six points in five games, a pace that would give them 98 points. You wouldn't be wrong to suggest they should have seven or eight points by now.

Is the glass half full? Or is it half empty?

The expectations of this team are so high that any loss seems like an incredible low.
