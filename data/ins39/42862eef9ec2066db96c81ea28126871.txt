Voice of America 27th May 2022, 22:05 GMT+10More than 12 million immigrants moved through Ellis Island, a primary U.S. federal immigration station in New York, between 1892 and 1954. The assimilation of these newcomers into the great U.S. "melting pot" in their pursuit of the American dream is a key part of the nation's story.Many Americans have come to idealize those early immigrants, mostly Europeans, as somehow more desirable than today's immigrants, who primarily hail from Latin America and Asia and are more likely to be viewed by some as slow to assimilate, potential criminals, a financial drain on the system, and as stealing jobs from the American-born.
