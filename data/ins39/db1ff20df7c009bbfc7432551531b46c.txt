Published: 00:09 EDT, 18 October 2022 | Updated: 00:09 EDT, 18 October 2022 WASHINGTON (AP) - Moments after she flipped a longtime Republican congressional seat in 2018, Iowa Democrat Cindy Axne declared that "Washington doesn't have our back and we deserve a heck of a lot better." Now seeking a third term in one of the most competitive House races, Axne is sounding a similar tone, telling voters she's delivered for Iowans "while Washington politicians bicker." But Axne and other Democrats from the class of 2018 are campaigning in a much different political environment this year.
