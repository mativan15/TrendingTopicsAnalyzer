Germany To Spend 6.3 Billion Euros On Push For Electric Car Charging Points
ibtimes.com
news
2022-10-19 13:00:40

A battery charger sign for electric cars is painted on the ground of a parking ground near the soccer stadium in Wolfsburg, Germany, April 6, 2016. 
The German government approved a plan on Wednesday to spend 6.3 billion euros ($6.1 billion) over three years to rapidly scale up the number of charging stations for electric vehicles across the country, as part of its push towards net zero emissions.The plan envisages a 14-fold increase in the number of charging stations, climbing to 1 million by 2030 from around 70,000 now.
