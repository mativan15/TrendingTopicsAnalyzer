Uncle Sam, the Silver King

BACK in 1933 we used to hear a good deal about President Roosevelt's prestidigitatorial performances. One of these had to do with our silver policy — verily a policy which, as events have demonstrated, now bears a singular kinship with Alice's experiences in Through the Looking-Glass. Alice, you will remember, discovered that ‘ it takes all the running you can do, to keep in the same place.'The silver policy is still in a more or less similar situation. On July 9, 1937, Secretary Morgenthau announced an agreement with China to swap America's surplus gold for China's surplus silver.
