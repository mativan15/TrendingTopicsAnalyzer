The Treasury Department this month began distributing nearly $200 million to five states under the State Small Business Credit Initiative.Photo: Ting Shen for The Wall Street JournalListen to article(2 minutes)A federal pandemic aid program aimed at boosting small businesses' access to capital is getting off the ground more than a year after it was authorized, when firms are now facing the headwinds of high inflation and the growing risks of an economic downturn.The Treasury Department this month began distributing nearly $200 million to five states through the State Small Business Credit Initiative, a $10 billion program that directs money to states, territories and tribal governments for programs that provide capital or encourage private lending.
