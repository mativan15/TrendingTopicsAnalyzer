AgenciesAnand Mahindra is a fan of British humour. (Image: BCCL & Getty)British humour is known for its for sarcastic and irony aimed at the absurd things that occur in everyday life. And, British Prime Minister Liz Truss's resignation on Thursday was the perfect premise for that. Truss's political career wilted faster than the leafy lettuce, thanks to a tabloid stunt. After the British weekly
 The Economist said that the 47-year-old's time would have 'roughly the shelf-life of a lettuce', the Daily Star tabloid in the UK set up a livestream featuring a photo of the UK PM beside a head of the vegetable, dressed in a wig and googly eyes.
