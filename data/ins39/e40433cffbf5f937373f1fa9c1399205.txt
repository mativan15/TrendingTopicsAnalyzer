Slug-eating snake seen feasting on invasive carrier of rat lungworm
newsweek.com
news
2022-10-17 10:37:18

A Terrestrial snail sucker, a snake common to South America, has been caught feasting on an invasive bean slug in a car park in Mexico.This finding could lead to new methods of control for this invasive species, with important implications for food security and human health."It is unknown how long the bean slug has been established in Mexico," Manuel de Luna, a researcher from the Herpetological Society of Northeast Mexico, told Newsweek."It is widespread, from America to Asia, as well as some European countries.
