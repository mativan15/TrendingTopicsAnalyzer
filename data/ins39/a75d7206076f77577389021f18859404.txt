From The Times: June 6, 1922This is the season when the 'confidence' trickster exercises his wiles on oversea visitors to London, and already within a month or so nearly a score of visitors, many of them Americans, have been induced to part with their money by these plausible rogues.To most English people the 'confidence' trick is well known, and they find it difficult to understand how its victims are so easily duped. But the fact remains that the tricksters, well-dressed and apparently well-to-do, often succeed in imposing even on men of the world.
