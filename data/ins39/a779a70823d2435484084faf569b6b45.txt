Industry middlemen driving up prices of generic drug prescriptions as much as 20 percent: research

U.S. consumers are overpaying billions for generic drugs due to the cost being driven up by middle-men in the pharmaceutical industry, according to a report from the University of Southern California (USC).

According to the white paper from USC, business practices carried out by pharmacy benefit managers (PBMs) have 'inflated' the cost of generic medications.
