People walk by Kakao Pangyo Agit, Kakao's headquarters in Seongnam, Gyeonggi, on Monday. [YONHAP] The Kakao crash rendering numerous online services useless disrupted the daily lives of people in Korea as many are reliant on a broad range of its services, from messaging and payments to ride-hailing and music streaming. About 83.6 percent of the Korean population, or 43.2 million people, were using KakaoTalk as of August, which serves as a primary communication channel in their work and private lives.
