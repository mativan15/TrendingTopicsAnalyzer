India's Tata Consumer Products posts strong profit growth on higher demand

BENGALURU, Oct 20 (Reuters) - India's Tata Consumer Products Ltd (TACN.NS) reported a bigger-than-expected rise in quarterly profit on Thursday, helped by higher salt prices and pent-up demand for everything from mineral water to Starbucks coffee.Discretionary spending has been on the rebound as offices reopen, dining out picks up and Indians gear up for the first festive season free of COVID curbs in two years.The consumer goods company, which sells the popular Tata Salt, Himalayan mineral water and runs a joint venture with Starbucks (SBUX.
