A massive coronal mass ejection (CME) was sparked into life on the Sun on June 1 and it can hit the Earth soon, says NASA. Find out the destruction this solar storm is capable of. After a brief spell of peace, a dangerous solar storm is Earth-bound again. According to NASA, a coronal mass ejection (CME) was spotted leaving the Sun on June 1, and there is a strong likelihood that it can hit the Earth on either June 5 or June 6. The solar storm eruption frequency has grown exponentially high in the months of March and April as a particularly notorious sunspot came in direct view of the Earth.
