A Community Thrives awards grants to organizations needing help in meeting the increased demand for their services.

COVID-19 may be loosening its grip on Oklahoma, but faced with a third year of revenue decline, nonprofit organizations won't be free of the pandemic's effects for several years. For many, funding has not kept pace with the increased demand for their services, which include things like rental assistance, mental health services and help for students who have fallen behind due to the shift to virtual learning.
