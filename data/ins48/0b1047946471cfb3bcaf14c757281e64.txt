A worker sorts marijuana plants at Aurora Cannabis' Aurora Sky facility in Edmonton on May 23, 2018.JASON FRANSON/The Globe and MailShares of Aurora Cannabis Inc., ACB-T a former star of Canada's legal weed sector, plummeted another 40 per cent on Friday morning after the company announced an unusual share sale.Aurora sold US$125-million worth of new shares priced at US$2.45 each, amounting to a 10-per-cent discount compared to the price at which the company's shares had last traded. Aurora and its lead underwriters, Canaccord Genuity and BMO Nesbitt Burns, added a warrant to the offering to entice buyers, and its inclusion made the discount even more extreme.
