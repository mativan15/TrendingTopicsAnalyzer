Uganda announces three-week lockdown in bid to stop spread of deadly Ebola virus

news
2022-10-17 08:31:35

Places of worship, bars, gyms, saunas and other entertainment venues will close for the next three weeks, but schools will remain open in the areas affected by Ebola, the Ugandan government saidUgandan Red Cross workers carry a coffin containing an Ebola victim Ugandan residents have been placed under a strict lockdown with a dusk-til-dawn curfew for three weeks in a bid to quell the deadly outbreak of Ebola. President Yoweri Museveni said on Saturday the government was implementing an overnight curfew in the Mubende and Kassanda districts in central Uganda, where all movement, aside from cargos, will be halted.
