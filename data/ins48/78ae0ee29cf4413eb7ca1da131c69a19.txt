Alex Wong/Getty Images News The Fed's mild dovish turn The Minutes of the Federal Open Market Committee from their May 3-4, 2022 FOMC meeting, released last week, point to a mild Fed dovish turn - from what it was an extremely hawkish policy stand. Specifically, the Fed implied that they will quickly or "expeditiously" increase the Federal Funds rate to a neutral level over the summer, and then possibly pause in September to evaluate the effect of the monetary policy tightening on the economy. Here is the quote: Many participants judged that expediting the removal of policy accommodation would leave the Committee well positioned later this year to assess the effects of policy firming and the extent to which economic developments warranted policy adjustments.
