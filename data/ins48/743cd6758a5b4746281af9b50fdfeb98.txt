More former TikTok employees are speaking out publicly about '996 culture' and 'crushing' productivity expectations that saw staff working until midnight and on weekends
businessinsider.com
news
2022-05-27 08:48:15

Former TikTok employees have openly criticized the ByteDance-owned company — which was founded and is headquartered in China — saying they experienced an intense culture of overwork and secrecy about pay.Ex-TikTok employees who worked in the company's San Francisco and New York offices between 2019 and 2022 described a "996 culture" — working 9 a.m. to 9 p.m., six days a week — saying this hurt their mental health and personal lives. The "996" culture is a common working practice in China, especially in its tech industry.
