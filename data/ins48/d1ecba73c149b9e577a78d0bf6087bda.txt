Getty ImagesThe performance of the US dollar index and emerging market currencies and stocks are inversely correlated, and fund managers often use the index as an indicator to measure the strength of emerging market (EM) currencies and stocks.NEW DELHI: The US Dollar index (DXY) that had hit a high of nearly 105 in mid-May thanks to its gains against the Japanese yen and euro, has cooled off significantly in recent days. The index, which measures the American currency's strength relative to a handful of other majors, often referred to as a basket of US trade partners' currencies, fell as low as 101.
