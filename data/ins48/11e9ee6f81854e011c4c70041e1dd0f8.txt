The Hardened Arteries of Business

EVER since October 1917 the Western world has been waiting for the Soviet regime to collapse. It has seemed inconceivable to the Western mind that an economic system so thoroughly imbued with communistic philosophy and so definitely committed to a programme of tearing capitalism out by the roots could maintain itself for any considerable period. Yet the fact is that Sovietism has more than survived, It has not only strengthened its hold upon the imagination of the Russian masses, but, since 1921, it has scored notable successes in increasing the production of the nation and in carrying through an ambitious modernization programme.
