POWER, wood, and water are the essentials of modern paper making, and the greatest of these is water. The sheet on which these words are printed was once almost fluid, a fragile web of fibrous particles in solution, and the central problem in its manufacture was that of adding and then subtracting water.It is water-born electric power which turns the giant machines of the mills, reducing an unending procession of tree trunks to their component fibres and then realigning them in thin sheets ready for the printer.
