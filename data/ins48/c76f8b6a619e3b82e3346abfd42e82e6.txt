How NJ Asian voters want their voices heard in politics

Her friends and family do not understand why she's doing what she's doing. But June Wang is determined.

After more than a decade of helping Republican candidates and not seeing diverse candidates rise through the ranks, Wang, 54, started to ask, "Why not me?"

With her own seed money of $10,000, Wang, of Bridgewater, is seeking one of four open seats in the Bridgewater-Raritan Regional Board of Education race with six other candidates. The Chinese immigrant is knocking on doors as a first-time political candidate for the unpaid position.
