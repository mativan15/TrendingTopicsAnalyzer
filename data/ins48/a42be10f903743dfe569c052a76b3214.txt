mihtiander/iStock via Getty Images If you have been following my Apple (NASDAQ:AAPL) articles, you will know I have been charting the skeleton of an extremely bullish wave structure. Until the Jackson Hole speech that was. The rhetoric of the speech outlined the severity of the economic situation and although the overall market was trying to find bullish traction, its demise ultimately came minutes after the speech. Although Apple is technically still lingering in what can be perceived as a bullish setup, both new product releases along with economic bad news have seen it sink slowly but surely towards the lower echelons of its perceived wave one, leaving this tech giant gasping for air as it desperately tries to cling to anything resembling a bullish third wave.
