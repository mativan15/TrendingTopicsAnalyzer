TV Insider AMC+ and Sundance Now have acquired the U.S. rights to Litvinenko, the upcoming poisoned spy drama starring David Tennant. As reported by Variety, AMC Networks' Sundance Now and AMC+ have picked up the exclusive U.S. rights to the series, while Amazon's Prime Video has landed the drama in Canada. This comes ahead of the show's U.K. debut, which will air on ITV's forthcoming streaming service, Itvx, which launches this fall. Written by George Kay (Lupin), the four-part series tells the real-life story of Alexander Litvinenko, a former Russian Federal Security Services and Kgb officer, who was poisoned to death via polonium in November 2006.
