The Washington Post via Getty Images Cordyceps are a type of mushroom often called 'zombie mushrooms' because, when they infect insects, they can actually take control of their behavior in a way that ensures other insects get infected by the fungus. That's cool enough to serve as something to study, but these mushrooms are also interesting to scientists for another reason: they produce a class of chemical called cordycepin, which is of interest in treating a variety of diseases.
