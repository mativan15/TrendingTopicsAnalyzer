The £230 saving on broadband bills that almost no-one knows about

Millions of households could potentially pay less for their broadband every month through a deal that hardly anyone knows about. And at this time when families are trying to save as much money as possible, this could be an easy way for low-income households to cut their bills.

© PA Social tariffs are available for some low-income households.

Many customers who receive Universal Credit and other benefits can access social tariffs at much lower prices from some providers. People on these tariffs will normally still get the same speed and quality of internet connection but will see their bills come down.
