STRUGGLING Brits on Universal Credit could be in line to get a one-off payment worth as much as £812 to help them pay their bills.
The cash, known as a Budgeting Advance is to help people pay for unexpected payments – but it will have to be paid back.
You have to sign into your Universal Credit account to claim the extra moneyCredit: Alamy
Universal Credit is designed to help with living costs if you're on a low income, out of work or unable to work. 
But sometimes, the welfare system can leave you short and the up-to-£812 you might be entitled to can be another lifeline.
