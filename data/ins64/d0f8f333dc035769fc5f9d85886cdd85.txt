The internet we have today is the one we unknowingly asked for over the past decade and change: Since the introduction of the Facebook like in 2009, we've been inputting countless likes, retweets, views, and various other nudges to prompt algorithmically-driven platforms to give us more of what we want. Or, at least, what we think we want. Somehow, we've arrived at the current chaotic hellscape of online content, where there's more of it than ever, but it's just as difficult—if not more laborious—to sift through it all and find the good stuff.
