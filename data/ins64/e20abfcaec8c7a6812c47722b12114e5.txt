Houston Astros players celebrate after defeating the Seattle Mariners.Photo: Steph Chambers/Getty ImagesBy Tim BrownUpdated Oct. 16, 2022 12:15 am ETListen to article(2 minutes)SEATTLE—Nearly 48,000 playoff-starved fans crammed into T-Mobile Park on Saturday for the Seattle Mariners' first home postseason game in three days short of 21 years. After more than six hours, 18 combined pitchers held the game exactly where it had begun, at 0-0. The Houston Astros—two games to none leaders in the best-of-five series—were not yet moving on, and the Mariners were stubbornly resisting elimination.
