REUTERS/Jennifer LorenziniMILAN, Oct 17 (Reuters) - Italy's Monte dei Paschi di Siena (BMPS.MI) launched on Monday a new share sale, its seventh in 14 years, seeking to raise up to 2.5 billion euros ($2.4 billion) to fund its latest turnaround plan.MPS, which is owned by the state following a 2017 bailout, is offering shareholders 374 new shares for each three shares owned at a price of 2 euros each.
