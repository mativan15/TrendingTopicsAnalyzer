Published: 08:56 EDT, 17 October 2022 | Updated: 09:01 EDT, 17 October 2022 Ajax was meant to be the British Army's first new armoured vehicle in a generation, designed to make the military more deadly and dangerous on the battlefield. A massive £5.5bn was set aside for the project - but it has been a disaster that has been plagued by glitches, with prototypes having damaged the hearing of the troops who tested it and made other personnel sick.Now, with £3.2bn of taxpayers' cash having been pumped into the scheme - which has so far failed to deliver a single one of the promised 589 tanks into service - fresh question marks are swirling over whether it is time for the Ajax to be axed entirely.
