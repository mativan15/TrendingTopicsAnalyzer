Upcoming summer season will be crucial for Newfoundland bus company, says owner
cbc.ca
news
2022-05-28 14:45:00

DRL Coach Lines is struggling with high fuel prices and pandemic losses. (Canadian Public Transit Discussion Board)The owner of a Newfoundland passenger bus company says ridership is down, gas prices are up — and he doesn't know how long his company can continue.Jason Roberts, owner of DRL Coach Lines, says his company is losing money every month."With the pandemic, we were down to about 10 or 12 per cent of our normal ridership. Even prior to the pandemic, our ridership, overall, is down over the last 15 years — probably 50-60 per cent of what it was at that time," Roberts told CBC News on Thursday.
