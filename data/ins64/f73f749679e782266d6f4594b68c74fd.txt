If the first days of the U.S. Supreme Court's new term felt dizzying, buckle up. It's going to be a scary ride, writes The Emancipator contributor Brandon Hasbrouck, a professor at Washington and Lee University School of Law. It is fitting the term opened in October: Cases that will likely bring an end to affirmative action at public colleges and universities, and private schools receiving federal funding will be argued on Oct. 31. What will play out is likely to become, as Hasbrouck writes, 'a Halloween massacre.
