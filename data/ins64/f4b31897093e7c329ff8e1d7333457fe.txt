"Spend some money!" That was the chant that came from the away end as Arsenal drew 0-0 with Leicester City on August 20, 2016. It was a familiar refrain from the Gunners fans and this one came after Arsenal had bought just one player in the summer transfer window, spending £35million to land Borussia Monchengladbach midfielder Granit Xhaka.In the latter years of Arsene Wenger's reign, the club had become synonymous with frugality. Supporters were frequently angry at what they saw as a lack of ambition coming from the hierarchy of their club, with their rivals frequently outspending Arsenal in the transfer market.
