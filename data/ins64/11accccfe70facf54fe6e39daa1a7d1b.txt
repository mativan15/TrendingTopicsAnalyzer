Published: 17:36 EDT, 21 October 2022 | Updated: 17:36 EDT, 21 October 2022 The federal government has set some lofty environmental ambitions including warding off new extinctions and protecting 30 per cent of Australia's land and oceans this decade. It's promised meaningful climate change action, super-charged efforts to shift to renewable energy, a once-in-a-generation rewrite of failing environment laws and an independent watchdog to enforce them. It has plans to reverse the fortunes of 110 priority threatened species and safeguarded 20 places of special environmental significance that will serve as "mini Noah's arks" for Australian wildlife.
