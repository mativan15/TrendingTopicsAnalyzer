A successful business isn't just one that makes money--it's one that serves as a pillar of its community, near and far.That paradigm--the one summed up by "corporate social responsibility" (CSR)--is now generally accepted as true. For proof, 92% of S&P 500 companies published sustainability reports in 2020. A mere ten years prior, only 20% did.CSR may be a modern necessity, but it's still brand new.As such, not every company knows how to "give back" meaningfully.As Andrew Carnegie's famous quote goes, "It is more difficult to give money away intelligently than to earn it in the first place.
