Published: 01:01 EDT, 16 October 2022 | Updated: 01:01 EDT, 16 October 2022 If exercise were a pill, say the experts, every woman in Britain would be prescribed it. According to a growing body of research, being physically active can reduce the risk of breast cancer by around 20 per cent. The latest study to support these findings, published in September ahead of Breast Cancer Awareness Month, found that women who did vigorous exercise, such as jogging at least three times a week, reduced their risk by 38 per cent.
