Scott Gillingham says he'll find the money to create a new safe space in a Winnipeg North End neighbourhood if he's elected mayor. (Sam Samson/CBC)Scott Gillingham says he'll find the money to create a new 24-hour safe space in a North End neighbourhood if he's elected mayor this fall.The St. James councillor made the announcement Wednesday morning in front of the Magnus Eliason Recreation Centre, on Langside Street in Winnipeg's West End.He committed to extending the funding for that site, and one other core-area safe space, until 2026, and increasing the amount of funding.
