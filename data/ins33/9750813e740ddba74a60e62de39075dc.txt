If Arsenal want to achieve their aim of becoming a team that fights for trophies on all fronts, then Kieran Tierney's situation should become the norm.Since joining the club, Tierney has been virtually undroppable, and whenever he has been out injured, the team's dip in performance has been stark.That, however, is no longer the case, as the arrival of Oleksandr Zinchenko has left Tierney fighting for his place. He has started just three Premier League games this season and it is why the Europa League has become crucial for the Scot, as he looks to win his place back.
