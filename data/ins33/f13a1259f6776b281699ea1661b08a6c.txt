An investor monitors a screen displaying stock information at the Abu Dhabi Securities Exchange June 25, 2014Oct 19 (Reuters) - Major Gulf stock markets eased in early trade on Wednesday, tracking oil prices lower, with the Dubai index on course to snap four sessions of gains.Crude prices, a key catalyst for the Gulf's financial markets, turned negative and extended losses from the previous session.On Tuesday, Brent fell by 1.7% and WTI by 3.1% to their lowest in two weeks on reports that U.S. President Joe Biden planned to release more barrels from the Strategic Petroleum Reserve (SPR).
