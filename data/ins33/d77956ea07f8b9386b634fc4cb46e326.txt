German cybersecurity chief Arne Schönbohm fired after claims of Russian ties

The head of Germany's national cybersecurity agency has been sacked and is facing disciplinary proceedings after he was accused of maintaining links to the Russian intelligence services.Arne Schönbohm, 53, had been heavily criticised for his involvement in an IT lobby group with ties to several arms of the Kremlin, including a company run by a former KGB agent.The German interior ministry said the allegations against Schönbohm had 'substantially damaged the public's trust in his impartiality . . .
