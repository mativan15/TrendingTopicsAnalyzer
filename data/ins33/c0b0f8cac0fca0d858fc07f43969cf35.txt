By Reuters Published: 14:09 EDT, 21 October 2022 | Updated: 14:09 EDT, 21 October 2022 By MacDonald Dzirutwe LAGOS, Oct 21 (Reuters) - Nigeria's presidential frontrunner Bola Tinubu on Friday promised to remove a fuel subsidy if elected next February, adding that his government would ramp up oil production and deregulate midstream gas prices within six months. Nigerians will elect a successor to President Muhammadu Buhari, who steps down next year after serving the two presidential terms mandated by the constitution.
