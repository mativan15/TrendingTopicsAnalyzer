Abstract
This paper is concerned with the development of a system to support Scientific
Visualisation by utilising a computer’s sound channel. The sound mappings presented
here are intended to support understanding and interaction with surface data. This is
done by using sound signals associated with methods of analysis of surfaces to help the
interpretation of surface shape, data, and classification. The approach to Supportive
Sonification Development is discussed. Several examples of sound mappings are
presented and the implications for data interaction are discussed.
