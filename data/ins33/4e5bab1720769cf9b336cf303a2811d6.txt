Still lifes were long considered a lesser genre in the art academies of Europe. How could fruits, flowers, candles and motley objects compete with sweeping landscapes and epic mythological scenes? Today, they are among the preferred genres in art history, and Paris is paying homage to them in a major Louvre Museum exhibition on the subject that examines the human attachment to 'things.
