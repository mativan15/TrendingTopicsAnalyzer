The U.S. economy isn't in a recession yet, even though some forecasters think it's heading there. The labor market remains strong and consumer spending is holding up. If there is a recession in 2023, it'll probably be a mild one.

The housing market is in for a much wilder ride. The rapid rise in interest rates this year, triggered by the Federal Reserve's aggressive inflation-fighting regime, was bound to end an epic housing boom. Existing-home sales, which account for most housing activity, have now declined for eight months in a row.
