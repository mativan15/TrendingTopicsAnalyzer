By Reuters Published: 23:23 EDT, 30 May 2022 | Updated: 23:23 EDT, 30 May 2022 TOKYO, May 31 (Reuters) - Japan's Nikkei index inched higher in see-saw trade on Tuesday, with investors being cautious about the global economic outlook, while oil explorers jumped to be the top gainer among major sub-indexes. The Nikkei share average rose 0.13% to 27,404.14 by the midday break, struggling to maintain the upbeat momentum seen in the previous session. The Nikkei had touched its highest in more than a month on Monday after Shanghai announced stimulus measures and a decision to ease a city-wide lockdown.
