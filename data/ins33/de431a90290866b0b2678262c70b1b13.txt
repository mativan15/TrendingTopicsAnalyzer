The country's most powerful man and its wealthiest man exchanged words Friday after President Joe Biden made a dig at Tesla CEO Elon Musk over a widely circulating report he hopes to shed a large portion of his company's workforce due to concerns about the economy. President Joe Biden, left, and Tesla CEO Elon Musk, right, engaged in a spat Friday. Getty Images Asked by a reporter about a Reuters report that Musk wants to fire 10% of Tesla's workforce due to his 'super bad feeling' about the economy, Biden pointed to several other American companies adding to their workforces, saying he wishes Musk 'lots of luck on his trip to the moon.
