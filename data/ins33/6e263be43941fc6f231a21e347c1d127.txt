REUTERS/Dylan MartinezLONDON, Oct 17 (Reuters) - This year's long, warm summer produced a bumper harvest for English wine producer Chapel Down (CDGP.ASE), it said on Monday, good news for fans of the flourishing local fizz industry.Chapel Down, based in Kent, southern England, is the country's wine largest producer. In a statement it said its harvest jumped 43% this year, enabling it to make 2 million bottles of sparkling and still wines, up by a third from the previous year.
