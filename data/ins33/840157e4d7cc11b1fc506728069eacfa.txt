Film-maker and theorist Nina Menkes takes to the stage in this docu-essay slash movie lecture: a fierce and focused polemic reviving the subject of the 'male gaze' for the #MeToo era. Starting with an interview with British critic Laura Mulvey (described by Menkes as the 'original gangster' who invented the term), Menkes shows us that the way the camera looks at women, and everything else, is not a transparent, value-free business. On the contrary: with men so overwhelmingly in charge, it is an activity of coercion and imposition, determined by gender politics.
