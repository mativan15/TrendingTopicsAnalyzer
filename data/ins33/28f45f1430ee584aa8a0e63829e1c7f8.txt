By Reuters Published: 22:58 EDT, 31 May 2022 | Updated: 22:58 EDT, 31 May 2022 TOKYO, June 1 (Reuters) - Japanese shares rose on Wednesday, with automakers leading the rally after a positive report, however caution ahead of the U.S. monthly non-farm payrolls numbers due this week capped gains. The Nikkei share average rose 0.71% to 27,472.49 by the midday break, while the broader Topic advanced 1.30% to 1,937.61, "For the Nikkei to cross its most recent high on April 21, we need to see some catalysts that would ease investor sentiment," said a strategist at a domestic brokerage, adding that Wall Street's gain would also be needed.
