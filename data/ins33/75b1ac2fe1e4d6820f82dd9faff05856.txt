RBI likely sold dollar to lift rupee off record lows: Traders

MUMBAI - The Indian central bank likely sold dollars via state-run banks on Thursday after worries over surging U.S. Treasury yields pushed the local currency to a record low, traders told Reuters. The Indian rupee was last trading at 82.9350 per U.S. dollar, compared with the day's low of 83.2625 and 83.0200 in the previous session.
