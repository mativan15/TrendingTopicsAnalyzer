Liz Truss has been a bit preoccupied, so she may have missed something that could transform our understanding of Britain's weak economic growth. But government officials are scrambling to work out what it means for the remains of the prime minister's 'growth, growth, growth' agenda.Economic growth is complicated and views differ on why Britain's has been so sluggish. Some, like Truss, blame taxes and red tape; others argue for more state intervention. But most agree on one thing: Britain has suffered from low investment in research and development.
