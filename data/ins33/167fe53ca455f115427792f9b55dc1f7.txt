At a national level we believe that Office for Health Improvement and Disparities (OHID) should be made independent of the Government both to ensure credibility and so that it can drive the strategy forward at pace.(8,9) It should also be renamed, include health promotion in its title and, base its actions on the principles and values set out in the Ottawa Charter for Health Promotion.(10,11)
Directors of public health will be critical to the success of a new positive health strategy, developing constructive working between national and local government, the NHS, and local organisations and communities.
