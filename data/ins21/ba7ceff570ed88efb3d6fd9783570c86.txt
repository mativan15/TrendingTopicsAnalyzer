By Reuters Published: 00:37 EDT, 31 May 2022 | Updated: 00:37 EDT, 31 May 2022 SHANGHAI, May 31 (Reuters) - Rallying consumer and high-tech firms helped lift Chinese shares on Tuesday, as investors cheered news of Shanghai's imminent reopening and new manufacturing surveys reflected the impact of easing COVID-19 curbs. ** An official Chinese manufacturing survey on Tuesday showed activity contracted more slowly in May as virus curbs were relaxed, but ongoing controls on movement continue to dim the growth outlook.
