Jerusalem may be about 14,000 kilometres away from Canberra, but its "status" continues to court controversy.Yesterday, Australia's Labor government reversed the 2018 decision by the Coalition to recognise West Jerusalem as Israel's capital. The flip is considered symbolic and has been welcomed by many Palestinians.However, critics argue it undermines Israel's ability to choose its own capital city.This is what the decision means, and the ramifications it could have.What has Australia done historically?Australia forged its diplomatic relations with Israel in 1949, with the opening of the Australian embassy in Tel Aviv, and the Israeli embassy in Canberra.
