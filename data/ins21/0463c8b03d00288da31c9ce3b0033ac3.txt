is fast closing the gap with India's No.2 carmaker Hyundai Motor in the number of passenger cars sold, helped by strong demand for its range of SUVs and improved sourcing of semiconductors. A maintenance shutdown at Hyundai's India plants last month also aided Tata Motors in this numbers game. In May, Tata Motors posted its highest-ever monthly sales at 43,341 units, overtaking Hyundai Motor India which sold 42,293 units. This is the second time in the last six months that Tata Motors has overtaken its South Korean rival in the highly competitive Indian passenger vehicle market.
