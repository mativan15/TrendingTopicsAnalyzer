Dondi Tawatao/Getty Images News Lockheed Martin (NYSE:LMT) rose 0.7% in premarket trading after the defense contractor reported Q3 adjusted EPS of $6.87, beating estimates by $0.15. Its revenue of $16.58 billion missed the consensus estimate by $110 million. The company added as much as $14 billion to its stock buyback program. It plans $4 billion in repurchases of common stock during the final three months of 2022, bringing the yearly total to $8 billion. Lockheed's stock had climbed 12% by the end of trading on Oct.
