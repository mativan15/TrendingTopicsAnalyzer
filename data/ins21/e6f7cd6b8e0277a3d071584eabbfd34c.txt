 A former London, Ont., MPP who was also the first woman to become the attorney general of Ontario passed away on Tuesday at the age of 76.
Marion Boyd was elected as the NDP candidate for London North in 1985.She was promoted to attorney general of Ontario in 1993, becoming the first woman to hold the position as well as the first non-lawyer.In the same year, Boyd introduced Bill 167 that would have provided same-sex couples with rights mostly equal to those of heterosexual couples.
