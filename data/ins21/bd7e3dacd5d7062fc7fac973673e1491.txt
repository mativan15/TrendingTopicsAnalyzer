Sweden Elects Right-wing PM Backed For First Time By Far-right
ibtimes.com
news
2022-10-17 12:45:31

Ulf Kristersson was elected premier by the Swedish parliament with a wafer-thin majority of three votes
Sweden's parliament on Monday narrowly elected conservative leader Ulf Kristersson as prime minister, leading the country's first government to be shored up by the far-right Sweden Democrats.Kristersson, 58, was elected by a wafer-thin majority of three votes, after announcing a deal on Friday to form a governing coalition comprising his Moderate Party, the Christian Democrats and the Liberals.
