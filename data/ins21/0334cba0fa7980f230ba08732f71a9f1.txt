By Reuters Published: 22:34 EDT, 18 October 2022 | Updated: 22:34 EDT, 18 October 2022 BEIJING, Oct 19 (Reuters) - Prices of base metals rebounded on Wednesday as traders found comfort in robust September U.S. factory production data that signalled the manufacturing sector in the world's biggest economy remains on reasonable footing. Three-month copper on the London Metal Exchange was up 0.6% at $7,463 a tonne, as of 0205 GMT.
