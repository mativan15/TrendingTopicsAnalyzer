Douglas Rissing Co-produced with Treading Softly Have you ever had to admit to being wrong? It can be incredibly hard. People often refuse to admit error, even when the facts to the contrary are clearly present in front of them. I'm not talking about divergent views of the same set of data. That happens frequently in the market when two investment methods or goals view data differently. Take a trader and a long-term investor for example: The trader sees a price drop as a missed call and a reason to pack it in and move on.
