Money Makeover: ‘How do we replace £9k-a-month income?'
telegraph.co.uk
news
2022-06-06 05:00:00

The diagnosis of a long-term illness can throw even the most carefully planned of retirement strategies off-course. Nicola Philips, 62, from Gerrards Cross, Buckinghamshire, is trying to plan around her husband's Parkinson's disease so that they don't have to compromise on the comfortable retirement that they have worked so hard for.
Tom Philips, who is 61, earns £9,500 a month from a health insurance scheme, which is their main source of income. However, this is due to end in four years – and the couple are worried about the shortfall.
