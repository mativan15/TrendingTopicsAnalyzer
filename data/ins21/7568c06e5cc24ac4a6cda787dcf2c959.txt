Published: 20:02 EDT, 16 October 2022 | Updated: 20:04 EDT, 16 October 2022 The Archbishop of Canterbury has said he is ‘deeply sceptical' of so-called trickle-down economics, warning there is ‘no moral case' for government budgets that disproportionately affect the poorest.Speaking on a tour of Australia, Justin Welby also backed climate activists, saying he is worried for a difficult winter.Amid chaos in the UK Government, the Archbishop expressed concerns about tax cuts for the wealthiest. And although he said he did not want to be ‘party political', the comments come as Liz Truss battles to retain her premiership following dramatic policy U-turns.
