Starbucks (SBUX -0.47%) has been one of the all-time great investments with shares of the world's largest coffee chain returning over 17,000% since its debut 30 years ago in 1992 .
But the year of its 30th anniversary of going public hasn't been kind to the Seattle-based company thus far with shares down 33% year to date and nearly 40% off their 52-week highs. The shares have slumped because of inflation (both wage- and commodity-related), staffing issues, and tensions with employees who are seeking to unionize.
