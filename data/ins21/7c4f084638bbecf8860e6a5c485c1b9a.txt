Image caption, Nicky has to have kidney dialysis for five hours every other dayWhile the cost of living is soaring, millions of people are coping with the uncertainty of whether their benefits will rise with inflation. In Sunderland, where one in 13 of the working-age population claims out-of-work benefits, Nicky and Alison are struggling to survive. "I'm freezing right now, I'm shivering while I'm on dialysis, but I won't put the heating on because it's too expensive," says Nicky Telford. The 43-year-old, who used to work in a factory, has to have kidney dialysis for five hours every other day, since suffering a blood clot.
