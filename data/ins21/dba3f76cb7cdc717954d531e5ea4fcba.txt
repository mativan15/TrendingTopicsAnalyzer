Photo: Stephen Lam/Associated Press Many parents and grandparents use tax-advantaged '529' savings plans to put away money for a child's college education. But what happens if the student wins a scholarship, graduates early or otherwise doesn't require the full amount already contributed to the plan? Many account owners with unused savings in 529s assume they have two options: Leave the money in the account to fund further education for the beneficiary—or withdraw it and pay the resulting taxes and penalties.
