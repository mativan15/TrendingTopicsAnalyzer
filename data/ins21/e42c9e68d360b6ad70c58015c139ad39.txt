Published: 21:48 EDT, 31 May 2022 | Updated: 21:48 EDT, 31 May 2022 BEIJING, June 1 (Reuters) - China's factory activity shrank less sharply in May as COVID-19 curbs eased and some production resumed, a private sector survey showed on Wednesday, improving from a 26-month low in April. The Caixin/Markit Manufacturing Purchasing Managers' Index (PMI) rose to 48.1 in May from 46.0 the previous month and was slightly above a Reuters poll of 48.0. May's contraction was the second-sharpest slump since February 2020, suggesting the recovery remains fragile.
